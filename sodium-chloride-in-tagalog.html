3. How many molecules of sodium chloride (NaCl) are there in 5.0 moles of sodium chloride?


How many molecules of sodium chloride (NaCl) are there in 5.0 moles of sodium chloride?

Sodium chloride has a molar mass of 22.99 g/mol (Na+) + 35.45 g/mol (Cl-) = x g/mol.

The number of moles is then calculated by dividing the mass of the sodium chloride sample (5 grams) by the molar mass.

Multiply this amount by 6.22*1023 molecules/mol, which is Avogadro's number. (Sodium )

A chemical species with at least one covalent bond is known as a molecule.

Sodium chloride is an ionic compound that contains no "molecular" or "polyatomic" ions (charged covalent molecules).

A "formula unit" is a collection of the lowest "net neutral" integer ratio of anion and cation: for example, NaCl, Mg(OH)2, and so on. (Sodium )
