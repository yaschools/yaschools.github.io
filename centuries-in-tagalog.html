"Sa Ilalim ng Walang Tower: 9/11" ni Art Spiegelmen noong 2004 na nagsasalaysay tungkol sa teroristang pag-atake noong Setyembre 11 sa Amerika. Ito rin ay nagpapakita ng mga traumang at tragikong karanasan ng mga Amerikano habang ang 9/11 teroristang pag-atake.

1 ENGLISH

In the Shadow of No Tower: 9/11 is a graphic novel by Art Spiegelman published in 2004. It tells the story of the terrorist attack on September 11, 2001 in America and its aftermath. The book depicts the traumatic and tragic experiences of American people during this event, as well as their resilience in the face of tragedy. Through a combination of illustrations and text, Spiegelman conveys both his own personal experience with 9/11 as well as those of other survivors. He also examines how this event has shaped our collective memory and identity since then. The book is an important work that captures both the horror and hope that came out of this tragedy.

2 TAGALOG

Ang "The Barn Owl's Wondrous Capers" ni Sarnath Banerjee na inilabas noong 2009 ay nagpapakita ng 18th century Calcutta na nagnanakaw sa alamat ng "Wandering Jew".

2 ENGLISH

The Barn Owl's Wondrous Capers is a 2009 graphic novel by Sarnath Banerjee. The story follows the adventures of a mysterious figure known as the "Wandering Jew" in 18th century Calcutta. The Wandering Jew is an old myth about a man who was cursed to wander the earth for eternity, and in this story, he is used as a metaphor for the exploitation of people from different backgrounds and cultures that took place during this time period. Through his journey, he encounters various characters who represent different aspects of life in Calcutta at that time, such as poverty, superstition, and religious intolerance. Ultimately, the Wandering Jew serves as an allegory for how people can be taken advantage of when they are seen as outsiders or "others".
