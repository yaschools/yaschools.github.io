6. This means restricted access as a security measures


What does restricted access mean?

This means that there is a limit on access to a certain service, product, location, etc. The limit is placed on people who do not meet certain criteria.

For example, the building administration may place restricted access to its swimming pool. It is accessible but only to its residents. The criteria here is being a resident, so if you are not one, you cannot enter the area where the swimming pool is.

Another example is the restricted access to a school's online portal. The website may be open to all so that they can view information on the school, news, and admission information. However, an online portal may only be limited to people who have an employee identification number or student identification number. If you do not have the identification number, you cannot access certain data or information like your own grades, your enrolled classes, or your performance evaluation.

Is restricted access a type of security measure?

Yes, it is a type of effective security measure. Before individuals or groups of people can gain access to a certain thing, they must have clearances that meet the criteria.

The criteria are set by the administration. If these people do not qualify, it means they are not meant to view, use, or be in the area at all. This prevents the leakage of important information.

Restricted access is also used in place to protect or preserve certain information, data, people, or areas.

14. in safety signs what is the meaning of 5t weight restriction?​


This sign is used to indicate that there is a maximum weight limit of 5 tonnes for the area in which the sign is placed. This is usually placed in areas where there is a risk of collapse or other damage if heavier objects are present. This sign is important in ensuring the safety of people and property in the area.

The normal hours of work an employee has to render must not exceed eight (8) hours a day and should be exclusive of the one (1) hour daily lunch break. Philippine laws, however, do not prohibit work done for less than eight hours.
