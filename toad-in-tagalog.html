1. are frogs and toads alike 5:example frogs and toads​


Answer:

Frogs and toads have a lot in common. They are both amphibians in the order Anura, which means “without a tail.” Toads are a sub-classification of frogs, meaning that all toads are frogs, but not all frogs are toads. They both reproduce in water, and they even look alike.

6. Is toad tail-less? Please answer.


Is toad tailless? The answer is YES.

Toads belong to the same order as frogs. This order is called Anura which literally means "tailless". Toads have start as a tadpole which had tails but are limbless. They use their tails to move around the water which is their usual habitat until they grow and metamorphose. Once a tadpole stars growing limbs, their tails also get shorter until it totally disappears. One reason speculated for the disappearance of their tail is the "theory of use and disuse". This theory implies that a body part which is less used by an animal and is deemed to not be a factor of survival will eventually be eliminated from the body. Hence, the tail of the toad disappeared because the toad do not need it once they become adults.

Answer:

Frogs and toads do have more similarities than differences. Here are some additional similarities and differences:

Similarities:

Both are cold-blooded and require a moist environment to survive.Both undergo metamorphosis from tadpole to adult form.Both have permeable skin that allows for gas exchange and can absorb moisture and nutrients.Both are important predators of insects and other small invertebrates.

Differences:

Frogs typically have smooth, moist skin, while toads have dry, warty skin.Frogs have longer hind legs built for jumping, while toads have shorter legs and tend to walk or crawl.Frogs tend to have a more aquatic lifestyle, while toads are more terrestrial.Frogs have teeth in their upper jaw, while toads do not.

It's worth noting that the terms "frog" and "toad" are not scientific classifications, but rather common names used to describe certain groups of amphibians. In some cases, animals that are commonly called "toads" are actually more closely related to frogs.
