Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a small village nestled at the foot of a majestic mountain. The villagers lived a simple and peaceful life, but they always felt a sense of awe and respect for the mountain, which they believed was home to powerful spirits.

One day, a group of strangers arrived in the village. They were a team of scientists who had come to study the mountain's geology and wildlife. The villagers were curious about the strangers, but also a bit wary. They had heard stories about outsiders who had come to exploit their resources and desecrate their sacred sites.

The leader of the team, a woman named Dr. Lee, approached the village elder, a wise and respected man named Kofi. "Greetings, elder," she said with a warm smile. "We come in peace and with respect for your ways. We only wish to learn more about your beautiful mountain and its secrets."

Kofi studied her for a moment, then nodded. "I see no harm in sharing our knowledge with those who seek it with humility and openness," he said. "But you must understand that our mountain is more than a mere object of study. It is a living being, with its own will and desires. We must treat it with care and reverence."

Dr. Lee listened attentively and nodded. "I understand, elder. We will do our best to honor your traditions and protect the mountain's integrity. We are not here to exploit or harm, but to learn and share."

Over the next few weeks, the scientists worked hard to collect data and samples from the mountain, while also respecting the villagers' customs and beliefs. They even invited some of the villagers to join them in their research, and showed them how their findings could benefit the local ecosystem and economy.

As they packed up to leave, Dr. Lee approached Kofi once more. "Thank you for your hospitality and wisdom, elder," she said. "We have learned so much from you and your people. We hope to continue this partnership and friendship in the future."

Kofi smiled and clasped her hand. "The mountain has spoken to me, Dr. Lee," he said. "It has told me that your intentions are pure and your hearts are open. You and your team are always welcome here, as long as you remember to listen to the mountain's voice and honor its spirit."

18. halimbawa ng fiction at di fiction


Ang piksyon o kathang-isip ay isang kwentong hindi totoo. Ito ay madalas binabasa upang malibang. Ang mga tauhan ay maaaring mayroong katangian gaya ng totoong tao, gayunman, sila ay gawa-gawa lamang ng manunulat.  

Ang mga uri ng piksyon ay nobela, maikling kwento, at maikling nobela (novella)

Halimbawa ng piksyon:

Les Miserables (Victor Hugo)

Harry Potter Series (JK Rowling)

Para Kay B (Ricky Lee)

Bakit Babae ang Naghuhugas ng Pinggan

Ang di-piksyon o ang di kathang-isip ay mga akdang naglalarawan ng katotohanan. Ang mga pangyayari ay nangyari sa totoong buhay. Tunay rin ang mga tauhan sa kwento. Minsan ito ay naisusulat gamit ang pampanitikang wika.

Ang mga halimbawa ng di-piksyon ay bayograpiya, awtobayograpiya, sulat, sanaysay, riserts recipe book , etc.

Halimbawa:

Diary of Anne Frank (Anne Frank)

Besties Book Club (Georgina Wilson at Solenn Heusaff)

Life and Works of Jose Rizal

Talambuhay ni Andres Bonifacio

Story of A Soul (St. Therese of Lisieux)

Didache (Feast Books)

Personal na diary
