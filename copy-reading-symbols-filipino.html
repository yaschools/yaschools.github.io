25. Symbol of Filipino Nationalism


truee

Symbol of Filipino Nationalism

Answer:

The Philippine flag, also known as "Pambansang Watawat" or "Ating Bandila," is considered as the ultimate symbol of Filipino nationalism. It is composed of a white equilateral triangle, symbolizing liberty, equality, and fraternity; a blue field, symbolizing peace, truth, and justice; and a red field, symbolizing patriotism and valor. At the center of the white triangle is a golden-yellow sun with eight primary rays, each representing the eight provinces that first revolted against Spanish colonization. The three stars within the sun symbolize the three major geographical regions of the Philippines, namely Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. The Philippine flag is a powerful representation of the Filipino people's struggle for freedom, sovereignty, and national identity.
