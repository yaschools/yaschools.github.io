28. explain this statement about the pathogen "All the pathogens are parasites but all the parasites are not pathogens".


Answer:

The pathogen takes away the nutrient and disrupts with the plant's necessary roles, while parasites only get food and do not interact with the host's basic functions. Organisms that obtain their food materials from other living things are known as parasites (the host). While all pathogens are parasites, parasites are not all pathogens. Some parasites, such as the Rhizobium bacterium in legume roots, mycorrhizae, and lichens, survive on their hosts without harming them in symbiotic relationships.
