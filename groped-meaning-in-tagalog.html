26. 3.what is the significance of groping known organisms into taxonomic kingdoms instead of the traditional plant-animal classification​


The traditional classification of organisms into plants and animals is based on their external appearance and habitat. However, as our knowledge of organisms has grown, it has become clear that this classification system is limited and does not accurately reflect the evolutionary relationships between different groups of organisms.

Taxonomic kingdoms, on the other hand, are based on the evolutionary relationships between organisms, as determined by genetic and biochemical studies. This approach provides a more accurate and comprehensive view of the diversity of life on Earth and allows for a more precise classification of organisms.

Here are some of the significant advantages of grouping known organisms into taxonomic kingdoms:

Reflects evolutionary relationships: Taxonomic kingdoms reflect the evolutionary relationships between organisms, allowing for a more accurate understanding of the origins and relationships between different groups of organisms.Provides a comprehensive classification system: Taxonomic kingdoms provide a more comprehensive and inclusive classification system that can accommodate the diversity of organisms that exist on Earth.Allows for more accurate identification of organisms: The use of taxonomic kingdoms allows for more accurate identification of organisms based on their genetic and biochemical characteristics, rather than their external appearance.Provides a basis for scientific research: Taxonomic kingdoms provide a foundation for scientific research into the relationships between organisms, their behavior, ecology, and other aspects of their biology.

Overall, the use of taxonomic kingdoms provides a more accurate and comprehensive classification system for known organisms, allowing for a better understanding of the diversity of life on Earth and providing a basis for further scientific research.
