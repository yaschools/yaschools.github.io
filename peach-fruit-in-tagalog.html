23. A basket contains three apples, three peaches, and four pears. You randomly select a piece of fruit. Find the probability that it is an apple or a peach.


Solution:

P(apple) = 3 / 10
P(apple) = 3 / 10

P(apple or peach) = P(apple) + P(peach)
P(apple or peach) = 3 / 10 + 3 / 10
P(apple or peach) = 6 / 10 or 3 / 5

Answer: 3 / 5

^_^Solution:

Let A for the probability of apple and P for the peach.

P[A] = 3/10
P[P] = 3/10

So, the probability that it is an apple or a peach is

P[AP] = 3/10 + 3/10
P[AP] = 6/10
P[AP] = 3/5

Therefore, the probability that it is an apple or a peach is 3/5.
