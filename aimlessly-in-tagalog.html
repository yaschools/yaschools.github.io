11. You spend days wandering aimlessly around a park with many different paths that end at different parts of the park. one day when you arrive at the park, you get a call on your cell phone from your cousin whom you haven't seen for years, and she says she is waiting for you in a particular section of the park. even though the paths are complicated and twisted, you manage to find the shortest route to your cousin. tolman would explain your efficient passage through the park as an example of ________.


By managing to find the shortest route to your cousin, you have just demonstrated EDWARD CHASE TOLMAN’S Sign Learning Theory. In this theory, the subject is expected to make a type of mental map as they perceive everything around him. These observable surroundings stimulate the person. So while searching for a way, the individual is then faced with Behavioral Determinants such as Expectations, Perceptions, Needs, Representations, and Environmental Variables. So in other words, an individual gains knowledge as he or she tries to figure out how to reach the cousin waiting at one side of the park.
