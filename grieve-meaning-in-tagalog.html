2. What is the meaning of A thing that grieves not and that never hopes? A brother to the ox?


Answer:

The meaning of this line from “The Man with the Hoe” by Edwin Markham.

“A thing that grieves not and that never hopes” points to the defeated soul of a man who has known no other life but hardship. Before that, the poem described the man as being dead to rapture and despair. When there is no distinction between a chance of meeting the lord during the end times and not being able to change anything can only mean that the person is in a hopeless place, just as he was described as someone who “never hopes”. The clause “A brother to the ox” seals everything by comparing the man to a beast with a single purpose, existing only to be exploited, given only enough to have strength for the next day, brought to the field until all usefulness is drained away from him.

The poem appears to place blame on what we refer to today as capitalism or a version of it, wherein the little people slave away by giving every second of their lives to make these industrialists their fortunes. The poem called them masters, rulers in all lands, and kings. Just as in the past, today's industrial, political and religious rulers create and control systems that favor them at the expense of everyone else.

4. different disadvantage of grieving​


Grieving is a natural and normal response to loss, but it can also have various disadvantages and challenges for individuals who are experiencing it. Some of the disadvantages of grieving include:

• Emotional distress: Grief can cause intense emotional pain, sadness, and feelings of emptiness, which can be overwhelming and difficult to manage. It can also lead to anxiety, depression, and a sense of hopelessness.

• Physical symptoms: Grief can manifest physically, with individuals experiencing symptoms such as fatigue, headaches, insomnia, loss of appetite, and other physical symptoms.

• Disruption of daily life: Grief can disrupt an individual's daily routine and make it difficult to perform normal activities, including work, school, and socializing with others.

• Social isolation: Grief can cause individuals to withdraw from social interactions, leading to feelings of loneliness and isolation.

• Difficulty in making decisions: Grief can make it challenging for individuals to make decisions, even about simple tasks or activities. It can lead to indecisiveness, procrastination, and a lack of motivation.

• Negative impact on relationships: Grief can place a strain on relationships, particularly if those around the grieving individual do not understand the grieving process or are unable to offer the support that is needed.

It is important to note that everyone experiences grief differently, and not all individuals will experience these disadvantages or challenges to the same degree. If you or someone you know is struggling with grief, it is important to seek support from a mental health professional or other trusted resource.

26. Grieving is important because


Answer:

Why Is It Important to Grieve?

When a loved one passes from our lives, the most common question we hear (both from ourselves and from those around us) involves the word "why". In the early days, it's part of "why did this happen?" And as we settle in to our grief, it becomes part of "why does it hurt so much?" It's possible the question then changes to the more philosophical inquiry, "why do we grieve?"

Grief is a natural part of our lives, and affects all aspects of our existence. It can cause us physical pain; and yet bring us to a deeper understanding of the true value and meaning of life. Grief can be very hard work; taking significant amounts of energy, it is a major force for change in our lives. We grieve naturally; which to us means it is a natural way for us to grow stronger and more resilient. But that certainly doesn't mean it's a pleasant path to take; no one chooses bereavement–it chooses us.
