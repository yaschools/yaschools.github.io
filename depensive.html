14. What are the affirmative depenses about climate change?​


In a legal proceeding, affirmative defenses are arguments made by defendants to justify their actions or to reduce their liability. Some of the affirmative defenses that have been used in relation to climate change include:

1. Defense of Necessity: The necessity defense argues that the defendant's actions were required to prevent a greater harm, such as a threat to life or property right away. Activists who engage in civil disobedience to raise awareness of the urgent need for climate change action could use this defense in the context of climate change.

2. Negligence Contribution: The plaintiff's own actions, according to this defense, contributed to the damage they are attempting to repair. A defendant who argues that the plaintiff's own actions, such as their own carbon emissions or failure to mitigate climate change, contributed to the harm could use this defense in the context of climate change.

3. Approval from the government: This defense argues that the defendant should not be held liable because the actions they took were approved or authorized by a government agency. This defense could be used by businesses in the context of climate change to argue that government agencies approved or authorized their emissions or other effects on the environment.

4. Absence of Cause: This defense contends that the plaintiff's claims of harm were not directly caused by the actions of the defendant. This defense could be used by defendants in climate change cases to argue that the plaintiff's harm was not directly caused by their emissions or other environmental effects.

It's important to remember that these affirmative defenses are still being used and debated in climate change-related legal proceedings.
