16. Terms to define what is the sollowing 1 Electron configuration Vilains election 3 Properties of Polar Molecule 6 Properties of Non-polar molecule 7. Metal elem & Non metal elements lonic Bund 1 to Non Tonic Bund 11 Coralent Bond​


Answer:

Electron configuration: The arrangement of electrons in an atom, molecule, or other physical structure, typically represented by a series of numbers, letters, or symbols indicating the distribution of electrons among the energy levels or orbitals.

Valence electrons: Electrons in the outermost shell of an atom that are involved in chemical bonding.

Polar molecule properties: Molecules with an uneven distribution of charge, resulting in a positive and negative end, exhibit properties such as dipole moment, ability to dissolve in polar solvents, and high boiling points.

Nonpolar molecule properties: Molecules with an even distribution of charge exhibit properties such as low solubility in polar solvents, low boiling points, and no dipole moment.

Metal elements and nonmetal elements: Metal elements are typically characterized by their high electrical conductivity, malleability, and ductility, while nonmetal elements are typically characterized by their poor electrical conductivity, brittleness, and tendency to gain electrons in chemical reactions.

Ionic bond: A chemical bond between two ions of opposite charges, typically formed between a metal and a nonmetal. The attraction between the positive and negative charges holds the ions together in a crystal lattice structure.

Covalent bond: A chemical bond between two nonmetal atoms, formed by the sharing of electron pairs between the atoms. Covalent bonds can be polar or nonpolar, depending on the electronegativity difference between the atoms.
