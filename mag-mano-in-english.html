22. what is mano or pagmamano​

Mano or pagmamano is an "honoring-gesture" used in Filipino culture performed as a sign of respect to elders and as a way of requesting a blessing from the elder.

Answer:

Mano or pagmamano is a gesture used in Filipino culture to show as a sign of respect to elders. "Pagmamano" is performed by touching the back of the elder’s hand against a younger one’s forehead.

25. Ano sa English ang Mano po tatay​


Answer: "Bless me, Dad"

"Tatay" means Dad. "Mano po" has no direct translation in English. The source term "Mano po, Tatay" may be translated as "Bless me, dad" to capture its essence. The phrase essentially means asking for your dad's blessing.
