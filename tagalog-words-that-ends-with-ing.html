3. a group of words that is introduced by a verb ending in-ing its is also called noun phrase​


Answer:

These are called gerunds.

Explanation:

Gerunds are verbs that end in -ing that are used as nouns.

Example:

Running is my favorite exercise.

In this case, running doesn't act like a verb, it refers to an activity. When you refer to it as an activity, it becomes a name or a noun. It also becomes the subject of the sentence.

In this regard, you can use gerunds like swimming, biking, reading, cleaning, eating, sleeping as nouns or verbs interchangeably.

24. what name do we give to a verbal ending ing use as a noun?


A gerund is an example of a verbal. Although it looks like a verb, it functions or is used as a noun.  


A gerund phrase is made up of two or more words that include the gerund with its modifiers and complements.


You can spot a gerund by looking for a verb + -ing that acts as a noun.




Gerunds and Gerund Phrases may be used as a/an:


1. Subject (the doer of the action and what is being talked about in the sentence)


Examples:  

Gardening is my favorite pastime. (gerund)


Listening to Classical music helps me relax after a long day. (gerund phrase)



2. Predicate Noun / Predicate Nominative (comes after a linking verb and appears near the end of the sentence)


Examples:


My favorite pastime is gardening.


What helps me relax after a long day is listening to Classical music.



3. Direct Object (comes after a transitive verb)


Tip: Ask “What?” after the verb to determine the direct object.


Examples:


I enjoy gardening during my free time. (“Enjoy what?”)


I like listening to Classical music after a long day because it helps me relax. (“Like what?”)



4. Object of a Preposition (comes after a preposition)


I am very fond of gardening.


I am interested in listening to Classical music because it is relaxing.



5. Indirect Object (appears between the transitive verb and the direct object)


I give gardening too much time.


Sheila gave listening to Classical music another chance, but it just wasn’t her taste.

29. What name do we give to a verbal ending in –ing used as noun?


Gerund is the grammatical term used to refer to a verb ending in -ing  which is used as a noun. It is formed by adding -ing to certain verbs to describe an action. Gerunds function as "subject" or "object" in a sentence.

Examples:

Reading is an essential skill that everybody should strive to develop. (subject)Callum and his dad love hunting. (object)

Gerunds can be used:

after certain verbs: "I miss listening to the sound of the waves on the beach."after certain adjectives: "She is obsessed with skateboarding."after certain nouns: "They have difficulties finding an efficient way to save money."
