13. what's the difference between infatuation and love?​


Answer:

What's the difference between infatuation and love?

Explanation:

Infatuation and love are two emotions that can be difficult to distinguish from one another, but they are not the same thing.

Infatuation is a strong, often short-lived attraction to someone. It is characterized by intense feelings of passion and desire, and it can be all-consuming. Infatuation often develops quickly and can be based on physical attraction or a superficial connection with another person. It is not uncommon for people to mistake infatuation for love, especially when they are young or have not experienced deep, enduring love before.

Love, on the other hand, is a deeper, more complex emotion that involves caring for and valuing another person for who they are, not just for their appearance or what they can do for you. Love involves a strong emotional and spiritual connection with another person and a willingness to support them and care for them through good times and bad. Love is often described as a choice, something that is actively cultivated and nurtured over time.

In summary, infatuation is a strong, intense emotion that is often based on physical attraction, while love is a deeper, more complex emotion that involves caring for and valuing another person for who they are.
