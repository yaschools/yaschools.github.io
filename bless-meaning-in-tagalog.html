11. Talambuhay ni blessed mother theresea tagalog


Ang Talambuhay ni Madre Teresa ng Kolkata

Si Madre Teresa or kilala sa tawag na Teresa ng Kolkata ay ipinanganak sa 1910 ika-10 ng Agosto. Siya ay kinilala bilang isang buhay na santo sa taglay nitong kabutihang loob at pagtulong sa sa mga taong may mga karamdaman at malulubhang sakit.

Ipinanganak siya at pinangalanan bilang Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu sa Skopje sa Macedonia. Nag-aral sya sa Irlanda at noong sya ay 18 taong gulang ay napagpasiyahan nyang maging madre dahil sa paniniwalang yun ang gusto ng Diyos para sa kanya.

Pagkatapos ng ilang buwan na pagsasanay ay agad siyang ipinadala sa Kolkata, India at doon ay pormal nyang tinanggap ang pagmamadre. Ang Kolkota ay lugar kung saan maraming tao ang hirap, may mga sakit at laganap ang kagutuman.

Noong 1948, itinatag nya ang samahan ng mga madre na tinawag na Mga Misyonera ng Kawanggawa na ang layunin ay magkaisa para makakapagbigay ng tulong sa mga maralita.

Dahil sa naipamalas niyang angking kabutihan, binigyan sya ng Gantimpalang Nobel noong 1979.

Ngunit sa huling dalawang dekada ng kanyang buhay, nagkaroon na sya ng ibat ibang uri ng sakit. Gayunpaman, hindi nya parin sya huminto sa kanyang misyon at patuloy na naglakbay sa buong mundo upang magbigay ng tulong. Isa si Prinsesa Diana sa kanyang nakausap bago sya tuluyang sumakabilang buhay. Pagkaraan ng ilang araw si Prinsesa Diana ay pumanaw din.

Si Madre Teresa ay pumanaw sa edad na 87 sa taong 1997, ika-5 ng Setyembre.

A blessing in disguise

A blessing in disguise is an English language idiom referring to the idea that something that appears to be a misfortune can have unexpected benefits.

Example:

“My car broke down again, but maybe it was a blessing in disguise; I've been wasting too much time driving around anyway.”

25. What do the blessings of jacob's sons mean?


Answer Jacob's blessing his Sons can be read at Genesis 49 here is what it means:

Reuben (Verse 3-4)

This is more of a curse to Reuben because he slept with one of Jacob's wife, Bilhah. Reuben lost his right as Jacob's first son.

Simeon and Levi (Verse 5-7)

At Genesis 34 the Sons of Jacob had a daughter named Dinah. During their stay at Schechem, Dinah was raped by Hamor's son also named Schechem. Simeon and Levi had their revenge by murdering the men of Schechem. They were cursed to not own a piece of territory in the "Promise Land" later in Deuteronomy.

Judah (verse 8-12)

This is a blessing that the messiah will come from the bloodline of Judah. In verse 10, the scepter refers to the power and authority of Jesus Christ.

Zebulun (verse 13)

The tribe of Zebulun will be assigned to the coast of the Mediterranean Sea and will become a prosperous port city.

Issachar (verse 14-15)

The tribe of Issachar was the third largest tribe of Israel. This made them a target for slavery when Israel lost to other civilizations.

Dan (verse 16-18)

The tribe of Dan had one of the best judges of Israel, Samson. But it is from the tribe of Dan that the Sin of Idolatry entered Jerusalem. And brought disaster to Israel. Jacob asks God for the salvation of Dan.

Gad (verse 19)

The tribe of Gad provided soldiers during King David's reign. They were oppressed by foreign armies but then ended with victory in their hands.

Asher (verse 20)

The territory that Asher settled in the Promise Land was fertile and produced a lot of goods.

Naphtali (verse 21)

This is a reference to the ministry of Jesus in the Galilee area because the tribe of Naphtali settled there. The "goodly words" refers to the preachings of Jesus.

Joseph (verse 22-26)

Joseph was blessed despited being a slave and oppressed by his brothers. His tribe would be prosperous amongst his brothers.

Benjamin (verse 27)

It could mean the tribe of Benjamin are fierce warriors.
