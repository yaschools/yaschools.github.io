17. Ethan had some solid-coloured socks and patterened socks. He had 3/4 as many solid-coloured socks as patterend socks. He threw away 16 pairs of solid-coloured socks and 16 pairs of patterend socks. 1/5 of his socks were now solid-coloured socks. How many pairs of patterned socks did Ethan have at first


Answer:

There were 40 pairs of patterned socks Ethan had at first.

Step-by-step explanation:

Solid colored socks: x

Patterned socks: y

Ethan had 3/4 as many solid-coloured socks as patterned socks:

x = (3/4)y

Total socks:

y + (3/4)y

= (4/4)y + (3/4)y

= (7/4)y or 7y/4 ⇒  total number of socks

He threw away 16 pairs of solid-colored socks and 16 pairs of patterned socks.

Solid colored socks:

(3/4)y - 16

Patterned sock:

y - 16

1/5 of his socks were now solid-colored socks:

(1/5)(7y/4) = (3/4)y - 16  ⇒  Working equation

7y/20 = 3y/4 - 16

Least common denominator (LCD): 20

7y/20 - 15y/20 = -16

Find the original number of pairs of patterned socks, y:

-8y/20 = -16

y = -16 (-20/8)

y = 40  

There were 40 pairs of patterned socks Ethan had at first.

sample space = 6 + 8 + 2 = 16
P(A) or Probability of picking blue sock or P(blue) = 2/16 = 1/8
P(B) or Probability of picking blue sock after picking a blue sock or P(blue following blue) = 1/15
.
Dependent Events
P(A and B) = P(A) x P(B following A)
P(A and B) = 1/8 x 1/15
P(A and B) = 1/120
.
Therefore, the probability of picking 2 blue socks from the drawer is 1/120.

28. Blue, green, and white socks are in the ratio of 6:2:5. There are 65 in all. How many more white socks than green socks?​


Answer:

15

Step-by-step explanation:

6 + 2 + 5 = 13

65 ÷ 13 = 5

Blue = 6 × 5 = 30 (# of Blue Socks)

Green = 2 × 5 = 10 (# of Green Socks)

White = 5 × 5 = 25 (# of White Socks)

25 (# of White Socks) - 10 (# of Green Socks) = 15

Therefore, there are 15 more white socks than green socks.
