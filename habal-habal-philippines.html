1. type of clue Habal habal​


Answer:

motorcycle taxis

Habal-habal is a local dialect for motorcycle taxis or motorcycle “for hire” which means “sitting close to each other”. Habal-habal passengers, who usually range from two to three persons, sit behind the driver, close to each other, thus the term.

Explanation:

The etymology of the word "habal-habal" is derived from the local Bisayan term for animals "copulating," or "habal" probably because of the position of the riders which somewhat mimics the act of animals copulating.
