26. Adam and Melissa went fly-fishing and caught a total of 32 salmon. Melissa caught three times as many salmon as Adam. How many salmon did Adam catch?


Out of the 32 total, 8 salmon have been caught by Adam. The word problem involves two unknown numbers. Therefore, we must assign variables to them to solve the problem. Adam’s catch is x. Melissa had 3 times as many as Adam; hence her catch is 3x. We then solve.

Adam’s catch = x

Melissa’s catch = 3x  

     x + 3x = 32

    4x = 32

    4 ÷ (4x = 32) ÷ 4

     x = 8

Hence, 8 salmon have been caught by Adam. Melissa’s catch is 3x. We substitute 8 to the x, then solve. 3 times 8 equals 24. So, 24 salmon have been caught by Melissa. In total, 32 salmon have been caught. We can check to verify this answer.

Adam’s catch = x = 8

Melissa’s catch = 3x = 3(8) = 24  

    x + 3x = 32

    8 + 24 = 32

    32 = 32
