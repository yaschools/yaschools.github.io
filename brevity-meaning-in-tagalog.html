11. Do you agree or disagree with the quote "Brevity is the soul of wit" Why?


Answer:

It is one of the countless phrases coined by William Shakespeare. It appears in his play, Hamlet, in the second act, where Polonius says, “Since brevity is the soul of wit / And tediousness the limbs and outward flourishes, I will be brief…” However, doubt about the creation of this phrase lurks among literary circles. Some critics argue that, by using this proverb in his play, Shakespeare made it popular, though the phrase was already in use. On the other hand, some doubt the veracity of this argument

15. what is the root word of the word brevity ?​


Answer:

Brevity comes from brevis, which means "brief" in Latin. You can use brevity for things that are literally short, like the brevity of an elevator meeting, or the quality of fleetingness, like the brevity of youth. Brevity is also a style of speaking or writing with economy.

29. why is there a need to consider appropriateness, brevity, clarity, ethics and vividness when engaging in verbal communication?


Answer

First, we define the words that need consideration

Appropriateness - the quality of being suitable or proper in the circumstances.Brevity - concise and exact use of words in writing or speech.Clarity - the quality of being coherent and intelligible.Ethics - moral principles that govern a person's behavior or the conducting of an activity.Vividness - producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind.

We consider these five qualities because we need to be sensitive, precise, understandable, logical, and clear in the message we are delivering.
