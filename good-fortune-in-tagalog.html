24. A legendary bird of the maranao said to be a symbol of good fortune


Answer:

SARIMANOK: The Legendary Bird of Maranao Tribe

Sarimanok came from the words “Sari” and “Manok”. Sari means cloth or garment with assorted colors while the Manok is a Tagalog word for chicken. Sarimanok is derived from a totem bird of the Maranao people, called Itotoro. For people of Maranao, Sarimanok is a symbol of good fortune.

What does a Sarimanok look like?

The Sarimanok also became the symbol of Maranao art because of its colors. It is described as a fowl with colorful wings, feathered tail and is holding a fish on its beak or talons. Its head is decorated with scroll and spiral motifs to symbolize good fortune.
