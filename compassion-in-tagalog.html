26. Difference between magnetic compass and gyro compass​


Answer:

A magnetic compass uses the earths magnetic field to determine heading or direction. Generally does not need to be powered. It is however vulnerable to magnetic distortion from other magnetic sources like a magnet or from other metals that could deflect earth's magnetism like a ships hull or submarine's steel body.

A gyro compass is not affected by these as it does not depend on magnetism.  Rather, it tells direction or heading based on the earth's rotation using a gyroscope. A gyroscope is a spinning disc that, when spun, will constantly seek to retain it's initial orientation relative to the earth's rotational axis. Thus it references the true north based on earth's rotational axis while the magnetic compass references the magnetic north. And since the disc need to be kept spinning, a gyro compas needs to be constantly powered.
