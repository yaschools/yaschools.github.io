10. Ano ang kahulugan ng quota?


A quota is a government-imposed trade restriction that limits the number, or monetary value, of goods that can be imported or exported during a particular time period. Quotas are used in international trade to help regulate the volume of trade between countries. They are sometimes imposed on specific goods to reduce imports, thereby increasing domestic production. In theory, this helps protect domestic production by restricting foreign competition.

14. what is quota criterion for apportionment


Answer:

In mathematics and political science, the quota rule describes a desired property of a proportional apportionment or election method. As an example, if a party deserves 10.56 seats out of 15, the quota rule states that when the seats are allotted, the party may get 10 or 11 seats, but not lower or higher.

20. These are government-induced restrictions, such as tariffs and quotas on imported products.​


Answer:

Government-induced restrictions on imported products are commonly known as trade barriers. Tariffs and quotas are two common types of trade barriers that governments use to restrict the flow of imported goods into their countries.

A tariff is a tax that a government places on imported goods. This tax increases the price of the imported product, making it less attractive to consumers and thus reducing demand. Tariffs are often used to protect domestic industries from foreign competition by making it more expensive to import similar products.

A quota, on the other hand, is a restriction on the quantity of a particular product that can be imported into a country. This limits the amount of foreign goods that can enter a domestic market and protect local industries.

Overall, trade barriers such as tariffs and quotas are used by governments to protect domestic industries from foreign competition, although they can also lead to higher prices for consumers and less competition in the market.

26. On the first day of enrollment, a school registrar enlisted 140 students which was 33 1/3% of its enrollment quota. How many more students must enroll to meet the quota?


RATIO AND PROPORTION

Problem:

On the first day of enrollment, a school registrar enlisted 140 students which was 33 1/3% of its enrollment quota. How many more students must enroll to meet the quota?

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

Given the following data:

140 students = 33 ⅓ or 33.33 percent of the enrollment quote

By ratio and proportion:

140 : 0.3333 = x : (1 – 0.3333)

1 represents 100% of the enrollment quote

1 – 0.3333 = 0.6667 or 66.67 or 66 ⅔ of the enrollment quota is what the school needs to reach its quota.

So we have:

140/0.3333 = x/0.6667

Then let us multiply both sides by 0.6667 to isolate the variable x.

0.6667(140/0.3333) = x

93.34/0.3333 = 280

Therefore, the school needs 280 more students to reach its enrollment quota.
