6. meaning of the following words accompaniment apology arduous banish bazaar beguile beseech bewildered cackle ceaselessly ceremonial coffin coma comb compromise confide​


Answer:

1. Accompaniment - something that is supplementary to or complements something else, typically food.

2. Apology - a regretful acknowledgment of an offense or failure.

3. Arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring.

4. Banish - send (someone) away from a country or place as an official punishment.

5. Bazaar - a market (as in the Middle East) consisting of rows of shops or stalls selling miscellaneous goods.

6. Beguile - charm or enchant (someone), sometimes in a deceptive way.

7. Beseech - ask (someone) urgently and fervently to do something; implore; entreat.

8. Bewildered - perplexed and confused; very puzzled.

9. Cackle - make a harsh, raucous sound when laughing.

10. Ceaselessly - continuously and without end

11. Ceremonial - relating to or used for formal events of a religious or public nature.

12. Coffin - a long, narrow box, typically of wood, in which a corpse is buried or cremated.

13. Coma - A coma is a deep state of prolonged unconsciousness in which a person cannot be awakened, fails to respond normally to painful stimuli, light, or sound, lacks a normal wake-sleep cycle and does not initiate voluntary actions.

14. Comb - a strip of plastic, metal, or wood with a row of narrow teeth, used for untangling or arranging the hair.

15. Compromise - an agreement or a settlement of a dispute that is reached by each side making concessions.

16. Confide - tell someone about a secret or private matter while trusting them not to repeat it to others.
