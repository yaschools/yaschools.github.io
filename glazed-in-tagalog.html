Its melting temperature is too high for ceramic kilns, so silica is combined with fluxes, substances that prevent oxidation, to lower the melting point.

19. glaze explanation for reportingthank you!!​


Answer:

In ceramics, glaze refers to a glass-like coating that is applied to the surface of a fired ceramic piece, typically pottery. Glazes can be transparent or opaque, and can come in a wide range of colors and textures. They are typically made from a mixture of powdered minerals and water, which is applied to the surface of the piece and then fired at a high temperature to fuse the glaze into a smooth, durable surface.

The primary purpose of glazing is to add decorative or functional properties to a ceramic piece. Glazes can protect the surface of the pottery from damage or staining, and can also create a smooth, glossy surface that is easy to clean. They can also enhance the appearance of the piece, adding color, texture, and depth to the surface.

Glazes can be applied in a variety of ways, such as brushing, dipping, or spraying. Different glazes will have different properties, such as hardness, durability, and texture. Some glazes may also react differently to different firing temperatures, producing unique effects on the surface of the pottery.

Overall, glazing is an important step in the ceramics process that adds both aesthetic and functional properties to a piece. Whether it is a functional dish or a decorative art piece, glazing can enhance the beauty and durability of the ceramic and bring out its unique qualities.
