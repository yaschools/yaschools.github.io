19. what is the example of civil liability without criminal liability ? ​


Answer:

An example of civil liability without criminal liability is a person being sued in a civil court for causing harm or injury to another person, but not being charged with a crime in a criminal court. For instance, if someone causes an accident that results in bodily injury or property damage, they may be held civilly liable for the damages in a civil court, but not be found criminally liable in a criminal court. This is because civil liability is based on the principle of compensation for harm or injury caused, while criminal liability is based on the principle of punishing the offender for breaking the law.
