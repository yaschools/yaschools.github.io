1. topic: FILIPINOS ARE LOSING THEIR FILIPINO IDENTIFY. Agreement​


Filipino identity has been shaped by various cultural, social, and historical factors. However, several studies suggest that modernization and globalization have led to the dilution of Filipino identity. Increasing exposure to Western media, migration and urbanization promoting a diverse, cosmopolitan identity among Filipinos have led to a weakening of traditional values, beliefs, and practices.

According to a 2018 study published in the Journal of International Migration and Integration, Filipino overseas workers' experiences often lead to cultural dislocation and hybridization, causing a shift in personal identity. Another study suggests that being a Filipino immigrant outside the Philippines can lead to a loss of language and the sense of identity that comes with it.

The younger generations in the Philippines are losing proficiency and interest in speaking their native language. The English language is increasingly becoming more common among younger Filipinos, and the lack of fluency in the Filipino language can be seen as a sign of distance from the country's culture.

Furthermore, there is a growing trend of people identifying themselves as "Filipino" but keeping the Western way of life. The Western way of life is often viewed as fashionable and cool among many younger Filipinos, leading to the adoption of Western values, beliefs, and practices. This further alienates Filipinos from their traditional culture and values.

In conclusion, the research suggests that Filipinos are losing their Filipino identity due to several factors, such as migration, globalization, urbanization, and Western influence. While some argue that the adoption of Western culture is a sign of progress, the loss of Filipino identity can lead to a fragmented society and a lack of a sense of belonging.
