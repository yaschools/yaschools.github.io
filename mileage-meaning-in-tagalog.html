2. Automotive engineers tested the gas mileage in kilometers per liter (km/L) of a certain passenger car which is normally distributed. A random sample of 36 cars resulted to a mean gas mileage of 15km/L and a standard deviation of 2.5 km per liter. Construct a 95.34% confidence interval for the true mean gas mileage of this car.


Answer:

To construct a 95.34% confidence interval for the true mean gas mileage of the car, we first need to find the critical value of the t-distribution with degrees of freedom (df) equal to n-1=35, where n is the sample size.

The confidence level of 95.34% corresponds to an alpha level of (1-0.9534) = 0.0466. Since the t-distribution is symmetric, we need to find the critical value that corresponds to an alpha level of 0.0233 (0.0466/2) in the upper tail. Using a t-distribution table or calculator with 35 degrees of freedom, we find the critical value to be 2.288.

The confidence interval for the true mean gas mileage of the car is then:

CI = X̄ ± t*(s/√n)

where X̄ is the sample mean gas mileage, s is the sample standard deviation, n is the sample size, and t is the critical value of the t-distribution.

Substituting the given values, we get:

CI = 15 ± 2.288*(2.5/√36)

= 15 ± 0.959

Therefore, the 95.34% confidence interval for the true mean gas mileage of the car is (14.041, 15.959) km/L.
