What is blockmate?When you say you're a blockmate, it means you're in the same class, which is similar to a section in college. Although this is plainly present at universities that arrange the students' class structure, "blockmate" can also refer to a student's classmate for the entirety of their college experience. For example, when you enter college, you will be assigned to one section, and that section will be your block section. Your "blockmate" is your "classmate" in your block section.

Classmate Classmate, on the other hand, is a term that can be used to refer to anyone in your class, regardless of what type of educational institution you're attending. It could be a secondary school, a college, or a university. Your classmates are simply students in your class. For example, if in later years like when you will be 2nd-year students, you may have taken classes from different sections or in one class there are various students from different sections. So, you will call the other student your "classmate".
