8. What is the mustard seed


In the Gospel of Matthew the parable is as follows: The Kingdom of Heaven is like a grain of mustard seed, which a man took, and sowed in his field; which indeed is smaller than all seeds but when it is grown, it is greater than the herbs and becomes a tree, so that the birds of the air come and lodge in its branches.

Answer:

Mustard seeds are the small round seeds of various mustard plants. The seeds are usually about 1 to 2 millimetres (0.039 to 0.079 in) in diameter and may be colored from yellowish white to black. They are an important spice in many regional foods and may come from one of three different plants: black mustard (Brassica nigra), brown Indian mustard (B. juncea), or white/yellow mustard (B. hirta/Sinapis alba).

Grinding and mixing the seeds with water, vinegar or other liquids creates the yellow condiment known as prepared mustard.

9. Explaine why okra, squash, mongo, mustard and bitter gourd or ampalaya are planted or are sown using direct method? ​.


Answer:

Okra, squash, mongo, mustard, and bitter gourd (also known as ampalaya) are often planted or sown using the direct method because they are typically grown in warm, sunny conditions and do not require a lot of special care or protection. The direct method involves planting or sowing the seeds directly in the ground where they will grow, rather than starting them indoors or in a greenhouse and transplanting them later.

Explanation:

Some reasons why these particular plants may be suitable for the direct method include:

They are relatively hardy and can withstand the elements: Okra, squash, mongo, mustard, and bitter gourd are all relatively tolerant of hot, dry conditions and can handle being exposed to the sun and wind. This makes them well-suited to being planted directly in the ground.They have a short growing season: These plants typically have a relatively short growing season and do not require a long period of time to mature. This means that they can be planted and grown to maturity using the direct method within a single growing season.They are not prone to diseases or pests: These plants are generally not prone to diseases or pests that would require special treatment or protection, so they can be grown using the direct method without the need for additional precautions.

Overall, the direct method is a simple and effective way to grow okra, squash, mongo, mustard, and bitter gourd, and can be a suitable option for gardeners and farmers looking to cultivate these plants.

[tex]\frac{1}{3}[/tex]  is second the remainder of the plot
