8. how did mandarin influence other chinese varieties?​


Explanation:

Mandarin and dialect are two systems, they do not affect each other.

Mandarin is the official language. It is not a modern product. It first appeared in the Zhou Dynasty. In the Zhou Dynasty, people from many countries spoke different languages and could not communicate with each other. But the upper class has a common language. Yayan is the language spoken by Zhou kings, and it is the Luoyang dialect today. This is the first version of Mandarin in China. Both Confucius and Laozi used it in class, and all kings also used it in meetings.

Dialects first appeared in the Qin Dynasty. The Qin Dynasty unified Chinese characters, but did not unify Chinese, but changed the languages of other countries into dialects. Every Chinese can speak dialect and Mandarin. People use Mandarin in their work and dialects in their lives. It not only preserves the local culture, but also promotes communication between people.

In the history of 2000, Mandarin and dialects have undergone tremendous changes. All Chinese versions have lost some of the original pronunciation. For example, Mandarin has lost the final sound, while Cantonese has lost the intermediate sound. Even Hokkien, which is the closest to the ancient Chinese pronunciation, has lost many elements.

Many Hong Kong people reject Mandarin for political reasons and insist on speaking only Cantonese. This will only isolate Hong Kong from other provinces in China. This is also one of the reasons for the decline of Hong Kong music and movies.

B.Mandarin

ANO ANG MANDARIN:

Mandarin is one of five major regional languages of China. It spreads wider than any other regional variety, from the whole northern part of China to Yunnan Province in the southwest corner of China. In that big area there are many regional differences in vocabulary, so somebody who moves from Beijing to Yunnan could not understand people there who were speaking their own dialect, Yunnanhua. The problem is bigger than for a person in Great Britain or the United States to go to Australia. Therefore, starting in the 1920s, the Chinese government set up a national language based on the Beijing dialect and on the most widely understood words and pronunciations.

23. Brainliest ko pag tamaWhat is the 4th tone marks in Mandarin Chinese?A.Falling and rising toneB.Flat tone or monotoneC.Falling ToneD.Rising Tone​


D.Rising Tone

The four tones of Middle Chinese, 平 píng "level", 上 shǎng "rising", 去 qù "departing", and 入 rù "entering", all evolved from different final losses from Old Chinese. The 上, or "rising" tone, arose from the loss of glottal stops at the end of words.
