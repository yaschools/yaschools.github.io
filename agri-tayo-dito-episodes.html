26. what plant grow when elevated Agri​


Answer:

There are many things that can be grown in such places like rice, vegetables, and fruits. Here’s a list of the easiest vegetables to grow in an elevated environment.

Lettuce – This crop is available in different varieties. As it grows from seed, it produces edible shoots of leafy greens within 30 days. If planting heading lettuce, allot 60 to 80 days for the heads to fully mature before harvesting, then cut down the whole plant once it has matured.

Spinach – Spinach is a cold and disease-tolerant crop. Its harvesting method is similar to that of the lettuce. It will flourish in full sunlight, but needs a little shade especially in areas with a warmer climate.

Radish – Radishes have a very high growth success rate, so beginners need not worry about having black thumbs. Another bonus is that it grows underground and is protected from the usual pests on the surface. In addition, this vegetable can be sown throughout warm weather.

Carrots – Another root vegetable, carrots take up nutrients underneath the soil. Unlike radishes, they require fertilizer. Every foot of carrots planted is expected to produce around one pound of carrots during the growing season. Plant new seeds every two weeks to enjoy a continuous supply of the vegetable.

Growing your own vegetables is a good way to save money on grocery bills. Plus, it could also be the start of a new business venture.


27. what is clearing of agri crop​


Answer:

Clearing of agricultural crops, also known as land preparation, is the process of preparing a field for planting. This includes a variety of tasks such as removing weeds, rocks, and debris, and leveling the land. Depending on the crop and the location, the process of clearing may also include other activities such as plowing, disking, harrowing, and tilling the soil. The goal of clearing is to create a smooth and level surface that is free of obstacles that could interfere with the growth of the crops, as well as to ensure the soil is in a good condition for planting. It is an important step in the process of agricultural production, as it sets the foundation for the growth and development of the crop.
