1. what does suede means and another word for suede​


Answer:

Noun. kid or other leather finished with a soft, napped surface, on the flesh side or on the outer side after removal of a thin outer layer. Also called suede cloth. A fabric with a napped surface suggesting this.

Another word for suede: Leather.

Suede (pronounced /sweɪd/ (SWAYD)) is a type of leather with a napped finish, commonly used for jackets, shoes, shirts, purses, furniture, and other items. The term comes from the French gants de Suède, which literally means "gloves from Sweden".

Answer:

Artist: Carl Perkins

Explanation:

"Blue Suede Shoes" is a rock-and-roll standard written and first recorded by Carl Perkins in 1955. It is considered one of the first rockabilly records, incorporating elements of blues, country and pop music of the time.

25. Find the meaning of the following words1.slung2.rattled3.stoop4.cocoa5.suede​


1. Slung is to suspend or arrange something, especially with a strap or straps, so that it hangs loosely in a particular position. Slung is the past participle/past tense of Sling.

2. Rattled is to make or cause to make a rapid succession of short, sharp knocking sounds, typically as a result of shaking and striking repeatedly against a hard surface or object. Rattled is the past tense/participle of Rattle.

3. Stoop is a posture in which the head and shoulders are habitually bent forward or to bend one's head or body forward and downward.

4. Cocoa is a chocolate powder made from roasted and ground cacao seeds.

5. Suede is a leather with the flesh side rubbed to make a velvety nap.
