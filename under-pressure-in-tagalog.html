10. under constant pressure condition brainly


:

Under constant pressure condition, a sample of hydrogen gas initially at 850C and 7.2 L is

cooled until its final volume is 4.1L What is its final temperature?

Answer

4.0/5

60

Charles Law

V1/T1 = V2/T2

where in, V1 = first volume

T1 = first temperature

V2 = second volume

T2 = second temperature

Given: V1 = 7.2L

T1 = 358.15K

V2 = 4.1L

Note: Dealing with temperature from each of the Gas Law must always be in Kelvin.

°C → K

85°C + 273.15K = 358.15K

Unknown: T2 = ?

Formula: T2 = V2T1 / V1

Note: To get the formula, apply cross-multiplication, division and cancellation of like terms using the original formula of Charles Law.

V1/T1 = V2/T2 ← cross-multiply

V1T2 = V2T1 ← divide both sides by V1

V1T2 / V1 = V2T1 / V1 ← cancel like terms

T2 = V2T1 / V1 ← final formula for T2

Solution: T2 = V2T1 / V1

= (4.1L)(358.15K) / 7.2L

= 1468.415L•K / 7.2L

= 203.95K

Note: "L" is cancelled out, so the unit for temperature will remain which is "K".

Reminder: Cancellation of units is applicable when the other same unit is on the denominator.

Final Answer: T2 = 203.95K

11. Fluid at rest at the bottom of a container isa Under less pressure than the fluid at the topb. Under the same pressure as the fluid at the topc. Under more pressure than the fluid at the topd Any of the above, depending on the circumstances​


Fluid at rest at the bottom of a container is C. Under (below) more pressure than the fluid at the top.

DEFINITION OF FLUIDS:

Fluid is a liquid, gas, or other material continuously deforms (flows) under applied shear stress or external force.

EXAMPLES OF FLUIDS:

Water.Air.Blood.Mercury.Honey.Gasoline.Any other gas or liquid.

PRESSURE OF FLUIDS:

Fluid pressure quantifies the force per object unit in the fluid or on the closed container surface. This pressure is caused by gravity, acceleration, or forces outside a closed container. Since a liquid has no definite shape, its pressure applies in all directions.The container bottom pressure is higher because of the atmospheric pressure. Plus the pressure because of the fluid weight. The force due to the liquid is equal to the importance of the liquid divided by the area. The significance of the fluid is similar to its mass times the acceleration due to gravity.Fluid pressure is higher at the bottom because hydrostatic pressure increases and an object liquid exerts the force per unit area. When you go deeper under the sea, water pressure will push down on you more. The fact is, every 33 feet (10.06 meters) you go down, the pressure will increase by one atmosphere. The pressure is higher because the particles at the bottom have more kinetic energy, giving up their gravitational potential energy.

HOW TO CALCULATE THE FLUID PRESSURE:

To count up fluid pressure, use p × g × h = fluid pressure, p represents the density of the liquid, g represents the acceleration of gravity, and h represents the height of the fluid. Then, multiply the variables and take the product of the three to solve the equation.

So, the answer is C. Under (below) more pressure than the fluid at the top

27. The volume V of gas varies inversely to the pressure P. The volume of a gas is 200 cm³ under pressure of 32 kg/cm². What will be its volume under pressure of 40 kg/cm?


INVERSE VARIATION

[tex] \red{\overline{\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad}}[/tex]

PROBLEM:

The volume V of gas varies inversely to the pressure P. The volume of a gas is 200 cm³ under pressure of 32 kg/cm². What will be its volume under pressure of 40 kg/cm?

MATHEMATICAL STATEMENT:

The volume V of gas varies inversely to the pressure P

EQUATION:

[tex]V=\frac{k}{P}[/tex] where k is the constant of the variation

GIVEN:

V = 200 cm³ under P = 32kg/cm²

UNKNOWN:

V under P = 40 kg/cm²

SOLUTION:

➨ First we will find the constant of the variation k using the given values.

[tex] \tt V = \frac{k}{P} \\ \\ \tt 200 = \frac{k}{32} \\ \\ \tt (32)(200) = ( \frac{k}{32} )(32) \\ \\ \boxed{\tt k = 6400}[/tex]

➨ Now that we have the value of k, we will use it to find the volume under pressure of 40 kg/cm²

[tex] \tt V = \frac{k}{P} \\ \\ \tt V= \frac{6400}{40} \\ \\ \red{ \boxed{ \tt V = 160 c {m}^{3} }}[/tex]

∴ The volume of a gas under pressure of 40kg/cm² is 160cm³.
