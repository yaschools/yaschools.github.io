8. propaganda of bandwagon


It is a psychological phenomenon whereby the rate of uptake of beliefs, ideas, fads and trends increases with respect to the proportion of others who have already done so. As more people come to believe in something, others also "hop on the bandwagon" regardless of the underlying evidence.

15. bandwagon effect kahulugan


Kung baguhan ka sa pagte-trade sa stocks market, iwasan mong sumali sa mga groups na pwedeng mang-hype sa iyo na bumili ng certain stocks. Baka mapasama ka sa kanilang “Bandwagon“.

BANDWAGON EFFECT



Alam niyo yung “Bandwagon Effect” sa psychology?

Isa itong phenomenon kung saan ang isang tao ay naniniwala sa isang bagay, kasi “marami” ang naniniwala na ganun yung bagay na yun. Parang pamahiin. Kahit di mo alam kung bakit naging malas makasalubong ng itim na pusa, naniniwala ka na rin. Kasi marami ang naniniwala. Kahit di mo alam kung totoong nakakatangkad kapag tumalon ka sa pagsalubong sa bagong taon, tumatalon ka. Kahit di mo naman kailangan ng cellphone bumibili ka, kasi lahat ng mga kaibigan mo ay may cellphone. Noong nakaraang eleksyon, ginamit din itong taktika ng mga politiko para iboto sila ng mga taong gusto lang makisabay sa boto ng “majority”.

Sa stocks trading ganun din. Kung mapaniwala ka ng isang grupo na ang tamang sistema ay “pagbobodega” kahit palugi ang stocks, gagawin mo rin yun at bibili ka kahit palugi ang isang stocks. Ang masakit, ay yung mapaniwala ka nila at tumibay ang paniniwala mo na tama ang ginagawa mo. Kaya kahit lugi na yung pera mo, feeling mo tama ka pa rin. Kasi yun din ang ginagawa ng karamihan sa mga kasama mo. Lahat kayo ay nakasakay sa isang “Bandwagon” ng “maling idea”. Pero dahil grupo kayo, isang pagtataksil kapag umalis ka sa idea ng grupo.

Naniniwala ka na profitable ang system nila, kahit wala naman silang pinapakitang proof sa iyo na kumita nga sila sa pinaggagagawa nila. Naniniwala ka dahil kasama mo sila sa group o sa forum kahit hindi mo sila kilala personally. Ni hindi mo alam kung ano ang tunay nilang intention o motibo. At hindi mo alam kung ano ang kaya nilang gawin sa pera mo.

Tandaan mo, walang magkakaibigan pagdating sa stocks trading. Survival of the Fittest ito. Isang digmaan na patibayan ng dibdib. Ang ma-intimidate ng kapwa trader ang siyang natatalo.

So kung hindi mo alam ang gagawin. Relax. Huwag kang magmadali. Mag-aral ka muna at magplano. Masarap mag-trade kapag alam mo na ang ginagawa mo. Maghanap ka ng pwedeng magturo sa iyo. Yung kayang magturo kung paano ka makakahuli ng malaking isda sa malawak na karagatan ng stocks market.

24. article about bandwagon?​


Answer:

BANDWAGON ARTICLE

The bandwagon effect is a psychological phenomenon whereby people do something primarily because other people are doing it, regardless of their own beliefs, which they may ignore or override.

The origin of the phrase comes from the use of a bandwagon, which is a float in a parade that encourages people to jump aboard and enjoy the music that is being played. The contagious effect of music and celebration ensures that large numbers of people will be jumping on. This principle was used from the 19th century in political campaigns to link candidates with the notion of having fun and to point out those who are not on the bandwagon as missing out.

27. some examples of BANDWAGON​


Below are some examples of the Bandwagon Effect:

Diets: When it seems like everyone is adopting a certain fad diet, people become more likely to try the diet themselves.

Elections: People are more likely to vote for the candidate that they think is winning.

Fashion: Many people begin wearing a certain style of clothing as they see others adopt the same fashions.

Music: As more and more people begin listening to a particular song or musical group, it becomes more likely that other individuals will listen as well.

Social Networks: As increasing numbers of people start using certain online social networking websites, other individuals become more likely to begin using those sites as well. The bandwagon effect can also influence how posts are shared as well as interactions within online groups

30. Bandwagon Personal Justification​


Answer:

Bandwagon is a fallacy based on the assumption that the opinion of the majority is always valid: that is, everyone believes it, so you should too. It is also called an appeal to popularity, the authority of the many, and argumentum ad populum (Latin for "appeal to the people").
