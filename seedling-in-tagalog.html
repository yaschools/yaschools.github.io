14. two-fourths of the garden is planted with guava seedlings. while ⅓ is planted with santol seedlings. How much bigger is the part planted with guava seedlings than the parts planted with santol seedlings?​


Answer:

It is 1/6 bigger

Step-by-step explanation:

Guava seedlings-2/4 (1/2 if simplified)

Santol seedlings-1/3

Find their least common denominator by looking at their multiples

2(from 1/2)- 2, 4, 6, 8, 12

3(from 1/3)- 3, 6, 9, 12, 15

*6 is the least common multiple so we will be using that as the denominator

To find the numerator, divide the least common multiple to the denominator then multiply it to the numerator

guava(1/2) - 6 divided by 2 times 1 =3

santol(1/3) - 6 divided bt 3 times 1 =2

The resulting fraction would be

guava-3/6

santol-2/6

To know how much greater the garden part where the guava seedlings are taken, we must subtract the 2 fractions

3/6 - 2/6 = 1/6

25. marketing strategies in seedling fruit and seedling​


Answer:

I only know first hand about selling from a farm stand. The stand location happened to be lucky in that it was on a major route into the downtown area of the city and to most of the residential areas in the north and western parts of the city, which also happened to be the wealthiest.

Tomatoes were by far the most popular and best sellers, and they are technically a fruit. They also had available and growing right there, strawberries and raspberries, and melons, so they were absolutely fresh, just picked that day, along with some other vegetables.

The key to selling more, according to one of the “brothers” (It was the Larson Brothers Farm) was a full display of the product. The produce had to be replenished promptly throughout the day either from fresh pickings from the field, or that stored in the cooler. It had to have a nice rounded, heaped-up appearance. No one wanted the “bottom of the barrel”. Also the bushel baskets displaying the produce all had false bottoms, to aid in the full effect without actually putting out in the warm summer air a full bushel of produce.
