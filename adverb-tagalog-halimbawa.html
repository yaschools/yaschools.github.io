there are three kinds of Adverbs, and they are as follows.

Simple Adverbs.

Interrogative Adverbs.

adverb of manner

An adverb of manner describes how you do an action and describe how something happens

For example,

They dress elegantly.

Some elderly people drive slowly.

She works very hard.

adverb of place

An adverb of place always talks about the location where the action of the verb is being carried out. Adverbs of place can refer to distances. For example: Nearby, far away, miles apart. Adverbs of place can be directional, indicate distant orcan indicate an object's position in relation to another object. For example, below, between, above, behind, through, aroundand so forth.

adverb of times

These adverbs of time are often used:

to talk about the past: yesterday, the day before, ago, last week/month/year.

to talk about the present: still, yet, while, when.

to talk about the future: soon, then, next week/month/year, in 2 days, tomorrow, the day after tomorrow.

Examples of Adverb of Time:

She stayed for a while then left.

She came at around 9.30 AM.
