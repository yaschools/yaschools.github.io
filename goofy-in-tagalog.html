20. how does the water cycle help in the formation of bodies of water on earth?​ help me pls (need a real answer) (goofy answer = report)


Explanation:

The water cycle plays a critical role in the formation of bodies of water on Earth. It is a continuous process that involves the movement of water from the Earth's surface to the atmosphere and back again. Here are some of the ways in which the water cycle helps in the formation of bodies of water on Earth:

1. Evaporation: The water cycle starts with the process of evaporation, where water from bodies of water like oceans, lakes, and rivers evaporates due to the heat from the sun. The evaporated water rises into the atmosphere and forms clouds.

2. Condensation: The water vapor in the atmosphere then condenses to form clouds, and when the clouds become heavy, they release the water in the form of precipitation.

3. Precipitation: Precipitation refers to any form of water that falls from the sky, including rain, snow, hail, and sleet. When precipitation falls on the Earth's surface, it can accumulate in depressions in the land and form bodies of water like lakes, rivers, and streams.

4. Runoff: When precipitation falls on the Earth's surface, it can also flow over the land and into rivers and streams. These rivers and streams eventually make their way to the ocean, where they deposit the water and help in the formation of bodies of water like oceans and seas.

In summary, the water cycle helps in the formation of bodies of water on Earth by constantly cycling water from the surface to the atmosphere and back again through processes like evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and runoff
