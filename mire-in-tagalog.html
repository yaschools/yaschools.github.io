16. are societies in the world becoming mire similar or more difficult?​


Answer:

Societies are becoming more similar every day, but they still retain cultural characteristics that make them distinct from one another.

Explanation:

There is no doubt that societies are more similar to each other today than they were 50 or 100 years ago. There are many reasons why this is so, including:

Ease of modern travel - As people continue to travel other places and interact with locals, they also begin to share and pick up characteristics from each other. Migrants from Asia to North America, for example, will begin to pick up American traditions while also introducing food and cultural beliefs to Americans.Technology - Talking to one another has never been easier. And with social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram available to people all over the world, our tastes and language become more similar.Modernization - Beyond just social media and technology, most societies in the world are modern, which means that we are using the same means of travel, we work in the same industries, and we spend our money on the same things. This makes us very similar to each other.

There is an umbrella term for all these factors that make us more similar and it is globalization. Globalization refers to the open borders (literally and figuratively) that we have between nations of the world. Globalization has made it easier for us to move to other places in the world and access their products even from our own homes.

But while globalization has made us very similar in some ways, it is important to note that we still have specific cultures that make us all special and distinct. Culture is informed by history, religion, myths, values, and our families and it is therefore much harder to make us culturally similar. So while we all use the same devices and we are all able to travel, our nation's history and our own families and values mean that we will always do certain things a little bit differently.
