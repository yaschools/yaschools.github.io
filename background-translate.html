What is codon, and what does it represent in protein synthesis? 3. How does mRNA help in transcription process? 4. What is the role of tRNA in translation process?​


1. Transcription is the process of copying the DNA sequence to form an mRNA molecule, while translation is the process of decoding the mRNA sequence to build a protein.

2. Codon is a sequence of three nucleotides in mRNA that codes for a specific amino acid or a stop signal. It represents the basic unit of genetic code that determines the order of amino acids in a protein.

3. mRNA helps in the transcription process by carrying the genetic information from the DNA to the ribosome, where it is translated into a protein. The mRNA molecule is synthesized by RNA polymerase, which uses the DNA template to create a complementary RNA sequence.

4. tRNA (transfer RNA) carries amino acids to the ribosome during the translation process. It recognizes the codon on the mRNA by base-pairing between its anticodon and the codon, and then transfers the corresponding amino acid to the growing protein chain. tRNA acts as a molecular adaptor that bridges the nucleic acid and protein worlds.
