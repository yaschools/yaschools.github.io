What is dizziness and what is the cause of dizziness?

brainly.ph/question/2037880

1. The person feels lightheaded, woozy and unbalance

2. It affects the sensory organs such as eyes and ears.

3. Feeling of faint

4. Feeling floating

5. Unsteadiness

6. Blurred vision

7. Numbness or tingling

Some common causes of dizziness

• Migraine

• Problem of the inner ear or ear infection

• Vertigo

• Meniere’s disease

• Sudden drop of blood pressure

• Heart muscle disease

• Decrease in blood volume

• Low iron

• Low blood sugar

• Dehydration

• Heart stroke

• Excessive exercise

• Motion sickness

Treatment of dizziness

1. The treatment of dizziness is depend on the case of the patient why he feel dizzy.

Example:  Migraines are treated by medication and lifestyle changes while dizziness due to excessive exercise must only treat through  drinking plenty of fluids

19. a dizzy weaving design of ilocanos that represents the waves of the sea?A.kainB.ginaspalawannes C.binakolD.inabel​


A weaving design of Ilocanos that represents the waves of the sea is D. inabel​. Inabel is a hand weaving technique of Ilocanos. It is produced using traditional wooden looms. It is an ancient weaving practice.

Inabel is a traditional woven fabric from the Philippines. It is typically made of cotton or silk and is decorated with intricate patterns. Inabel fabrics are used for a variety of purposes, including clothing, home decor, and even as gifts.

Inabel fabrics have a long history in the Philippines. They were first introduced to the country by Spanish colonists in the 16th century. Since then, they have been an important part of Filipino culture. Inabel fabrics are known for their beautiful designs and colors. They are often used to make traditional Philippine clothing, such as the barong Tagalog (a formal shirt worn by men). Inabel fabrics are also commonly used to make home decor items, such as curtains and tablecloths.

The history of inabel dates back to the 16th century when it was first introduced to the Philippines by Spanish colonizers. Inabel weaving was initially only done by men, but over time, women began to participate in the craft as well. Inabel became increasingly popular in the 18th and 19th centuries as a trade good between the Philippines and other Asian countries. Today, inabel remains an important part of Filipino culture. It is used in traditional ceremonies and celebrations and is also sold commercially.

Binakael (binakel, binakol, binakul) (transliterated, "to do a sphere") is a type of weaving pattern traditional in the Philippines. Patterns consisting entirely of straight lines are woven so as to create the illusion of curves and volumes. A sense of motion is also sought.
