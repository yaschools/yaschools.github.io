3. Shad has a lemonade stand and sold 20 cups of lemonade. Each cup was 500 milliliters. How many liters did Shad sell in total?


WORD PROBLEMS INVOLVING UNIT CONVERSION

In order to solve the following problem, we have to identify first the given information.

Given:  20 cups of lemonade Shad sold

1 cup contains 500 milliliters (mL)

Required:

20 cups = _____ liters (L) of lemonade Shad sold

Solution with step-by-step explanation:

The first thing that we need to find out is the amount of lemonade Shad sold in milliliters. We have a conversion factor: 1 cup = 500 mL

So as we calculate:  20 cups × (500 mL/1 cup) = 10,000 mL

But the final answer requires liters as the unit, so we need to know that 1 liter = 1,000 mL.

Using this conversion factor:

10,000 mL × (1 L/1,000 mL) = 10 L

Therefore, Shad sold a total of 10 L of lemonade.  

TRICKS USING A CONVERSION FACTOR IN CONVERTING UNITS

There are lots of advantages when you know about the use of conversion factor during measurement conversion. It may be from a smaller unit to a larger one or the other way around.

When dealing with conversion factors, it is helpful to determine what to put in the numerator and denominator when multiplying to it.  Trick #1: Know the unit of the original quantity to convert. When you place the conversion factor, the same unit should be opposite of it. This will allow elimination of the units so the desired unit will left in the conversion.

For example:  20 cups to mL  

The possible expressions for this conversion could either be:

20 cups x (1 cup/500mL)

or

20 cups x (500 mL/1 cup)

Which do you think is the right one?

If your answer is the second expression, then your answer is correct. Similar units should be oppositely across each other.  Trick #2:  Determine the unit of the original quantity and the unit of the required quantity.

If you are able to identify them, it could be a lot more easier to figure out how to execute the problem. It could either be a one-step, two-step or sometimes, multi-step conversion process depending on how will you solve the problem.  Trick #3: Review about measurements and conversion. If you are familiar about the Metric system and English system, this can help you in your computations. To know better about conversions can help you formulate more conversion factors without scanning in your notes, books or even searching them in the internet. It promotes time efficiency and can help enhance your memorization skills.

CONVERSION OF UNITS

Conversion is the process of altering or causing something to transform from one form to another. Converting units of measurement for the same quantity is a multi-step operation.

Steps in Converting one unit to another:

Step 1: Identify the unit you are starting with.Step 2: Identify the unit you wanted to end with. Step 3: Find the conversion factor/s that will convert the starting unit to the ending unit. Using the fractional form, the unit you want to end will be the numerator. The unit to be canceled will be the denominator. Step 4: Set up Mathematical expression so that all units except the unit you want to end with will not be canceled.

Now that we know the steps, let's go back to the problem:

How many LITERS did Shad sell in total?

To solve this, we must first analyze the given:

Number of cups sold: 20Each cup of lemonade has 500 milliliters.

Therefore, if each cup has 500mL. and she sold 20 cups, then

20 x 500 = 10,000 mL.

To answer the problem, we simply need to convert milliliters to liters.

Step 1. Identify the unit you are starting with: Milliliters

Step 2. Identify the unit you wanted to end with: Liters

STEP 3. Find the conversion factor/s that will convert the starting unit to the ending unit. Using the fractional form, the unit you want to end will be the numerator. The unit to be canceled will be the denominator.

1L = 1000mL ---- [tex]\frac{1L}{1000mL}[/tex]

STEP 4. Set up Mathematical expression so that all units except the unit you want to end with will not be canceled.

10,000mL × [tex]\frac{1L}{1000mL}[/tex]= ______ (cancel the same unit, which is the mL)

10,000mL × [tex]\frac{1L}{1000mL}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{10,000L}{1,000}[/tex]  

[tex]\frac{10,000L}{1,000} = 10L[/tex]  

Therefore, Shad sold 10 liters of lemonade.

In today's world, there are two basic systems of measurement:

The Metric System — This system includes units such as meters, grams, and liters and is widely used around the world. The names are formed with prefixes, and the system is based on base ten. It was based on one of the first French measurement methods.The English System – This system is mostly used in the United States and includes units such as inches, pounds, and gallons. It was derived from an early English measuring system, which had its origins in the Roman system.
