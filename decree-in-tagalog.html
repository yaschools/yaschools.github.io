1. ANO ANG ISINASAAD NG ROYAL DECREE 1863? in tagalog


Answer:

Ang Batas Pang-edukasyon noong 1863 ay isang pagsisikap ng Espanya na repormahin ang sistemang kolonyal na edukasyon sa Pilipinas. Ang Decree ay nagtatag ng isang kumpletong sistema ng edukasyon sa arkipelago - nangangailangan ito ng dalawang paaralang elementarya sa bawat munisipalidad (isa para sa mga batang babae at isa para sa mga lalaki), ginawang pamantayan ang kurikulum, at nagtatag ng mga normal na paaralan, sa gayon ay ginawang magagamit ng sistematikong edukasyon sa masa. Sa ikalabinsiyam na siglo, ang mga oportunidad sa edukasyon ay binuksan sa isang segment ng lipunan na dati ay pinigil ng mga utos ng relihiyon sa pamamagitan ng isang piling kurikulum ng mga walang katuturang akademiko at isang mabibigat na dosis ng catechism. Ang lohikal na lohika ay upang lumikha ng isang kadre ng mga clerks at opisyal sa paglilingkod sa bago, liberal na kolonyal na estado, ngunit ang Educational Decree noong 1863 ay may epekto na kabaligtaran ng nilalayon ng Espanya. Ang pormal na sistema ng edukasyon na nilikha sa Pilipinas sa ilalim ng Espanya, kahit na hindi pantay na ipinatupad, ay nagbibigay sa mga Pilipino ng mga tool upang gumana sa labas ng kolonyal na pamamahala.

19. summarize marine pollution decree of 1976 (presidential decree no.979​


Answer:

Explanation:

The Marine Pollution Decree of 1976, also known as Presidential Decree No.979, was a landmark legislation that aimed to address the growing problem of marine pollution in the Philippines. The decree established a comprehensive framework for regulating and controlling all sources of marine pollution, including oil spills, sewage discharges, and industrial waste.

Under the decree, all vessels operating within Philippine waters were required to comply with strict environmental standards and regulations. The government was also given the power to impose fines and penalties on violators of these regulations.

Moreover, the decree established a National Marine Pollution Control Commission (NAMPOLCOM) tasked with overseeing and enforcing the provisions of the law. NAMPOLCOM was responsible for conducting regular inspections of ships and other facilities that could potentially cause marine pollution.

Overall, the Marine Pollution Decree of 1976 represented a significant step forward in protecting Philippine waters from environmental degradation caused by human activities. It remains an important piece of legislation today as we continue to face new challenges in preserving our oceans for future generations.

The Marine Pollution Decree of 1976 (Presidential Decree No.979) was introduced to regulate and prevent the dumping of pollutants into Philippine waters. It established penalties for activities that created marine pollution, such as oil spills and dumping of toxic chemicals. The decree also mandated the creation of a National Marine Pollution Monitoring System that would enforce regulations and monitor water quality. Additionally, it required all vessels within Philippine waters to comply with international pollution prevention and control standards. The overall purpose of the decree was to prevent the degradation of the country's water resources and protect the health and livelihoods of those dependent on them.

29. Claveria decree of 1849


Answer:

The Spaniards issued the Claveria Decree in 1849 in an attempt to assign all Filipinos surnames. This decree was inconsistently enforced, and there are no records describing its institution. In many cases the local magistrates simply assigned surnames to those who did not already have one.

On the 21st of November 1849, the Governor General of the Philippines, Don Narciso Claveria y Zaldua, issued a law (thereafter called the Claveria Decree) requiring Filipinos to adopt Spanish AND indigenous names from the Catalogo Alfabetico de Apellidos for civil and legal purposes
