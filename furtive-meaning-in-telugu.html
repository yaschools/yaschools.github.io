The answer to the blank spaces in the question is furtive and predictable.

This question is a "fill in the blank" type of question. In English, it is very useful to understand ranges of vocabulary and the meaning of the words that would suit best with the context of the question. Each word, although similar, may imply that it is not suitable to be used in certain situation.

As for the original question, the choice E (furtive and predictable) makes the most sense considering the little information acquired from that one sentence alone. First, we must look at the dictionary about the definition of each answer choice in the question, then we have to try to fill them should they become grammatically correct or match the context of the situation.

Details of the multiple choices

A. Sinister & despondent. Sinister here does not fit the context since the woman should be terrified instead of "remembered their upcoming anniversary".

B. Clandestine & translucent. According to the definition, clandestine matches the context, but a translucent situation does not seem to be correct in this case.

C. Circumspect & indubitable. The meaning of the word "circumspect" wouldn't make sense in this context, and the word indubitable is too much of a stretch when placed in this question's context.

D. Droll & insidious. The meaning of the word "droll" is quite off when put in that question since the man is obviously not doing it on purpose (behavior), also an insidious situation doesn't match the context of the situation.

E. Furtive & predictable. The definition of furtive matches the behavior of the man because the phrase "she remembered the upcoming anniversary" implies that something is wrong with this couple. The man is hiding something, thus making the situation predictable in a way that a fight would most likely happen.
