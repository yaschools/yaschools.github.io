5. Allowing individual to use copyrighted material without the permission from a copyright holder.


Fair Use

Allowing copyrighted material without the permission from a copyright holder called fair use. For limited uses, such as criticism, parody, news reporting, research and scholarship, and teaching, fair use permits the use of copyrighted material without the copyright holder's consent. The following are the four fair use criteria:

The usage's nature and purpose, particularly whether it's being done for profit or nonprofit educational objectives;The nature of the copyrighted work, including its publication status and whether it is fiction or nonfiction;The percentage of the work utilized in relation to the full copyrighted work, such as when a poem is completely used or a chapter from a large book is used;The impact of the use on the possible market for the work protected by a copyright.

12. Give 5 example of copyright ( give its background and description of the said copyright) ​


Copyright is a type of legal protection provided to authors, artists, and creators of original works. It grants them exclusive rights to their creations, allowing them to control how their work is used, distributed, and copied. Here are five examples of copyright:

1. Literary works: This type of copyright protects original literary works such as novels, poems, and short stories. It includes the right to reproduce, distribute, and perform the work.

2. Musical works: This type of copyright protects original musical compositions, including both lyrics and melodies. It includes the right to make and distribute copies of the work, as well as the right to perform it in public.

3. Dramatic works: This type of copyright protects original plays and other dramatic works, including screenplays and scripts. It includes the right to perform the work in public and to create derivative works based on the original.

4. Artistic works: This type of copyright protects original visual and graphic art, including paintings, drawings, and photographs. It includes the right to reproduce and distribute copies of the work, as well as the right to display it in public.

5. Computer programs: This type of copyright protects original software programs and applications. It includes the right to reproduce and distribute copies of the program, as well as the right to create derivative works based on the original.

20. ano ang kahulugan ng copyright? tagalog.


Ang kahulugan ng copyright ay ang ekslusibong legal na karapatan na binibigay sa isang maylika or kaya ay lisensyadong assignee para magprint, magperform ng isang materyal. Para magkaroon ang isang tao ng ekslusibong karapatan o copyright sa isang imbensyon o likha, kailangan nya ito ipa rehistro. Dito sa Pilipinas ang IPO Philippines ang syang may hawak ng pagpaparehistro. May bayad ito at kailangan din na wala pang kaparehas na marka na ipaparehistro. Kung may kaparehas na marka na, pwedeng madeny ang aplikasyon ng copyright.  

Simbolo

Ang simbolo ng copyright ay (c) or kaya ay ©. Ito  ay ang simbolong ginagamit para ipagbigay alam a ibang tao na ang likhang iyon (maliban sa musika/ sound recordings) ay rehistrado at may copyright ng isang tao. Hindi pwede na gamitin ng iba ng walang pahintulot ang likhang iyon ng walang pahintulot na isinulat. Kapag ginamit ito, maaring kasuhan ang lumabag at hingan ng danyos.

22. What is an example of a 'Description of your copyrighted work' on YouTube requirements for copyright counter-notifications?


A "Description of your copyrighted work" on YouTube refers to a detailed explanation of the original content that you own the copyright to and that was removed from YouTube due to a copyright infringement claim. This description should include information about the nature and scope of your copyrighted work, as well as specific details about how the alleged infringing material on YouTube is related to your work.

Here is an example of a "Description of your copyrighted work" that might be used in a YouTube copyright counter-notification:

"The copyrighted work at issue is a video titled 'My Original Song' that I uploaded to YouTube on January 1, 2020. This video consists of a song that I wrote and recorded, as well as accompanying visual content that I created. The alleged infringing material on YouTube is a video titled 'Song Rip-Off' that was uploaded by User X on February 1, 2020. This video contains a recording of my song, without my permission, along with visual content that is substantially similar to the content in my original video. I am the sole owner of the copyright in my song and the accompanying visual content, and I have not granted permission for User X to use my work in this manner."

Imprisonment of 3 years and 1 day to six years plus a fine of between 150,000 to 500,000 pesos for the second offense.
