12. 4) Which hormone triggers sleepiness?​


Answer:

The 'sleep' hormone. Melatonin is the hormone released by your brain to make you feel either sleepy at night time or awake during the day. When it's dark, melatonin is slowly released, telling your body it's time to go to sleep.

1.Routinely taking more than 30 minutes to fall asleep.

2.Regularly waking up many times and having trouble falling asleep again.

3.Frequent sleepiness during the day, frequent naps, or falling asleep unintentionally or at inappropriate times during the day.
