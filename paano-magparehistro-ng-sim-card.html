11. give viewpoint/opinions in Sim Card Registration Act​


Answer:

The Philippines is yet again exploring legislation which would require people to link their national identity card or passport to register a SIM card. After similar laws were squashed in shocking fashion earlier this year, new bills are enjoying renewed support from a number of influential groups in the country.

The registration of a SIM card, to which your national identity card or passport is linked, has become the norm in over 150 countries around the world, despite questions over its effectiveness and other collateral issues like security risks, state surveillance, and social exclusion.

Its most ardent supporters hail from law enforcement and national security agencies that are fond of pitching the idea that requiring a proof of identity to register a SIM and thus use a mobile device will deter and hamper the efforts of a wide array of boogeymen: terrorists, criminals, and a bevy of other bad actors.

Things have played out the same way in the Philippines. For years, the uniformed services and their patrons in Congress have lobbied hard for a SIM card registration law. In the wake of bombings or a spike in phone-enabled fraud, they influence public conversations to stoke support for a central SIM card registry — as if it were a panacea for many of society’s ills.

21. Gumawa ng reaction paper tungkol sa Mandatory Sim Card Registration​


Answer:

Ang Mandatory Sim Card Registration ay isang polisiya na layuning matugunan ang mga isyu sa seguridad na may kaugnayan sa paggamit ng teleponong selular. Sa ilalim ng polisiyang ito, kinakailangan na ang lahat ng gumagamit ng mga sim card ay magpa-rehistro upang maibsan ang mga posibleng krimen tulad ng pangha-hack, pandaraya, at pagkalat ng mga pekeng balita.

Nararapat na maging maingat sa pagpapatupad ng polisiyang ito. Dapat matiyak na ang mga impormasyon na ibinigay ng mga gumagamit ay ligtas at hindi magiging sanhi ng paglabag sa kanilang karapatang pang-pribado. Dapat din maging patas sa pagpapatupad ng polisiyang ito at hindi lamang pang-isa o pang-grupo ng mga indibidwal.

Sa isang banda, maaaring mabawasan ang krimen na may kaugnayan sa teleponong selular sa pamamagitan ng pagpaparehistro ng mga gumagamit ng sim card. Maaari ring magamit ang mga datos na nakalap upang mapanatili ang seguridad sa bansa. Sa kabila nito, hindi natin dapat kalimutan ang mga posibleng negatibong epekto ng polisiyang ito sa mga mamamayan.

Napapansin din na may ilang mga gumagamit ng teleponong selular na hindi nakakapagpakita ng mga papeles na kailangan para sa pagpaparehistro. Sila ay maaaring mawalan ng kanilang koneksyon sa telepono at maaaring magdulot ito ng paghihirap sa kanilang pang-araw-araw na buhay. Kinakailangan ng mga otoridad na maglaan ng tamang tulong sa pagproseso ng rehistro upang hindi mahirapan ang mga mamamayan.

Sa huli, hindi natin dapat isantabi ang mga benepisyo ng Mandatory Sim Card Registration, ngunit dapat din nating isaalang-alang ang mga posibleng epekto nito sa ating mga kababayan. Kinakailangan ng maingat na pag-aaral at pagpapatupad upang masiguro ang kaligtasan ng lahat sa panahon ng pagpapatupad ng polisiyang ito.

30. what is the advantages and disadvantages of a sim card​


A Subscriber Identity Module (SIM) card is a small, removable card that is inserted into a mobile device to identify and authenticate the user. Here are some advantages and disadvantages of using a SIM card:

Advantages:
1. Convenience: SIM cards allow users to easily switch between mobile devices without losing their personal data or contacts.
2. Security: SIM cards help to secure mobile devices by providing authentication and encryption of voice and data communications.
3. Cost-effective: SIM cards are relatively inexpensive and can be easily replaced if lost or damaged.
4. International roaming: SIM cards allow travelers to use their mobile devices in other countries by purchasing a local SIM card instead of paying expensive roaming charges.

Disadvantages:
1. Limited storage: SIM cards have limited storage capacity for contacts, messages, and other data compared to the internal storage of mobile devices.
2. Compatibility: SIM cards may not be compatible with all mobile devices, particularly older models.
3. Risk of loss or theft: Since the SIM card is removable, there is a risk of losing or having it stolen, which could result in unauthorized access to personal data.
4. Activation process: SIM cards often require a complex activation process, which can be confusing for some users.

Overall, the advantages of using a SIM card outweigh the disadvantages, making it a convenient and secure option for mobile users.
