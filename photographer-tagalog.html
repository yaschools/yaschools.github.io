16. photograph is the produced of photograph​


PHOTOGRAPH

a photograph (also known as a photo) is an image created by light falling on a photosensitive surface, usually photographic film or an electronic image sensor, such as a CCD or a CMOS chip. Most photographs are created using a smartphone/camera, which uses a lens to focus the scene's visible wavelengths of light into a reproduction of what the human eye would see. The process and practice of creating such images is called photography.

17. what are the properties of photographic rays and give the uses of photographic rays?​


Properties Of Photographic Rays

On a photographic plate, the passage of radiation (such as x-rays, gamma-rays, or neutrons) through a material is captured.

A substance used in photographic cameras for recording images is called photographic film. It is made of translucent plastic in the form of a strip or sheet, and one side of it is covered in gelatinous emulsion made of light-sensitive silver halide crystals. A photographic film undergoes chemical changes when exposed to light by a photographic camera, depending on how much light is absorbed by each crystal. In the emulsion, these modifications produce an intangible latent image that is later fixed and developed into a visible photograph. While color film has three layers, each of which is sensitive to a different color, black and white photographic films only have one layer of silver halide crystals. There are even more levels in some color movies.

Early attempts at photography used silver-plated copper sheets, paper, and even leather that were covered in light-sensitive chemicals rather than film. Glass plates became the norm by the middle of the 19th century since early transparent polymers could not match glass's opaqueness and were still much more expensive. George Eastman created the first roll-based, flexible film in 1885, although it was made of paper rather than synthetic material.
