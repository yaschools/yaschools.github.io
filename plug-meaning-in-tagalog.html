25. Assignment:Give the meaning and draw the following. 1. Switch2. Fuse3. Electric meter4. Breaker5. Plug6. Outlet​


1. Switch - A switch is a device that is used to turn on or off an electrical circuit. It is typically a small lever or button that controls the flow of electricity to a specific device or set of devices.

2. Fuse - A fuse is a safety device that is designed to protect electrical circuits from damage caused by overloading or short circuits. It contains a metal wire or filament that melts when too much current flows through it, interrupting the electrical circuit and preventing damage to the rest of the system.

3. Electric meter - An electric meter is a device that measures the amount of electricity that is consumed by a household or business. It is typically installed by the utility company and is used to calculate the amount of electricity that is used and the associated charges.

4. Breaker - A breaker is a device that is used to protect electrical circuits from damage caused by overloading or short circuits. It works by automatically shutting off the flow of electricity when a fault is detected, preventing damage to the rest of the system.

5. Plug - A plug is a device that is used to connect an electrical device to a power source. It typically has two or three prongs that are inserted into an outlet, providing a secure connection and allowing electricity to flow to the device.

6. Outlet - An outlet is a device that is installed in a wall or other surface and is used to provide a connection point for electrical devices. It typically has two or three slots that are designed to accept the prongs of a plug, allowing electricity to flow from the power source to the device.

30. 5. What is the function of the spark plug? A. The spark plug ignites the mixture making it temperature high. C. The spark plug pushes the piston up. B. The spark plug pushes the piston down D. The spark plug pushes the gases out of the cylinder​


Answer:

Letter A

Explanation:

Functions of Spark Plugs

A spark plug has two major functions in internal combustion engines which include:

Igniting fuel/air mixture: as electrical energy is transmitted through the component, it ignites the gasoline/air mixture in the combustion chamber.Removing of heat: spark plugs cannot create heat, but they can only be used to remove heat. The temperature of the end of the plugs firing end must be low enough to prevent pre-ignition but should be high enough to prevent fouling. Spark plugs can serve as a heat exchanger by eliminating the unwanted thermal energy from the combustion chamber. The heat is then transferred to the engines cooling system.
