Answer:

B.

Explanation:

Upon inhalation, the diaphragm contracts and flattens and the chest cavity enlarges. This contraction creates a vacuum, which pulls air into the lungs. Upon exhalation, the diaphragm relaxes and returns to its domelike shape, and air is forced out of the lungs.

When you exhale, muscles decrease the size of your chest cavity and squeeze your lungs. This decreases their volume, so pressure inside the lungs increases. As a result, air rushes out of the lungs, flowing from an area of higher to lower pressure.

8. PAST PARTICIPLE OF shrink​


Answer:

Generally, shrank is the simple past tense form of "shrink" like in "I shrank the shirt in the wash." Shrunk is the past participle being paired with "have" as in "I have shrunk the jeans." There are rarer examples of shrinked and shrunken in literature but not enough to support those usages as standard.

23. the cell will shrink due to..​


TONICTY

[tex]__________________________[/tex]

The cell will shrink due to a hypertonic solution.

A hypertonic solution is when there is more solute outside the cell, that causes water to leave the cell which results the cell to shrink.A hypotonic solution is when there is more solute outside the cell, that causes water to go inside the cell which results the cell to burst. Plants a prefer hypotonic solution since they have a cell wall that prevents the cell from busting.A isotonic solution is when the amount of solute and water is equal both inside and outside of the cell. Animal cells prefer an isotonic solution since there is equilibrium, which helps them to function.

29. Causes cell to shrink


A hypotonic solution causes a cell to swell, whereas a hypertonic solution causes a cell to shrink. Although it is related to osmolality, tonicity also takes into consideration the ability of the solute to cross the cell membrane

Answer:

A hypotonic solution causes a cell to swell, whereas a hypertonic solution causes a cell to shrink. Although it is related to osmolality, tonicity also takes into consideration the ability of the solute to cross the cell membrane.
