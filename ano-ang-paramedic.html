9. Paramedics usually perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) on people who are unconscious after an accident. How does it save people’s lives?


Answer:

CPR can be life-saving first aid

If no CPR is performed, it only takes three to four minutes for the person to become brain dead due to a lack of oxygen. By performing CPR, you circulate the blood so it can provide oxygen to the body, and the brain and other organs stay alive while you wait for the ambulance.

The Allied Health or Medical Profession

The medical jobs and specializations are wide and have a different kind of specialization depending on the illness that is being cured or focused on.

With being said, there is a wide option of cross-training and upskilling knowledge of the way illnesses and injuries can be diagnosed and cured.

The Allied Health Profession or Jobs

These are types of jobs that have a focus entirely on prevention, diagnosis, and recovery process.

They are broad in classifications, though they are not the same profession as nurses and doctors, they work in different health teams for their specialization. Allied health professionals apply scientific principles and evidence-based practice in order to optimize patient or client outcomes.

Some examples are:

-Clinical Officer

-Social Worker

-Radiation therapist

-Dental Hygienist

-Paramedics

-Music Therapist

-Cardiovascular Technologist

-Physical Therapist

The Allied Medical Professions

These are the primary medical physician professionals that undergo extensive studies and hands-on practice to get and pass the licensure specialization.

They are known as the health professionals that possess a license and their main role is to maintain and restorative human health conditions through the practice of medicine.

Patients undergo examinations and laboratory processes to further review and evaluate their medical history, illnesses, and injuries.

Some examples are:

-Medical Assistant

-ORTHOPTIST
