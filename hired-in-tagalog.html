8. whos hires deadalus?​


Answer:

Daedalus is one of the characters of the famous Greek myth "Daedalus and Icarus". Here, Daedalus was portrayed like an architect or engineer wherein he made various architectural designs and built magnificent and complicated structures. Daedalus is very talented and had many inventions, a sample of which are the wings he made for him and his son Icarus.

As narrated in the story, it was King Minos who hired Daedalus. King Minos hired him to create the plan for the Minoan Palace of Knossos, one of the most important archaeological sites in Crete and Greece today. The Palace has 1,300 rooms which are decorated with stunning frescoes and artifacts.

Daedalus was also the one hired by King Minos to build the infamous Labyrinth. King Minos wanted a building suitable to imprison the mythical monster Minotaur. According to the myth, King Minos used to imprison his enemies in the Labyrinth, making sure that they would be killed by the monster.

However, later on, King Minos and Daedalus had a misunderstanding which severed their relationship which led to the imprisonment of Daedalus and his son Icarus inside the Labyrinth.

27. Identify and explain the various human resource management practices in the pre-hiring, and post-hiring stages of the employment cycle.


HUMAN RESOURCE MANAGEMENT PRACTICES IN THE PRE-HIRING AND POST HIRING STAGES OF THE EMPLOYMENT CYCLE

The human resource management practices in the pre-hiring and post hiring stages of the employment cycle  are sourcing, screening, shortlisting and selecting the right candidates for the filling the required vacant positions, orientation, training and motivation. The first process in human resources practices the organization is doing selective hiring to assure that they can hire the right people. Next process in human resources practices is  skimming through resumes and cover letters to find the closest applicant-job-description matches keeping in mind qualifications, experience, skill sets, and projected candidate-organization fit. Then, the human resources practices process is giving training to the employees to improve their productivity and performance. Human resource practices process of hiring helps develop a competent staff who make the business achieve its targets in an efficient and effective way.

Human resource process during pre-hiring and post hiring stages are as follows:

1. Recruitment

2.Selection

3. Employee orientation

4.Training

5. Motivation
