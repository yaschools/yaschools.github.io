11. How did Filipino women respond to oppression and inequality in various stages of womens struggle throughout philippine history?​


Answer:

Filipino women have responded to oppression and inequality in various ways throughout Philippine history. During the Spanish colonial period, many women participated in the revolution against Spanish rule, including Gabriela Silang and Melchora Aquino. During the American colonial period, women played a significant role in the Philippine-American War, including Gregoria de Jesus and Lucia Zulueta.

During the 20th century, women's rights movements began to form, led by figures such as Josefa Llanes Escoda, who founded the Girl Scouts of the Philippines, and Rosa Sevilla de Alvero, who helped pass the Women's Suffrage Bill in 1937.

In recent years, Filipino women have continued to fight for equal rights and representation in politics and the workforce. The #MeToo movement and the fight for reproductive rights have also been prominent issues for Filipino women.

In summary, throughout Philippine history, Filipino women have been active participants in the struggle against oppression and inequality. They have fought for their rights and representation through various means, including revolution, war, and activism.

21. Explain how oppression emerged in the Philippine society


Oppression in the Philippine society emerged due to a combination of factors such as colonization, feudalism, and class struggle. The Philippines was colonized by different European powers for centuries, starting with Spain in the 16th century followed by the United States, which took over after the Spanish-American War in 1898. During this time, Filipinos were deprived of their rights and resources, and their culture and identity were suppressed and erased.

Feudalism was also prevalent in the Philippines, particularly in the rural areas where landlords and hacienderos owned vast tracts of lands and exploited the labor of the peasantry. The elites controlled the government and the economy, while the working class, peasants, and indigenous peoples were marginalized and oppressed.

The class struggle intensified during the 20th century, as the Philippine Revolution against the Spanish colonial government, the national democratic movement, and the labor and peasant struggles challenged the status quo and demanded social justice and equality. However, these movements were met with violent repression, including massacres, torture, and extrajudicial killings, by the ruling elite and their military forces.

Today, oppression in the Philippines continues to manifest in various forms such as human rights violations, gender discrimination, poverty, and social inequality. The struggle for a just and democratic society remains a fundamental challenge for the Filipino people.
