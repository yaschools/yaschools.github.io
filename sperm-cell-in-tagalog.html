5. In sexual reproduction, what source of the genetic material in a zygote?WHY?A. egg cell only B. a pollen and a sperm cell C. sperm cell only D. an egg cell and a sperm cell


The answer is letter D.

In a sexual reproduction, the source of the genetic material in a zygote is both the egg cell and sperm cell. Both of the cells share their genetic materials.

During fertilization, the nuclei of both cells combine or fuse with each other. The nucleus of both cells contain half of the genetic material of the individual giving a whole set of genetic material for the zygote, half from the egg cell or mother and half from the sperm cell of the father.

Zygote, fertilized egg cell that results from the union of a female gamete (egg, or ovum) with a male gamete (sperm). In the embryonic development of humans and other animals, the zygote stage is brief and is followed by cleavage, when the single cell becomes subdivided into smaller cells.

ANSWER: Sperm is the male reproductive cell, or gamete, in anisogamous forms of sexual reproduction.

The purpose of a sperm cell is to be released during sexual intercourse and to eventually meet with an ovum (egg cell), which is produced by a biologically female body.

D

Explanation:

The zygote is formed after the fertilization event takes place where the male gamete which is the sperm fuses with the female gamete which is the ovum or the egg cell. Hence, the zygote consists of the genetic material of both the male and the female parent in the sexual mode of reproduction.
