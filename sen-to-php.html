14. differentiate the two modes of Japanese music (yo-sen and in-sen​


Answer:

•Yo-sen- The yo scale, which does not contain minor notes, according to a traditional theory is a pentatonic scale used in much Japanese music including[1] gagaku and shomyo. The yo scale is used specifically in folk songs and early popular songs and is contrasted with the in scale which does contain minor notes.[2] The in scale is described as 'dark' while the yo scale is described as 'bright' sounding.[3]

•In-sen- Insen is a tuning scale adapted from shamisen music by Yatsuhashi Kengyō for tuning of the koto. It only differs from the hirajoshi scale by one note. In D mode it consists of: D-E♭-G-A-C so it has the same notes as the Phrygian chord.

ANSWER

$ 370.25

SOLUTION

AINNO EARNS $2, 221.50 every week but except Sundays.

In a week, there is 7 days, but without Sunday, so only 6 days

6 ÷ 2, 221.50

6÷2 = 0

6÷22 = 3 × 6 = 18. 22 - 18 = 4, bring down 2, 42

6÷42 = 7 × 6 = 42, bring down 1

6÷1 = 0 × 6 = 0. 1 - 0 =1, bring down .5, 1.5 or 15

6÷15 = .2 × 6 = 12. 15 - 12 = 3, bring down zero, 30

6÷30 = 5 × 6 = 30

= 370.25
