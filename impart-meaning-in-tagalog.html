In Latin, the term scandare means to climb.

According to wordsense website, scandare is translated as "I climb" if it is a verb. It originated from Latin language. To add some details, it is the inflection of scando (second-person singular present passive subjunctive). And, this scando means to jump. Besides to climb, scando as a verb can also be defined as to ascent and to mount.

Meanwhile, here is the explanation to the remaining answer choices in the original questions:

to exist: sum. This might be included in the most popular Latin word there is, thanks to the quote "Cogito ergo sum" as said by Renee Descartes. For your information, cogito ergo sum is translated as "I think therefore I am". If that seems confusing to you, it actually means that one is sure that he exists because he is capable of thinking. Should you be interested, check out Descarters' philosophy.to impart: induo. Here, induo is similar to the English form, which is in verb.to deduce: colligo. To deduce in English means to form a conclusion from thinking logically. The word deduce is highly related to philosophy as a subject.
