18. Bakit mangangalakal ang nasa pangalawang palapag ng ziggurat?huhu pahelppp!!​


Answer:

Ang nasa pangalawang palapag ng ziggurat ay maaaring nangangalakal dahil sa pangangailangan sa mga produkto at serbisyo na hindi nila maabot sa ibaba. Ang mga tao sa ibabaw ay may mas mataas na posisyon sa lipunan kaya mas may kakayahan silang bumili at magpakalakal. Sa ganitong paraan, sila ay maaaring makabili ng mga bagay na hindi nila maabot sa ibaba, at maaaring magbenta ng mga produkto na hindi nila maibenta sa ibaba. Ang pagkakalakal sa ibabaw ay maaaring magbigay ng kabuhayan sa mga tao at magdagdag ng pera sa ekonomiya ng lugar.

27. ito ay templo ng summeruan na binibuo ng ilang palapag?​


Answer:

The original "Stairway to Heaven". The grand staircase of the great ziggurat at Ur. The temple was dedicated to Nanna, the moon god.

OR:

Ziggurats were built by ancient Sumerians, Akkadians, Elamites, Eblaites, and Babylonians for local religions. Each ziggurat was part of a temple complex that included other buildings. The precursors of the ziggurat were raised platforms that date from the Ubaid period[9] during the sixth millennium. The ziggurats began as platforms (usually oval, rectangular, or square). The ziggurat was a mastaba-like structure with a flat top. The sun-baked bricks made up the core of the ziggurat with facings of fired bricks on the outside. Each step was slightly smaller than the step below it. The facings were often glazed in different colors and may have had astrological significance. Kings sometimes had their names engraved on these glazed bricks. The number of floors ranged from two to seven.
