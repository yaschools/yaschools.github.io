16. what does external bleeding mean?​


Answer:

External bleeding happens when blood exits through a break in the skin. Blood loss from bleeding tissue can also be apparent when blood exits through a natural opening in the body, such as the: mouth.

Bleeding, also called hemorrhage, is the name used to describe blood loss. It can refer to blood loss inside the body, called internal bleeding, or to blood loss outside of the body, called external bleeding.

Blood loss can occur in almost any area of the body. Internal bleeding occurs when blood leaks out through a damaged blood vessel or organ. External bleeding happens when blood exits through a break in the skin.

OBSERVABLE AND EXTERNAL BEHAVIOR

Observable behaviors are those that you can observe someone else doing. This includes activities such as walking, talking, sitting, singing, embracing, eating, sleeping, and solving math problems. Mental and emotional actions and states that cannot be directly witnessed are considered unobservable behaviors.

Problem behaviors that are focused toward the outside world are known as externalizing behaviors. Physical aggressiveness, disobedience of regulations, cheating, theft, and property destruction are among them.
