Answer:

Maaaring tagalog ng against ay labag sa, laban sa, salungat o kontra.

Halimbawa:

1. Ang simbahang katoliko ay laban sa aborsyon

(The Catholic Church is against abortion.)

2. Labag sa kanyang kalooban ng kanyang ginawa.

( What he did was against his conscience.)

3. Ang mga rebelde ay kontra sa gobyerno.

(The rebels are against the government)

4. Salungat sa kanyang paniniwala ang kanyang ginawa.

(What he did was against his beliefs)

10. Pa-explain naman po ito sa tagalog po sana yung mas maiintindihan po.ASAP PO.ThankyouDISPLACED AGGRESSIVE -defense mechanism in which a person transfers and diverts his aggressive behavior against himself or some other person or objects.​


Answer:

Displaced aggression is a defense mechanism that occurs when a person takes out their anger, frustration, or aggression on someone or something that is not the actual cause of their negative emotions. Instead of expressing their feelings towards the actual source, they displace it onto a safer target, such as a friend, family member, or even an object like a punching bag or pillow. This allows them to release their emotions without facing the consequences of confronting the actual source of their anger or frustration. However, this can be harmful to the person or object being displaced upon and may lead to further negative consequences.

Ang Displaced Aggressive ay isang mekanismo ng pagdepensa kung saan naglilipat ang isang tao ng kanyang galit at pagsisimula ng kanyang agresibong pag-uugali sa kanyang sarili, sa ibang tao, o sa ibang bagay.

Explanation:

Emilio Aguinaldo donated his house, which is now known as the Aguinaldo Shrine, to the Philippine government to serve as a symbol of his patriotism and his contribution to the country's struggle for independence. He wanted the house to become a national shrine that would remind Filipinos of their hard-won freedom and inspire them to uphold the values of liberty, democracy, and national sovereignty.

The topmost level of Aguinaldo's house served as a watchtower that allowed the Philippine revolutionary forces to monitor the movements of the Spanish troops in the surrounding areas. From this vantage point, the revolutionaries could detect any imminent threat and prepare their defenses accordingly. This helped them to mount a successful defense against the Spanish attack during the Battle of Alapan on May 28, 1898, which ultimately led to the hoisting of the Philippine flag.
