1. how to find your height in meters​


Answer:

Since height is normally given in both feet and inches when these units are in play rather than using a decimal, the easiest way to convert height from feet to meters is to convert height entirely to inches and then divide by 39.37 to get meters. For example, 5 ft 10 in is 70", and 70/39.37 = 1.778 m.

12. do you know how to find the height of your schools flagpole without directly measurin it


What are the possible things you are able to get a hold of?
If you have your own height, and the length of the shadow of the flagpole and your height, you can use ration and proportion.

ex. Your height is 1.5m, and  your shadow is 1m, and you find out that the flagpole's shadow is 5m. You can do this simple equation.

1.5 :1 = x:5

x being the height of the flagpole. You will need to multiply the extremes (1.5 and 5) and the means (1 and x) to get x. x would be, in this case 7.5. That would be the height of the flagpole.

If ever the flagpole is indoors, try to find a source of light.
