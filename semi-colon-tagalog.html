Answer:

Colons (:) are used in sentences to show that something is following, like a quotation, example, or list. Semicolons (;) are used to join two independent clauses, or two complete thoughts that could stand alone as complete sentences.

17. How to correctly use semi-colons


Answer:

Use a semicolon between closely related independent clauses which are not joined by a coordinating conjunction. This rule means that semicolons are used between two complete sentences which are not already linked by words like and, but, or, nor, for, so, yet.

Answer: The semicolon or semi-colon; is a symbol commonly used as orthographic punctuation. In the English language, a semicolon is most commonly used to link two independent clauses that are closely related in thought. When a semicolon joins two or more ideas in one sentence, those ideas are then given equal rank. Use a semicolon between closely related independent clauses which are not joined by a coordinating conjunction. This rule means that semicolons are used between two complete sentences which are not already linked by words like and, but, or, nor, for, so, yet. A semicolon may be used between independent clauses joined by a connector, such as and, but, or, nor, etc., when one or more commas appear in the first clause. Example: When I finish here, and I will soon, I'll be glad to help you; and that is a promise I will keep.

28. How do we use a Semi colon


Answer:

Semicolons are used to connect two independent clauses (sentences that can stand alone) that are related in thought. Basically, semicolon is longer than a comma but shorter than a period.

For example,

These clauses become two sentences when a period is used:

My laptop is not working anymore. The battery is dead.

These clauses that are related in thought are joined together by a semicolon:

My laptop is not working anymore; the battery is dead.

Answer:most commonly used to link (in a single sentence) two independent clauses
