26. Activities that use sieving and benefits of sieving it in the community


Sieving is the separation of various mixtures of solid particles that have various sizes of materials using a sieve. The sieving process is also used as a cleaning tool, separating contaminants whose size is different from the raw material.

Sieving activities are widely used in the world of science and technology (in food laboratories, building materials laboratories, soil laboratories, in soil testing fields, and so on) and in the culinary world. In addition to separating powdered or bulk materials, a sieve is also used to separate the dough or mixture from the liquid, for example when making traditional coconut milk.

Sieving is useful because it makes it easier for people to get sand of uniform size. Thus sieving can be defined as a method of separating various mixtures of solid particles so that uniform particle sizes are obtained and free from contaminants of different sizes by using a sieving tool.

Screens with various designs have been widely used and developed extensively in the process of separating grains by size.

Sieving is the separation of materials based on the size of the sieve wire machine, materials that have a size smaller than the diameter of the machine will pass and materials that have a larger size will be retained on the surface of the sieve wire. The materials that pass through the sieve are of uniform size and the retained material is returned for re-milling.

Sieve is a tool used to separate unwanted parts based on their size, from bulk materials and powders that have small particle sizes and dough materials or mixtures of their liquids.

Sieves can be made of metal, polymers, plant fibers (cotton yarn, jute, etc.), and wood. Many wooden sieves were made before the Industrial Revolution and are still made by traditional people.

In a sieve made of wood, generally in the form of woven wooden slats. Wooden sieves are also used in various scientific experiments and experiments when metal and polymer sieves cannot be used on the sieved material because they can contaminate the material.

Sieving is done by placing the bulk material on top of the sieve while shaking the sieve. Particles smaller than the mesh number will fall, while the larger ones will remain on the sieve. Depending on the purpose, large particles can be ground back to make them smaller or discarded because they are not needed.
