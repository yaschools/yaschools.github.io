Answer:

1.)

If a war were to happen in the Philippines, it would be declared by the President of the Philippines. The President is the Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces of the Philippines, and is responsible for maintaining the national security and defending the country.

The President has the authority to declare war if the country is under attack, or if there is a threat to the sovereignty or territorial integrity of the Philippines. The President may also declare war if the country is facing an imminent danger, or when there is a need to protect Filipinos abroad. It is up to the President to determine whether or not a war should be declared, and he or she has the power to do so.

The Constitution of the Philippines also states that the Congress has the authority to declare war, provided that the declaration is approved by at least two-thirds of all the Members of the House of Representatives and of the Senate. This means that the President would need the support of the Congress should he or she decide to declare war.

It is important to note that the decision to declare war is a serious one, and it should not be taken lightly. The consequences of declaring war can be far-reaching and devastating, both for the people of the Philippines and for the country as a whole. If a war were to happen in the Philippines, the President and the Congress must consider the potential ramifications before deciding to go to war.

2.)

The military is a unique political institution and is characterized by a number of distinct features. One of the most important features of the military is its hierarchical structure. This structure is based on rank and authority, with officers and enlisted personnel occupying different positions within the chain of command. At the top of the hierarchy are the highest-ranking officers, who are responsible for making major decisions and strategy for the military. This hierarchical structure is essential for the efficient functioning of the military, as it ensures that orders are followed and that discipline is maintained.

Another important feature of the military is its non-partisan nature. The military is apolitical, meaning that it is not affiliated with any particular political party or ideology. This neutrality ensures that the military can serve to protect the nation’s interests without being influenced by partisan politics.

The military is also highly organized and disciplined. This is essential in order to ensure that it can quickly and effectively respond to threats and conflicts. The organization of the military also ensures that resources are used efficiently and that personnel are adequately trained and prepared for their roles.

Finally, the military is a professional institution. This means that military personnel are highly trained and experienced in the use of weapons and tactics. The professionalism of the military ensures that it is well-equipped to handle a wide range of tasks, from peacekeeping operations to combat operations.

Overall, the military is a unique political institution that is characterized by its hierarchical structure, non-partisan nature, organization and discipline, and professionalism. These features ensure that the military remains an effective tool for protecting the nation’s interests.
