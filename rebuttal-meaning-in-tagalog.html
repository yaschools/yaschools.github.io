13. rebuttal of video games should be a sport


Answer:

While some gamers may argue that video games should be considered a sport, there is no denying that there are many differences between traditional sports and video games. Traditional sports require physical activity that contributes to physical fitness, whereas playing video games does not. Additionally, traditional sports involve competition against other players, whereas video games involve competing against computer-controlled opponents or one's own personal best. Finally, traditional sports involve a certain set of skills and strategies that must be practiced and perfected in order to be successful, whereas video games are often mastered through trial and error. It is these differences that make it difficult to consider video games a sport.

26. definition of rebuttal/refutation​


Answer:

Refutation - the action of proving a statement or theory to be wrong or false.

Rebuttal (in law) - is a piece of evidence used to refute or dismiss other evidence provided by the opposing party.

Note:

Know that rebutting an idea involves making a counter-argument, whether or not that counter-argument is right. To refute an idea implies effectively demonstrate that it is incorrect.
