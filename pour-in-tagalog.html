14. Is it impolite in Japan to pour your own drink when eating with others--you pour your companion's drink and your companion pours yours? What is your justification?


Yes.

Pouring your own drink may be a sign of disrespect to the Japanese; however, the Japanese also will understand especially if you are not from Japan. This is of those things of "What is a no no in Japan".

The Japanese behavior when it comes to drinking dates back all the way to ancient Japan. The practice of pouring a drink for another is a sign of respect for each other. This especially applies to the kouhai and senpai practices of the Japanese.

Although the Japanese etiquette may differ from other countries, we all have our cultures. Often times when people from other countries visit your own homeland, you would expect that they do things the way they do in your own culture.

It just best to follow the old saying "When in Rome, do as the Romans do"

29. how does the sound produced from pouring hot watediffer from the sound from pouring cold water?


Explanation:

In cold water, the molecules carry less energy and are less 'excited'.

This means that they move more slowly and are more likely to 'stick' to other molecules.

Cold water's higher viscosity also causes it to bubble less when poured.

This creates a lower frequency sound when the water is poured.

Hot water produces a higher pitched sound when poured because the energised molecules are moving around more rapidly
