10. what is the meaning of lyric and epic poetry


Lyric poetry flows and conveys more-or-less peaceful emotions; compare a lyric poem to a song and you won’t go far wrong.

Epic poetry tells a large story, hence tends to be large itself, and because stories involve conflict and epic stories get that way by embodying epic conflicts, an epic poem tends to have few if any lyrical passages.

11. folk epicWith meaning​


Answer:Folk epics are long heroic narratives in verse which recount the adventures of tribal heroes and in the process express the customs, beliefs, and ideals of the people who sing them.

Explanation:i hope it helps

Answer:

A folk epic is a long narrative poem or story that reflects the cultural beliefs, values, and traditions of a particular society. It is typically passed down orally from generation to generation, and may have been created or adapted by multiple authors over time.

One example of a folk epic with meaning is the "Mahabharata," an ancient Indian epic that tells the story of a great war between two royal families, the Pandavas and the Kauravas. The epic is believed to have been composed between the 8th and 9th centuries BCE and is one of the longest poems in the world, with over 100,000 verses.

The Mahabharata explores complex themes such as duty, honor, loyalty, and the consequences of human actions. It includes numerous subplots and characters, each with their own motivations and perspectives, which adds to its richness and depth.

The epic also contains many philosophical and spiritual teachings, including the concept of dharma (the cosmic order), the importance of self-knowledge, and the idea that all beings are interconnected. Through the Mahabharata, Indian culture and mythology are preserved and passed down to future generations, making it an important cultural artifact that continues to inspire and inform people today.
