12. what does incomplete dominance mean?give an example.​


Answer:

Incomplete dominance means that an allele is neither dominant nor recessive.

Example of incomplete dominance in humans

When one parent with straight hair and one with curly hair have a child with wavy hair.

Incomplete dominance is when a dominant allele, or form of a gene, does not completely mask the effects of a recessive allele, and the organism's resulting physical appearance shows a blending of both alleles.

Example:
In humans, skin color is an an example of incomplete dominance because the genes responsible for melanin production and light or dark skin can't establish dominance.

22. what does incomplete dominance means in genetic​


Answer:

Incomplete dominance is a form of intermediate inheritance in which one allele for a specific trait is not completely expressed over its paired allele. This results in a third phenotype in which the expressed physical trait is a combination of the phenotypes of both alleles. Unlike complete dominance inheritance, one allele does not dominate or mask the other.
