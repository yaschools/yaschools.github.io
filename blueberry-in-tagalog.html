the value of two strawberries with a blueberry, divided by a blueberry with an apple, is 6/7 in terms of apples or fruit.

Explanation:

To solve this problem, we need to first determine the value of each fruit in terms of apples. Based on the information given, we can set up the following conversion factors:

1 blueberry = 1/2 strawberry = 1/6 apple

1 apple = 1 apple

1 strawberry = 2 blueberries = 1/3 apple

Using these conversion factors, we can convert the two strawberries and a blueberry to apples as follows:

2 strawberries + 1 blueberry = 2 strawberries * (1/3 apple/strawberry) + 1 blueberry * (1/6 apple/blueberry) = 2/3 apple + 1/6 apple = 4/6 apple = 2/3 apple

We can then convert the blueberry and apple to apples as follows:

1 blueberry + 1 apple = 1 blueberry * (1/6 apple/blueberry) + 1 apple * (1 apple/apple) = 1/6 apple + 1 apple = 7/6 apple

Finally, we can divide the value of the two strawberries and a blueberry by the value of the blueberry and an apple as follows:

(2/3 apple) / (7/6 apple) = (2/3) / (7/6) = 6/7

Therefore, the value of two strawberries with a blueberry, divided by a blueberry with an apple, is 6/7 in terms of apples or fruit.

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to accurately compare the price for blueberries of the two stores, let us compare them in terms of pounds as that is what is provided in the choices.

TA Grocery Store

16/8 [8 oz = $2.80] 16/8

16 oz = $5.60

1 pound = $5.60

Farmer's Market

16/14 [14 oz = $4.20] 16/14

16 oz = $4.80

1 pound = $4.80

Price Difference

$4.80 - $5.60 = -$0.80

Therefore, C) blueberries are $0.80 per pound less expensive at the farmer's market.

9. At a grocery store, blueberries come packaged in 8-ounce containers for $2.80. At a farmer's market, blueberries cost $4.20 for 14 ounces. There are 16 ounces in one pound. Which statement accurately compares the cost of blueberries? Blueberries are $0.23 per pound more expensive at the farmer’s market. Blueberries are $0.50 per pound less expensive at the farmer’s market. Blueberries are $0.80 per pound less expensive at the farmer’s market. Blueberries are $1.40 per pound more expensive at the farmer’s market.


At the grocery stores, we have $2.80 for an 8 ounce package. This means that we have $2.80/8 = $0.35 per ounce.

At the farmer's market, it costs $4.20 for 14 ounces. This means that we have $4.20 / 14 = $0.30 per ounce.

Now, since the choices are in a "per pound" comparison, we have to convert these to pounds first. It says that there are 16 ounces in one pound.

Then, for the $0.35 per ounce in the grocery, we just multiply, ($0.35 / oz)(16 oz / 1 pound) = $0.35 x 16 = $5.6 / pound.

Similarly for the farmer's market, $0.30 x 16 = $4.8 / pound.

Now it's a matter of subtracting these: $5.6 - $4.8 = $0.8.

This means the answer is C: "Blueberries are $0.80 per pound less expensive at the farmer's market."

28. is blueberry a brain food?


Answer:

Blueberries are often considered a "brain food" because of their high antioxidant content, specifically a type of antioxidant called anthocyanins. Anthocyanins are thought to protect the brain from oxidative stress and inflammation, which may contribute to age-related cognitive decline and neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. Additionally, some studies have suggested that regular consumption of blueberries may improve cognitive function, including memory, attention, and decision-making.

While the evidence on the specific cognitive benefits of blueberries is not yet conclusive, adding blueberries and other fruits and vegetables to a balanced diet can provide many health benefits. So, including blueberries in your diet may be a great way to support your overall brain health, but it should be part of a well-balanced diet and a healthy lifestyle.
