Big Bang Theory

According to the big bang theory, the universe consists of a very large mass and a very large density as well. Then, the nuclear reaction causes the mass to explode and expand very quickly until it moves away from the center of the explosion. In the past 13.7 billion years, a cataclysmic explosion had occurred. In 1922, a physicist from Russia named Alexandra Friedman proclaimed this hypothesis official. An American astronomer named Edwin Hubble was responsible for the theory's formulation. Hubble asserts that the stars were once congregated in a single mass point identified as zero volume. However, at one time the zeroed volume once burst into creation and extended.

After a massive explosion occurred at volume zero, all galaxies and stars experienced a shift in the light of the stars closer to the red spectrum. In other words, the shift caused by the big bang caused the stars to move away from Earth and slowly drift away from each other.

Explanation:

The basics of the Big Bang theory are fairly simple. In short, the Big Bang hypothesis states that all of the current and past matter in the Universe came into existence at the same time, roughly 13.8 billion years ago. Suddenly, the Singularity began expanding, and the universe as we know it began.
