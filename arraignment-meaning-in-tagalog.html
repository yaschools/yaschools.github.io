In the United States, the constitutional provision that provides the basis for arraignment and plea is the Sixth Amendment, which guarantees the right to a speedy and public trial, the right to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation, the right to confront witnesses, the right to a lawyer, and the right to a trial by an impartial jury.

Alternate Answer:

Arraignment and plea are based on the constitutional provision of the right to due process, which is provided for under Article III, Section 14 of the Philippine Constitution. This provision guarantees that all individuals accused of a crime shall be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation against them, and shall be given the opportunity to enter a plea and to be heard by a competent and impartial court.

During the arraignment, the accused is formally charged with the crime and is informed of the specific allegations against them. The accused then has the opportunity to enter a plea of guilty, not guilty, or non-appearance (if the accused does not appear in court).

This constitutional provision ensures that the accused is given the opportunity to challenge the allegations against them and to defend themselves in court, thereby protecting their rights and ensuring a fair and just legal process.
