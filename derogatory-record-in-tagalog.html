1. this term is derogatory in nature.​


Slang, unconventional words or phrases that express either something new or something old in a new way. It is flippant, irreverent, indecorous; it may be indecent or obscene. Its colourful metaphors are generally directed at respectability, and it is this succinct, sometimes witty, frequently impertinent social criticism that gives slang its characteristic flavour. Slang, then, includes not just words but words used in a special way in a certain social context. The origin of the word slang itself is obscure; it first appeared in print around 1800, applied to the speech of disreputable and criminal classes in London. The term, however, was probably used much earlier.

6. 7. When the teachers practices professional responsibility, which of the following does she avoid?A. Maintain cordial relations with parents and peersB. Be proud of being a teacherC. Engage in derogatory talks about colleagueD. Support the objectives of the school.nico of residence in the same city. To whom would​


A teacher who does not practice professional responsibility is someone who C. Engage in derogatory talks about a colleague.

To engage in derogatory talks means to insult and belittle someone. Professional responsibility is when you do as your profession requires and this also includes cultivating an environment that would not make your colleagues' jobs more difficult as well. Derogatory talks or remarks are different from valid criticisms, these kinds of talks and remarks do not improve anything its active purpose is to hurt someone or someone’s reputation.

This actively makes the workplace more hostile and difficult to work in. Derogatory remarks are often rooted in discrimination or personal vendetta against someone. Examples of derogatory talks and remarks include:

Did you know that Mrs. Bautista’s husband cheated on her? I could understand, she’s always been a little too serious I can’t imagine surviving being married to her.He’s such a clumsy teacher! I don’t think he’s qualified to teach children.Ugh, Ms. Lopez’s fashion style is so terrible. I want to just grab those ugly glasses of hers that she wears every day out the window!

In conclusion, a teacher with professional responsibility is someone who maintains cordial relations with parents and peers, is proud of being a teacher, supports the objectives of the school, and DOES NOT engage in derogatory talks.

This kind of behavior is not only a professional responsibility of a teacher but all across professions and even in one’s interpersonal relationship with others. Remember, that this kind of behavior is not helpful to anyone and is not conducive to a productive workplace and life. If a professional engages in this behavior they do more harm than good no matter how good they are at their job. If your workplace has a Human Resources (HR) department to escalate to, do it if after personally warning them does not change their behavior.

2 examples of roleplay about marginalization

rp 1

[Scene: A job interview in a corporate office. The interviewer, John, is sitting behind a desk, and the interviewee, Sarah, is sitting in front of him, nervously clutching her resume.]

John: [smiling] So, Sarah, tell me a little bit about yourself.

Sarah: [smiling back] Well, I graduated from XYZ University with a degree in Marketing, and I've been working in the field for the past five years. I have a lot of experience in developing marketing strategies and executing successful campaigns.

John: [nodding] That's great. And what made you interested in this particular position?

Sarah: [excitedly] I've always been interested in the technology industry, and this company is at the forefront of innovation. I think I could make a real impact here, and I'm eager to learn and grow in my career.

John: [skeptically] I see. And how do you plan to handle the long hours and high-pressure environment of this job?

Sarah: [confidently] I've always been able to manage multiple projects and meet tight deadlines, and I'm not afraid of hard work. I'm very dedicated to my job, and I'm confident that I have the skills and drive to succeed in this role.

John: [eyebrows raised] Hmm. I have to admit, I'm a little concerned about your ability to fit in here. This is a very competitive and male-dominated industry, and I'm not sure a woman like yourself would be able to keep up.

Sarah: [surprised and angry] Excuse me? I'm qualified for this job, and I don't think my gender should be a factor in whether or not I'm hired.

John: [defensively] Look, I'm just being honest. I have a responsibility to make sure that our employees can handle the demands of this job, and I don't think you're cut out for it.

Sarah: [sternly] I'm tired of being told that I'm not good enough simply because of my gender. I've worked hard to get where I am, and I deserve to be evaluated based on my qualifications, not my gender.

John: [sighing] Fine. I'll take your application into consideration. But I have to say, I'm not very hopeful about your chances.

Sarah: [resolutely] That's okay. I'm confident in my abilities, and I'm not going to let someone like you stop me from achieving my goals.

[End Scene]

This roleplay highlights the experience of a woman facing sexism in the workplace, and the impact that gender discrimination can have on one's career opportunities. By playing out this scenario, participants can gain a deeper understanding of the impact of marginalization and discrimination, and learn to recognize and challenge these attitudes in their own lives.

Rp 2

You can play the role of the person who experiences marginalization, and I will play the role of someone who is perpetuating it.

You: Hi, my name is [Name of a person] and I just applied for a job at your company.

Me: Oh, hi [Name of a person]. Can you tell me a little bit about yourself?

You: Sure, I have a degree in computer science and 5 years of experience in the field. I'm really excited about the opportunity to work at your company.

Me: That's great, [Name of a person]. Can I ask, where are you from?

You: I'm from [Country that you want Ex:America].

Me: Oh, I see. Well, I have to be honest with you, [Name of a person] We already have a lot of diversity in our company and I'm not sure we need someone from your background.

You: What do you mean by that? I'm qualified for the job and I have the necessary experience.

Me: Well, I just don't think you would fit in with our culture here. We prefer to hire people who are more like us.

You: That's not fair. You're denying me an opportunity because of my race. That's marginalization.

Me: I'm sorry, [Name of a person], but that's just the way things are. There's nothing I can do about it.

You: This is unacceptable. I have the right to equal access to opportunities and resources, regardless of my race. Marginalization is a serious issue and it needs to be addressed.

Me: I understand what you're saying, but I still can't change my decision.

You: That's disappointing, but I'll continue to fight for my rights and for the rights of others who experience marginalization.

This is a very basic example of marginalization in the workplace, but it highlights the impact it can have on someone's life. The perpetuation of marginalization through actions like this must be actively addressed and stopped.

Answer:

1. Access to records means the general right of the public to examine and copy records.

It also means the right of the subject of a confidential record to authorize its release, in writing, to a named third party. Access to records means and INCLUDES the right to inspect or have copies of RECORDS.

2. Sexual relationship a relationship involving sexual intimacy.

A healthy sexual relationship consists of a relationship in which all parties are having their sexual needs met. In other words, they are satisfied with their sex life, having an adequate amount of sex, and not being abused in any way.

3. Physical contact is the act of touching physically.

Example:

Her fingers came in contact with the light switch.

It can also include physical intimacy, it includes all forms of contact such as holding hands, hugging, cuddling, kissing, and sex.
