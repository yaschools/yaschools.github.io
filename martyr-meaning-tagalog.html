Meaning

8. Why the moth for Rizal is a martyr?​


The moth story has an effect on Rizal's life since it causes him to love and cherish his mother even before he dies. He is close to Rizal's mother, Teodora Alonso Realonda.

Further explanation:

In the tale of the moth, a mother and her children are seen flying close to a candle lamp. The mother moth told her child to stay away from the lamp since it could burn his wings if he flew too close. The young moth disobeyed and his wings caught fire, causing him to be burnt.

His mother had an impact on Rizal. She was his role model, and he appreciated everything she had taught him throughout his life. He obeys her instruction by being submissive and remembering her life's lessons.

His sisters made fun of him when he was six years old for taking so long to make those clay and wax figurines instead of joining them in their games and playing with them. He said, "All right, laugh at me now! " as they departed as continued to laugh with childish glee. After I'm gone, people will sculpt monuments and other artwork in my honor!

Rizal predicted his death on December 30th fourteen years prior to the actual date. He was studying medicine in Madrid, Spain, at the time. He wrote the following in his diary:

1st of January, 1883

I experienced a terrifying nightmare where I nearly died two nights ago, on December 30. I had a vivid dream that I was losing strength and my breath while imitating an actor who was dying on stage. Then my vision went blurry, and a thick blackness engulfed me; those are the death throes.

The Spanish military doctor, Dr. Felipe Ruiz Castillo, kindly asked permission to take his pulse before doing so. Dr. Castillo was shocked to learn that it was typical, showing that Rizal wasn't scared to die.

In his final poem's third stanza, Rizal penned the following:

“I die just when I see the dawn break,

Through the gloom of night, to herald the day;

And if color is lacking my blood thou shall take,

Pour’d out need for thy dear sake,

To dye with its crimson the waking ray.”
