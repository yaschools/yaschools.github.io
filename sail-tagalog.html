9. PLEASE HELP The sail of a sailboat resembles a right triangle. If the canvas that makes up the sail has an area of 24 square feet, what is the height h of the sail?


Answer:

Let's assume that the base of the right triangle is the length of the boat and the height of the right triangle is h, which is the height of the sail. Then, the area of the sail is given by:

Area of sail = (1/2) * base * height

We know that the area of the sail is 24 square feet, so we can plug in the values we have:

24 = (1/2) * base * h

Simplifying this equation, we get:

48 = base * h

We don't know the value of the base, but we do know that the sail resembles a right triangle, which means that the base and height are related by the Pythagorean theorem:

base^2 + h^2 = c^2

where c is the length of the hypotenuse (which represents the diagonal edge of the sail). However, we don't need to know the value of c to solve this problem. Instead, we can use the fact that the sail is a right triangle to relate the base and height:

base = c * cos(theta)

h = c * sin(theta)

where theta is the angle between the base and the hypotenuse. Since we don't know the value of theta, we'll need to use some trigonometry to solve for h. But first, we can rearrange the equation we got earlier:

48 = base * h

to get:

h = 48 / base

Now, we need to find the value of base. We know that the area of the sail is 24 square feet, and we can use this to solve for base:

24 = (1/2) * base * h

24 = (1/2) * base * (48 / base)

24 = 24

This equation is true for any value of base, which means that we can choose any value for base as long as it satisfies the conditions of the problem. For simplicity, let's choose a value of base that makes the calculations easy. We could choose a value of 1, but that would make the sail impossibly small. Instead, let's choose a value of 8, which gives us:

48 = base * h

48 = 8 * h

h = 6

So the height of the sail is 6 feet.

17. Essay writing, Explain the significance of sailing methods in navigation, Describe the appropriate use of “parallel sailing” method in navigation, apply “parallel sailing” method in order to determine required course, distance and or position​


Sailing methods are critical in navigation because they determine the path that a vessel will take to reach its destination. Different sailing methods are used depending on the situation, including parallel sailing, rhumb line sailing, great circle sailing, and others.

Parallel sailing is a method where the vessel maintains a constant latitude, and sails along a line of constant latitude, rather than a straight line between the start and end points. This method is used when a vessel must avoid areas of shallow water, adverse currents, or other navigational hazards.

To apply the parallel sailing method, the navigator must first determine the starting point and destination, as well as the line of constant latitude that lies between them. They then use a chart or navigation software to plot the course along this line of constant latitude, making adjustments as needed to avoid obstacles.

The navigator must also determine the required course, distance, and position using navigational tools such as a compass, nautical charts, and a marine sextant. By using these tools, the navigator can determine the exact course to steer, as well as the distance traveled and the vessel's current position.

In conclusion, parallel sailing is an important method of navigation that is used when a vessel must navigate around obstacles or navigate in areas with adverse conditions. Navigators use a combination of charts, navigational tools, and their own experience and expertise to apply this method and reach their destination safely.

Sailing methods are crucial in navigation, as they determine the course and direction that a ship takes to reach its destination. These methods have been developed over centuries of seafaring, and have greatly improved the accuracy and efficiency of navigation. One such method is called parallel sailing.

The parallel sailing method is used to determine the required course, distance, and position of a ship by using two or more navigational points. This method involves sailing parallel to the coast, using the shoreline as a reference point, while continuously taking bearings of landmarks or other navigational aids. By comparing these bearings, a navigator can determine their position and adjust their course accordingly.

This method is particularly useful when navigating in areas with limited visibility, such as during foggy conditions, or when the navigational charts are unreliable or outdated. The parallel sailing method provides a way to determine a ship's position even if the exact location is not known, by using landmarks or other navigational aids along the shoreline.

To apply the parallel sailing method, a navigator must first identify two or more navigational points along the shoreline, such as a lighthouse, a bridge, or a prominent hill. The navigator must then take a bearing of these points, either by using a compass or by plotting their position on a chart.

Once the bearings have been taken, the navigator can compare them to determine their position and make any necessary adjustments to their course. If the bearings indicate that the ship is moving away from the shore, the navigator must adjust their course to steer back towards the shoreline. If the bearings indicate that the ship is moving closer to the shore, the navigator must adjust their course to avoid running aground.

In conclusion, parallel sailing is a significant method in navigation as it provides a way to determine a ship's position, course, and distance even in areas with limited visibility. Its effectiveness and versatility make it an essential tool for all navigators, and it has played a vital role in the history of seafaring and exploration.
