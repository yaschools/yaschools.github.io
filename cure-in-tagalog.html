9. А. Learning Task 4: Describe and explain each method of curing meat. Do this on a separate sheet of paper. 1. Dry cure 2. Immersion/pickle curing 3. Injection Curing​


Answer:

1. Dry Cure

Is a method wherein a piece of meat is submerged in a container of salt (or with herbs and spices) for an extended period of time. This salting process where moisture is drawn out of the meat, preserves the ingredient while fostering an extremely savory flavor.

2. Immersion / Pickle Curing

Is a method of preserving food by brining (soaking food in a saltwater solution), or salting (packing food in salt). The food should be brined, or marinated, or both for it to be considered as pickle. Pickles that are brined are fermented meaning the salt encourages certain good microbes to flourish, while preventing the growth of other microbes. In contrast, pickles that are dunked in acid are unfermented meaning the vinegar stops the growth of the spoilage-causing microbes and helps flavor whatever is being pickled, without stimulating the microbe growth that causes food to ferment.

Most importantly, pickle depends on the salt concentration, fermentation length, and temperature.

3. Injection Curing

Is a method where a prepared solution (i.e. brine) containing the ingredients is injected by needle into the meat, either manually or by machine, to achieve a rapid deposition of curing salts and salt throughout the mass.
