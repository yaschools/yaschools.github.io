17. How are the meanings of manipulation and change related?


Answer:

Manipulation and change can be related in the sense that manipulation can be a way of attempting to bring about a desired change. However, manipulation usually involves using deceptive or underhanded tactics to influence a situation or person to achieve a specific outcome.

Change, on the other hand, can refer to any alteration or modification in a situation, behavior, or condition. Change can be intentional or unintentional, and it can occur naturally or as a result of deliberate actions.

While manipulation and change can both result in a shift or modification in a situation, the key difference lies in the intent and methods used to bring about that shift. Manipulation usually involves an element of control and deception, while change can occur through a variety of means, including honest communication, negotiation, or simply the passage of time.

25. what does it mean to manipulate information?​


Answer:

Information manipulation theory (IMT) is a way of looking at interpersonal communication. It deals with the way a sender might assemble information packages (in the form of messages) to a receiver in order to give an impression that is false from the perspective of the sender. The sender chooses certain facts in the message from an available amount of information—but omits, alters, or falsifies others. The act of trying to get someone to believe something untrue is deceit. The type of communication created by such deceitful intent is called a deceptive message. The senders management of given information to provide a receiver with a false perception of that same information is called information manipulation. Departing from the focus on the communication mode found in Interpersonal Deception Theory (IDT), IMT is more concerned with the content of the deceptive messages, the situational contexts that bring them about, the degree to which the detection of such a message affects perception of deception and the relational consequences associated with deceptive messages.
