16. What are the Prevention and Control of measles disease​​


Answer:

1. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent measles. The measles vaccine is part of the MMRV (measles, mumps, rubella, and varicella) vaccine, which is given to children when they are 12 to 15 months old, and again when they are four to six years old.

2. Avoid contact with infected individuals and wash your hands frequently with soap and water.

3. Practice proper cough and sneeze etiquette and avoid sharing cups, eating utensils, and other items with people who are infected with measles.

4. With prompt and proper medical treatment, prognosis of most patients is good. Administering vitamin A capsules can reduce the severity of the disease.

5. Keep your immunity levels up, eat healthy, exercise regularly, and get enough rest.

6. Stay up-to-date with your measles vaccinations, following the CDC's (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention) advisory.

29. What is measles malaria tuberculosis tetanus


Measles, malaria, tuberculosis, and tetanus are all infectious diseases caused by different pathogens:

1. Measles is a highly contagious viral disease that is spread through respiratory droplets. Symptoms include fever, cough, and a characteristic rash.

2. Malaria is a parasitic disease transmitted by the bite of infected mosquitoes. Symptoms include fever, chills, and flu-like symptoms.

3. Tuberculosis (TB) is a bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs, but can also affect other parts of the body. Symptoms include cough, fever, and weight loss.

4. Tetanus is a bacterial disease caused by the toxin of the bacterium Clostridium tetani. It enters the body through a wound or cut and can cause muscle stiffness, spasms, and lockjaw.

All of these diseases can be prevented through vaccination, proper hygiene, and avoiding exposure to infected individuals or areas. It is important to seek medical attention if you suspect you may have contracted any of these diseases.
