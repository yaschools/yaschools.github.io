1. What is orthography???


Answer:

An orthography is a collection of conventions to make a language writable. It requires spelling, hyphenation, capitalisation, word breaks, emphasis and punctuation standards. It has developed a standard orthography for most transnational languages. Orthography is mostly based on a common language range, and thus shows less dialect variation than the language spoken. Often variation can occur in the orthography of a language, such as between American and British spelling.

3. How is perspective drawing related to orthographie projection?​


Answer:

In the perspective view (the default), objects which are far away are smaller than those nearby. In the orthographic view, all objects appear at the same scale. Since some prefer one over the other, both options are available

Answer:

Orthographic projection is a means of representing three-dimensional objects in two dimensions. It is a form of parallel projection, in which all the projection lines are orthogonal to the projection plane, resulting in every plane of the scene appearing in affine transformation on the viewing surface

6. explain the difference between orthography axonometric and oblique drawing​


Answer:

Another way to look at it is that in an orthographic projection, the projector lines intersect the plane being projected on to at a perpendicular angle (thus, they are orthogonal, thus the name of the projection), whereas in an oblique projection those lines form oblique angles (non-right angles) with the projection plane.
