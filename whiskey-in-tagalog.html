5. What is the different and similarities of lambanog and whiskey​


Answer:

Lambanóg is a traditional Filipino distilled palm liquor made from coconut or nipa palm sap while Whisky or whiskey is a type of distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash or by distilling beer.

Explanation:

Lambanóg is a traditional Filipino distilled palm liquor made from coconut or nipa palm sap. It is derived from tubâ that has been aged for at least 48 hours. It originates from Luzon island in the northern Philippines.

Whisky or whiskey is a type of distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash or by distilling beer. Various grains are used for different varieties, including barley, corn, rye, and wheat. Whisky is typically aged in wooden casks, generally made of charred white oak.

13. Propaganda used:Explanation:"ONLY REAL MEN DRINK BANAYAD WHISKEY, ARE YOU A REAL MAN? ​.


Explanation:

This statement is an example of propaganda, which is a type of communication that is designed to influence or persuade people by appealing to their emotions, values, beliefs, or biases. The goal of propaganda is often to promote a particular ideology, product, or point of view. In this case, the propaganda is trying to appeal to men's sense of masculinity and suggest that only "real men" drink Banayad whiskey. This type of propaganda often relies on stereotypes and reinforces certain social norms or expectations. It is important to recognize propaganda for what it is and to be critical of the messages being presented, rather than blindly accepting them.
