9. A deposit was made into a 7% annual simple interest account. Another deposit, ₱15,000 less than the first, was placed in a certificate of deposit earning 9% annual simple interest. The total interest earned on both investments for one year was ₱5,050. How much money was deposited in the certificate of deposit?


CALCULATING THE AMOUNT OF MONEY DEPOSITED USING SIMPLE INTEREST

There were two kinds of deposit made in the problem. The first deposit is  with a 7% annual simple interest rate while the other one have  a 9% annual simple interest rate. The first deposit  was P15,000 less than the first, and the total interest earned was P5,050. The certificate of deposit was for P25,000.00.

Step by step solution

Given:

The amount of the first deposit serves as the X

The second deposit is then "15,000 less than the first," hence it is "x - 15,000.

To compute the interest earned in the first deposit we will use the formula :

I = Prt,

I-    is the interest earned

P - is the principal (the amount invested),

r - is the annual interest rate

t-  is the time period in years.

So the interest earned on the initial deposit is:

I1 = x0.071 = 0.07x

Similarly, the interest earned on the second deposit is:

I2 = (x-₱15,000)0.091 = 0.09(x-₱15,000)

We are told that the total interest earned on both assets over the course of a year was $5,050:

I1 + I2 = ₱5,050

When the equations for I1 and I2 are substituted, we get:

0.07x + 0.09(x-₱15,000) = ₱5,050

When we simplify and solve for x, we get:

0.07x + 0.09x - ₱1,350 = ₱5,050

0.16x = ₱6,400

x = ₱40,000

So the initial deposit was 40,000, and the subsequent deposit was 15,000 less, or 25,000.

26. 1. It refers to deposit that has maturity period and it is evidenced by a certificateof deposits.​


Answer:

A time deposit is an interest-bearing bank account that has a pre-set date of maturity. A certificate of deposit (CD) is the best-known example. The money must remain in the account for the fixed term in order to earn the stated interest rate.

Time deposits generally pay a slightly higher rate of interest than a regular savings account. The longer the time to maturity, the higher the interest payment will be.

Another name for this type of investment is term deposit.
