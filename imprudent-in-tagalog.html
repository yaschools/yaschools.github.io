CONTEXT CLUES

Contextual cues can be found in a statement itself, the text immediately before or after it, or both. We must be able to recognize and use context clues because reading is how we learn the majority of our vocabulary.

Answer:

Despite ELLUCIDATING his points with clarity and providing compelling evidence for the need to hire new support staff, his efforts to persuade his FRUGAL boss were in vain.

Here is the supporting details:

Ellucidating means to clarify a text, especially by explanation or analysis unchanging verb and to provide an elaborative justification.

Explain, explicate, expound, and interpret are some typical synonyms for "elucidate." While all of these words refer to "making something clear or understandable," the word "elucidate" emphasizes the process of shedding light on something by providing information that was previously hidden or just implicit.

Frugal means to be extremely careful while using money or resources, and avoid using them until absolutely necessary. He shops frugally.

Economical, parsimonious, and thrifty are some typical synonyms for frugal. All of these terms refer to being "careful in how one spends one's money or resources," but frugal suggests a lack of luxury and an easygoing way of living.

Additional information:

His attempts to convince his boss were unsuccessful despite the fact that he clearly presented his case and offered convincing justification for the requirement to hire new support employees.

Explaining is the appropriate word to use in the space after the word despite This is due to the absence of a verb in the phrase's first section (before the comma), even if the subject is already present (a man, as indicated by "his point" and "his effort").

The second statement, however, is already grammatically correct because "His endeavor to persuade his employer were in vain" stands alone without the need for a verb or other word type. Additionally, the entire sentence is already coherent by simply including the verb in the first phrase.

17. Reckless imprudence resulting to homicide elements


Reckless impruduce must be proved to state that reckless impruduce resulting to homiced elements.

Explanation:

Reckless imprudence consists of voluntarily, but without malicious intent, performing or failing to perform an act that results in material loss due to an unforgivable lack of precautions on the part of the person committing or failing to perform the act, taking into account the occupation or occupation, degree of intelligence, physical condition and other circumstances regarding people, times and places.

The prosecution must demonstrate a direct causal relationship between the driver's negligence and the injury suffered to support the allegation for reckless imprudence resulting to homicide.

According to a G.R. No. 210816. December 10, 2018 there are several elements of reckless imprudence are :

That the offender does or fails to do an actThat the doing or the failure to do that act is voluntaryThat it be without maliceThat material damage results from the reckless imprudenceThat there is inexcusable lack of precaution on the part of the offender, taking into consideration his employment or occupation, degree of intelligence, physical condition, and other circumstances regarding persons, time, and place.

Instructor: Christopher Muscato

Noh theater is one of the oldest still-practiced forms of performance in the world. In this lesson, we'll explore its history and characteristics and see what makes it so special.

Explanation:

If someone asked if you knew what Japan's oldest form of theater was, you could say no and still be correct. Technically it's spelled Noh, but close enough.

Noh is one of Japan's most cherished traditional art forms, a musical and poetic type of stage drama that has been practiced continually since the 14th century and is still prominent in Japan today. It's elegant, intense, subtle, and ephemeral all at once, and has some remarkable costumes as well. How could you say no to that?

History of Noh Theater

Let's start by getting to know the history of this revered art form. Noh is a form of high theater developed from the folk traditions of Japan that emerged in the earliest years of the Muromachi Period (1333-1573). The person most directly responsible for its development was a man named Kan'ami. He was the first to turn these folk traditions into a serious, rich practice that the upper classes could begin to appreciate as fine art. Noh drama is serious and intense, so a brief and comical form of theater is used in between acts as an intermission. This less-serious attraction is called kyogen. Together, the entire spectacle of Noh and kyogen is called nogaku.
