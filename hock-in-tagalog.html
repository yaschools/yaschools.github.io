12. What can we learned about the story "THE DUBBING OF GENERAL GARBAGE" by Hebie Hock?pasagot po plsssss​


Answer:

The story "The Dubbing of General Garbage" by Hebie Hock is a satirical commentary on the absurdity of war and the glorification of violence. The story is set in a fictional country where the military is revered and celebrated, and the citizens are taught to idolize war heroes and generals. The main character, General Garbage, is a bumbling and incompetent leader who is hailed as a hero despite his many failures and mistakes.

Through the story, Hock highlights the dangers of blindly following authority and the consequences of valuing violence and aggression over reason and diplomacy. The story also emphasizes the importance of critical thinking and questioning authority, as the protagonist ultimately realizes the senselessness of war and the need for peace.

Overall, "The Dubbing of General Garbage" is a cautionary tale that warns against the dangers of blind patriotism and the glorification of violence. It encourages readers to think critically about the messages they receive from authority figures and to strive for peace and diplomacy instead of war and aggression.
