18. which makeup is applied to villain and heroesa.kumadori makeup b.normal makeup c. Ordinary makeup d. Standard makeup ​


A.kumadoru makeup
Kumadori is the stage makeup worn by kabuki actors, mostly when performing kabuki plays in the aragoto style. The term also applies to a painting method in which two brushes are used simultaneously, one for the color and the other used to create shading or other details.

a.kumadori makeup

The most famous role to use red kumadori is that of the hero in Shibaraku, Kamakura Gongoro, and has come to stereotypically represent kabuki in the West. Blue makeup is used to represent a villain, human or not, and represents negative emotions such as fear and jealousy.


19. 6. It is a type of Kabuki makeup that is applied to villains and heroes? A. standard makeup C. Kumadori makeup B. kesho D. dark-faced mask non in the field of arts and culture?​


KUMADORI MAKEUP IN KABUKI PERFORMANCES

Answer:

The correct answer is C. Kumadori makeup

Explanation:

There is a unique makeup technique known as kumadori that is used for supernatural heroes and villains, which occur frequently in Kabuki performances. Kumadori is composed of dramatic lines and forms in a variety of hues, each signifying a particular quality. Dark red, which denotes fury, passion, or brutality, and dark blue, which depicts melancholy or despair, are the most widely utilized hues in kumadori makeup. Other typical hues include pink, which represents youth or joy; light blue or green, which represents tranquility; purple, which represents nobility; brown, which represents selfishness; and black, which represents dread. Kumadori makeup comes in over a hundred various mask-like forms.

Kabuki performers' makeup is regarded such a crucial component of their performance that it is typical for them to press a silk cloth to their faces to make them look younger. These fabric face-prints become valuable Kabuki memorabilia in Kumadori makeup.
