What is a boil?A boil, sometimes also referred to as a furuncle, is an infection involving a hair follicle on the skin. Boils are typically caused by Staphylococcus aureus bacteria, although they may be caused by other bacteria or fungi present on the skin surface. In addition to affecting the hair follicle, infections that cause boils will also typically involve the surrounding skin.

What is a boil?A boil, sometimes also referred to as a furuncle, is an infection involving a hair follicle on the skin. This intrusion of bacteria triggers an immune response that includes drawing white blood cells into the infected follicle to fight the infection.
