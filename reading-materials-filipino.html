The culture of the Philippines is a combination of cultures of the East and West.[1] Filipino identity was created primarily as a result of pre-colonial cultures, colonial influences and foreign traders intermixing and gradually evolving together. In pre-colonial times, the Philippines was a divided set of nations, islands and tribes being ruled by their own kings, chieftains, lakans, rajahs, datus and sultans. Every nation has its own identity and some are even part of a larger empire outside of what is now the Philippines. Manila, for example, was once part of the Islamic Sultanate of Brunei, and the Sulu Archipelago was also part of the Hindu Majapahit. 6. Read We Filipinos are Mild DrinkersQuestions1. What is the setting of the story? Describe the place of the physical environment of the setting. What role does the setting play in the story? Explain why.2. What examples of the barrio's material and non material culture does the story mention? What is the influence of culture on the story? Explain why.3. What historical event plays a role in the story? What is the significance of this event in the story? Explain why.​


Answer:

(Answer by Knowledge original and Valid Opinions to help and guide the questioners)

Answer:

1. The setting of the story is a small barrio in rural Philippines during the early 1900s. The physical environment is described as being comprised of mango trees, bamboo orchards and thatched-roof huts. The setting plays a crucial role in the story in that it is representative of the cultural and historical events that the narrator is relating.

2. The story mentions various examples of the material culture of the barrio, such as mango trees, bamboo orchards, and thatched-roof huts, as well as cultural elements, such as the taboo against drinking alcohol in the presence of elders and the collective responsibilities of the barrio residents. The influence of culture on the story is demonstrative of the shared values, beliefs, and norms between the people of the barrio, which shape the narrator’s experiences, and ultimately, the story’s message.

3. The historical event that plays a role in the story is the strict prohibition in the Philippines of the “import and sale of any foreign drink” (later clarified as alcoholic beverages). The significance of this event in the story is that it reinforces the idea that the barrio’s population is comprised of mild drinkers, further highlighting the cultural taboo against alcohol consumption in the presence of elders and the respect for its collective responsibility of the people.
