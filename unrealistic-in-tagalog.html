23. Write a short story with unrealistic characters


Answer:

The Peculiar Banana

A Short Story

Wenna Jones looked at the peculiar banana in her hands and felt calm.

She walked over to the window and reflected on her urban surroundings. She had always loved dull Manchester with its mouldy, misty mountains. It was a place that encouraged her tendency to feel calm.

Then she saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Mavis Noris. Mavis was a sweet ogre with brunette legs and ugly warts.

Wenna gulped. She glanced at her own reflection. She was a callous, special, port drinker with feathery legs and scrawny warts. Her friends saw her as a precious, powerful painter. Once, she had even helped a colossal chicken recover from a flying accident.

But not even a callous person who had once helped a colossal chicken recover from a flying accident, was prepared for what Mavis had in store today.

The sleet rained like jumping dogs, making Wenna worried.

As Wenna stepped outside and Mavis came closer, she could see the grubby smile on her face.

"Look Wenna," growled Mavis, with a brave glare that reminded Wenna of sweet pigeons. "It's not that I don't love you, but I want a pencil. You owe me 7912 pounds."

Wenna looked back, even more worried and still fingering the peculiar banana. "Mavis, I am your mother," she replied.

They looked at each other with calm feelings, like two powerless, purple puppies swimming at a very giving snow storm, which had jazz music playing in the background and two tactless uncles sleeping to the beat.

Wenna studied Mavis's brunette legs and ugly warts. Eventually, she took a deep breath. "I'm afraid I declared myself bankrupt," explained Wenna. "You will never get your money."

"No!" objected Mavis. "You lie!"

"I do not!" retorted Wenna. "Now get your brunette legs out of here before I hit you with this peculiar banana."

Mavis looked ecstatic, her wallet raw like a pickled, panicky piano.

Wenna could actually hear Mavis's wallet shatter into 7912 pieces. Then the sweet ogre hurried away into the distance.

Not even a glass of port would calm Wenna's nerves tonight.

THE END

30. Is communism an ideal society or is it unrealistic for humans to maintain? Explain your answer. ​


The goal of communism is to achieve equality and fulfill the needs of the many.

It is unrealistic because it expects people to be the same. They can't just be satisfied with having their needs fulfilled. Not only that, but it also expects people to be subservient to the state which dictates where they should work.

One of the goals of communism is to have a 'moneyless' economy. The workers would only be paid with things that fulfill their needs.

Will they just really be content with that? Or would they still wish they could earn money to purchase what they want (such as clothing, house, gadgets, etc.) even if it is not necessary?

In communism, you can't decide what you want to have since the state decides it for you.
