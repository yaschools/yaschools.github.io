The crust usually folds out onto the rim of the pie plate but doesn't hang over it. Decorate the edge of this crust by "fluting" it, pinching the edge with your fingers to form scallop shapes or waves; press it with fork tines or another decorative pattern; or leave it plain. To make a single crust, roll out the dough into a circle and place it in the pan, trimming the edge to match the plate's rim, perhaps a little larger; however, some pie crusts are actually phyllo dough, folded and placed in the pan. Some recipes call for pre-baked pie shells, when the crust is baked before adding the filling. This is called "blind baking."
