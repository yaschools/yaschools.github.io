15. Would Rizal's works deem irrelevant and futile because of his retraction? why or why not?


Answer:

Jose Protacio Rizal did not write a retraction letter. Instead, on the eve of his execution, he wrote a beautiful and poignant poem called “Mi Ultimo Adios” - My Last Farewell - to his fatherland. There was nothing there that hinted of a retraction. If he indeed wrote a retraction, that would somehow tint negatively his works and writings, but the truth in them would still stand.

Rizal embodied the ideals of the Filipino, and he died a martyr. For this, he was chosen as the country’s national hero, and you will find his bust in almost every municipality and city in the Philippines. Also there is a Rizal Street or its equivalent in every town.

But he, perhaps did a disinformation in the Filipino mindset, because of his portrayal of the Spanish Friar in the character of Padre Damaso in his twin books Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo - that of an abusive, lecherous, racist, etc., Friar. Since every Filipino has to read his two novels as a curricular requirement in school, most Filipinos think of the Spanish Friars in the same way. Which is far from the objective truth.

The Spanish Friars built schools, churches, hospitals, orphanages and other works of charity and mercy throughout the country. If you go around the Philippines, you will find these in almost every town and city. native Filipinos were called, instead of killing them off, which colonial Americans did to the native population in America. Rizal and the nationalists of his time were all products of this education.
