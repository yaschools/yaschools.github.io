21. What is the meaning of Relief, Intaglio, Lithography?


The Meaning of Relief, Intaglio, and Lithography

The meaning of relief, intaglio, and lithography are these: relief is a sculpture which is "raised" or carved from a wall or a flat-base; intaglio is a type of printing in which a figure or design is engraved on a stone and then hard-pressed on a flat surface to yield an image; lithography is another type of printing in which an image is printed from a flat, smooth surface to another by treating it with ink.

Additional Description and Meaning of Relief, Intaglio, and LithographyReliefRelief sculptures look like three-dimensional figures on a flat surface.It is also known as relievo (Italian: relievare, meaning "to raise).The 3 basic types of relief include low relief, high relief, and sunken relief.Another type of relief is called Stacciato relief is a subtype of low relief.Famous relief sculptures include Arch of Constantine, Angkor Wat Khmer Temple, and Venus of Laussel.IntaglioThis type of printing utilizes a plate bearing the image to be printed and pressed deeply onto a surface.Common intaglio techniques include engraving, etching, and aquatint.Some of the famous intaglio artists are William Blake, Stanley William Hayter, and Helen Frank.LithographyThis is a method of printing that uses a piece of flat surface material that is chemically treated so that ink may stick to some parts while the others remain blank.The image that ink adheres to is called ink-receptive while the non-image area is called ink-repellent.Lithography employs oil, water and, originally, limestone (lithos).This type of printing is commonly used in magazines, high-volume books, etc.Lithography was discovered by Alois Senefelder of Munich.

22. what is relief printing


Answer:

Relief Printing

A family printing methods in which a block, plate or matrix that had ink applied to its surface,but not to any recessed area, was brought into contact with paper.The areas of the ink printing plate will leave ink on the paper , while the recessed areas of the printing plate will leave the paper free from ink.Relief printing together with the intaglio and planographic families is one of the traditional families of printmaking techniques.A sculptural technique where the sculpted elements remain attached to a solid background of the same material.The term relief is from the Latin word relevo, to raise

25. what is high relief​


Answer:

properties of relief sculpture

In a high relief, or alto-relievo, the forms project at least half or more of their natural circumference from the background and may in parts be completely disengaged from the ground, thus approximating sculpture in the round.

Answer:

In a high relief, or alto-relievo, the forms project at least half or more of their natural circumference from the background and may in parts be completely disengaged from the ground, thus approximating sculpture in the round. Middle relief, or mezzo-relievo, falls roughly between the high and low forms.
