Using sight, the descriptive words are rocky, hairy, and drenched. Rough, sharp, and slippery are descriptive words using touch. Using sounds, the descriptive words are quiet, jangling, and barking. Aromatic, fresh, and scented are descriptive words using smell, while bitter, sour, and acidic when using taste.

Other Kinds of Words

There are many kinds of words. There are nouns, verbs, adverbs, adjectives, and many more. Some of them are descriptive words. Descriptive words are words that help describe, explain, or imagine people, things, places, events, and others.  

A specific kind of descriptive words are sensory words. Sensory words are descriptive words that specifically describe how we sense or experience certain things. Several other sensory words include:

monstrous,  fluffy,  thundering,  stinky and  salty.  

These words describe something you see, feel, hear, smell, and taste, respectively.
