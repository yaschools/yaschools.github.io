1. what is the bulacan baguio sundot kulangot relationship to philippine culture?​


Sundot kulangot (literally “picked snot”) is a type of kalamay (a sticky sweet delicacy made of coconut milk, brown sugar, and ground glutinous rice) from Baguio City, Philippines. It is sweetened with molasses which adds to its color. It is uniquely packed into halved pitogo (Sago Palm of the genus Cycas) shells and sealed with red crepe paper in a similar manner to the Bohol Kalamay. It is the smallest known traditional packaging of Kalamay. They are sold in bundles, several of these kalamay balls are nestled inside split bamboo and tied with a string.
