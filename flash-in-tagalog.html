18. Three lighthouses flashed simultaneously at 11:00 AM. The first lighthouse flashes every 18 seconds, the second lighthouse flashes every 60 seconds, and the third lighthouse flashes every 66 seconds. Find the next time the three lighthouses flash together again.​


Answer:

Concept

We are looking for the lowest common multiple of 18, 60, and 66.

We can do this by first looking for the lowest common multiple of two of them, and then the result with the third number.

Full explanation

The multiples of 60 are: 60, 120, 180,...

Now 60 is not a multiple of 18, nor is 120, but 180 is.

So the LCM of 18 and 60 is 180.

Now we look for the lowest common multiple of 180 and 66.

180 = 2^2 x 3^2 x 5

66 = 2 x 3 x 11

We take the largest available powers:

2^2 x 3^2 x 5 x 11 = 1980

Thus, the LCM of the three numbers is 1980 second = 33 minutes.

So 33 minutes after 11:00am the lighthouses flash in sync, they will do so again.

Hence, the answer is 11:33am.

27. 2. The red bulbs of a Christmas light flash every 8 seconds, the blue bulbsflash every 9 seconds and the green bulbs flash every 10 seconds. If they allstart flashing together, in every how many minutes will they flashsimultaneously?​


Answer:

360 minutes

Step-by-step explanation:

Simply find the LCM of the three values,

Use prime factorization to easily find the LCM,

8 = 4 x 2 = 2 x 2 x 2

9 = 3 x 3

10 = 5 x 2

As you can see, we simplify each number to its prime factors.

Now multiply each factor by by itself by how much it occurs the most in each number,

2 occurs 3 times in 8,

3 occurs 2 times in 9,

5 occurs 1 time in 10,

Therefore,

LCM = 2 • 2 • 2 • 3 • 3 • 5

= 8 • 9 • 5

= 72 • 5

= 360

Therefore the lights flash together every 360 minutes.
