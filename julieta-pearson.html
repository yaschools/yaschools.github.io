18. What is the value of Pearson's r?​


Pearson's r can range from -1 to 1. An r -1 indicates a perfect negative linear relationship between variables, an r of 0 indicates no linear relationship between variables, and an r of 1 indicates a perfect positive linear relationship between variables.

Answer:

In statistics, the Pearson correlation coefficient, also referred to as Pearson's r, the Pearson product-moment correlation coefficient, or the bivariate correlation, is a measure of linear correlation between two sets of data.

30. when to use pearson's moment correlation coefficient​


Answer:

Basically, a Pearson product-moment correlation attempts to draw a line of best fit through the data of two variables, and the Pearson correlation coefficient, r, indicates how far away all these data points are to this line of best fit (i.e., how well the data points fit this new model/line of best fit).
