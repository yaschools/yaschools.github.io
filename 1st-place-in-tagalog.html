Explanation:

Assuming P represents the number of outcomes, n the number of competitors and r the number of places to be awarded.

n!

P= --------------

(n -- r) !

10!

P= --------------

(10 -- 3) !

10!

P= --------

7 !

Calculating using a calculator:

P = 720

There are 720 different outcomes possible in the race.

15. How many ways can we award a 1st, 2nd and 3rd place prize among 6 contestants?


Hello!
.
n = 6
r = 3
.
P(n,r) = n! / (n-r)!
P(6,3) = 6! / (6-3)!
P(6,3) = 6x5x4x3! / 3!
P(6,3) = 120
.
Therefore, there are 120 ways that we can award a 1st, 2nd and 3rd place prize among 6 contestants.
