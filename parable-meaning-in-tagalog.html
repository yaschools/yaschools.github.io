Explanation:

The seed is the word of God,” Jesus says. (Luke 8:11) And the soil is the heart. That is a key to getting the sense of his illustration.

Of the seed sown on the trampled-down soil alongside the road, he explains: “The Devil comes and takes the word away from their hearts so that they may not believe and be saved.” (Luke 8:12) In speaking of the seed sown on rocky ground, Jesus means the hearts of people who receive the word with joy but the word does not take root deep in their hearts. “After tribulation or persecution has arisen on account of the word,” they are stumbled. Yes, when “a season of testing” comes, perhaps opposition from family members or others, they fall away.—Matthew 13:21; Luke 8:13.

What of the seed that falls among the thorns? Jesus tells his disciples that this refers to people who have heard the word. These, however, are overcome by “the anxiety of this system of things and the deceptive power of riches.” (Matthew 13:22) They had the word in their heart, but now it is choked and becomes unfruitful.

The last type of soil that Jesus comments on is the fine soil. This refers to those who hear the word and accept it into their heart, getting the real sense of it. With what result? They “bear fruit.” Because of their circumstances, such as age or health, not all can do the same; one produces 100 times more, another 60 times more, and another 30 times more. Yes, blessings in serving God come to “ones who, after hearing the word with a fine and good heart, retain it and bear fruit with endurance.”—Luke 8:15.


19. Find two parables of Jesus heavenly meaning, and explain their two paragraphs each,plz po with two paragraph meaning​


This parable is found in the Bible in Matthew 13:3-9 and Luke 8:5-8. In this parable, Jesus compares the Kingdom of God to a farmer who sows seed on different types of soil. Some of the seed falls on a path, some on rocky ground, some among thorns, and some on good soil. The seed that falls on the path, rocky ground, and among thorns are quickly consumed and do not produce any fruit. But the seed that falls on good soil produces a bountiful harvest.

The heavenly meaning of this parable is that the message of the Kingdom of God is like a seed that is sown in the hearts of people. Some people will hear the message and not understand it, and it will be taken away by Satan. Others will receive the message with joy, but later fall away because of persecution or temptation. Still, others will hear the message, but allow the worries of this life and the deceitfulness of wealth to choke the word, and it will become unfruitful. But those who hear the message and understand it, and then bear fruit, are like the seed that fell on good soil. They will produce a bountiful harvest of righteousness.

The Parable of the Talents:

This parable is found in the Bible in Matthew 25:14-30 and Luke 19:12-27. In this parable, a man entrusts three of his servants with talents (a type of currency) before going on a journey. Two of the servants invest the talents and double the amount while the third servant, out of fear, buries the talent and returns it to the master unchanged. The master rewards the first two servants but punishes the third.

The heavenly meaning of this parable is that God has given each of us different abilities, gifts, and resources, and we are expected to use them to serve him and others. Those who use what they have been given will be rewarded, but those who do not use what they have been given will be punished. It also teaches us about accountability and the importance of being faithful with the resources God gives us. It shows us that we are accountable for our actions, and that we will be judged based on how we use what we have been given.
