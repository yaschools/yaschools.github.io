12. what are the different punctuation marks may use in writing and and give the name and the symbol different punctuation mark​


Answer:

Full Stop or Period (.)

The period or full stop (.) is used for ending a declarative statement, believed to be complete. It is also used following abbreviations.

Question Mark (?)

We use a question mark whenever we ask questions.

Exclamation Mark (!)

We use an exclamation point or exclamation mark (!) when we wish to express sudden joy, disgust or despair, or for generally emphasizing something.

The Comma (,)

The comma helps us to separate ideas, phrases, or elements within a sentence.

Semicolon (;)

We use the semicolon for connecting independent clauses. It helps to show a closer relationship between the clauses than a period would show.

Colon (:)

We use a colon (:) for three primary uses.

Dash (-)

We use a dash when we need to separate words into statements. The two types of dashes that we use include:

Hyphen

We use a hyphen merge two or more words for enhanced meaning. These compound words aren’t separable by spaces.

Brackets ([])

We use for brackets for explaining technical matters, but even without their inclusion, your sentence alone will make sense.

Braces ({})

We use braces for carrying two or more lines of text or listed items that are considered as a unit.

Parenthesis ( () )

We use Parentheses to include further detail or qualifying remarks.

Apostrophe (‘)

We often use an apostrophe to indicate

Quotations marks (” “) and (‘ ‘)

We use quotations marks to indicate the exact words someone has used.

Ellipsis (. . .) or (***)

We usually use an ellipsis with three periods (. . .)

We usually use an ellipsis with three periods (. . .)while occasionally you will see them used with three asterisks (***).

We use an ellipsis to indicate when we’re omitting something, such as letters or words.
