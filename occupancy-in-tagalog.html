17. Compare and Contrast Spanish occupation American Occupation ​


Spanish occupation and American occupation are two periods in the history of the Philippines where foreign powers controlled the country. Here are some of the similarities and differences between the two:

Similarities:

- Both Spain and America colonized the Philippines for several decades.

- Both colonial powers introduced their language, culture, and religion to the Filipino people.

- Both Spain and America established their respective governments and imposed their laws and regulations on the Philippines.

- Both colonial powers experienced resistance and rebellion from the Filipino people.

Differences:

- The Spanish occupation lasted for over 300 years (1565-1898), while the American occupation lasted for around 50 years (1898-1946).

- The Spanish colonial government was characterized by a rigid caste system that favored the peninsulares (Spaniards born in Spain) over the criollos (Spaniards born in the colonies) and the indios (native Filipinos). In contrast, the American colonial government introduced democratic ideals and promoted equality among Filipinos.

- The Spanish introduced Roman Catholicism to the Philippines, which became the dominant religion. The Americans introduced Protestantism and allowed for the practice of other religions.

- The Spanish regime focused on maintaining the status quo and preserving the traditional way of life in the Philippines. The Americans introduced modernization, education, and infrastructure development.

Overall, while both Spain and America colonized the Philippines, their approaches and legacies were vastly different.

20. compare and contrast Spanish Occupation vs.American Occupation ​


Answer:

The Spanish Occupation of the Philippines lasted from 1521 to 1898, while the American Occupation began in 1898 and ended in 1946.

One major difference between the two occupations is that Spain was a colonial power, while America was an imperial power. This meant that Spain sought to control and exploit the resources of its colonies for its own benefit, while America sought to establish a more direct form of control over its territories.

Another difference is that Spain had a policy of assimilation, which meant it tried to impose Spanish culture on its colonies. On the other hand, America had a policy of cultural pluralism which allowed different cultures and languages to coexist within their territories.

In terms of economic policies, Spain focused on extracting resources from their colonies for their own benefit. They also imposed high taxes on local businesses and people in order to fund their colonial projects. In contrast, America adopted an open-door policy which encouraged foreign investment into their territories as well as free trade with other countries.

Finally, when it came to social policies both occupations were quite different; under Spanish rule there was little effort made towards improving education or health care for Filipinos whereas under American rule there were significant improvements made in these areas as well as infrastructure development such as roads and bridges being built throughout the country.

28. What do you know about Juan de Plasencia, the author of customs of the Tagalog, like his nationality, occupations, and/or position? Do any of these matter?


Juan de Plasencia was a Spanish Franciscan friar who lived in the Philippines during the 16th century. He was born in 1549 in Trujillo, Spain, and arrived in the Philippines in 1578 as part of a group of Franciscan missionaries.

In addition to his work documenting the customs of the Tagalog people, Juan de Plasencia also served as a missionary and pastor, and was known for his efforts to promote Christianity among the indigenous people of the Philippines. He was also involved in establishing schools and promoting education, particularly for children and young people.

Juan de Plasencia's nationality, occupation, and position are all relevant to his work and legacy. As a Spanish Franciscan missionary, he was part of a larger effort by the Spanish government and the Catholic Church to colonize and convert the people of the Philippines. His documentation of Tagalog customs and traditions was intended to aid in this effort, as was his work in establishing schools and promoting education.

While Juan de Plasencia's role in the Spanish colonization of the Philippines is a complex and controversial issue, his contributions to the documentation of Philippine culture and history are significant, and his work continues to be studied and celebrated today.
