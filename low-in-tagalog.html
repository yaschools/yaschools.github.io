4. aActivity 4. Tagalog Folksongs! Directions. Are you familiar with Tagalog folksongs like "Bahay Kubo", "Leron-Leron Sinta", "Sitsiritsit", "Magtanim ay Di Biro", and "Paruparong Bukid"? If not, ask a family member to sing the songs for you. Write the message of each song in the space be- low. Bahay Kubo​


Answer:

Explanation:

ALL songs are Tagalog folksongs.

BAHAY KUBO tells of the typical set up of a nipa hut at the middle of the field surrounded by a garden filled with various vegetable plants.

LERON LERON SINTA is a work song which depicts the activity of harvesting fruits such as the papaya with "Leron" and tamarind with Neneng". The breaking of the branch in the picking of papaya by Leron is regarded as a bad luck by the suitor, and so the suitor decides to find for another lady to court. Neneng in the second stanza is told by her companion to hold lest she will fall off the swaying tamarind branch.

SITSIRITSIT is a folksong about a flirtatious woman who told a store owner that the ant will devour her if she won't give her a credit for cookies. Also it tells of the exchange of the baby for a doll or "bagoong" or fish paste at Malabon.

MAGTANIM AY DI BIRO , Planting Rice is Never Fun reflects the positive hardworking trait of the Filipino rice farmers who endure the whole-day labor with bent position under

the sun and with no tine to sit or stand. Then it also tells the rice farmer to his listener to continue on with the hard work hopeful for a brighter future.

PARUPARONG BUKID is what a lady in the 1900 is likened to - a field butterfly, wearing a glamorous dress with a tall butterfly sleeves with an ornamental comb and a who sways and moves her hips at the middle of the church aisle.
