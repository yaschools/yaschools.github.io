13. scissors characteristics​


pa star, hearth and brainliest po :)

Scissors are a hand-held cutting tool with two blades joined together by a pivot point. Here are some of their characteristics:

Blades: Scissors have two blades that are made of metal and sharpened to form a sharp cutting edge. The blades are usually curved or straight, and may have serrated edges for cutting through tougher materials.

Handle: The handle of scissors is designed to fit comfortably in the hand, with a finger hole for each finger. The handle may be made of plastic, metal, or wood, and may be coated with a non-slip material for better grip.

Pivot: The pivot is the point where the two blades are joined together. It allows the blades to open and close smoothly, creating a cutting motion.

Size: Scissors come in different sizes, from small craft scissors to large dressmaking scissors. The size of the scissors determines the size of the blades and the amount of cutting power they can produce.

Uses: Scissors are used for cutting various materials, such as paper, cardboard, fabric, and hair. There are specialized scissors for different tasks, such as sewing scissors, kitchen scissors, and hair-cutting scissors.

Maintenance: Scissors need to be kept clean and sharp to work properly. They should be wiped clean after each use, and sharpened regularly with a sharpening stone or honing tool.

Safety: Scissors are sharp tools that can cause injury if not used properly. It is important to keep them away from children and to use them with caution. Some scissors have safety features, such as a locking mechanism to keep them closed when not in use.
