4. what is a rainforest why is rainforest known as the lungs of the earth​


Answer:

The rain forest is known as the lungs of the Earth because it regulates the planet's climate by absorbing CO₂. Similar to lungs, trees absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen necessary for animal and human survival. Amazon rainforest is the world's largest tropical rainforest in Brazil.

What is a rainforest?
A forest rich in biodiversity, found typically in tropical areas with consistently heavy rainfall.
Why is rainforest known as the lungs of the Earth?
Because rainforests generate about 20% of the world's oxygen and its trees play a key role in reducing pollutant levels.

24. Describe Rainforest Ecosystem?What are the components of the Rainforest Ecosystem?Give examples of Rainforest ecosystem​


Answer:

Describe Rainforest Ecosystem?

A rainforest ecosystem is composed of tall evergreen trees. Rainforests are featured with heavy annual rainfall, high average temperature, poor quality soil due to lack of nutrients, and rich in biodiversity.

What are the components of the Rainforest Ecosystem?

Most rainforests are structured in four layers: emergent, canopy, understory, and forest floor. Each layer has unique characteristics based on differing levels of water, sunlight, and air circulation.

Give examples of Rainforest ecosystem.

The largest rainforests on Earth surround the Amazon River in South America and the Congo River in Africa. The tropical islands of Southeast Asia and parts of Australia support dense rainforest habitats. Even the cool evergreen forests of North America's Pacific Northwest and Northern Europe are a type of rainforest.
