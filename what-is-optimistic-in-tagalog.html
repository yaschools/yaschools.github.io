14. what can you say about the power of positive, optimistic and resilience ​


Answer:

Not only do optimists feel less stress, but they also handle it better. Instead of being overwhelmed and demoralized by negative events, they concentrate on making constructive changes that will enhance their lives. They also tend to be more resilient and bounce back from setbacks more quickly.

While optimism can make us feel good temporarily, it can also assist us to permanently remodel our brains. In reality, practicing positive thinking can help us develop more optimistic thought patterns and activate brain regions that are essential for developing resilient attitudes and behaviors.

Our brain develops connections that encourage more positive thinking and behavior when we regularly practice gratitude, optimism, and other positive thinking and acting. Over time, we discover that we are naturally more upbeat and begin to see the world more optimistically. At the same time, the negative relationships we had previously established will start to deteriorate.

Answer:

6Q:

Optimist is to cheerful as Pessimist is to

A) Helpful

B) Mean

C) Petty

D) Gloomy

Answer: D) Gloomy

Explanation:

The given words are related in a way that, an optimist is a person whose outlook is cheerful. Similarly, a pessimist is a person whose outlook is gloomy.

The answer is not (b) because a pessimist does not have to be mean. Options (c) and (a) are incorrect because neither words describe the outlook of a pessimist.
