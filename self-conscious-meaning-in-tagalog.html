25. How can i manage self conscious​


Answer:

. Shrug Away Your Negative Thoughts

Don't Put People On A Pedestal

what you should do first is REALIZE that we as humans are hard wired to care about people's judgements. Apart from this anytime we attempt to do something we are not accustomed to do doing our brain RESISTS it.

Our brain resists change. It does not like doing something new and it expresses its resistance in the form of anxiety and ultimately self consciousness. Think about this, do you feel self conscious about brushing your teeth or taking a bath. My probable answer is NO, because you are used to doing it.

But if you are a person who hates attention and were suddenly told to stand in the middle of a party with everyone looking at you, of course you would feel conscious.

So now the question arises, how do you beat it; how do you beat self consciousness. Well, this is where the old saying." Fake it till you make it" really comes into play. Go back to step 1 in which you listed all the situations and (here comes the hard part), WILLINGLY put yourself in these situations. I know it sounds insane, like why would you want to voluntarily trigger anxiety and feel self conscious. But unfortunately, exposure is the only way forward when it comes to dealing with this thing. Find your motivation, go out there, put yourself in situations which trigger your self conciousness and act as if you are the person who is really confident. Act as if you control the situation and not the other way around. If you keep going, your brain will become familiar with it and one day it will become second nature. You'll find yourself doing things you were most afraid of without thinking twice.

REMEMBER- You'll fail a lot, some days you'll feel as if you have accomplished something, some days you might feel as if you have hit rock bottom BUT keep going forward and eventually you'll overcome it.

Good luck out there
