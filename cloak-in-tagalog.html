Corruption is considered a major issue in the Philippines due to a combination of factors, including a weak rule of law, lack of political will to address corruption, and a culture of impunity. Additionally, poverty and underdevelopment contribute to corruption as people are more likely to engage in corrupt activities to survive and make ends meet. The government institutions tasked with combating corruption, such as the judiciary and law enforcement agencies, are also often subject to corruption themselves, making it difficult to effectively address the problem. The persistence of corruption has serious consequences for the Philippines, including reduced economic growth, reduced trust in government institutions, and unequal distribution of resources.
