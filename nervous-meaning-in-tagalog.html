2. meaning ng peripheral nervous system Tagalog explanation po please for presentation❤​


Answer:

Ang peripheral nervous system (PNS) ay ang bahagi ng nervous system na nasa labas ng utak at spinal cord. Ito ay binubuo ng mga nerves at neurons na nagpapadala ng impormasyon mula sa mga sensory receptor sa buong katawan papunta sa central nervous system (CNS), at nagpapadala rin ng mga signal mula sa CNS pabalik sa mga muscles at glands sa buong katawan.

Sa Tagalog, ang peripheral nervous system ay tinatawag na "periperal na sistema ng nerbiyos" o "PNS." Ito ay ang bahagi ng ating nerbiyos na nasa labas ng ating utak at spinal cord. Ito ay binubuo ng mga nerve at neuron na nagpapadala ng impormasyon mula sa ating mga sensory receptor sa buong katawan patungo sa ating utak at spinal cord. Ito rin ay nagpapadala ng mga signal mula sa ating utak at spinal cord papunta sa ating mga kalamnan at glandula sa buong katawan.

Some conditions of ejaculation dysfunction are associated with autonomic nerves. However, the exact effects of autonomic nerves on ejaculation are not well defined. Autonomic agonists induce different recorded trace patterns of seminal vesicular contraction. The different traces contain different components of phasic and tonic contraction, which may have physiological implications. In this study, we examined isolated rat seminal vesicle (SV) contraction by phenylephrine (PE), acetylcholine, and their respective antagonists and then speculated upon physiological roles of sympathetic and parasympathetic nerves on SV during ejaculation. Introduction

Ejaculation is composed of emission, ejection, and orgasm. Ejaculatory disorders include pre‐mature ejaculation, inhibited ejaculation, retrograde ejaculation and anejaculation, which are all bothersome problems to patients and their partners (McMahon et al., 2004). Of these ejaculatory disorders, pre‐mature ejaculation, retrograde ejaculation and anejaculation are common problems of male sexual medicine (Aschka et al., 2001; Hatzimouratidis et al., 2010). The emission stage, through sympathetically mediated neurons, leads to the contractions of the seminal vesicles (SV) and the prostate, with expulsion of spermatozoa and seminal fluid into the posterior urethra (Birowo et al., 2010). The SV is a secretomotor organ, which is composed of outer smooth muscle layer and subepithelial glandular tissue. The major innervation to the smooth muscle layer is through both sympathetic and parasympathetic nerves, while the innervation to glandular tissue is mainly through parasympathetic nerve terminals (Kepper & Keast, 1997). 28. What is the meaning and function of Major divisions and parts of nervous system?​


Answer:

The nervous system is a complex network of cells that transmits signals between different parts of the body. It is divided into two major parts: the central nervous system (CNS) and the peripheral nervous system (PNS).

The CNS includes the brain and spinal cord, which are the primary control centers for the body. The brain is responsible for processing sensory information and controlling behavior, while the spinal cord serves as a pathway for sensory and motor signals to travel between the brain and the rest of the body.

The PNS includes all of the nerves that extend from the CNS to the rest of the body. It is further divided into two parts: the somatic nervous system and the autonomic nervous system.

The somatic nervous system controls voluntary movement and sensation, while the autonomic nervous system controls involuntary processes such as heart rate, breathing, and digestion. The autonomic nervous system is further divided into two parts: the sympathetic nervous system, which prepares the body for action, and the parasympathetic nervous system, which promotes relaxation and rest.

The major divisions and parts of the nervous system work together to control and coordinate all of the body's functions. They are responsible for everything from conscious thought and movement to the automatic regulation of vital bodily processes. Dysfunction or damage to any part of the nervous system can lead to a wide range of disorders and diseases.
