The safety pin is a variation of the regular pin which includes a simple spring mechanism and a clasp. The clasp serves two purposes: to form a closed loop thereby properly fastening the pin to whatever it is applied to, and to cover the end of the pin to protect the user from the sharp point.

8. What type of simple machines is safety pins​


Answer:

Safety pins are an example of a simple machine called a lever. Specifically, safety pins use a type of lever called a third-class lever, where the effort is applied between the fulcrum (the hinge of the pin) and the load (the fabric or material being held together). In this case, the effort is applied by pressing down on the top of the pin to open it, which lifts the clasp and releases the tension on the spring. When the pressure is released, the clasp snaps back into place and secures the pin in its locked position.

17. safety precaution using pins​


Answer:

Safety precautions when working with needles, pins and knitting needles:

1. Keep needles and pins at a certain place (a special box, cushion, etc.), do not leave them at the workplace, never take the needle, pins in your mouth and do not stick them in the clothes. Do not leave a needle and pins in the product;

2. Use thimble when sewing;

3. Do not use rusty needles and pins in your work;

4. Attach patterns to fabric with sharp ends of pins away from you;

5. Collect and dispose of pieces of broken needles or pins, wrapped in paper;

6. Count the number of pins taken before work and the number of pins at the end of the work, it must be the same;

7. Keep knitting needles and the hook in the case, at the end of the work remove them out of reach of small children;

8. When working with knitting needles keep them no closer than 35 centimeters from your eyes.
