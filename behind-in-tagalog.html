24. Conclusion: Why is it that you can clearly see the objects behind the plastic but cannot see them clearly behind the wax cover and totally cannot see them behind the cardboard?​


The reason why you can see objects clearly behind plastic is because plastic is a transparent material that allows light to pass through it without scattering the light too much. When light passes through a transparent material like plastic, it bends slightly but remains relatively unchanged, which allows you to see the objects behind it with relatively little distortion.

On the other hand, wax and cardboard are opaque materials that do not allow light to pass through them. When light hits an opaque material like wax or cardboard, it is either absorbed or reflected back, causing the light to scatter in different directions. This scattering of light makes it difficult to see objects behind the material, and in some cases, makes them completely invisible.

However, wax and cardboard can be made translucent by thinning them out. For example, if the wax cover is very thin, some light may pass through it, but it will still scatter the light and make the objects behind it blurry. Similarly, if the cardboard is thin or has small holes in it, some light may pass through it, but it will still scatter the light and make the objects behind it difficult to see clearly.

In summary, the transparency or opacity of a material determines how much light can pass through it and how much the light will scatter, which in turn affects how clearly you can see objects behind it.

It means that the international community has agreed to make a concerted effort to identify and lift up those who are furthest behind first.
