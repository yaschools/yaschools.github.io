28. These play during the pot dance performed by maidens depicting the task of fetching water


This dance that is described above is called "The Banga" or others may refer it as "The Ragsaksakan". The dance itself is the pot's name in its native language. This is about women fetching water as their daily chore. They carry around seven to eight pots. While the pots are on their head, they dance with the wind chimes or as they call it "gangsa". This dance originated in Kalinga, a mountain province of the Philippines. They balance the pots with their strength and stamina.
