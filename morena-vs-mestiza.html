8. 7. you will be asked to construct a two srorey house following architectura mestizawhat will be the structure of it​


Answer:

Bahay na bato (Tagalog, literally "house of stone") is a type of building originating during the Philippines' Spanish Colonial period. It is an updated version of the traditional bahay kubo. Its design has evolved throughout the ages, but still maintains the bahay kubo's architectural basis which corresponds to the tropical climate, stormy season, and earthquake-prone environment of the whole archipelago of the Philippines and fuses it with the influence of Spanish colonizers and Chinese traders. Thus created was a hybrid of Austronesian, Spanish, and Chinese architecture with American influence during the American era, supporting the fact that the Philippines is a result of these cultures mixing together. Its most common appearance is that of an elevated, overhanging wooden upper-story nipa hut (with balustrades, ventanillas, and capiz shell sliding windows) that stands on Spanish-style solid stone blocks or bricks and posts as foundation instead of just wood, bamboo stilts, or timber posts. Roofing is either Chinese tiled roof or thatch (nipa, sago palm, or cogon), of which many today are being replaced by galvanized or other modern roofing. It followed the bahay kubo's arrangements such as open ventilation and elevated apartments used as living space with the ground floor used for storerooms, cellars, and other business purposes. Like bahay kubo, much of this ground level was reserved for storage; in business districts, some spaces were rented to shops. Horses for carriages were housed in stables called caballerizas.[1] Bahay na bato had a rectangular plan that reflected vernacular Austronesian Filipino traditional houses integrated with Spanish style.[2]


9. explain the difference of JPEG vs. GIF vs. PNG vs. BMP vs. TIFF


JPEG

(Joint Photographic Experts Group)

is a File Format with a lossy compression. It allows some data loss for a smaller file size. It compresses grayscale and color information but allows for images to be displayed in 16,000,000 colors.

GIF

(Graphics Interchange Format)

is a File Format with a lossless compression. Meaning, there is no data lost in the compression. GIF supports animation and is preferred for images with only a few colors. (GIF only allows for 256 colors)

PNG

(Portable Network Graphics)

is a File Format that takes off from the transparency capabilities of GIF, primarily developed due to GIF's licensing requirement.

TIFF

(Tagged Image File Format)

is a File Format that is a flexible bitmap image that is supported by many software. It has a much larger file size compared to JPEG and GIF and is commonly recommended by professional printers since it is supported by most press machines.

BMP

(Bitmap)

The standrad Windows file format on DOS and Windows–compatible platforms. It has a lossless compression.

27. " UNA MESTIZA by Juan Luna Painting" -Introduction-Background and historical-Reflection -Bakit mo ito napili​


Introduction:

"Una Mestiza" is a painting by Juan Luna, a renowned Filipino artist who gained international recognition for his works. The painting depicts a beautiful mestiza woman who is wearing a traditional Filipino dress or terno.

Background and Historical Context:

Juan Luna created "Una Mestiza" in 1885, during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. At that time, mestizos or people of mixed Spanish and Filipino ancestry were often portrayed as the idealized beauty in art and literature. "Una Mestiza" is a prime example of the Filipino-Hispanic style that was popular during this era.

The painting is significant in Philippine history because it showcases the country's rich cultural heritage and highlights the influence of Spanish colonization on Filipino art. Juan Luna, together with other Filipino artists, challenged the European-dominated art scene and paved the way for the emergence of a distinct Filipino art movement.

Reflection:

"Una Mestiza" is a strikingly beautiful painting that captures the essence of the Filipino-Hispanic culture. It's fascinating how the artist was able to combine the traditional Filipino dress with the Western style of painting. The intricate details on the dress and the woman's facial features are impressive, showing the artist's attention to detail and skill in painting.

As a Filipino, seeing this painting reminds me of our country's rich and diverse history. It is a representation of the blending of cultures that has shaped the identity of the Filipino people. Juan Luna's legacy as an artist and patriot is something that we should be proud of and continue to celebrate.

Bakit mo ito napili:

Pinili ko ang "Una Mestiza" ni Juan Luna dahil ito ay isang magandang halimbawa ng likha ng mga Filipino artist noong panahon ng kolonyalismo. Hindi lamang ito nagpapakita ng magandang sining ngunit nagbibigay rin ito ng kasaysayan ng kulturang Pilipino at ng kanyang pagbabago sa panahon ng pananakop. Tila ito'y isang kombinasyon ng dalawang kultura na nagdulot ng pagkakakilanlan ng mga Pilipino. Ito rin ay nagpapakita ng kagalingan at husay ni Juan Luna bilang isang pintor.
