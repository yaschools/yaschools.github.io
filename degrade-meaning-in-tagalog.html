9. Explain environmental degradation?​


Answer:

Question: Explain environmental degradation?

Answer:

Environmental degradation is a process through which the natural environment is compromised in some way, reducing biological diversity and the general health of the environment. This process can be entirely natural in origin, or it can be accelerated or caused by human activities.

23. degradable/ biodegradable​


Answer:

Biodegradable means that it can be broken down by living things like bacteria and fungi (these are microorganisms). Degradable means they do not require living organisms to break down. Instead, chemical additives are used in the plastic to make it crumble more quickly than it would otherwise.


24. Meaning of Habitat degradation​


Answer:

Habitat destruction is the process by which a natural habitat becomes incapable of supporting its native species. The organisms that previously inhabited the site are displaced or dead, thereby reducing biodiversity and species abundance. Habitat destruction is the leading cause of biodiversity loss.
