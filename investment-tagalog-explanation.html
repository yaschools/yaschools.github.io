14. Two investment produce an annual interest income of 4200. The amount invested at 14% is 6000 less than the amount invested at 10%. How much is the amount invested at 14%?


Answer:

Let x be the amount invested at 10%.

Then the amount invested at 14% is x - 6000.

The total interest earned from these two investments is 4200, so we can write:

0.10x + 0.14(x - 6000) = 4200

Simplifying and solving for x, we get:

0.10x + 0.14x - 840 = 4200

0.24x = 5040

x = 21000

Therefore, the amount invested at 14% is:

x - 6000 = 21000 - 6000 = 15000

So the answer is that $15,000 was invested at 14%.

18. In accounting for debt investments that are classified as trading investments


Answer:

Debt investments classified as trading are reported at fair value, with unrealized holding gains and losses reported as part of net income. Trading and available-for-sale debt securities should be reported at fair value, whereas held-to-maturity debt securities should be reported at amortized cost.

22. Explanation of the The State recognizes the indispensable role of the private sector, encourages private enterprise, and provides ihcentives to needed investments.​


Answer & Explanation:            PA BRAINLIEST

The statement "The State recognizes the indispensable role of the private sector, encourages private enterprise, and provides incentives to needed investments" implies that the government acknowledges the importance of the private sector in driving the economy and creating jobs. The private sector includes businesses owned by individuals or corporations, as opposed to the public sector that is owned and operated by the government.

By encouraging private enterprise, the government is promoting economic growth and development. The private sector is usually more efficient and innovative than the public sector, which can lead to greater productivity and economic benefits. Incentives provided by the government, such as tax breaks, subsidies, and grants, can help to stimulate investment in key areas such as research and development, infrastructure, and technology.

Overall, this statement suggests that the government recognizes the important role of the private sector in creating jobs and driving economic growth, and is committed to supporting and encouraging private enterprise through policies and incentives.

24. discuss the economic investment and financial investment​


Answer:

economic investment

- This investment refers to the money spent on the purchase of new or replacing the capital assets of a company. The capital assets here are all things necessary for the production of goods or services.

financial investment

- Financial investment is a much broader concept, and we can say that economic investment is a part of it. This type of investment involves the purchase of an asset with the goal of financial gain. This investment could be made in the new asset or in any old assets.
