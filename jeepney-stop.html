12. 1how long will it take a drivee to bring a jeepney travelling 50.0 m/s to a complete stop if it's accelerating 0.500m/s²​


Answer:

It will take the driver 100 seconds to bring the jeepney to a complete stop.

Explanation:

To solve for the time it will take the jeepney to come to a complete stop, we need to use the equation:

v = u + at

where:

v = final velocity (0 m/s)

u = initial velocity (50.0 m/s)

a = acceleration (-0.500 m/s², negative because it's decelerating)

t = time

Rearranging the equation to solve for t, we get:

t = (v - u) / a

Plugging in the given values, we get:

t = (0 - 50.0) / (-0.500)

t = 100 seconds

Therefore, it will take the driver 100 seconds to bring the jeepney to a complete stop.

27. How long will it take a driver to bring a jeepney travelling 50.0 m/s to complete stop if its acceleration is -0.500 m/s.​


Answer:

It will take 100 seconds for the jeepney to come to a complete stop.

Explanation:

We can use the kinematic equation:

v^2 = u^2 + 2as

where:

v = final velocity (0 m/s, since the jeepney comes to a complete stop)

u = initial velocity (50.0 m/s)

a = acceleration (-0.500 m/s^2)

s = distance

Rearranging the equation to solve for s, we get:

s = (v^2 - u^2) / (2a)

Substituting the values given, we get:

s = (0^2 - 50.0^2) / (2(-0.500))

s = 2,500 meters

Therefore, the distance required for the jeepney to come to a complete stop is 2,500 meters.

To find the time it takes to stop, we can use another kinematic equation:

v = u + at

where:

v = final velocity (0 m/s)

u = initial velocity (50.0 m/s)

a = acceleration (-0.500 m/s^2)

t = time

Rearranging the equation to solve for t, we get:

t = (v - u) / a

Substituting the values given, we get:

t = (0 - 50.0) / (-0.500)

t = 100 seconds

Therefore, it will take 100 seconds for the jeepney to come to a complete stop.
