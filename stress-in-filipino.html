"Stress and Filipino" By Michael L. Tan Reaction Paper.

         In this article, Michael writes about the phenomenon of stress experienced by Filipinos. This stress can be caused by many things, from the demands of work, noise, economic problems, to pressure caused by expectations of gender. What Michael highlighted in this article is that Filipinos find it difficult to express that they are under stress so there is no serious treatment that makes them suffer from several serious conditions due to stress.

         In my opinion, this article shows well the stress phenomenon that is happening in the Philippines, maybe even the world. Saying that we are under stress and then seeking help to deal with it is still not very common in society. Unlike the flu or fever which is immediately treated, stress is considered not a serious problem that can be reduced by looking for something that can give pleasure. Even without realizing it, stress is also a condition that is as serious as flu and fever and must be handled properly to prevent serious conditions that are more difficult to cure.

        One example of stress cause that interests me in this article is stress caused by gender pressure, where men are considered weak when they cry, and cannot be lower than women in terms of income and others. I see a lot of these situations around me and I think it's important for society to start realizing that men have feelings too and crying is not a form of weakness but a form of expression.

         By reading this article, I have become more aware of mental health and realize the importance of knowing and learning steps to deal with stress. In addition, it is also important for us to start looking back at the habits and stigma that have been formed in our society so far and examine what is right and what is wrong.
