3. why is bicol known as bicol volcanic arc​


The Bicol region, located in the southeastern part of Luzon island in the Philippines, is known as the Bicol Volcanic Arc because it is an active volcanic chain composed of numerous stratovolcanoes and volcanic edifices.

The Bicol Volcanic Arc is part of the Pacific Ring of Fire, an area in the Pacific Ocean where many earthquakes and volcanic eruptions occur due to the convergence of several tectonic plates. The subduction of the Philippine Sea Plate under the Eurasian Plate has resulted in the formation of the volcanic arc, with magma rising from the Earth's mantle and causing volcanic activity.

The Bicol Volcanic Arc consists of several active and inactive volcanoes, including Mayon Volcano, one of the most active and iconic volcanoes in the Philippines. Other notable volcanoes in the area include Bulusan Volcano, Mount Iriga, Mount Masaraga, and Mount Malinao.

The volcanic activity in the Bicol region has contributed to the fertility of its soil and the abundance of natural resources, including geothermal energy. However, it also poses risks to the local communities, as volcanic eruptions can cause damage to infrastructure, crops, and homes, as well as put the lives of people in danger.

Overall, the Bicol Volcanic Arc is a significant natural feature of the Philippines and a source of both opportunities and challenges for the people and communities living in the area.

Ang Bicol ay tumutukoy sa isang rehiyon na matatagpuan sa timog-silangang bahagi ng Pilipinas. Binubuo ito ng anim na lalawigan: Albay, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, Catanduanes, Masbate, at Sorsogon. Ang rehiyon ay kilala sa mayamang kultura, kasaysayan, at magagandang natural na tanawin, kabilang ang sikat na bulkang Mayon.

Ang wikang Bicol, na kilala rin bilang Bikol, ay sinasalita ng mga tao sa rehiyong ito at itinuturing na isa sa mga pangunahing wika sa Pilipinas. Mayroon itong ilang diyalektong sinasalita sa iba't ibang lalawigan ng rehiyon. Kilala ang mga Bikolano sa kanilang mainit na mabuting pakikitungo, masasarap na pagkain, at makukulay na pagdiriwang at tradisyon.

Ang rehiyon ng Bicol ay kilala rin sa mayamang kasaysayan at pamana nitong kultura. Naimpluwensyahan ito ng iba't ibang kultura sa buong taon, kabilang ang Malay, Espanyol, at Amerikano, na makikita sa wika, kaugalian, at arkitektura nito. Ipinagmamalaki ng mga Bikolano ang kanilang pamana at ito ay makikita sa kanilang mga kaugalian, tradisyon, at pagdiriwang.

Ang rehiyon ay kilala rin sa masasarap na pagkain nito, na may mga sikat na pagkain kabilang ang laing (isang ulam na gawa sa dahon ng taro at gata), Bicol express (isang maanghang na ulam ng baboy), at kinunot (isang ulam na gawa sa ginutay-gutay na karne at gata ng niyog).

Acrostic Poem
An acrostic is when the first letter in every line of a text spells out a specific word. Of course, an acrostic could also be constructed where the last letter of each line of a text spells out the word, but this is harder to create. An acrostic poem is a poem where the first letter of each line (or the last letter of each line) spells out a specific word.
Examples of Acrostic Poem:
Sunshine warming my toes,
Underwater fun with my friends.
Making homemade ice cream on the porch,
Many long nights catching fireflies.
Early morning walks to the creek,
Reveling in the freedom of lazy days.
Edgar Allan Poe's "An Acrostic":
Elizabeth it is in vain you say
"Love not"-thou sayest it in so sweet a way:
