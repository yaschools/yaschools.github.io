27. the volcano is like a pimple?​


The similarities are superficial. The appearance of a pimple and a stratovolcano may be similar, but the mechanics of pimples and volcanoes aren’t, particularly. A pimple occurs because of a minor infection, and may eventually form a whitehead as pressure from the expanding infection forces it out of the pore. Granted, you could call that a sort of eruption.

Volcanoes erupt because of pressure from a combination of the heat of magma and from volatile gases. The volcano may have a lot of gas or very little. It may be a broad shield volcano, a cone-shaped stratovolcano, a caldera volcano (like yellowstone), a cinder cone, a volcanic mound — it may even be a rift that releases huge lava flows. The source material for a volcano comes from the mantle, and it’s created by plate tectonics.

In other words, the only thing they have in common is an eruption caused by an expanding mass. If you’re looking for a good comparison for the eruption of a stratovolcano, you might ask if your listeners have ever opened a can of soda pop and had it come spraying out. The opening of the can releases pressure, causing the gases in the can to expand.

A pimple isn’t a terrible example, but the soda can might be more descriptive of the sort of pyroclastic explosion stratovolcanoes usually unleash.

(By the way, the pimple may be a better comparison with volcanic mounds, since they have very little gas and slowly squeeze their “eruptions” out.)

EXPLANATION:"SKIN"

- The Skin is the largest organ in the human body and accounts for about 15% of your total body weight. The skin is made of three main layers, the epidermis, the dermis, and subcutaneous tissue (hypodermis).

There are five organs in the integumentary system.

- They are the skin, hair, nails, sebaceous glands (oil glands), and sudoriferous glands (sweat glands).

The skin has a tiny opening called pores through which sweat and oil leave the body.

SUBCUTANEOUS LAYER or HYPODERMIS

(beneath the skin)

- It is the lowermost layer of the integumentary system. It consists of mostly adipose tissue and is the storage site of most body fat. It serves to fasten the skin to the underlying surface, provides thermal insulation, and absorbs shocks from impacts to the skin.

Accessory StructureA. Sebaceous Glands or Oil Glands

- The sebaceous gland is a very small gland that can be only be seen under a microscope. You can find it anywhere on your skin, but mainly where it's hairy. The sebaceous gland also produces an oily secretion on hairs called sebum. Sebum lubricates the hair and allows it to stay healthy and shiny, but when large quantities of sebum are created, it protects the skin and the hair from water by reducing the growth of microorganisms. Sebum prevents skin from cracking, turning dry, and brittle. The main function of sebaceous gland is to protect the skin.

That is why when there is overproduction of sebum by the oil glands that clogs and it infects the glands causing pimple or acne.
