23. This distinction is used in communication as a code to produce information about people and culture. The challenge is to be cognizant of social realities and regard local culture in the advent of localization​


The distinction that this statement refers to is the use of codes or symbols in communication to convey information about people and culture. When using these codes, it is important to be aware of the local social and cultural context, especially the context of place. Localization is the process of tailoring communication materials to specific audiences or cultures, and failure to recognize and respect local culture may result in misunderstanding or offense. Therefore, it is important that

approaches communication with sensitivity and understanding of the cultural context in order to communicate effectively and promote cultural awareness.
