6. The sum of the reciprocal of two numbers is 9. Four times the reciprocal of the secondis equal to twice the reciprocal of the first increased by 12. Find the numbers.​


The numbers are:

x = 0.25 and y = 0.20

Solution:

Let the two numbers be x and y

First equation:

[tex] \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = 9[/tex]

Second equation:

[tex]4 \times \frac{1}{y} = 2 \times \frac{1}{x} + 12[/tex]

or

[tex] \frac{4}{y} = \frac{2}{x} + 12[/tex]

Solve for 1/y:

[tex] \frac{4}{y} = \frac{2}{x} + 12 \\ \frac{4}{y} = \frac{2 +12x }{x} \\ \frac{1}{4} (\frac{4}{y} = \frac{2 +12x }{x}) \frac{1}{4} \\ \frac{1}{y} = \frac{2 + 12x}{4x}[/tex]

Solve for x by substituting the value of 1/y to the first equation:

[tex] \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{y} = 9 \\ \frac{1}{x} + \frac{2 + 12x}{4x} = 9 \\ \frac{4 + 2 + 12x}{4x} = 9 \\ 36x = 6 + 12x \\ 36x - 12x = 6 \\ 24x = 6 \\ x = \frac{6}{24} or \: 0.25[/tex]

Solve for y by substituting the value of x to the second equation:

[tex] \frac{4}{y} = \frac{2}{.25} + 12 \\ \frac{4}{y} = \frac{2 + 3}{.25} \\ \frac{4}{y} = \frac{5}{.25} \\ \frac{4}{y} = 20 \\ 20y = 4 \\ y = \frac{4}{20} or \: 0.20[/tex]

CHECKING:

First equation:

[tex] \frac{1}{.25} + \frac{1}{.20} = 9 \\ 4 + 5 = 9 \\ 9 = 9 \: (checked)[/tex]

Second equation:

[tex] \frac{4}{.20} = \frac{2}{.25} + 12 \\ 20 = 8 + 12 \\ 20 = 20 \: (checked)[/tex]


7. the reciprocal of 2 plus the reciprocal of 6 is the reciprocal of what number?


The reciprocal of 2 is 1/2.
The reciprocal of 6 is 1/6.
Thus, 1/2 + 1/6 = 3/6 + 1/6 = 4/6 = 2/3.
Therefore, the reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2 or 1.5.
