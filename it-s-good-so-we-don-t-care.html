21. What is the most important natural resource in the Philippines that we should take care of and why? No s t u p i d answers


Answer:

The Philippines is rich in natural resources, and the most important one to take care of may depend on the context and specific needs of the country. However, one of the most important natural resources in the Philippines that should be taken care of is its forests.

Forests in the Philippines are home to a wide variety of plant and animal species, many of which are found nowhere else in the world. They also play a crucial role in maintaining the country's biodiversity and ecological balance. Additionally, forests are an important source of livelihood for many Filipinos, particularly those living in rural areas. They provide timber, non-timber forest products, and also act as carbon sinks, helping to regulate the Earth's climate.

Another important natural resource in the Philippines is its marine resources. The Philippines is an archipelago and it's surrounded by seas and oceans, which are an important source of food, income, and recreation for many Filipinos. The country's coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds are also important habitats for a wide variety of marine life, which play a crucial role in maintaining the country's biodiversity.

Lastly, the Philippines is rich in mineral resources such as nickel, gold, copper, and chromite. These resources are important for the country's economic development, but their extraction needs to be done in an environmentally sustainable manner to avoid negative impacts on the communities and the environment.

Explanation: In summary, the most important natural resource in the Philippines that we should take care of is the country's forests and marine resources, as they are vital to maintaining the country's biodiversity, ecological balance, and the livelihoods of many Filipinos.
