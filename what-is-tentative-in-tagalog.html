Explanation:

Tents were used by crusaders during the medieval Crusades as a form of mobile shelter and housing. They provided a convenient way for crusaders to set up camp and rest during their long and often arduous journey to the Holy Land. Tents were also used as a form of temporary housing for soldiers and their families, as well as for storage of supplies and weapons. They were often made of heavy canvas or other durable materials, and were designed to withstand the harsh conditions of the Middle East. In addition to providing shelter, tents also served as a symbol of the crusaders' temporary presence in the region and their intention to retake the Holy Land.
