18. Silencer genes halt the transcription of DNA true or false


The answer is FALSE. Technically, silencer genes do not halt or totally stop the transcription of DNA or the process where a DNA information is copied on to a new molecule such as the RNA.

Instead, these silencers genes only lower the rate of transcription. Silencer genes are the opposite of enhancers, which have the ability to increase the rate of gene transcription.

24. The expansion of the universe will eventually come to halt.


Though, it is not proven yet, the Universe will eventually come to halt and which is explained by the "Big Crunch Theory".

The Big Crunch is one of the possibilities that scientists believe will end the Earth in. Unlike several others, it is based on the Theory of General Relativity by Einstein. That is, if the Big Bang describes how the Universe most possibly began, the Big Crunch is it's counterpart and it describes how it will end as a consequence of that beginning.

It tells us that the expansion of the Universe, as to the Big Bang, will not continue forever. Instead, it will avoid expanding and fall into itself at a certain point in time, taking everything with it until finally it becomes the largest black hole ever. Thus, the "Big Crunch".
