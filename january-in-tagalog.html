27. your savings double each day. If you save $1 on January 1, $2 on January 2, $4 on January 3, $8 on January 4, and so on, how much will you save during the month


The problem is what we call Geometric Progression

To properly approach the problem, we must define first the formula of the sum of gemetric progression:

[tex]sum = \frac{a( {r}^{n} - 1)}{r - 1} [/tex]
where a = first term of the sequence
r = common ratio
n = number of terms

As stated in the problem, the our a = 1 because it is the first term

The common ratio is the ration by which the nth term is divided by (n-1)th term. Common ratio is constant for all terms. We demonstrate this by,

2 ÷ 1 = 2
8 ÷ 4 = 2

The n number of terms would be the number of days in January which would be 31.

Solving for the sum of geometric progression, it will yield:

$ 2,147,483,647
