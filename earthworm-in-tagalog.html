13. what is the characteristics of an Earthworms ​


Explanation:

Each segment on an earthworm’s body has a number of bristly hairs, called setae (sometimes written as chaetae). These hairs provide some grip to help the earthworm move through the soil.

An earthworm has a streamlined body with no antennae or fins or arms or legs! This streamlined shape is an adaptation to living in narrow burrows underground and the need to move easily through the soil.

An earthworm has circular muscles that surround each body segment. It also has longitudinal muscles that run the length of its body. These two groups of muscles work together to help the earthworm move.

In order to get food into its mouth, an earthworm pushes its pharynx out of its mouth to grasp hold of its food. It then pulls the food back into its mouth and wets it with saliva

25. earthworms productive by​


Answer:

Earthworms are extremely productive and play a vital role in many ecosystems. They are known as "nature's plow" because they help to aerate and enrich the soil by burrowing through it, which improves soil structure and fertility. Here are a few ways that earthworms are productive:

Soil Fertility: Earthworms consume organic matter and excrete nutrient-rich castings that improve soil fertility. They also help to mix organic matter into the soil, which increases the availability of nutrients for plants.

Soil Aeration: Earthworms burrow through soil, creating channels for air, water and roots to move through. This improves soil structure, making it more porous and easier for plants to establish roots.

Composting: Earthworms help to break down organic matter in compost piles, which speeds up the composting process and produces nutrient-rich compost.

Plant Growth: Earthworm activity can increase plant growth by improving soil fertility and structure, which provides better growing conditions for plants.

Carbon Sequestration: Earthworms play a role in carbon sequestration, which is the process of capturing and storing carbon in soil. They help to bury organic matter in soil, preventing it from releasing carbon into the atmosphere.

Supporting the food web: Earthworms are an important food source for many animals, including birds, fish, and mammals. Their presence supports the biodiversity of the ecosystem.
