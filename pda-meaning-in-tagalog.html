Dating and PDA Among School StudentsClaims include personal touching, holding hands, kissing, and snuggling at school

Public Display of Affection, or PDA, is the act of showing your partner affection in public, such as holding hands, hugging, and kissing. It's wonderful that you want to show affection to your partner and that you want the world to know how proud you are to have them in your life, but there are limits to certain actions, especially when you're in a public place where everyone can see your movements because you never know who might witness them. Couples should be aware of the limits of their actions and know how to control them, as anyone, including children, may catch a glimpse of their intimate act and mistakenly believe that it is acceptable. If one wishes to display affection for his or her partner, they should do so in a private or respectable location where few people can observe. There is always a proper setting and occasion for displaying affection for one's partner.

Public Display of Affection is a growing concern on a number of school campuses for a variety of reasons. Here are some arguments for prohibiting Public Displays of Affection in schools:

A PDA causes distraction.

School is a location for instruction. It should be their top priority, but instead of learning and concentrating on their studies, students lose focus and become distracted.

PDA causes disruption.

There is a possibility that those involved and bystanders will feel uneasy about what they are witnessing. Sure, it's simple to say, "If you don't like what you're seeing, don't look," but as much as others try to avoid looking, your distressing intimate activities would nonetheless attract their attention.

PDA is a violation.

Appendix 8 of the Lasallian Student Handbook for the 2014-2015 school year states that students who are caught violating this rule will be immediately reprimanded and reported to the Discipline Office via the Office of Student Affairs.

13. Is PDA (Public display of affection) an ethical or a moral issue?


Answer:

PDA (Public display of affection) is any act of physical intimacy in one’s perspective. This may include holding hands, hugging, or kissing. PDA is neither an ethical nor a moral issue.

Explanation:

A suitable showing of affection may vary with reference to culture and context. Sometimes, it also depends on an individual’s point of view. In most of the European countries like Australia and Canada, showing of affection in public is just a common gesture or act. On the other part of the world, mostly Asian countries like India and China, PDA is not socially acceptable.  

Moral and ethics are interchangeable and closely related, however, there's still differences. Morals ask guiding principles, and ethics ask specific rules and actions, or behaviors. Additionally, “ethics” leans towards decisions based upon a private character, whereas “morals” emphasizes the widely-shared communal or societal norms about right and wrong.
