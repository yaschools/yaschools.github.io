6. explian the similarities and/or differences of a church,dinomination,sect,and cult


Answer:

Here are the characteristics of church, denomination, sect, and cult:

(You can find their similarities and/or differences based on these characteristics)

Church: Large membership size Low commitment, inclusive membership Claim a monopoly over the truth Closely connected to the state like in some country they have an “official religion” or state religion Organizational form- hierarchical bureaucratic structure Accepts mainstream values EXAMPLES: Roman Catholic Church, Church of England

Denomination: Medium membership size (larger than a sect but less than church) Higher commitment than church, more exclusive membership They often do not claim a monopoly over the truth  Not closely connected to the state and will comment and campaign on social issues Organizational form- they have some bureaucracy and hierarchy, but less than church Accepts mainstream values EXAMPLES: Methodism, Pentecost, Jehovah’s Witness

Sect: Smallest membership size (often made up of marginalized, deprived groups, like the marginalized minority-ethnic groups) Highest commitment, very exclusive membership Claim a monopoly over the truth (and are often hostile to other religious organizations) They are usually hostile to the state and to mainstream society Organizational form- hierarchical, led by a charismatic leader Rejects mainstream values EXAMPLE: Branch Davidians Cult: Medium membership size Low commitment, very inclusive membership They often do not claim a monopoly over the truth  Generally apolitical Organizational form- loose knit networks, client-customer relations Reject the values of the host culture and hold the host culture in distrust EXAMPLES: Transcendental Meditation, The Human Potential Movement, Scientology
