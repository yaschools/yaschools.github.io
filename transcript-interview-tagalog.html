9. 16-20. When is it appropriate to use a naturalistic observation in qualitative research? Explain your answer. 21-25. Cite some of the major issues in using structured interviews. 26-30. Why is it important to have a verbatim transcription of interview data?


Answer:

16-20. Naturalistic observation is appropriate in qualitative research when the researcher wants to study a phenomenon in its natural setting, without manipulating or controlling the environment. This method allows the researcher to gather detailed information about the behavior and interactions of participants in their natural environment, and can provide insight into how a phenomenon occurs naturally.

21-25. Some major issues in using structured interviews include:

Respondent bias, where participants may provide socially desirable answers instead of their true beliefs or experiences

Interviewer bias, where the interviewer's own beliefs or expectations may influence the way they conduct the interview or interpret the responses

Lack of flexibility, where the structured format may not allow for exploration of unexpected or sensitive topics

Limited ability to probe or follow up on responses, as the interviewer may be limited to a set list of questions

26-30. It is important to have a verbatim transcription of interview data because it provides an accurate and complete record of the interview. This allows for later analysis and interpretation of the data, as well as the ability to review the responses in more detail. Verbatim transcription also helps maintain the context and meaning of the responses, and can help identify nonverbal cues or other important information that might be lost in a summarized or paraphrased version of the interview. Additionally, it's useful for a researcher to check for any errors or inaccuracies that may have been made during the interview process.
