7. carter sells cashews for $3 a pound hazelnuts for $ 2.50 a pound and peanuts for $1.75 a pounds.how many pounds of cashwes and hazelnuts should be mixed with 50 pounds of peanuts to obtain a mixed bag of 100 pounds of nuts that will sell for $2.30 a pound?


MIXTURE PROBLEMS are one of the most common WORDED PROBLEMS in algebra. Like other worded problems, it is difficult to solve when your imagination doesn’t work well.

It usually talks about some materials (given their masses and prices) being mixed together and a resulting mixture of a different price. Other most common scenarios with these problems are combining solutions in chemistry laboratory and adding ingredients to a recipe.

The very first step in solving problems like this is to literally draw them. Draw the scenario the problem stated to have a better understanding on it. Mostly, it only INVOLVES ADDITION AND SUBTRACTION. Because you will just  mix things up.

Refer to the attached picture for this problem.

It said that Carter sells cashews for $3/lb,hazelnuts for $2.5/lb, and peanuts for $1.75/lb . But take note that the problem only gave the mass of peanuts. However, we still have another given that would help us solve this, and that’s the total mass of the mixture (100lbs) and its price ($2.3/lb).

We can first assume that the mass of cashews as “c” and hazelnuts as “h”. But letting two different variables would mean we should also obtain 2 equations. However, we cannot have 2, because we can only provide 1. Recall that there is a total mass, therefore, the sum of all the masses of the nuts should be 100lbs. We have to have only one variable appearing on our equations.

So we let cashews as “x”. Thus we can now solve for the mass of hazelnut.

If we subtract the mass of peanuts from the total, we can get the combined mass of cashews and hazelnuts. So we have

Cashews + Hazelnuts = 100 - 50 = 50 lbs

But recall that we assumed the mass of the cashews as “x”. So subtracting again from there, we have

“50-x” as the mass of the hazelnuts.

All we have to do then is multiply each mass to its corresponding price/lb and, add them all and equate it with the product of the total mass and price/lb. So that we have,

(x)($3/lb) + (50-x)($2.5/lb) + (50)($1.75/lb) = (100)($2.3/lb)

Simplifying, we get

3x + 125 - 2.5x + 87.5 = 230

0.5x = 230 - 87.5 - 125

0.5x = 17.5

x = 35 lbs of Cashews

50 - x = 50 - 35 = 15 lbs of Hazelnuts

Therefore, in order to have a total mass of 100lbs and a price of $2.3/lb, Carter should add 35lbs of cashews and 15lbs of hazelnuts.
