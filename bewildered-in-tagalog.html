17. What is the root for the word bewildered


Answer:

bewilder (v.)

1680s, "confuse as to direction or situation," also, figuratively, "perplex, puzzle, confuse," from be- "thoroughly" + archaic wilder "lead astray, lure into the wilds," which probably is a back-formation from wilderness. An earlier word with the same sense was bewhape (early 14c.)
