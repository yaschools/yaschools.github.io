Difference:

There's a distinction between a stressor and actual stress. A stressor can be a person, place, or situation that's causing you stress. Stress is the actual response to one or a combination of those stressors.

12. what are your stressors? Give 10 stressors and explain briefly what makes them a "stressor".​


Answer:

1. Work-related stress: Deadlines, workload, conflicts with colleagues, and job insecurity can cause work-related stress.

2. Financial stress: Financial difficulties, such as debt, job loss, or unexpected expenses, can be a significant source of stress.

3. Relationship stress: Problems in personal relationships, such as conflicts with family members or friends, can be a source of stress.

4. Health-related stress: Illness, chronic pain, or disability can cause stress, as well as concern about one's health or that of loved ones.

5. Academic stress: Pressure to perform well in school, academic deadlines, and fear of failure can cause stress among students.

6. Social stress: Social pressure to fit in, bullying, or discrimination can cause stress and anxiety.

7. Trauma and grief: Traumatic experiences such as abuse, natural disasters, or the loss of a loved one can cause stress and emotional distress.

8. Personal stressors: Life events such as moving, divorce, or starting a new job can be stressful.

9. Environmental stress: Noise pollution, overcrowding, and poor air quality can cause stress.

10. Technological stress: Overuse of technology, such as social media or smartphones, can cause stress and anxiety.

Explanation:

These stressors can cause physical and emotional responses in people, including anxiety, headaches, fatigue, and difficulty concentrating. It is essential to identify and manage stressors to reduce the negative impact of stress on our health and well-being.

27. what is personal stressor?​


Explanation:

Personal stressors are events or conditions that occur in a person's life that may adversely impact on the individual's or their family's health or wellbeing. A stressor may occur directly, such as personally experiencing a serious illness, or indirectly, such as having a family member with a serious illness.
