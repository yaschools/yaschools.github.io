3. What are the different varieties of English? How different is Filipino English from Singaporean English, British English, American English, and Australian English?​


Answer:

English is a global language with many varieties spoken worldwide. The most prominent varieties of English include:

Filipino English: This is the form of English spoken in the Philippines. It has been influenced by the country's history of colonization by the United States, as well as its own languages and dialects. It has its own unique pronunciation, vocabulary, and grammar rules.

Singaporean English: This is a form of English spoken in Singapore, which has been heavily influenced by the country's multilingual and multicultural environment. It has its own unique vocabulary and grammar rules.

British English: This is the form of English spoken in the United Kingdom. It is the oldest form of English and has several regional variations, including Scottish, Welsh, and Northern Irish English.

American English: This is the form of English spoken in the United States. It is known for its distinctive pronunciation and vocabulary, which has been influenced by many different languages.

Australian English: This is the form of English spoken in Australia. It has developed its own distinct accent and vocabulary, influenced by the country's history and geography.

The differences between these varieties of English can be significant, including differences in pronunciation, vocabulary, grammar, and even spelling. However, there are also many similarities, and speakers of different varieties of English are generally able to understand each other with few problems.

28. What are the themes in Australian poetry?


Answer:

Modern Australian poetry seeks to tell Australian stories and truths with a poetic significance, so that, 'they sear into the soul and can never be untold' (Dorothy Porter). The aim for clarity is central. Following early intense debate about the meaning of modern poetry for Australia – its essence, innovation and international context – today, it is seen as cosmopolitan as well as laconic but still lucid.

The richness, strength and vitality of Australian poetry is marked by its prodigious diversity. Yet themes persist through this diversity. An abiding interest is the Australian landscape and how to relate to it – its presence and its visual power. Significantly, a great deal of modern Australian poetry has been innovative and experimental, whether consciously or not. The search for modernism itself had an urgency in its explorations.


29. the culture of communication for australians


Australians tend to put directness before diplomacy and therefore can be considered quite blunt on occasions — especially by people from those cultures in which the majority are wary of speaking plainly for fear of creating a negative emotional impact upon the people they are talking to. Directness is cherished in Australia and failure to say what you mean and mean what you say can be mistaken for evasiveness and even hypocrisy.
