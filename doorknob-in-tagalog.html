29. how does doorknob work​


Answer:

A standard doorknob lock set consists of a pair of knobs, one on each side of the door, that are connected through the door by a spindle and held together by mounting bolts. The spindle runs through the latch bolt, which is inside the door perpendicular to the spindle. The latch bolt is connected to the latch, which protrudes from the edge of the door and engages the door frame, preventing the door from opening. When either knob turns, it rotates the spindle, and the spindle retracts the latch and allows the door to open. In most cases, the spindle is spring-loaded so that the latch springs back into the closed position when the doorknob is released.
