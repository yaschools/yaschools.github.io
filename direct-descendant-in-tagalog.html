19. Analyze the following melodic directions. WRITE A: if Ascending Step tones B: if Descending Step tones C: it Ascending Skip tones D: if Descending Skip tones E: if Repeated Tones​


Answer:

1. A
2. E
3. D
4. C
5. B
6. E
7. B
8. D
9. C
10. A

Explanation:
In the major scale or any of its modes, a step will always be a movement of 1 or 2 semitones, and a skip a movement of 3 or more semitones.

Simply put, in a staff, a step means a note goes up or down a whole step.

A skip means a note skips the note a whole step above or below the previous note.

Ascending tones simply means a note goes up the staff, while for descending tones the note goes down.

Repeated tones are self-explanatory, just repeating notes.
