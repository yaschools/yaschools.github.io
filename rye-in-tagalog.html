2. THE FULL SUMMARY OF ''An catcher in the rye''


Answer:

Holden is a young man who is expelled from school for failing most of his classes. He goes to New York City for Christmas break, but his parents do not know he has been expelled. He rents a hotel, where he sees sexually charged scenes through the windows of other rooms. His loneliness causes him to seek out human interaction, which he does at the hotel's nightclub.

Holden tells his sister his fantasy of being "the catcher in the rye" The novel ends with Holden explaining that he has fallen "sick" and is expected to go to a new school in the fall. The words to the poem "Comin thro' the Rye" are taken from a poem by Robert Burns. The novel was written by James Holden and was first published in September 2013. It is one of the best-selling novels of all time.
