Here are the steps to prepare a basic vegetable salad recipe:

Choose your vegetables: Select your favorite vegetables for your salad, such as lettuce, spinach, cucumbers, tomatoes, carrots, peppers, and onions.

Wash and chop your vegetables: Rinse your vegetables under cold water to remove any dirt or debris. Then, chop them into bite-sized pieces. You can also use a food processor or a mandoline slicer to speed up the process.

Add protein: Add a source of protein to your salad, such as grilled chicken, tofu, chickpeas, or hard-boiled eggs.

Add toppings: Add toppings to your salad, such as nuts, seeds, croutons, or cheese. These will add some extra flavor and texture.

Make the dressing: You can make your own dressing by mixing olive oil, vinegar, lemon juice, mustard, honey, and some salt and pepper. You can also use store-bought dressing if you prefer.

Mix everything together: In a large bowl, mix together your vegetables, protein, and toppings. Add the dressing and mix everything together until well coated.

Serve and enjoy: Serve your salad immediately or store it in the fridge for later. You can also add some extra toppings or dressing before serving. Enjoy your delicious and healthy vegetable salad!

Answer:

Definitely YES, I certainly would still cook the vegetable recipe. Since vegetables come in such a wide array of flavourful varieties, it is much easier to find taste matches than it is for meats (since there are only a few types of meats in comparison to types of vegetables).

Some simple herbs and spices can easily make your vegetable recipe come to life. Lemongrass is delicious and versatile, it also smells awesome! Italian seasonings are pretty great too (rosemary, thyme, oregano, basil, garlic salt, pepper, parsley, sage) - you can literally purchase a pre-mixed "Italian Seasoning Blend" from the supermarket/grocery store. Mint is another lovely herb, although it can be a bit more difficult to pair with the right vegetable dishes sometimes. Cumin and cilantro are often used in spicy and in Mexican recipes, but if you're like me (who loves cilantro more than any human being should! lol), it is also a very versatile herb that can be utilized in so many endless ways because it is hard to find a "bad" match for cilantro. Cumin is a very similar spice. You do not need much, as it can add to the saltiness of a recipe. Paprika is another tasty and colourful spice that is quite versatile. I often use it on many vegetables, and sometimes on poultry too.

There are many small staples that can also act as "add-on" vegetables are another great way to cook with flavours. Some of these "add-on" vegetables include: garlic cloves, shallots, green onions/scallions, spanish onions, yellow onions, pabalano,  tomatoes, fennel, hot peppers (such as jalapenos, chilli peppers, ghost peppers - depending on who is eating the recipe and if they enjoy spicy food of course), various kinds of nuts (pine nuts, walnuts, pecans, peanuts, brazil nuts, almonds, etc. Nuts are even tastier if they are candied!).

Even some basic fruits can change a vegetable recipe for the better. Coconut and coconut milk are both excellent ingredients, especially with curries. Lemons and limes (most often their juices) are another wonderful addition to a lot of different vegetable dishes, especially curries and spicy dishes. Oranges (mandarines, tangerines, clementines, etc.) are another great fruit which can be used both as whole slices or for its juices.  Pears (green pears, red pears, asian pears) are yummy additions to certain vegetable recipes, as are apples (red, green, yellow, sweet, sour, so many kinds!).

Ginger is another great root vegetable with such a variety of uses, everything from homemade tea to an addition to a rice or vegetable dish. Brown sugar, agave syrup/nectar, honey, soy sauce - these are also all amazing options for flavouring your vegetable recipe. For example, I make this sweet glazed baby carrot recipe that's been passed down for generations in my family. It involves the use of brown sugar and honey!

Various types of vinegars (apple cider, white wine, red wine, balsamic vinegar) mixed in with some olive oil can add a HUGE boost in flavour to vegetables. You can also make homemade dressing/vinagerette for salads. I usually use apple cider vinegar, some olive oil, salt and pepper, and a couple of different spices I have on hand (differs, depending on what kind of meal we're having and its ingredients).

Noodles and rice. When in doubt, add noodles or rice. It makes for a more filling and satisfying vegetable dish, still meets the vegetable recipe requirements, and you won't be complaining of hunger in an hour or two!

30. 3 canned vegetables recipes​


Corn

The resounding favorite, these canned golden kernels taste as sweet as candy but pack in way more nutrients without any added sugar. We're ready to throw them on tacos and add to casseroles, salsas and salads. And though corn is sweet, it has a low glycemic index, meaning its carbs are slowly released into your bloodstream and are unlikely to spike blood sugar levels.

Tomatoes

With whole, diced, crushed and flavored tomatoes all available in cans, the only thing that kept them from the number one spot is the fact that you (most of the time) need to cook them. Keep canned tomatoes on hand for making quick sauces, adding to pasta dishes and for making easy soups. Lycopene, found in fresh tomatoes and even more in cooked tomatoes, helps protect your skin from damaging UV rays and may reduce your risk of some cancers.

Carrots

Maybe the most surprising on this list were these sweet little carrot discs. We would use them straight out of the can on a salad in a pinch, but they're also good for bulking up soups (whole or pureed) and adding to casseroles like chicken potpie.
