Unlike other gods from more popular religions, the ancient Greeks believed in beings that acted very much like humans in terms of their treatment of each other and of the people they rule. Here are some of their practices which would be considered abominations in the present:

1)Cannibalism was performed by Kronos against his children to prevent them from ever dethroning him in the future. He swallowed all his children but Zeus, who later set them free.


2)Poseidon suffered from Narcissism, since he specifically wants all sailors to pay respect to him before going out to sea.or suffer being disappearance underneath the waves.


3)Incest is also an act that would be frowned upon today. In Greek myth, Zeus married his sister Hera.


4)The Gods occasionally performs covetous acts such as taking maidens or laying in bed with unsuspecting women.


5)Some were involved in Decadence, such as Dionysius who participated in certain acts of immorality.


The Greek Gods were not perfect, nor did they claim to be. Aside from their powers, they were very human in their desires, weaknesses and temperament. In some ways, they represent a more honest or transparent version of our modern Deities.
