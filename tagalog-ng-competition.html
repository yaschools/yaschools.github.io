10. katangian ng perfectly competitive market​


Answer:

Ang tatlong pangunahing katangian ng perfectly competitive market ay (1) walang kumpanyang humahawak ng malaking bahagi sa merkado, (2) ang output ng industriya ay standardized, at (3) mayroong kalayaan sa pagpasok at paglabas. Ang mahusay na balanse ng merkado sa isang perpektong kumpetisyon ay kung saan ang marginal na kita ay katumbas ng marginal na gastos.

Lumilitaw ang kumpetisyon tuwing dalawa o higit pang mga partido ang nagsusumikap para sa isang pangkaraniwang layunin na hindi maibabahagi: kung saan ang nakuha ng isang tao ay ang pagkawala ng isa pa. Homogenous Products

Ang mga homogenous na produkto ay itinuturing na homogenous kapag ang mga ito ay perpektong pamalit at ang mga mamimili ay walang makitang aktwal o tunay na pagkakaiba sa pagitan ng mga produktong inaalok ng iba't ibang mga firm. Ang presyo ay ang nag-iisang pinakamahalagang sukat kasama ng kung aling mga firm ang gumagawa ng mga homogenous na produkto na nakikipagkumpitensya.

________________________________________________

Heterogenous

Ang heterogenous ay tumutukoy sa isang istraktura na may hindi magkatulad na mga bahagi o elemento, na lumilitaw na hindi regular o sari-sari. Halimbawa, ang isang dermoid cyst ay may magkakaiba na pagpapalambing sa CT. Ito ang antonym para sa homogenous, nangangahulugang isang istraktura na may katulad na mga bahagi. Ang heterogenous ay tumutukoy sa isang istrakturang pagkakaroon ng dayuhang pinagmulan.

13. Ibigsabihin ng perfectly competitive market


Answer:

In economics, specifically general equilibrium theory, a perfect market is defined by several idealizing conditions, collectively called perfect competition. In theoretical models where conditions of perfect competition hold, it has been theoretically demonstrated that a market will reach an equilibrium in which the quantity supplied for every product or service, including labor, equals the quantity demanded at the current price. This equilibrium would be a Pareto optimum.

16. halimbawa ng monopolistic competition roleplay​


Answer:

Imagine there are many clothing stores in a mall that sell similar products such as shirts, jeans, and pants, but each store has its unique style and designs. For example, one store may specialize in trendy streetwear, another in formal attire and another in vintage clothing. Consumers have a variety of options to choose from, but each store has a degree of control over the prices they charge. The competition among the stores is based on factors such as style, design, and quality, rather than just price. This is an example of monopolistic competition.

18. * Explain the difference of the followinga. Perfect Competitionb. Imperfect Competitionc. Monopolistic Competition​


Answer:Perfect vs. Imperfect Competition: An Overview

Perfect competition is a concept in microeconomics that describes a market structure controlled entirely by market forces. If and when these forces are not met, the market is said to have imperfect competition.

While no market has clearly defined perfect competition, all real-world markets are classified as imperfect. That being said, a perfect market is used as a standard by which the effectiveness and efficiency of real-world markets can be measured.

Perfect Competition

Perfect competition is an abstract concept that occurs in economics textbooks, but not in the real world. That's because it's impossible to attain in real life.

Theoretically, resources would be divided among companies equally and fairly in a market with perfect competition, and no monopoly would exist. Each company would have the same industry knowledge and they would all sell the same products. There would be plenty of buyers and sellers in this market, and demand would help set prices evenly across the board.

30. pagkakaiba at pagkakapareho ng (pcm)perfectly competitive market at (icm)imperfectly competitive market ​


Answer:

Pagkakaiba:

1. Ang Perfectly Competitive Market ay may maraming mga kalakal at mga serbisyo na magagamit sa pamilihan, habang ang Imperfectly Competitive Market ay may mas maliit na bilang ng mga kalakal at mga serbisyo.

2. Ang Perfectly Competitive Market ay may malinaw na presyo na nakikipag-ugnayan sa kalakal at serbisyo, habang ang Imperfectly Competitive Market ay may mga presyo na hindi malinaw at maaaring lumikha ng oligopoli.

3. Ang Perfectly Competitive Market ay may mga maliliit na tagagawa at konsyumer na nag-aangkin ng pag-aari, habang ang Imperfectly Competitive Market ay may mga malalaking tagagawa at konsyumer na nag-aangkin ng pag-aari.

Pagkakapareho:

1. Ang Perfectly Competitive Market at ang Imperfectly Competitive Market ay parehong hindi makontrol ang presyo ng mga kalakal at serbisyo.

2. Ang Perfectly Competitive Market at ang Imperfectly Competitive Market ay parehong naglalayong makamit ang pinakamataas na kita para sa mga tagagawa at konsyumer.
