6. write an essay about Borongan city that have atleast 150 words​


Answer:

Borongan, officially the City of Borongan (Waray: Siyudad han Borongan; Cebuano: Dakbayan sa Borongan; Tagalog: Lungsod ng Borongan), is a 4th class city and capital of the province of Eastern Samar, Philippines. According to the 2015 census, it has a population of 69,297 people.

BORONGAN CITY, Eastern Samar -- The newly-elected mayor of this city will focus on improving the city’s agriculture sector to raise the income of farmers and attain food sufficiency.

“The first thing that I will do is I will request the Bureau of Soil and Management to conduct a soil test to determine what particular crop can grow in a particular village. Then we will draft the necessary ordinance to help farmers in terms of capitalization,” said Mayor-elect Jose Ivan Dayan Agda in an interview Tuesday.

Soil testing also enables the farmers to find out the composition of the soil and helps determine how much lime and fertilizer one needs to apply and what are the suitable crops to be planted based on the soil nutrients, he said.

Agda was the city administrator under the term of outgoing mayor and Representative-elect Fe Abunda, sister of the country’s top talk show host Boy Abunda.

During the elections last May 13, he garnered 9,939 votes, slightly higher than the 9,482 votes earned by incumbent City Councilor Vic Franco.

“The government should help sustain the agriculture sector. All the social programs on agriculture should be institutionalized to have the force of law behind them,” he added.

Agda added that having sufficient food supply and better agriculture sector is good, not only for the city’s economy but also for the tourism sector.

Borongan, a fourth-class city and the capital of Eastern Samar province, is blessed with numerous wonders of nature with potentials for tourism promotion.

Aside from being known as one of the surfing havens in Eastern Samar, several beaches, caves and magnificent waterfalls are also located in the city, which are worth visiting by tourists, Agda said.

"I've been to many places here and abroad, but there's nothing that I can compare to my home. Borongan has many places, destinations that are worthy to offer, we just need to develop these places so that this will be tourist-ready," Agda said.

Agda added that aside from agriculture and tourism, he will also look into improving the city’s delivery of health services. (PNA)
