How can an ordinary student be an advocate of moral courage in school? Give a situation that exemplifies moral courage.

We would have to be careful here. There is a multitude of people and cultures that thought that they were doing the right thing, having been persuaded by powerful people. The horrors of Nazism and the pogroms of Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union are not all that far into the past. There were many people who supported, for various reasons that seemed to be moral for them, the violence and murder of those regimes. Are you sure that the morality that you aim to champion is a reflection of Objective Morality or just an opinion of Subjective Morality.?

Advocating for behavioural change is others is iffy at best. People resist being told what to do in the present culture and to make suggestions of a moral variety will only cause derision and dismissal without a hearing. The best you can do is be an example of morality and, through exemplary behaviour yourself, be a model for others to see the utilitarian benefits of moral behaviour.

In your conversations related to your beliefs which stem from your behavioural choices, needs to be conducted with gentleness and respect. Belligerence will not accomplish what you believe it will, being less than compelling in other people’s minds as the dig their heels in as an almost automatic response to any challenge of their worldview. Put aside your message and ask a lot of questions to let them talk about their point of view. Wait until the contradictions become obvious and suggest a second look, giving them space for rethinking their position. You will retain friends that way… and relationships are more important than being right. It’s a moral thing.
