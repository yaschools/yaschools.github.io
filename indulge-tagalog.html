Indulgent parenting, also called permissive, non-directive, lenient or libertarian, is characterized as having few behavioral expectations for the child. "Indulgent parenting is a style of parenting in which parents are very involved with their children but place few demands or controls on them".while the Uninvolved parenting — also called neglectful parenting, which obviously carries more negative connotations — is a style of parenting where parents don't respond to their child's needs or desires beyond the basics of food, clothing, and shelter

23. Define the following: Renaissance, Reformation, Humanism, Indulgence, and Predestination.​


Answer:

1. Renaissance

2. Reformation

3. Humanism

4. Indulgence

5. Predestination

Explanation:

1. The Renaissance was a period in European history marking the transition from the Middle Ages to modernity and covering the 15th and 16th centuries.

2. The Reformation was a major movement within Western Christianity in 16th-century Europe that posed a religious and political challenge to the Catholic Church and in particular to papal authority, arising from what were perceived to be errors, abuses, and discrepancies by the Catholic Church.

3. Humanism is a philosophical stance that emphasizes the value and agency of human beings, individually and collectively.

4. In the teaching of the Catholic Church, an indulgence is "a way to reduce the amount of punishment one has to undergo for sins".

5. Predestination, in Christian theology, is the doctrine that all events have been willed by God, usually with reference to the eventual fate of the individual soul.
