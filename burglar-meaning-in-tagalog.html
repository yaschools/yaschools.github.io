1. What is the meaning of burglars?


Burglars are individuals who illegally enter a building or premises with the intent to commit a crime, typically theft or robbery. They often break into homes, businesses, and other properties to steal valuable items or money. Burglars can cause property damage, and their actions can result in emotional and financial distress for the victims. Burglary is considered a serious crime in most countries and can result in significant legal consequences for the perpetrators if caught and convicted.

15. Identify the infinitive in each sentence and tell how it is used1. The lady lives in a condominium unit to feel safe from burglars.​


Answer:

to feel

Explanation:

Whenever you spot the term "to" followed by a verb in base form (i.e. eat, sing, play), that is a hint that you have an infinitive in the sentence.

In this sentence, the infinitive is "to feel". Thus, to guide you, the general form for an infinitive would be "to + verb". Do not be confused. For some cases, "to" is used as a preposition but in this case, it is an infinitive.

The infinitive "to feel" denotes the concept of infinity—that it is not bounded by time because in the context of the lady, she lived in a condominium to feel safe but we cannot say that this security is only for the mean time. It is unnecessarily bounded by time.

17. A burglar drops a bag of loot fr a window hotel. The bag takes 0.15s to pass the 1.6m tall window of your room as it falls toward the ground. How far.above the top of the window is the burglar who dropped the bag?


Distance between top of the window and the burglar is about 5.0 m

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Further explanation

Given:

Height of windows = h = 1.6 m

Time Taken = t = 0.15 s

Gravitational Acceleration = g = 9.8 m/s²

Asked:

Distance between top of the window and the burglar = d = ?

Solution:

Firstly, we will calculate the initial speed of the bag at the top of the windows:

[tex]h = ut + \frac{1}{2}gt^2[/tex]

[tex]1.6 = u(0.15) + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)(0.15)^2[/tex]

[tex]1.6 - 0.11025 = u(0.15)[/tex]

[tex]u = 1.48975 \div 0.15[/tex]

[tex]u = \frac{5959}{600}[/tex]

[tex]u \approx 9.93 \texttt{ m/s}[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Next , we could calculate the distance between top of the window and the burglar:

[tex]v^2 = u^2 + 2gd[/tex]

[tex]9.93^2 = 0^2 + 2(9.8)d[/tex]

[tex]d = 9.93^2 \div 19.6[/tex]

[tex]d \approx 5.0 \texttt{ m}[/tex]
