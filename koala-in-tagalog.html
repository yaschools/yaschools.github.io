KOALA: MAMMALS OR NOT?

Answer:

Yes. It is true that Koalas are mammals.

Explanation:

Koalas are marsupials, a species of mammal that gives birth to offspring that aren't fully mature. They are so distinct from other marsupials that they have been grouped together as Phascolarctidae, a family of marsupials.

Even though they appear to be friendly to people, koalas are not. They're wild creatures who, like many other wild animals, don't appreciate being in close touch with humans. Koalas are timid and solitary by nature, and they prefer to go about their business without being disturbed by humans or other animals. The claws of koalas are razor-sharp and can injure you severely. They are also capable of biting really hard.

At any zoo, koalas are a really adorable and drowsy species that can undoubtedly captivate an audience. According to a recent study that followed the travels of the Australian species in suburban Brisbane, they are also pretty smart.
