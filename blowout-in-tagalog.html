10. What is the first thing to do if you experience a tire blowout


Answer:

If you experience a tire blowout while driving, it's important to remain calm and take the following steps:

1. Ease off the gas pedal and allow your car to slow down gradually.

2. Hold the steering wheel firmly and keep your car in a straight line. Avoid braking or making any sudden movements.

3. Once you have slowed down and are in a safe location, carefully pull off the road and turn on your hazard lights.

4. If you have a spare tire, jack, and lug wrench in your vehicle, you can change the tire yourself. If you don't have these tools or don't feel comfortable changing the tire, call for roadside assistance or a tow truck to take you to a nearby tire repair shop.

It's important to note that a tire blowout can be dangerous, so it's important to react quickly and calmly to the situation.

16. why there are more tire blowouts on the highways in april than in December​


Good Day

Why there are more tire blowouts on the highways in april than in December​?

April have a higher temperature compared to December which causes blown out tire related to high temperature.

According to Charles Law

Volume is directly proportional to temperature when pressure and the amount of gas are constant. When temperature increases, volume increases.

According to Gay-Lussac's Law

Pressure is directly proportional to temperature when volume and the amount of gas are constant. When temperature increases, pressure increases.

High temperature during April causes the pressure of the tire to increases, since tire will not expand and when the container cannot hold the pressure it will blow.
