7. what is the connotation of skinny​


Here is the connotation of skinny.

First off, we must define what is being meant by connotation. Connotation (can be positive, neutral, or negative) is the meaning of a word that goes beyond its original meaning. More often than not, a connotation is associated with a negative impression.

For example, the connotation of the word unique is peculiar. According to yourdictionary website, there is a more neutral connotation of the word "unique". So, if we use peculiar, then it implies something negative.

However, when we use the word peculiar instead, then the impression that people get from this is something negative, or simply, the negative version of the word unique. There are of course other instances as well, such as courageous as the positive connotation with "confident" being the neutral connotation, and conceited as the negative connotation.

Meanwhile, the opposite of connotation is called denotation. In Linguistics, a denotation is the literal meaning of the word. So, if we happen to see a denotation, then there wouldn't be any positive or negative impression of it.

Going back to the original question, the connotation of skinny is definitely negative. We should consult the dictionary about the synonym or definition of a word before looking upon whether it is a negative connotation of a similar word. Here, we have variations of saying "skinny" as in other words: slim and slender.

Although the meaning of those three words might not differ much from each other, we get the impression that here, skinny is used as a negative connotation. Meanwhile, slim is more positive and slender is more neutral. It is useful that we learn about connotation, so that we will not hurt others accidentally when we use words that have a negative impression on it.
