5. lebel the parts of the electric circuit​


Answer:

- An electric circuit is a closed path through which electricity flows. It comprises several components that work together to facilitate the flow of electric current. The essential parts of an electric circuit include a power source, conductors, switches, and loads.

- The power source provides the energy required for the circuit to function. A battery or an electrical outlet can serve as a power source. Conductors are materials that allow electricity to flow through them easily, such as copper wires. Switches control whether electricity flows through a circuit or not by opening and closing the path of the current.

- Loads are devices that consume electrical energy in a circuit, such as light bulbs or motors. They convert electrical energy into other forms like light or motion.

- In summary, every electric circuit must have a power source to provide energy, conductors to carry it along, switches to regulate its flow and loads to utilize it effectively. Understanding these components is crucial in designing and troubleshooting circuits for various applications.

12. lebel the parts of the flower​


Answer:

Peduncle: This is the stalk of the flower.

Receptacle: It is that part of the flower to which the stalk is attached to. It is small and found at the center of the base of the flower.

Sepals: These are the small, leaf-like parts growing at the base of the petals. They form the outermost whorl of the flower. Collectively, sepals are known as the calyx. The main function of the calyx and its sepals is to protect the flower before it blossoms(in the bud stage).

Petals: This layer lies just above the sepal layer. They are often bright in color as their main function is to attract pollinators such as insects, butterflies etc to the flower.
