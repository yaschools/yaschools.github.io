3. Movie Review Introduction on the movie "Les Miserables"


Answer:

Les Misérables is a 2012 musical film based on the 1862 French novel of the same name by Victor Hugo. The film tells the story of Jean Valjean, a French peasant, and his quest for redemption after serving nineteen years in jail for stealing a loaf of bread to feed his sister's starving child. It stars Hugh Jackman, Anne Hathaway, Russell Crowe, Amanda Seyfried, Eddie Redmayne, and Helena Bonham Carter. The movie was nominated for eight Academy Awards and won three, including Best Supporting Actress for Anne Hathaway. It is a beautiful, passionate story of love and sacrifice that is sure to be remembered as an all-time great.

Answer:

"Les Misérables" is a musical film adaptation of the popular stage musical of the same name, which is based on the classic novel by Victor Hugo. The movie was released in 2012 and directed by Tom Hooper. The film features an all-star cast, including Hugh Jackman as Jean Valjean, Russell Crowe as Javert, Anne Hathaway as Fantine, Amanda Seyfried as Cosette, and Eddie Redmayne as Marius.

Plot Summary

The movie is set in 19th century France and follows the story of ex-convict Jean Valjean as he tries to start a new life after serving 19 years in prison. He is being pursued by the ruthless inspector Javert, who is determined to bring him back to prison. Along the way, Valjean encounters the struggling single mother, Fantine, and takes care of her daughter, Cosette. Valjean raises Cosette as his own and eventually becomes embroiled in the Paris student revolution of 1832.

Review

"Les Miserables" is a powerful and emotional film that brings the classic story to life in a truly magnificent way. The musical numbers are beautifully sung, especially Anne Hathaway's powerful performance of "I Dreamed a Dream". The film also boasts excellent cinematography and a strong score. The cast delivers strong performances, especially Hugh Jackman, who brings a great deal of emotion and depth to the role of Jean Valjean.

In conclusion, "Les Miserables" is a touching and memorable film that will appeal to fans of musicals and the stage production. The film is a powerful reminder of the timeless themes of love, sacrifice, and redemption, and is a must-see for anyone who loves musicals or the original novel.

24. compare and contrast a restaurant review from a movie review


COMPARISON A RESTAURANT REVIEW FROM A MOVIE REVIEW:

The restaurant review consists of foods/drinks, feasibility of dining room and amenities, service and payment method, accessibility and price. While the movie review consists of orientation, interpretation, evaluation and conclusion.

EXPLANATION:

The restaurant review:

1. Food/drink

This aspect describes not only the taste but also the many variations of the menu, matching food/drinks (no-gluten, vegetarian, suitable for children) and plating of food/beverages.

2. Feasibility of dining room and amenities

In food/beverage shops that carry order and go, without chairs, tables and plugs, this aspect alone contains a minus value. In this aspect the explanation is quite a lot, namely narrow or wide, indoor and outdoor, AC and non-AC, high, low or sitting tables, music, wifi and plugs, prayer rooms, toilets, sinks, smoking areas and parking and other aspects that make visitors feel at home.

3. Service and payment method

Aspects of the two can be combined because there are interrelationships. This includes cashier services, waitresses, cooks (as well as cashiers at lower-middle class food/beverage shops), delivery people (if any) and parking guards and payment methods (cash, credit card or barcode scan).

4. Accessibility

This aspect includes access to the location and the way in and out of the location. Seen in the first example, there is a sentence "only the layout must be improved again, because it is confusing with the flow of the road."

5. Price

This aspect describes how worth it the price is and its comparison with the taste of food, service and place. This explains the positive reviews by comparing the price and taste of the food.

The movie review:

1. Orientation

The orientation contains some general information about a film related to the Film Name, year, director, screenwriter, and main actor. Orientation also contains "background" for the reader on what will be reviewed.

2. Interpretation

Contains a summary of the story, contains a detailed description of a work (film) being reviewed, for example parts of the work, uniqueness, excellence, quality, and so on.

3. Evaluation

Contains the views of reviewers regarding the work reviewed. This is done after doing a sufficient interpretation of the results of the work. Contains an assessment related to (strengths) or parts that are less valuable (lack) of a work (films and dramas).

4. Conclusion

Contains conclusions from a review of a work (film and drama). This section also contains the author's comments whether the work is of value/quality or not to be watched/witnessed. Who deserves to watch this movie.
