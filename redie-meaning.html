10. Francesco Redi experiment


Answer:

In 1668, Francesco Redi, an Italian scientist, designed a scientific experiment to test the spontaneous creation of maggots by placing fresh meat in each of two different jars. One jar was left open; the other was covered with a cloth. Days later, the open jar contained maggots, whereas the covered jar contained no maggots. He did note that maggots were found on the exterior surface of the cloth that covered the jar. Redi successfully demonstrated that the maggots came from fly eggs and thereby helped to disprove spontaneous generation. Or so he thought.

14. what was the problem in redi's experiment?​


Francesco Redi’s famous experiments confirmed that living things originated when other organisms were already alive, as it indicated that maggots stemmed from eggs. Experiments were commissioned by putting meat in containers, some of which were open and others closed. In the open container, maggots appeared, while in the closed container they did not appear. Redi then watches the dead maggots transform into flies. Then, if a dead body or a maggot is put in a jar of meat that's also closed, no maggots will emerge. However, in the presence of live maggots, dead creatures turn into maggots. Redi concludes, on the basis of her observations, that maggots come from other organisms, namely flies that lay eggs in meat, and will grow into flies. Absolutely theory held that maggots got here from rotten meat just the same way that this new theory does.

The problem with Francisco Redi's experiment was that the research only resulted in the theory that living things came from eggs. As we know that not all living things come from eggs. For example, mammals that produce new individuals by giving birth.
