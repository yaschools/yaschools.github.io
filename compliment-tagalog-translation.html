26. if an angle is 60 less than twice its compliment,find the ratio of the angle to its compliment​


Answer: The complementary angle is 50°.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Angle = x; complementary angle = y

x = 2y - 60

Review: Angles that are complementary to each other has a sum of 90°

x + y = 90

Solve:

Let us replace the x in (x + y = 90) with (x = 2y - 60)

x + y = 90(2y - 60) + y = 90

Let us separate the y variables by moving the 60 to the right side (with 90)

2y + y = 90 + 603y = 150y = 50

If y = 50, then if we subtract it from 90, x should be 40. But just to double check:

x + y = 90  >  40 + 50 = 90x = 2y - 60  >  40 = 2(50) - 60  >  40 = 100 - 60
