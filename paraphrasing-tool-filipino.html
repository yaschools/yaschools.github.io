Paraphrasing means formulating someone else's ideas in your own words. To paraphrase a source, you have to rewrite a passage without changing the meaning of the original text. Paraphrasing is an alternative to quoting, where you copy someone's exact words and put them in quotation marks.

Example 1. She made me angry when she was rude at dinner. This paraphrase is an example of a rewording which shortens and simplifies while maintaining the same meaning.

13. Paraphrasing must be done properly to avoid plagiarism. What is paraphrasing?


Paraphrasing is an art of copying a phrase or a sentence without exactly copying the phrase or the sentence itself. This can be done by rearranging the sentence structure and also substituting words with its synonyms, thus altering the appearance of the sentence without altering the meaning of it.

For example:
"Playing video games for a long time may affect your attitude and character"
You can paraphrase it to:
"Gaming for extended periods of time might change one's values and personality"
