20. 31) Which sentence is written using formal language? A) Erin has a crucial appointment early Monday morning. B) That was a real bogus tale Charlie told the teacher. C) Let me have a sneak-peak or I am simply gonna explode. D) I do not think Tim will be here today; he's playing hookie.


The sentence using a formal language is:  A) Erin has a crucial appointment early Monday morning.

What is a formal language?

A formal language is used in certain situations that are serious. Contractions such as can't which means can not should not be used in formal sentences. Formal vocabularies are also used. In the given answer, the word crucial is used (formal) instead of important. The word appointment is used instead of meeting. Formal sentences are basically used in writing while informal sentences are usually used in speaking. There are time however that we write in an informal language (writing an informal essay) and we speak in a formal language ( speeches in a formal meeting).

Comparing formal and informal language

Formal                                  

1. Formal language doesn't use contractions.

She has decided to implement the new rules.

(informal: She's decided to apply the new rules.)

2. formal language uses specific vocabularies

The whole team worked on this endeavor for 1 whole year.

(informal: The whole team tried their best for a year.)

3. Formal language uses relative pronoun

The lady whom I met during the party is interested in taking a new venture.

(informal: The lady I met in the party is interested in taking a new venture.)

4.  Certain modal verbs are used in a forma language:

Might I suggest you create new proposal for this project?

(Informal: I suggest you create a new proposal for this project.)

29. how to handle a bogus buyer ​


Answer:

Ask for relevant buyer information. Ask for official proof of identity as part of your purchase process, especially when selling expensive merchandise. 30. What methods can you employ to distinguish between bogus and authentic material on the internet?​


Answer:

Throughout the 2016 U.S. election cycle, politicians and the media would frequently use the term “fake news.” Originally intended to describe the type of falsified, non-factual news content that was proliferating via social media, the term has taken on another meaning as President Donald Trump and other politicians shout “fake news” to attack and discredit reporting from media organizations running news stories the politicians don’t like, even though the reporting is factual and verified.It is becoming increasingly difficult for students and other segments of the population to know how to identify fake news. A typical example of where fake news originates is a bogus website that looks similar to an actual news outlet, but was created by an individual or organization, often operating outside the U.S., to promote a particular opinion or political philosophy. The entity publishes articles on the site that contain false information in an attempt to attack opponents and promote their views in the guise of news reporting. The articles are shared among targeted users on social media as a way to spread the misinformation.
