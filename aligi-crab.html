This type of association is called as Mutualism.

Mutualism is a symbiontic association between organisms of different species in such a way that both are benefitted.

Here, Spider crabs are benefitted so that, it is comoflauged and thus preventing further predation. Algae is also benefitted as it gets a good place to live.

They are mutually benefitted and hence, mutualism.

Answer:

Mutualism

Explanation:

"The algae are provided with a good place to live while the crab receives camouflage."

This type of symbiotic relationship is called mutualism. Mutualism exists when two organisms in an ecosystem 'work together' or 'do actions that benefits each other'. In this case, the benefits that each received can easily be noticed.

Benefits each of the organisms received:

- The algae on the back of the spider crab benefits in a way that it has a good place to live.

- The spider crab is camouflaged due to the algae living on its back and is most likely to be hidden from its predators.

Other examples of mutualism:

- Plants and plants pollinators/agents of pollination (such as bee)

- Oxpeckers and rhinoceroes

- Remora fishes and sharks

Mode of Reproduction

Crabs reproduce sexually, with males and females mating to produce offspring. Males use their claws to grab and hold the female during mating. After mating, the female will lay her eggs in a mass outside her body. Depending on the species, females can lay thousands of eggs in a single clutch.

What are Crabs?

Crabs are a type of decapod crustacean found in oceans and freshwater habitats around the world. They have an oval-shaped body with two large claws that they use to capture prey, defend themselves, and communicate. Crabs have a hard exoskeleton and six pairs of legs, which are used for walking and swimming. They can range in size from a few millimeters to over one meter in width.

Proliferation of Crabs?

Crabs have a variety of methods for propagating their species. Some crabs, such as hermit crabs, are hermaphroditic and can reproduce without the presence of other crabs. Other crabs, such as mud crabs, rely on a strong population to survive and reproduce. These crabs will gather in large groups to mate.
