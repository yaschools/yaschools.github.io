What is a pronoun?

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

A pronoun is a word that is used instead of a noun or noun phrase. Pronouns refer to either a noun that has already been mentioned or to a noun that does not need to be named specifically.

The most common pronouns are the personal pronouns, which refer to the person or people speaking or writing (first person), the person or people being spoken to (second person), or other people or things (third person). Like nouns, personal pronouns can function as either the subject of a verb or the object of a verb or preposition: "She likes him, but he loves her." What Is a Pronoun?

Pronouns make up a small subcategory of nouns. The distinguishing characteristic of pronouns is that they can be substituted for other nouns. For instance, if you’re telling a story about your sister Sarah, the story will begin to sound repetitive if you keep repeating “Sarah” over and over again.

25. Give the meaning, function, and usage of the following Pronouns.1. Personal Pronoun2. Possessive Pronoun3. Reflective Pronoun4. Intensive Pronoun5. Demonstrative Pronoun6. Indefinite Pronoun7. Interrogative Pronoun8. Relative Pronoun9. Reciprocal Pronoun10. PRONOUN​


Answer:

Personal Prounon- Personal pronouns are pronouns that are associated with number, grammatical or natural gender, case, and formality.

Possesive Prounon- Possesive Pronoun is a form of word that shows possesion or ownership.

Reflective Pronoun- To put it for short, Reflexive Pronoun will end in ‑self or ‑selves, and refer to a previously named noun or pronoun.

Ex.  Themselves, yourselves, etc.

Intensive Pronoun- Intensive pronoun is almost identical to a reflexive pronoun.

Ex. Myself, Yourself, etc.

Demonstrative Pronoun- Demonstartive Pronouns that point to specific things: this, that, these, and those.

Ex. Those are the apples you were looking for.

Indefinite Pronoun- An indefinite pronoun is a pronoun that refers to non-specific beings, objects, or places.

Ex. anyone, everyone, no one, someone, anybody, everybody, nobody.

Interrogative Pronoun- Interrogative pronouns are used to ask questions.

Ex. Who, What, etc.

Relative Pronoun- A relative pronoun is a pronoun that marks a relative clause.

Ex. The kid who got suspended just got back

Reciprocal Pronoun- A reciprocal pronoun is a pronoun that indicates a reciprocal relationship.

Ex. Milly and Seal shared their bracelets with everyone.

Pronoun- A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun.

Ex. A pronoun (I, me, he, she, herself, etc.)
