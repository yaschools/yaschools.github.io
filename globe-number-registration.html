5. What is the number of 1-degree longitude, or latitude lines that can be drawn on a globe?


Answer:

360 degrees

Explanation:

Longitude is measured in degrees (+180° eastward and 180° westward). The Prime Meridian is a line that divides the earth into the Eastern and Western Hemispheres at a longitude of 0 degrees.

Longitude lines that travel from the north pole to the south pole form full circles, covering 360 degrees, which is why there are 360 longitudes. Latitudes indicate how distant a position is from the equator and are expressed by a positive value when facing the North Pole and a negative value when facing the South Pole.

what is the maximum number of 1 degree longitude or latitude lines that can be drawn on a globe

In total there can be 360 longitudinal degrees possible.

[tex]\huge{\mid{\underline{\overline{\tt Explanation:}}\mid}}[/tex]

The longitude and latitude lines are imaginary lines that have been set by the humans so that they have better orientation and coordination on the planet. The longitude lines run from north to south and they measure the distance of a place from the prime longitude line, or rather prime meridian, Greenwich. The latitude lines run from east to west and they measure the distance from the Equator.

There are 180 degrees of longitude on each the east and west, meaning that if lines are set at 1 degree there can be a maximum of 360. There are 90 degrees of latitude on each north and south, meaning that if lines are set at 1 degree there can a maximum of 180.
