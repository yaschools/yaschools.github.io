8. Food Preference Survey. A school conducts a survey of their students’ food preference during lunch and gets the following information: i. All students prefer to have at least one viand. ii. 70% of the students prefer to have more than one viand iii. 20% of the students prefer to have fish as viand. iv. Of those students who prefer to have more than one viand, 15% prefer fish as viand. Find the probability that a randomly selected student prefers to have exactly one viand and that is fish. Show your solution.


Answer:

9.5%

Step-by-step explanation:

In order to determine the probability of a student having only one viand that is fish, we may approach this problem by first determining how many of the students with more than one viand prefer fish and subtract it those who prefer fish as a viand.

People with more than one viand that prefer fish

P (more than one viand) * P (more than one viand that prefer fish)

70% * 15% = 0.70 * 0.15 = 0.105

People with only one viand that is fish

P(prefer fish as a viand) - P (more than one viand that prefer fish)

20% - 0.105 = 0.20 - 0.105 = 0.095

The probability that a randomly selected student prefers only one viand that is fish is 0.095 or 9.5%.
