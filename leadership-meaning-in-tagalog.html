24. Leadership's not a title. It's a behavior. Live it. What does it mean?​


We all have the ability to be effective an leaders, but the majority of us prefer to be followers. Leaders are not, contrary to popular belief, persons who are followed by large crowds. Leaders are individuals who go their own path without regard for whether or not others are following them. "Leadership traits" are those that enable someone to function without followers, rather than those that enable them to recruit them. Courage, perseverance, patience, humor, adaptability, ingenuity, a sharp sense of reality, and the capacity to retain a calm and clear head, even when things are going horribly, are just a few of them. In summary, true leaders do not turn people into followers, but into other leaders. Before you can be a leader, you must first grow as a person. When you're a leader, it's all about helping people flourish.

27. differentiate the meaning of leader and leadership.​


Answer:

Leadership, as opposed to a leader, is not classified by a person, but rather as a process. Leadership can be defined as the process of influencing a group, team, or organization to help reach its goal. Leadership can therefore be exercised by anyone in the group and not necessarily by the formal leader.
