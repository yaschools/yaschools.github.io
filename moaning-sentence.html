23. (11) And still, between each mighty stroke there is a long low moaning heard; and ever the moaning ends with a sound of sobbing and of complaining, as though a weeping woman should murmur, “Hiai!” Which words are closely related in the statement? a. between, low, end, stroke, ever b. stroke, long, low, murmur, sound c. moaning, sobbing, complaining, weeping, murmuring


Answer:

C. Moaning, sobbing, complaining, weeping, murmuring

Explanation:

And still, between each mighty stroke there is a long low moaning heard; and ever the moaning ends with a sound of sobbing and of complaining, as though a weeping woman should murmur, “Hiai!” Which words are closely related in the statement?

a. between, low, end, stroke, ever

b. stroke, long, low, murmur, sound

c. moaning, sobbing, complaining, weeping, murmuring

In order to know the answer, we first need to find the meaning of each words and see if they can be related to each other.

A. Between, low, end, stroke, ever

Between means that it is covered from side to side with something. (Ex. Between two countries lie a very deep river). Low means that it is little in quantity (Ex. My cellphone is dying, it has a low battery). End is synonymous to last (Ex. Michael was at the end of the line). Stroke is the past tense of strike, meaning to hit (Ex. Men shouldn't strike women). Ever is used to express time (Ex. Ever since I tasted the ice cream, I always buy it in the supermarket)

Among these words, we can notice that they aren't closely related with each other.

B. Stroke, long, low, murmur, sound

Stroke is the past tense of strike, meaning to hit (Ex. Men shouldn't strike women). Long and low aren't related with each other. Murmur is a soft sound, hence, murmur and sound can be related with each other.

Among these words, only murmur and sound are related to each other. Therefore, the words aren't closely related with each other.

C. Moaning, sobbing, complaining, weeping, murmuring

Moaning, sobbing, complaining, weeping, and murmuring are different types of sound produced by humans. Moaning expresses pleasure, sobbing for pain, complaining for exasperation or frustration, weeping for pain as well, murmuring is produced with soft sound. Therefore, the words are closely related with each other.
