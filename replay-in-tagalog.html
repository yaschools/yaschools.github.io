16. A rally which is not scored and being replayed. (tennis)


While there are no fouls in tennis, in a rally a player will not be scored if the ball hits outside of the line. If it is unclear whether it is in or out, a replay may be needed for further checking. Another no-score is LET, where the server hits the net and the ball falls inside his service box. He will just do a re-serve.

20. A rally which is not scored and being replayed. (tennis)


A rally starts from the service. There are numerous reasons why a rally needs to be replayed in order to determine the score. One is LET. This means that the ball served hit the net but bounced back to the service box. This is not a fault and the server can do his re-serve. No score is given here. The score will only be given to the other player if the ball hit the net and fell on the opponent’s side (fault). Another situation where a replay is needed is deciding whether it is in or out with a near-outer line hit.
