25. it determines a landmark​


Answer:

In modern usage, a landmark includes anything that is easily recognizable, such as a monument, building, or other structure. In American English. it is the main term used to designate places that might be of interest to tourists due to notable physical features or historical significance.

Explanation:

Each of the levels of government with the authority to designate landmarks determines its own criteria for designation. Typically, in order to qualify for designation, a building or other property must be at least 50 years old, must retain a high degree of integrity, and must have some level of historic significance.
