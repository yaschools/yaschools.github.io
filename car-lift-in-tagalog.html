3. Jose owns a 1500-lb car and it needs to visit the mechanic. The car was lifted by a manually operated hydraulic lift, and the area of the shaft of the lift is 50cm^2; the area of the piston that forces liquid into the system is 4cm^2. What force must be exerted on this piston to lift the car?​


Pascal's law was stated by a French philosopher and scientist, Blaise Pascal (1623-1662), saying:

“If external pressure is applied to a closed system, the pressure at any point in the fluid will increase proportion to the external pressure applied.”

Pascal's law describes that any increase in pressure on the surface of a fluid must be transmitted in all directions of the fluid. Pascal's law can only be applied to fluids, generally liquids.

Pascal's law in a closed system can be written as follows.

[tex]P_{1} = P_{2}[/tex]

As we already know that pressure is a force divided by the cross-sectional area (P = F/A), then the above equation can be rewritten as follows:

[tex]\frac{F_{1} }{A_{1} } = \frac{F_{2} }{A_{2} }[/tex]

Based on the questions above, the data are given:

Mass of the car (m) = 1,500 lb = 680.3886 kg (1 lb equal to 0.453592 kg)

The area of the piston for a lifted car ([tex]A_{1}[/tex]) =  [tex]50 cm^{2}[/tex]

The area of the piston that forces liquid into the system ([tex]A_{2}[/tex]) = [tex]4 cm^{2}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: find the car’s weight.

[tex]F_{1} = m . g[/tex]

[tex]F_{1} = (680.3886) . (9.8)[/tex]

[tex]F_{1} = 6,667.80828 N[/tex]

Step 2: find the force that must be exerted on this piston to lift the car.

[tex]\frac{F_{1} }{A_{1} } = \frac{F_{2} }{A_{2} }[/tex]

[tex]\frac{6,667.80828}{50} = \frac{F_{2} }{4}[/tex]

[tex]F_{2} = \frac{(6,667.80828) . 4}{50}[/tex]

[tex]F_{2} = 533.425 N[/tex]

So, the force that must be exerted on this piston to lift 1,500 lb of Jose’s car is 533.425 N.

8. How much potential energy does a car gain if a crane lifts the car with a mass of 1500kg 20m up


Answer:

To calculate the potential energy gained by the car when lifted by the crane, we can use the formula:

PE = m * g * h

where PE is the potential energy gained, m is the mass of the car, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and h is the height that the car is lifted.

Substituting the given values, we get:

PE = 1500 kg * 9.81 m/s^2 * 20 m

= 294,300 J

Therefore, the potential energy gained by the car when lifted 20m up by the crane is 294,300 J.

19. How will you compare the force needed to transport the car by lifting and by using an inclined plane?


Answer:

Moving an object up an inclined plane requires less force than lifting it straight up, at a cost of an increase in the distance moved. The mechanical advantage of an inclined plane, the factor by which the force is reduced, is equal to the ratio of the length of the sloped surface to the height it spans.


20. The piston of a hydraulic automobile lift is 0.30 m in diameter. what gauge pressure, in pascals, is required to lift a car with a mass of 1200 kg? also express this pressure in atmospheres


GAUGE PRESSURE

The piston of a hydraulic automobile lift is 0.3 meters in diameter. The gauge pressure, in Pascal, need to lift a car with a mass of 1200 kilograms is 166.4 KPa or 1.64 atm

How did we get the answer?

We are going to use the formula where force is directly proportional to the product pressure and area.

F= PA

First, let us calculate the pressure in Pascal.

P = [tex]\frac{1200(9.81)}{\frac{\pi}{4}(0.3)^2 }[/tex] = 166.4 KPa

Converting from Pa to atm:

Note: 1 atm is equal to 101,325 Pa

166.4 KPa x [tex]\frac{1 atm}{101,325Pa}[/tex] = 1.64 atm
