23. Complete the sentence with the correct form of the verb in parentheses. If the worst possible thing had happened to you, how you (feel)?


“IF Salad”

The purpose of this task is to see if the students were able to apply their learning about the tenses of verbs and its application in if statements. If statements has a corresponding effect in the next part of the sentence, like “If I run in the field, I will improve my running skill” and so on. It is best to first understand the simple tenses of verbs before moving on to perfect tenses.

Simple Tense:

Simple Present- He is staying in their vacation house near the beach. Simple Past- He stayed in their vacation house near the beach. Simple Future- He will stay in their vacation house near the beach.

In simple tenses mostly the verb will have its transition and not add an auxiliary verb for every tense except for the future tense. It is also important to know your verbs before using them with ease.

The root word is “stay”:

In present tense it is “staying” (root word + -ing) In past tense it is “stayed” (root word + -ed) In future tense it is “will stay” (root word + auxiliary verb “will”)

Perfect Tense:

Present Perfect- I had drunk coffee, since I came here this morning. (had/has + past participle) Past Perfect- She had arrived after the program. (had/have + past participle) Future Perfect- He will have sung that same song till next week. (will/shall + have + past participle)

The present perfect indicates an action that began in the past. The past perfect indicates an action that began in the past and was done in the past. The future perfect indicates an action that will be done in the long future.

If the worst possible thing had happened to you, how you (feel)?

Answer: If the worst possible thing had happened to you, how will you feel?

The persona is futuristically speaking or asking a specific thing that is followed up by a question, therefore we use future tense.
