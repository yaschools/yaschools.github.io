1. Chemical used for insecticide, fungicide, and fumigant?​


Answer:

The chemicals used for insecticide include hydrogen cyanide, naphthalene, nicotine, and methyl bromide which are mainly used for killing insect pests by stored products or for fumigating nursery stock.

Chemicals used for fungicides are inorganic compounds based on sulfur or metal ions such as copper, tin, cadmium and mercury that are toxic to fungi. Copper and sulfur are also widely used. Most other fungicides used today are organic compounds and thus contain carbon.

The chemicals used for fumigant are methyl bromide and phosphine used for the treatment of stored grain and similar commodities. They are also used to treat products stored in the nursery or stock including hydrogen cyanide, naphthalene, nicotine, and methyl bromide.

3. ano ang ibig sabihin ng fumigation?


Fumigation is a method of pest control that completely fills an area with gaseous pesticides—or fumigants—to suffocate or poison the pests within. It is used to control pests in buildings (structural fumigation), soil, grain, and produce, and is also used during processing of goods to be imported or exported to prevent transfer of exotic organisms. IT IS USED TO CONTROL PEST IN BUILDING

Subject Health

Answer:

Quarantine

The correct answer among the listed series of words is Quarantine. Quarantine does NOT belong to the group of disinfection, sterilization  and fumigation.

Explanation:

Quarantine is the only control measure directed at the host by limiting the movement of an individual to prevent the spread of the contagious disease while disinfection, sterilization  and fumigation are measures to kill or destroy the pathogen or agent.
