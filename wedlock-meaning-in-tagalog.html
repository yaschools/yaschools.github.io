15. (9) Gold and brass will never meet in wedlock, silver, and iron never will embrace, until the flesh of a maiden be melted in the crucible; until the blood of a virgin be mixed with the metals in their fusion.” What does this mean? a. The blood of a virgin maiden is made up of gold, brass, silver, and iron. b. Gold, brass, silver, and iron will fuse when mixed with the blood of a virgin c. Gold, brass, silver, and iron will never be fused together by a virgin maiden.


Answer:

B. Gold, brass, silver, and iron when mixed with the blood of a virgin

Explanation:

Reading the entire paragraph, notice the use of transition word until. The use of the word until signifies a condition that is to yet to be met in order to fulfill the precedence.

Transition words, (or transitional words) are words that are used in linking and connecting two or more ideas in a sentence or in a paragraph. There are different types of transition words in the English language. Some are used to express agreement or oppostition, while others are used to know the cause and effect of an idea or statement. Sometimes, it is used to convey time and progress as well.

In the paragraph, we can analyze it one-by-one and we'll observe that the transition word used is until, which is of sequence or time.

First, "gold and brass will never meet in wedlock [until the flesh of a maiden be melted in the crucible; until the blood of a virgin be mixed with the metals in their fusion]". We'll see here that until signals the start of the conditions. It means that gold and brass can never be mixed until the flesh [and blood] of the maiden be included with them.

Second, "silver, and iron never will embrace [until the flesh of a maiden be melted in the crucible; until the blood of a virgin be mixed with the metals in their fusion]". Similar to the first one, until signals the start of the conditions.

Lastly, we will combine the first and second sentences in order to understand and interpret the whole paragraph.

"Gold and brass will never meet in wedlock, silver, and iron never will embrace, until the flesh of a maiden be melted in the crucible; until the blood of a virgin be mixed with the metals in their fusion."

From it, we can safely deduce that gold, brass, silver, and iron, will fuse when mixed with the blood of a virgin.
