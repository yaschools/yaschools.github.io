In music, metre, usually spelled Meter, is a rhythmic pattern made up of constant measures or bars formed by grouping basic temporal units, called beats. In Western notation, each measure is separated from the ones next to it by bar lines. Meter is a sequence of strong and poorly beats that is repeated over and over. Meters are distinguished by the number of beats between one strong beat and the next.

Simple, compound and complicated meters are the three types of meters. All rhythmic patterns in Western music may be explained by these three types. The subdivision of beats explain each of the meter groups. The period associated with that meter is determined by the number of beats per measure.
