2. What are the 3 dimensions of Agape love?


Answer:

There are Three (3) Dimensions of the Agape of Love and this are the following:

There is man’s religious responsibility to God

As a higher being and as the creator of all things on earth, we as God's creation must have the freewill to show reverence to him. It is our duty and obligation to take part in actions needed to prove our faithfulness and gratefulness to our creator.

There is one’s moral obligation

Created equally in the image and likeness of God, we are therefore beings of dignity. We deserve to treat each other equally and with equal dignity. As such, we need to be moral and responsible in our decisions and actions in life.

There is personal obligation

As a dignified being, we have self-worth. This is given importance by loving our selves and enhancing our spirituality.

To sum this 3 pillars, the man's earthly service is to love god, love thy neighbor and love one's self.

11. ano ibig sabihin ng agape love​


Answer:

Ang salitang Griyegong agape ay laging isinasalin sa salitang "pag-ibig" sa Bagong Tipan. Ano ang ipinagkaiba ng pag-ibig na "agape" sa ibang uri ng pag-ibig? Ang esensya ng pag-ibig na agape ay magandang hangarin, walang kundisyon, at sinasadyang pagkalugod sa isang iniibig. Hindi ginamit ang salitang agape sa Bagong Tipan para tukuyin ang romantiko o sekswal na pag-ibig. Hindi rin ito tumutukoy sa malapit na pagkakaibigan o pag-ibig sa kapatid na siyang pinaggagamitan ng salitang Grieyegong philia. Kinapapalooban ang pag-ibig na agape ng katapatan, pagtatalaga, at ng gawang ayon sa kagustuhan. Naiiba ito sa ibang uri ng pag-ibig dahil sa mataas na moral na kalikasan at malakas na karakter nito. Eros, Philia, and Agape are three different types of love that were identified by the ancient Greeks .

Eros - This type of love is passionate and intense. It is often associated with physical attraction and desire . An example of Eros love is a romantic relationship between two people.

Philia - This type of love is based on friendship and mutual respect. It is often associated with loyalty and trust . An example of Philia love is a strong bond between two friends.

Agape - This type of love is selfless and unconditional. It is often associated with compassion and charity . An example of Agape love is a mother's love for her child.

27. Which of following is the purest form of love?A. AgapeB. ErosC. PragmaD. Storge​


Answer:

A. AGAPE

Explanation:

Agape, Greek agapē, in the New Testament, the fatherly love of God for humans, as well as the human reciprocal love for God. In Scripture, the transcendent agape love is the highest form of love and is contrasted with eros, or erotic love, and philia, or brotherly love.

Eros (/ˈɪərɒs/ or /ˈɛrɒs/; Ancient Greek: ἔρως érōs "love" or "desire") is a concept in ancient Greek philosophy referring to sensual or passionate love, from which the term erotic is derived. Eros has also been used in philosophy and psychology in a much wider sense, almost as an equivalent to "life energy".

Pragma is a kind of practical love founded on reason or duty and one's longer-term interests. Sexual attraction takes a back seat in favour of personal qualities and compatibilities, shared goals, and "making it work". In the days of arranged marriages, pragma must have been very common.

Storge (/ˈstɔːrɡi/, from the Ancient Greek word στοργή storgē) or familial love refers to natural or instinctual affection, such as the love of a parent towards offspring and vice versa. In social psychology, another term for love between good friends is philia.
