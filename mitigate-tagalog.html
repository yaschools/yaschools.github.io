11. Disaster Mitigation? ​


Answer:

Disaster mitigation measures are those that eliminate or reduce the impacts and risks of hazards through proactive measures taken before an emergency or disaster occurs.

Examples of mitigation strategies include: hazard specific control activities such as flood levees or bushfire mitigation strategies. design improvements to infrastructure or services. land use planning and design decisions that avoid developments and community infrastructure in areas prone to hazards.

18. Preparedness and Mitigation​


(Bullock, 2013) While, preparedness is the steps taken to deal with an event once it occurs, mitigation is the steps taken to eliminate or reduce the risks created by hazards. Previously, mitigation factors were discussed for wildfire, tornado, and tsunami.

Answer:

Preparedness refers to being prepared to respond to a disaster, whereas mitigation also seeks to either prevent the disaster or lessen its impact. Preparedness can only be enacted prior to a disaster, whereas mitigation can be implemented before a disaster starts or during the disaster.

27. ANO ANG DISASTER RISK MITIGATION TAGALOG


Konsepto ng Disaster Mitigation

Ang Disaster Mitigation ay isang konseptong tumutukoy sa tuluyang pagtanggal o pagbaba sa epekto at panganib sa pamamagitan ng mga karampatang pamamaraan. Ito ay kinakailangang maipatupad bago pa man dumating ang isang sakuna o panganib upang tuluyang maiwasan ang paglala ng sitwasyon. Isa sa mga halimbawang naipatupad na naging maganda ang epekto ay ang ginawang konsepto ng Canada na tinatawag na Red River Floodway. Ang proyektong ito ay nilikha upang maiwasan ang malaking pinsalang inaasahan sa tuwing tatama ang malakas na pag-ulan na nagdudulot ng matinding pagbaha sa mga lugar sa paligid nito.
