Quotation

A quotation is the repetition of a sentence, phrase, or passage from a speech or text that has been said or written by someone. It is the representation of an utterance (that is, something that a speaker actually said) that is introduced by a quotative marker, such as a verb of saying, in oral speech.

Extensive quotations

Place quotations longer than four lines of prose or three lines of verse in a standing block of text and omit quotation marks. Begin the quotation on a new line, with the entire quotation indented 1/2 inch from the left margin and double-spaced.

27. paki explain po salamat 1.)PROPER CITATION EMPLOYS EFFECTIVE USE OF QUOTATION MARKS. 2.)QUOTATION MARKS ARE ALSO USED TO HIGHLIGHT DIALOGS EXAMPLE: ALLEN SAID, "GUESS WHAT? I KNOW TO USE QUOTATION MARKS PROPERLY!"​


The first statement "PROPER CITATION EMPLOYS EFFECTIVE USE OF QUOTATION MARKS" means that when you are citing or referencing information from another source in your writing, it's important to use quotation marks effectively. Quotation marks should be used to indicate the exact words that someone else has said or written, and they should be placed around the quoted text to show that it is not your original writing. This helps to avoid plagiarism and gives credit to the original source.

The second statement "QUOTATION MARKS ARE ALSO USED TO HIGHLIGHT DIALOGS" means that quotation marks can also be used to indicate when someone is speaking in a dialogue or conversation. In the example provided, Allen is speaking and his exact words are enclosed in quotation marks to show that they are his words and not the writer's. This is a common use of quotation marks in writing, especially in fiction or creative writing.

Answer:

1.Proper citation employs effective use of quotation marks:

Quotation marks are an essential tool in proper citation, which is the act of giving credit to the original source of information or ideas that you use in your work. When quoting directly from a source, you should always enclose the quoted material in quotation marks. This helps to clearly indicate that the words or ideas you are presenting are not your own, but rather come from another author or source.

2.Quotation marks are also used to highlight dialogues:

Aside from their use in citation, quotation marks are also commonly used to highlight dialogue in written works. When a character in a story or play speaks, their words are often enclosed in quotation marks to distinguish them from the narrative text. For example, in the sentence "Allen said, 'Guess what? I know how to use quotation marks properly!'", the quotation marks indicate that the words "Guess what? I know how to use quotation marks properly!" are being spoken by the character Allen, rather than being part of the narrative text.
