8. What do "protection and empowerment" mean for achieving Human Security? Please explain.​


The two basic elements for reaching the aim of human security are protection and empowerment of individuals.

The CHS defines protection as "measures put in place by nations, international organizations, non-governmental organizations, and the business sector to protect individuals from threats." It refers to the rules, practices, and institutions that must be in place to safeguard individuals against serious dangers.The term "protection" indicates a "top-down" strategy. It acknowledges that humans are subjected to threats over which they have no influence. Nevertheless, regional and international institutions, civilized society and non-governmental actors, as well as the corporate sector also play critical roles in protecting people from threats. The CHS defines empowerment as "ways that help people to improve their resilience to tough conditions."

Empowerment necessitates a "bottom-up" strategy. Its goal is to empower individuals and groups to make educated decisions and act according to their own interests. Empowering individuals not only helps them to reach their fullest potential, and moreover allows them to identify and engage in solutions to secure social protection for themselves and others.

Protection and empowerment, as made quite clear by the CHS, are constantly strengthening and cannot be dealt separately: "these are both essential in virtually all instances of human insecurity, albeit their shape and balance may vary greatly throughout contexts."

17. what is the meaning of political empowerment.​


Political empowerment refers to the process of transferring various elements of power (resources, capabilities, and positions) to those who do not have it. Political empowerment requires inclusion in democratic decision-making processes.

Answer:

Political empowerment refers to the process of transferring various elements of power (resources, capabilities, and positions) to those who do not have it. Political empowerment requires inclusion in democratic decision-making processes.

Description

A common understanding of political empowerment (the transfer of power to those who do not have it) embraces the definition of power in distributive terms. Following this understanding, political empowerment must include giving access to various political offices, including positions in the government, to the members of disadvantaged populations. The literature on political empowerment attempts to assess the impact of access to political offices on the overall well-being of minorities.
