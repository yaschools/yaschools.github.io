4. what is national planning policy of india?ano anu ung mga policies nya at objectives? pls explain in tagalog​


Answer:

Ang National Planning Policy (NPP) ng India ay isang pangmatagalang plano ng pag-unlad na naglalayong mapabuti ang ekonomiya ng bansa, mapababa ang kahirapan at mapalawak ang kaunlaran sa pamayanan.

Narito ang ilan sa mga pangunahing layunin at polisiya ng NPP:

1. Pagpapalawak ng sektor ng agrikultura - layunin nito na mapalawak ang produksyon ng agrikultura at mapalakas ang kabuhayan ng mga magsasaka.

2. Pagpapalakas ng mga industriya sa bansa - layunin nito na mapabuti ang produksyon ng mga lokal na industriya at mapataas ang kita ng bansa sa pamamagitan ng pag-export ng mga produkto.

3. Pagpapalawak ng sektor ng serbisyo - layunin nito na mapabuti ang serbisyo sa mga sektor ng edukasyon, kalusugan, at iba pang sektor ng serbisyo.

4. Pagkakaroon ng malawakang imprastraktura - layunin nito na mapabuti ang mga imprastraktura tulad ng mga kalsada, tulay, airports, at iba pang mga pasilidad upang mapabuti ang konektibidad ng mga pamayanan.

5. Pagpapalawak ng sektor ng teknolohiya - layunin nito na mapalakas ang mga teknolohikal na kasanayan upang magkaroon ng mas maraming oportunidad sa trabaho at mapataas ang produksyon ng mga lokal na industriya.

6. Pagpapalakas ng sektor ng turismo - layunin nito na mapabuti ang turismo sa bansa upang mapataas ang kita ng bansa at magkaroon ng mas maraming oportunidad sa trabaho sa sektor ng turismo.

Explanation:

Ang NPP ay naglalayong magtaguyod ng sustainable at inclusive na pag-unlad para sa bansa sa pamamagitan ng pagtutulungan ng mga sektor ng pamahalaan, pribadong sektor at mga lokal na komunidad.

8. What is the STATE OF THE NATION ADDRESS of Elpidio Quirino in Tagalog



MR. PRESIDENT, MR. SPEAKER, MEMBERS OF THE CONGRESS:

Circumstances beyond my control make me forego the pleasure of appearing in person to deliver this message following a beautiful tradition established many years ago and adhered to most punctiliously by my predecessors in office. Although during your short period of special session coincident with my inauguration I had an opportunity to thank you informally for your prompt response on the measures that I recommended, I reiterate my deep appreciation for your fine spirit of cooperation from the start of this administration. I wish to assure you of the same cooperation in your legislative labors.

It give me great satisfaction to be able to report to you that, in spite of the unfavorable conditions under which we labored during the past year due to the intense and bitter political controversies which raged throughout the nation, we have achieved notable progress in our constructive endeavors.

There is relative peace and order in the entire country today. The sporadic activities of outlaws in isolated and widely dispersed localities are under control. There is no organized movement of any consequence that can be considered a threat to the stability of our Government. The recent attempt to challenge the authority of this Government by a few disgruntled elements has definitely ended with the surrender and offer of cooperation of these elements, and the amnesty granted by the Government. We can now say that, generally, our farms and our countryside are only awaiting the hand that would till the soil to contribute in proper measure to our productive efforts.

Our social amelioration program has shed its beneficent blessings on the people inhabiting even the remotest confines of the archipelago; it has given a relief to small independent farmers within and beyond the once called troubled areas. It has so spread the gospel of peace and so inspired the people with faith in their Government, that it is well nigh impossible for the subversive elements to indoctrinate them now with their pernicious theories and ideas.

19. What do you know about Juan de Plasencia, the author of customs of the Tagalog, like his nationality, occupations, and/or position? Do any of these matter?


Juan de Plasencia was a Spanish Franciscan friar who lived in the Philippines during the 16th century. He was born in 1549 in Trujillo, Spain, and arrived in the Philippines in 1578 as part of a group of Franciscan missionaries.

In addition to his work documenting the customs of the Tagalog people, Juan de Plasencia also served as a missionary and pastor, and was known for his efforts to promote Christianity among the indigenous people of the Philippines. He was also involved in establishing schools and promoting education, particularly for children and young people.

Juan de Plasencia's nationality, occupation, and position are all relevant to his work and legacy. As a Spanish Franciscan missionary, he was part of a larger effort by the Spanish government and the Catholic Church to colonize and convert the people of the Philippines. His documentation of Tagalog customs and traditions was intended to aid in this effort, as was his work in establishing schools and promoting education.

While Juan de Plasencia's role in the Spanish colonization of the Philippines is a complex and controversial issue, his contributions to the documentation of Philippine culture and history are significant, and his work continues to be studied and celebrated today.

Answer:

Nationality

refers to the country of citizenship. Nationality is sometimes used to mean ethnicity, although the two are technically different. People can share the same nationality but be of different ethnic groups and people who share an ethnic identity can be of different nationalities.

Race

A race is a grouping of humans based on shared physical or social qualities into categories generally viewed as distinct by society. The term was first used to refer to speakers of a common language and then to denote national affiliations. By the 17th century the term began to refer to physical (phenotypical) traits.
