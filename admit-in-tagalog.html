Answer:

Total number of patients are:

X + Y = 400. ____(2)

Patients admitted in Hospital X were forty more than twice as many patients admitted in Hospital Y.

X = 40 + 2Y. ____(1)

⊱ ──────  ✯  ────── ⊰

Now again you can use any method to solve it.

Substitution method:

X + Y = 400

X = 400 - Y_____(3)

Substitute (3) in (1)

400 - Y = 40 + 2Y

Y = 120.

Substitute the value of Y in any equation.

X + Y = 400

X + 120 = 400

X = 280.

So, in Hospital X there are 280 patients and in Hospital Y there are 120 patients. This is the answer.

14. in one town there were 400 patients admitted to hospitals x and y with symptoms of covid 19 last most.patients admitted in hospital x were forty more than twice as many patients admitted in hospital y.how many patients were admitted in each hospital? c.elimination method​


✒️ELIMINATION

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

In one town there were 400 patients admitted to hospitals x and y with symptoms of Covid 19 last most. Patients admitted in hospital x were forty more than twice as many patients admitted in hospital y. How many patients were admitted in each hospital?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \large \rm{Hospital \: x = 280 \: patients} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \large \rm{Hospital \: y = 120 \: patients} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Represent x and y as the Hospitals x and y respectively. Formulate equations by the given statements.

[tex] \begin{cases} x + y = 400 \\ x = 2y + 40 \end{cases} \quad \begin{align} \tt{(eq. \: 1)} \\ \tt{(eq. \: 2)} \end{align} [/tex]

» Since we're solving this in elimination method. First is we align the terms.

[tex] \begin{cases} x + y = 400 \\ x - 2y = 40 \end{cases} [/tex]

» Eliminate x by subtracting the two equations.

[tex] \begin{align}x+\:\; y=400\:\:\,\\ \underline{-\:(x - 2y= \;\:40) \:} \\ 3y=360\:\:\,\end{align} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{\,3y\,}{3} = \frac{\,360\,}{3} \\ [/tex]

[tex] y = 120 [/tex]

» Thus, there are 120 patients admitted in Hospital y. Find how many patients admitted in Hospital x by substituting y to either of the given equations.

[tex] x = 2y + 40 \:;\quad y = 120 [/tex]

[tex] x = 2(120) + 40 [/tex]

[tex] x = 240 + 40 [/tex]

[tex] x = 280 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] 280 patients were admitted in hospital x and 120 patients were admitted in hospital y.
