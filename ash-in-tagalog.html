4. how is volcanic ash different from ash that result from burning​


Volcanic ash is a mixture of rock, mineral, and glass particles expelled from a volcano during a volcanic eruption. Unlike the ash produced by burning wood and other organic materials, volcanic ash can be dangerous. Its particles are very hard and usually have jagged edges.

13. A thick layer of volcanic ash can be heavy enough to collapse the roofs of building beacause of ash ___


Volcanoes releases volcanic ashes which are carried by the wind and by the explosive force and distributed in the nearby areas surrounding the volcanoes. This ashes might land on the rood of house and building. To much deposits of these volcanic ashes, the roof might collapse and would threaten the life of the people inside the house if they did not evacuate.

In the Philippines (Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology) monitor the activity of a Volcano. They provide warning if they observe any sign of volcanic eruption. The people in the affected areas with the help of LGU's will be evacuated safe from volcanic eruption. Relief operation and necessary materials are provided in the evacuation center. People are then given mask to prevent inhaling the ashes of the erupting volcanoes.  When the volcanic eruption already subside by the investigation of (Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology), people are then release to go back to their respective home. Government will help the affected people to cope up the crisis situation and provide help to them especially to damages houses.

Disaster management controls the effects of Volcanic eruption by minimizing its damage like loss of life and damage in the environment.

Volcanoes are formed when two plates collide or along the convergent boundary. As the plate collide, denser plate usually oceanic plate will subduct beneath the other and moves towards the mantle. As the oceanic plate subduct and moves towards the mantle beneath the other plate, the leading edge of the subducting plate will melt and this melted material will turn into magma and rise up on the surface of the other plate forming a volcano on the surface.
