7. description of AngklungTulaliKalagongBumbongKiskisPan PipesKalatokTalunggating​


Answer:

Angklung- refers to the broken tone or in complete tone. Angklung is an instument made of bamboo tubes attached to a bamboo frame. The use of Angklung is swayed and shaken by hand to prudoce a note.

Tulali- end-blow n flute of the Philippines.It is made of bagakay,a small variety of bamboo.

Kalagong- It is a wind intsrument that produce a hallow gong -like sound.

Bumbong- "Musikong Bumbong" bands,play piccolos, tubas, clarinets,flutes ang saxophones crafted from bamboo.Musikong Bamboo translate literally as bamboo music,many band member craft thier own instrument.

Kiskis - is a bamboo piece with ridges and serves as a percussion instrument."kiskis" is a filipino word for 'to scratch' or 'to rub against something'. It is played by 'rubbing' a rattan stick on the ridges.

Pan Pipes- also called syrinx,wind instrument consisting of cane pipesof different lenghts tied in a row or in a bundle held toghether by a wax or cord (metel,clay,wood, and plastic instruments are also made) and generally closed at the bottom.

Kalatok- is a pecussion instrument, with pieces of bamboo with varying tones strung together. The instruments name was derived from the frist two syllable of the conductor's surname "Kala" and the "Tok" sound that is produces when struck by a rubber mallet.

Tulunggating- (Bamboo Marimba) KALAGONG.

Wind instrument that produces a hallow gong-like sound. Played by tapping the top wholes of the bamboos by the two flat and flexible surface tapper. It served as a bass of the group..

20. classification of kiskis​


Answer: Idiophone

Explanation:

Idiophone is a kind of instrument that can produce sound by "scratching", striking, or shaking it.

Kiskis, also known as bamboo scratcher, is an intrument that has ridges and is a percussion instrument. “Kiskis” is a Filipino word for “to scratch”.

To play the Kiskis, you rub a stick or a proper tool against the ridges of the Kiskis.
