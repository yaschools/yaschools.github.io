Kami ay, ito ay inaasahan, na nagiging mas kamalayan na ang mga araling ito ay tungkol sa kung paano kumilos ang Kalikasan bilang sila ay tungkol sa kung paano namin naiintindihan at tumugon dito.

Aralin 1: Ang kahinaan sa mga natural na sakuna ay hindi pare-pareho sa buong lipunan. Ang malawakang kahirapan ay tila ang pinakamalaking pinagmumulan ng kahinaan. Yaong mga nagpapatakbo ng gobyerno sa iba't ibang antas ay may posibilidad na hikayatin ang ganitong paraan ng pag-iisip sa pamamagitan ng pag-uugnay sa mga kalunus-lunos na resulta ng mga natural na sakuna sa katigasan ng ulo, kamangmangan, at pagsuway sa awtoridad ng mga tao.

Ito ang teknolohiya kung saan may access na ang aming mga weather specialist.

19. 1 what happens to your body when you grow up?in tagalog​


Answer:

Height is just one of the thousands of features your genes decide. In fact, because you have two parents, your genes act like a referee, giving you a height that usually lands somewhere between the height of each parent. If both your parents are tall, then most likely you will be tall, too, but if you have questions about how tall you're going to be, ask your doctor if he or she can help you figure it out.

But genes don't decide everything. For example, eating an unhealthy diet can keep you from growing to your full potential. Getting plenty of sleep, enough exercise, and nutrients will help you grow just like you should.

No doubt you're wondering how fast you should grow. It depends. There's no perfect or right amount. On average, kids grow about 2 inches (6 centimeters) a year between age 3 and when they start puberty (when your body starts changing and becoming more grown up).

Your doctor will know how your growth has been going over the years. Two centimeters here and 2 inches there are not nearly as important as the height you're at now, how you've been growing up to this point, and what other changes your body may be going through.

29. what happened to the method of writing of the tagalog after arrival of the spanish?​


Answer:

Explanation:

Printing and publishing began in the Philippines with the arrival of the Spanish in 1565. Encountering an enormous number of native languages, the Spaniards felt a pressing need to describe the languages most commonly spoken in the archipelago in order to communicate with the Filipinos. With the establishment of Spanish sovereignty over the Philippines, the Spanish Crown issued several contradictory laws regarding language. The missionaries were urged to learn the vernacular languages but were subsequently required to teach Spanish. For this reason, missionaries learnt the Philippine languages by writing vocabularies, grammars, and catechisms.
