28. Write a reflection about Romans 12⁚9 Let love be genuine, Abhor what is evil; hold fast to what is good. Even if it's just a short reflection


Answer:

This passage from Romans 12:9 is a reminder that the key to living a life of love is to focus on what is good and stay away from what is evil. It is a call to be genuine in our love and to be intentional in our actions and attitudes. In doing so, we can demonstrate God’s love to others and live out the Gospel. It is easy to be drawn to the evil in the world, but it is more important that we focus on what is good and true. We should be intentional in our relationships and our choices, and strive to do what is pleasing to God. This passage reminds us that it is not enough to simply avoid evil; we must actively pursue what is good and pleasing to God.

30. Directions: Undertine the verb and write the gerund on the space provided. 1. Smoking is dangerous to our health.__________ 2. Fester and Jester amused the stern king by joking.___________ 3. God hates and abhors lying of any kind.___________ 4. Salvation is not a matter of hoping and knowing._________ 5. His mistake was running away from the woman who loved him._________


Answer:

1. Smoking

2. Joking

3. Lying

4. Hoping and Knowing

5. Running

Answer:

1. Verb: smoking

Gerund: smoking

2. Verb: amused

Gerund: joking

3. Verb: hates

Gerund: lying

4. Verb: matter

Gerund: hoping/ knowing

5. Verb: running away

Gerund: running

Explanation:

A gerund is the –ing form of a verb that functions the same as a noun. One way to spot a gerund is to notice that they always end in -ing.
