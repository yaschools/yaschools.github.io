3. traffic signage and meaning​


Traffic signs are an essential part of road safety and are used to convey important information to drivers, pedestrians, and other road users. Here are some common types of traffic signs and their meanings:

1. Regulatory Signs: These signs are used to indicate traffic laws and regulations that must be followed. Some examples include:

- Stop Sign: Drivers must come to a complete stop at the marked stop line or before entering the crosswalk or intersection.

- Yield Sign: Drivers must slow down and give the right of way to pedestrians or other vehicles in the intersection or merging lane.

- Speed Limit Sign: Indicates the maximum speed limit that drivers must not exceed.

- No Parking Sign: Indicates that parking is prohibited in the designated area.

2. Warning Signs: These signs are used to warn drivers of potential hazards and dangers on the road ahead. Some examples include:

- School Zone Sign: Indicates that drivers must slow down and exercise caution as they enter a school zone.

- Slippery When Wet Sign: Indicates that the road surface may be slippery and drivers should reduce their speed and exercise caution.

- Construction Zone Sign: Indicates that there is roadwork ahead and drivers should slow down and follow directions from flaggers or construction workers.

3. Informational Signs: These signs provide information about the road ahead, including directions, distances, and locations. Some examples include:

- Route Signs: Indicate the route number and direction of travel.

- Exit Signs: Indicate the exit number and direction of the next exit.

- Mile Marker Signs: Indicate the distance traveled or remaining on the road.

It's important to pay attention to traffic signs while driving to ensure a safe and efficient journey.
