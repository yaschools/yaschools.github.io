12. 1. The regular prize of a shirt was reduced by P10. Mely paid P120 for the shirt. What was the regular prize?


Answer:

The regular price of the shirt was P130.

Step-by-step explanation:

Amount discounted/reduced on shirt → P10

Paid Amount → P120

Regular Price → 120 + 10

                       = P130

Answer:

If the regular price of the shirt was reduced by P10, then the selling price is equal to the regular price minus P10.

Let's assume that the regular price of the shirt is "x". Then, the selling price is "x - 10".

We know that Mely paid P120 for the shirt, so we can set up the following equation:

x - 10 = 120

To solve for "x", we can add 10 to both sides of the equation:

x - 10 + 10 = 120 + 10

Simplifying the equation, we get:

x = 130

Therefore, the regular price of the shirt was P130.

HOW MANY BOXES IS THE GUARANTEE?

Problem:

Suppose a cereal company puts a prize in each box. There are four possible prizes, and  you want to get two identical prizes. How many boxes would you need to purchase to guarantee you get two identical prizes? Say the prizes are A, B, C, and D.

Answer with Step-by-step explanation:

At first look, you might say that this word problem will require long calculations. Actually, this only requires common sense and critical thinking skills. So here is the scenario. The company's prizes in each box are maybe A, B, C and D, and let us say that in the first 4 boxes that you bought and opened, you have all 4 unique prizes. On the fifth box, you may either open prize A, B, C or D by which you will already have two identical prizes.

Therefore, you are guaranteed to have two identical prizes of that certain cereal company when you purchased 5 cereal boxes.

26. 1. A lottery offers one 400,000 prize, two 150,000 prizes, and four 25,000 prizes. Fifty thousand tickets are sold at 50 each. Find the expectation if a person buys 25 tick. Ets. ".


Answer:

The expected value of a lottery ticket is the sum of the probability of each prize multiplied by the prize value. The probability of winning a prize is equal to the number of tickets sold for that prize divided by the total number of tickets sold.

In this case, the expected value of a ticket is:

(1/50000) * 400000 + (2/50000) * 150000 + (4/50000) * 25000 = 8 + 3 + 1 = 12

Since a person buys 25 tickets, the expected value of the 25 tickets is 25 * 12 = 300. This is the amount of money that the person can expect to win on average if they buy 25 tickets.
