10. Reflection on malakas ata maganda


The story of MALAKAS AT MAGANDA provides a Filipino take on the creation of everything we know and see today. It explained how all the lands were created. In the story, the world’s first couple emerged from a large bamboo culm with the help of a great bird. That event may remind readers of the Biblical story of Adam and Eve. The faith of their children was a way of explaining how the different races and their place in the social structure were accidentally created. The returning children whose descendants became Europeans also gave a hint of a post-colonial foundation with regards to this legend. THE LEGEND OF MALAKAS AT MAGANDA is probably one way of explaining the intricacies of race, class and everything else that makes up our society.

20. If the DNA template reads "ATA",then which of the following would be the corresponding sequence on the mRNA?A. UAUB. ATAC. TUTD. UCU​


DNA TEMPLATE

Although DNA is double-stranded, only one strand is ever used as a transcription template. The noncoding strand is the name given to this template strand. Because its sequence will match that of the new RNA molecule, the nontemplate strand is known as the coding strand.

A. UAU

The DNA template in this case reads "ATA." The complement of adenine is supposed to be T or thymine, but in mRNA, it is replaced by Uracil. Next, the complement of thymine is A or adenine, and adenine is complement with Uracil. Finally, the complement of adenine is complement with Uracil. The sequence in mRNA is "UAU."

Supporting details:

mRNA a kind of RNA that exists in cells. The genetic information required to produce proteins is carried by mRNA molecules. They transport the data from the DNA in the cell's nucleus to the cytoplasm, where proteins are produced. Likewise known as messenger RNA.

Adenine, thymine, guanine, and cytosine are the compounds that are represented by the code letters A, T, G, and C in DNA, respectively. Adenine always pairs with thymine in base pairing, while guanine always couples with cytosine.

MRNA serves as the cell's intermediary messenger and is a crucial safety mechanism. Any RNA that is outside of the cell is immediately targeted for destruction by enzymes known as RNases, preventing invaders from using the cellular machinery to make foreign proteins.

The function of mRNA is to transport protein information from DNA in a cell's nucleus to the cytoplasm, or watery interior, where the machinery responsible for making proteins reads the mRNA sequence and converts each three-base codon into an amino acid that belongs in a protein chain that is expanding.

Three processes make up the method by which RNA is translated into protein: initiation, elongation, and termination. A tRNA and ribosome join an mRNA during initiation.
