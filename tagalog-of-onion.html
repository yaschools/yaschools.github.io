[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

In a bag of red and white onions, the ratio of red onions is to white onions is 3:4. If the bag contains 120 white onions, how many red onions are there?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \: \rm{90 \: red \: onions} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Write the proportion and let x be the number of red onions compared to 120 white onions equal to 3:4.

[tex] x \::\: 120 = 3 \::\: 4 [/tex]

» Multiply the means and the extremes.

[tex] 4x = 120(3) [/tex]

[tex] 4x = 360 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{\,4x\,}{4} = \frac{\,360\,}{4} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = 90 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 90 red onions in the bag.

10. 1. Why didn't you see the chloroplast in the onion cells?A. The onion is very thinB. An Onion is not a plantC. The Onion is too soft to seeD. The onion bulb grows in the dark​


Answer:

D

Explanation:

Most of the cell parts (called organelles) are almost invisible because they are colorless. We also looked at the cells of an onion bulb. Since the onion bulb grows underground, it doesn't see any sunlight and so it doesn't have any chloroplasts for photosynthesis.
