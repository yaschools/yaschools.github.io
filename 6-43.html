Let's say our sequence is: a1, a2, a3, a4, a5, ...

Recursive formula: an = an - 1 + 2

To find the 5th term, a5, we must first find a4.

a4 = a3 + 2

a3 = a2 + 2

a2 = a1 + 2

a5 = 6 + 2 = 8

Our recursive formula can be expressed as an = 2 + 2n.

To convert the recursive formula to an explicit expression, we need to solve for n.

2 + 2n = 8

Our explicit expression is an = 3n(n + 3).

Therefore, the 5th term in our sequence is 3n(n + 3) where n = 3.
