10. Why do you think Starbucks decided to enter the Japanese market via a joint venture with a Japanese company? What lesson can you draw from this?​


As Starbucks entered the unfamiliar Japanese environment, it likely partnered with Sazaby Corp.for several reasons. First, the Japanese market was already in the midst of financial issues, but secondly, the island nation has a reputation of hostility towards foreign businesses and investments within Japan’s borders (Inagaki, 2013). The joint venture with a local company increased Starbucks’ chances of success in this otherwise hostile environment.As it turns out, the decision was a smart one – Starbucks was able to take full ownership of its Japanese unit, starting in September of 2014 (Baertlein, 2014). Moreover, Japan is now Starbucks second-largest market, as the company operates over 1,000 locations and has plans to open even more in the coming year (Bartlein, 2014). If I had to take a lesson from this, I would say the joint venture approach is an effective one when applied properly

14. A ______ provides for the greatest degree of continuity: Select one: a. Sole proprietorship b. Corporation c. General partnership d. Joint venture


answer: b. Corporation

A CORPORATION provides for the greatest degree of continuity.


Here’s an explanation:

a. Sole proprietorship – there could be an unlimited liability which is why it cannot provide greatest degree of continuity.

c. General partnership – there’s liquidation and it cannot be continued by succession.

d. Joint venture – the business activity or sharing as well as the transfer of ownership in the deal can only be project based or according to a limited contract.


A CORPORATION provides for the greatest degree of continuity because of its powers and right of succession.

A CORPORATION provides for the greatest degree of continuity and the reason to this is most probably SUCCESSION.

In business law, the perpetual succession is in fact the continuation of the existence or life of the corporation or other organization despite the death, insolvency, bankruptcy, insanity, membership change, exit from business of any part of it, or any transfer of stock, etc.

A corporation, according to the law, is an artificial being created by operation of law. It possesses the right of succession and the powers, attributes and properties expressly authorized by law or by the incident to its existence.

This comes from the definition stated by the Corporation Code - as artificial being created by operation of law, having the right of succession and the powers, attributes and properties expressly authorized by law or incident to its existence. As an artificial being, corporation has personality separate and distinct from stockholders. The general rule is that a corporation may not be made to answer for acts or liabilities of its stockholders and vice versa. It is the corporation, and not its stockholders, is personally liable for the corporate debts and / or liabilities.

However, the above rule isn’t actually that absolute. There’s this veil of corporate fiction. It may be pierced, though, when the fiction of corporate entity is being used as a cover for the fraud and / or illegality or to ruin and defeat public convenience, validate the wrong, protect the deception and / or defend any misconduct and crime.

20. Assume you are a manager of a fast food restaurants in Russia, in partnership with a Russian company. One day you discover that a senior officer of your Russian joint-venture partner has been "borrowing" equipment from the joint-venture company and utilizing it in another of his business ventures. When you confront him, the Russian officer defends his action, arguing that as an owner of both companies he is entitled to share use of the equipment. What would you way?​


Answer:

If I were a manager of a fast food restaurant in Russia and found out that a senior officer of my Russian joint-venture partner was using equipment from the joint-venture company for another business venture, I would take the following steps:

I would check the joint-venture agreement to see each partner's rights and responsibilities regarding the equipment.

I would meet with the senior officer to discuss the situation and explain that using the equipment for personal gain is not allowed.

If the senior officer disagreed, I would seek legal advice to find out if his actions broke the joint-venture agreement.

Based on the legal advice, I might have to cancel the joint-venture agreement or discuss new terms that both parties can agree on.

I would work to resolve the situation fairly while protecting the fast food restaurant and joint-venture's interests.


21. explain how joint ventures are being taxed.​


Answer:

Because any profits made from a joint venture flow through to the individual members of the venture, the portion of the profit that each member receives is claimed on that member's individual or corporate tax returns. The venture itself does not make a tax filing on any of the funds that flow through it.
