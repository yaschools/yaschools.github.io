1. WHAT TRANSFORMS A "CRAZY VISIONARY" INTO A "VISIONARY LEADER"?


"CRAZY VISIONARY" INTO A "VISIONARY LEADER"

A visionary is a person who has a clear sense of the future. The ideas of a visionary may either succeed spectacularly or fail miserably because such visions aren't always correct. Visionary is typically a good word, though.

Visionaries are continuously thinking creatively and in advance. You may say that a visionary is able to foresee the future and develop solutions that correspond to that vision. But those crazy concepts aren't always well received. Skeptics believe that visionaries lack a realistic foundation. The visionaries typically advise them to broaden their horizons as well.

Visionary leaders inspire groups of people to work together or follow them in achieving their goals, and they require structure and organization to help them. Having a vision for the business implies that the owner has visualized a path for it, and in order to put plans into motion, they must share this vision with their team.

Clear goals, a strategic plan for reaching those goals, and enabling each member to take action on the plan at the organizational, team, and individual levels are all ways that a visionary leader ensures that the vision is realized.

Action and make future into a reality is what transforms "crazy visionary" into a "visionary leader"

19. what do these words mean to you?1.Teacher2.Philosopher3.Visionary4.Entertainer5.Historian6.Pragmatist​


Answer:

1. teacher (also called a school teacher or, in some contexts, an educator) is a person who helps students to acquire knowledge, competence or virtue. Informally the role of teacher may be taken on by anyone (e.g. when showing a colleague how to perform a specific task).

2. philosopher is someone who practices philosophy. The term philosopher comes from the Ancient Greek: φιλόσοφος, romanized: philosophos, meaning 'lover of wisdom'. The coining of the term has been attributed to the Greek thinker Pythagoras.

3. Visionary

Defined broadly, a visionary is one who can envision the future. For some groups this can involve the supernatural. The visionary state is achieved via meditation, drugs, lucid dreams, daydreams, or art. One example is Hildegard of Bingen, a 12th-century artist and Catholic saint.

4. An entertainer is a person whose job or goal is to perform for the amusement of others. If you're an entertainer, you might learn how to tap dance and take singing lessons. If you make your living as a comedian, you're an entertainer. You're also an entertainer if you're a dancer, singer, actor, or puppeteer.

5. A historian is a person who studies and writes about the past and is regarded as an authority on it. Historians are concerned with the continuous, methodical narrative and research of past events as relating to the human race; as well as the study of all history in time.

6. Pragmatism is a philosophical tradition that considers words and thought as tools and instruments for prediction, problem solving, and action, and rejects the idea that the function of thought is to describe, represent, or mirror reality.

20. the visionary advantage and disadvantage ​


A leader who is visionary is one who looks to the future, has a clear idea of what they want to accomplish, and can inspire and motivate their team to work toward that goal. They can lead their team through change and uncertainty and see opportunities where others may only see obstacles. One advantage of having a visionary leader is that they can encourage the organization's growth and innovation. To assist the business in maintaining its competitive edge, they are able to devise novel strategies and concepts. Additionally, visionary leaders have the ability to inspire employees with a sense of purpose and direction for the business.
