3. What was significant about Don Bosco's faith?


Answer:

Don Bosco was an Italian priest and educator who founded the Salesian order of the Catholic Church in the 19th century. He was known for his strong faith and dedication to helping poor and disadvantaged young people. He believed in providing education and vocational training to young people as a way of helping them to escape poverty and build better lives for themselves. He also emphasized the importance of moral values and spiritual guidance in the lives of young people. As a result of his work, he became known as the "Father and Teacher of Youth" and his approach to education and youth ministry continues to be influential in the Catholic Church today.
