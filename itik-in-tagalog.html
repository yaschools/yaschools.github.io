Answer:

1. Itik-itik is a mimetic folk dance in The Philippines. It originated in the province of Surigao in Mindanao. In Itik-itik (from the Tagalog word for "duck"), the dance steps imitate the movements of ducks among rice paddies and swamplands, such as wading, flying, and short, choppy steps.

2. Itik Itik is a very happy song by Bayanihan Philippine Dance Company with a tempo of 182 BPM. It can also be used half-time at 91 BPM. The track runs 2 minutes and 15 seconds long with a G key and a major mode. It has average energy and is not very danceable with a time signature of 3 beats per bar.

3. Girls wear patadyong or balintawak style costume and Boy wear barong tagalong or camisa de chino and red trousers.

4.

1. Step to your left and raise your left arm up in a flowing motion.

2. Step to your right and raise your right and raise your right arm up in a flowing motion.

2. Itik-itik - According to history of this dance, a young woman named Kanang (short for Cayetana) happened to be the best performer in the province of Surigao del Norte.

3. The itik-itk is named after a species of duck (itik), whose movements the dance imitates. This example of Philippine folk dance from Surigao del Sur mimics how the itik walks and splashes water to attract a mate.

4. This dance originated from a dance called "Sibay" (a dance influenced by a bird's movements) , which was performed to the tune of "Dejado".

5. The attire generally worn includes the baro't saya (traditional Philippines dress for women), patadyong (a wrap around loose skirt) or the balintawak (a native dress of Filipino women consisting of dress and skirt woven of local fibers with a kerchief and apron to match).

6. The attire worn generally includes barong tagalog (embroidered formal shirt considered the national dress of Philippines) or the camisa de chino (a collarless Chinese shirt with short sleeves worn under the barong tagalog), and white trousers.
