1. Recognize Filipinos in Physics and Engineering​


Explanation:

There are many talented Filipinos who have made significant contributions to the fields of physics and engineering. Here are some notable individuals:

• Dr. Lourdes J. Cruz - A distinguished professor of marine biology and the first Filipina to be elected to the US National Academy of Science. She is known for her research on cone snail venom and its potential uses in medicine.

• Dr. Edgardo D. Gomez - A marine biologist and conservationist who is best known for his research on the ecology and conservation of coral reefs.

• Dr. Gregorio Y. Zara - A Filipino engineer and physicist who is credited with inventing the first videophone, the solar-powered engine, and the Zara Effect, a phenomenon in physics involving the reflection and refraction of light.

• Dr. Paulo Campos - A Filipino physicist who has contributed to the development of the field of nanotechnology, specifically in the area of carbon nanotubes.

• Dr. Rogel Mari Sese - A Filipino astrophysicist who has contributed to the study of dark matter and the formation of galaxies.

• Dr. Josefino Comiso - A Filipino oceanographer who has contributed to the study of sea ice and its role in climate change.

• Dr. Reynaldo V. Castro - A Filipino chemical engineer who has contributed to the development of process control systems and the optimization of chemical processes.

These are just a few examples of the many talented Filipinos who have made significant contributions to the fields of physics and engineering.

3. E-Jeepney Buy this Jeepney toy today Source: Ejeepney After the Americans colonized the Philippines, the Filipinos were able to create their own version of the “jeepney” from scratch. Ever since, this vehicle can be seen over almost every street and road in the Philippines and is the primary mode of transportation in the country. This Philippine vehicle has undergone many transformations and the modern “E-jeepney” was introduced in Metro Manila and Bacolod City. The E-jeepney uses electricity which is significantly cheaper than that of ordinary diesel.
4. Erythromycin Buy this topical solution today Erythromycin is an antibiotic that is derived from the bacterium Streptomyces erythreus which normally used as anti-acne or remove pimples. Ilonggo doctor/scientist Abelardo Aguilar discovered this strain in 1949 using the soil in his home province. Unfortunately, when he sent it to his US employer in Eli Lilli Co. to separate the said strain, the Indiana-based company owned the drug and Aguilar was not given any credit for this discovery.
5. Patis Buy this Patis sauce today Patis or Fish Sauce is a condiment that is used as a salty partner for many Filipino dishes. Ruperta David (aka Aling Tentay) was responsible for its accidental discovery in 1949.
6. Banana Catsup Banana Catsup is a condiment that many Filipinos love to use for almost every known dish in the Philippines. This was the brainchild of food technologist Maria Orosa y Ylagan (1893–1945). According to historical accounts, she created the first recipe for banana catsup and experimented with foods that are native to the Philippines and created food products such as calamansi nip, which is a powdered form of calamansi to make calamansi juice, and a powdered version of soya beans called Soyalac which was used to help save the lives of many Filipinos, Americans and others who were held prisoners in World War II. #
7 Anticancer Cream Rolando dela Cruz is a Filipino inventor who won the gold medal for creating an anti-cancer cream at the prestigious International Inventor’s Forum in November of 2005. It is called “DeBCC” cream, and was developed from cashew nuts and other local herbs. It is used specifically for basal skin carcinoma (BSC), which is known as the most prevalent type of skin cancer worldwide.
8. 16-Bit Microchip Source: WazzupPilipinas Diosdado Banatao, or more popularly known as Dado Banatao, he is responsible for designing the first single chip, 16-bit microprocessor-based calculator. Also, in 1981, he developed the first 10-Mbit Ethernet CMOS with silicon coupler data-link control and transreceiver chip. He is also credited for the first system logic chip set for IBM’s PC-XT and the PC-AT as well as the local bus concept and the first Windows Graphics accelerator chip for personal computers.
