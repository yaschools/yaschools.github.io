[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at an angle θ and at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd \: \cos \theta}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

θ = angle of the force with the horizontal

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd cos θ

W = (80 N)(10 m)(cos 30°)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{692.82 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 692.82 J.

12. suppose you pull a 120kg sled so that the net force on the sled in 52 N.​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F_{net} = ma}[/tex]

where:

[tex]F_{net} =[/tex] net force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the acceleration

[tex]F_{net} = ma[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{F_{net}}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F_{net}}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{52 N}}{\text{120 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{0.433 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of the sled is 0.433 m/s².

24. Rocket-powered sleds are used to test the human response to acceleration. If a rocket-powered sled is accelerated to a speed of 444 m/s in 1.83 seconds, then what is the acceleration and what is the distance that the sled travels?​


Answer:

a. The acceleration of the rocket-powered sled is 242.62 m/s²

b. The distance of the sled travels 812.52 m

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to solve for the acceleration and distance of a rocket-powered sled using a simple formula for acceleration and distance.

Formula to use

a. a = v/t

b. d = vt

where

a     is the acceleration, the unit is in m/s²

v     is the velocity, the unit is in m/s

t      is the time, the unit is in seconds (s)

d     is the distance, the unit is in meter (m)

Given information

v = 444 m/s

t = 1.83 seconds

a = ?

d = ?

Solving the problem

a. First, let us solve for the acceleration of the rocket-powered sled using the given information

a = v/t

Then substitute the given data

a = 444 m/s / 1.83 s

a = 242.62 m/s²

b. Finally, let us solve the distance traveled by the sled. Let us use the given formula

d = vt

Then substitute the given data

d = (444 m/s)(1.83 s)

d = 812.52 m

Therefore, the distance traveled by the rocket-powered sled is 812.52 m

28. You are pushing a friend on a sled. You push with a force of 20N. Your friend and the sled together have a mass of 60kg. Ignoring friction, what is the acceleration of your friend on the sled.​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The force acting on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F = ma}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for a

F = ma

[tex]\dfrac{F}{m} = \dfrac{ma}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{F}{m}[/tex]

[tex]a = \dfrac{\text{20 N}}{\text{60 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a = \text{0.333 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the acceleration of your friend is 0.333 m/s².

29. Suppose that a sled is accelerating at a rate of 2 m/s2. If the new force is tripped and the mass is halved, then what is the new acceleration of the sled?


Answer:

12 m/s²

Explanation:

F = ma

⇒ a = F/m

a = 2 m/s² = F/m

If the force is trippled

(3 F) /m

Mass is halved

(3 F) /(m/2)

(3 F) /(m/2) = 3 F ÷ m/2 = (3 F)(2/m) = [2(3 F)] / m

= 6 F/m = 6 (F/m)

Recall that F/m = 2 m/s²

⇒ 6(2 m/s²) = 12 m/s²
