13. What is your level of anxiety after taking the burn anxiety inventory?​


Answer:

Abstract

The main purpose of this study was to analyze the psychometric properties of the Spanish version of the Burns Anxiety Inventory (Burns-A: Burns, 1993). The sample consisted of 417 participants, 387 (29.71% male) healthy participants (control group: M = 35.5 years; SD = 8.40) and 30 (36.66% female) patients (clinical group: M = 35.8 years; SD = 12.94). The internal consistency evaluated through Cronbach’ Alpha was 0.95 for the Total Score in both subsamples. The test-retest reliability (two weeks) for Total Score was 0.86 (F = 13.2, p ≤ .001) for the non-clinical subsample, and 0.95 (F = 36.5, p ≤ .001) for the clinical subsample. Confirmatory factor analyses supported the initial three-factor model. However, modifications to the initial three-factor model improved the goodness-of-fit indices. Results showed statistically significant differences in the mean scores of the Burns-A between the clinical and control groups. This study supports the Spanish version of the Burns-A as a brief and useful tool for the screening of anxiety symptoms in adult populations. Future studies should investigate measurement invariance across cultures.

17. what is the meaning of anxiety whenever you probably having anxiety and depression? and why?


Answer:

Anxiety is a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about something with an uncertain outcome. It is a normal response to stress and can be beneficial in some situations as it can help to motivate a person to take action. However, when anxiety becomes excessive or long-lasting, it can become a disorder and affect a person's daily life and well-being.

Depression, on the other hand, is a mental health disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, loss of interest or pleasure, and a lack of energy. It can also cause physical symptoms such as changes in appetite or sleep patterns. Depression and anxiety often co-occur and they have similar symptoms such as fatigue, poor concentration and irritability.

Anxiety and depression can have a variety of causes, such as genetic predisposition, traumatic events, and stress. These conditions can also be triggered by certain life events such as a loss of a loved one, job loss, and financial stress.

It is important to note that everyone experiences anxiety and depression differently, and that it is essential to seek professional help if you feel that your anxiety or depression is affecting your daily life.
