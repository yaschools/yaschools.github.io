23. 1. When is decantation applicable as a means of separating the components of a mixture?2. When is evaporation applicable as a means of separating the components of a mixture?3. What is the difference between solubility and miscibility?4. Can evaporation be used to retrieve all the components of a mixture? Why or why not?:)​


Answer:

Decantation can be used to separate immiscible liquids that have different densities.Evaporation can be used as a separation method to separate components of a mixture with a dissolved solid in a liquid. The liquid is evaporated, meaning it is convert from its liquid state to gaseous state. This often requires heat. Once the liquid is completely evaporated, the solid is all that is left behind.The term miscibility refers to the ability of a liquid solute to dissolve in a liquid solvent. Solubility is a more general term, but it is more often used to mean the ability of a solid solute to dissolve in a liquid solvent.Evaporation is a technique used to separate out homogeneous mixtures that contain one or more dissolved salts.Prior to using this method, the mixture should only contain one liquid component, unless it is not important to isolate the liquid components. This is because all liquid components will evaporate over time.
