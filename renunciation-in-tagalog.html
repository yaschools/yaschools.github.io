1. what is great Renunciation ​


Answer:

A traditional term for the departure of Gautama Buddha from his palace at Kapilavastu to live a life as an ascetic. It is called the Great Renunciation because it is regarded as a great sacrifice.


2. Which of the following renunciations shall not be subject to donor's tax?


Answer:

The revenue regulations clarified that a general renunciation by an heir of his/her share in the inheritance is not subject to donor's tax unless specifically and categorically done in favor of identified heir or heirs to the exclusion of the other co-heirs in the hereditary estate

The term "voluntary renunciation of office" in the context of the parliament typically refers to a situation where a member of parliament chooses to relinquish their position or voluntarily resign from their office before the completion of their full term of office. The length of time for such voluntary renunciation of office can vary depending on the specific circumstances and regulations in place.

In some cases, a member of parliament may choose to resign from their position for personal or political reasons, or due to other circumstances such as health issues, family obligations, or a change in career. The length of time for voluntary renunciation of office can vary, and it may be for a temporary or permanent period depending on the member's intentions and the rules or laws governing the parliament.

The length of time for voluntary renunciation of office may also be influenced by the specific rules or procedures in place for filling vacancies or conducting by-elections in the parliament. For example, in some countries, a by-election to fill a vacant seat in the parliament may be required within a certain timeframe, while in others, the seat may remain vacant until the next scheduled election.

It's important to note that the rules and regulations regarding voluntary renunciation of office, including the length of time for which a member of parliament can resign, can vary depending on the country's constitution, laws, and parliamentary procedures.
