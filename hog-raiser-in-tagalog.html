6. Why is it important for poultry raiser to cull their birds?In what way culling help poultry raiser in business?what will happen if poultry raisers fail to do the culling process?​


Answer:

It is important for poultry raisers to cull their birds to remove any sick or diseased birds from the flock. This is essential to prevent the spread of disease to other birds and to ensure the overall health and well-being of the flock. Culling also helps to remove any unproductive birds that may be consuming resources without contributing to the profitability of the business.Culling can help poultry raisers in business in several ways. By removing sick or unproductive birds, the overall health and productivity of the flock can be improved, which can lead to increased profitability. Culling can also help to maintain the genetic quality of the flock by removing any birds that do not meet desired standards for traits such as growth rate, egg production, or meat quality.If poultry raisers fail to cull their birds, there can be serious consequences for the health and productivity of the flock. Sick or diseased birds can spread illness to other birds, leading to reduced productivity and potentially even death. Unproductive birds can also consume resources without contributing to the profitability of the business. In addition, failing to cull birds that do not meet desired genetic standards can lead to a decline in the overall quality of the flock over time, which can negatively impact the profitability of the business.

14. how can breeding system helps our backyard hog raiser here in the Philippines to increase the production of swine to be market?​


Answer:

Pig breeding systems influence the genetic background of your pigs and thus play an important role in performance and meat quality.

Feed for pigs is formulated to maximize nutrition, optimize growth and reduce levels of nitrogen in pork manure, so each pig produces more meat and less methane. Less land is needed to grow feed, thanks to higher yielding varieties of alfalfa, corn and soybeans.

It's no surprise that the local swine industry is one of its most lucrative trades, worth PHP200 billion or USD5 billion and contributing 18.28 percent to the country's agricultural output in 2015, ranking second only to rice and staying way ahead of poultry.
