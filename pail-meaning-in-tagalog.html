7. 6 L of water is poured into 2 empty pails. After pouring, 1 pail contains 800 ml more water than the other pail. How much water is there in the other pail?


Answer:

One pail contains 3.4 L of water while the other pail contains 2.6 L of water.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

- 6 L of water is poured into 2 pails:

x + y = 6000

(1 liter = 1000 mililiters)

- One pail (x) contains 800 mL more water than the other pail (y):

x = y + 800

- How much water is there in the other pail? (solve for y)


Solution:

1. Solve for x in the first equation:

x + y = 6000

x = 6000 - y

2. Solve for y:

6000 - y = y + 800

6000 - 800 = y + y

5200 = 2y

y = 2600

There are 2,600 mL or 2.6 L of water in the other pail.


Proof:

- Insert the value of y in the second equation to find x.

x = 2600 + 800

x = 3400

- Insert the values of x and y in the first equation.

3400 + 2600 = 6000

6000 = 6000

29. A pail of water can be whirled in a vertical path such that no water is spilled. why does the water stay in the pail, even when the pail is above your head?


Answer:

The water has inertia. The water tends to move along a straight line, but the bucket pulls it in and around in a circle

Explanation:

Let's consider the moment the pail is at the top of the circular path. If this is considered as a static picture, that is, not moving, then of course the water would fall.

However, what one might forget is that the pail is moving, that is, the water had a velocity with some vertically upward component. So, the force of gravity and the normal of the pail force the water to change the path. In other words, if it wasn't for them, the water would keep going further up.

As you will see in other chapters, a minimum speed is needed for the water not to fall.
