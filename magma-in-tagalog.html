Viscosity of Magmas

Viscosity depends on primarily on the composition of the magma, and temperature. Higher SiO2 (silica) content magmas have higher viscosity than lower SiO2 content magmas (viscosity increases with increasing SiO2 concentration in the magma).14 Sep 2015

Explanation:

The composition of magma depends on the rock it was formed from (by melting), and the conditions of that melting. Magmas derived from the mantle have higher levels of iron, magnesium, and calcium, but they are still likely to be dominated by oxygen and silicon. All magmas have varying proportions of elements such as hydrogen, carbon, and sulphur, which are converted into gases like water vapour, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen sulphide as the magma cools.

16. Difference between magma and magma chamber​


Answer:

The main difference between magma and a magma chamber is that magma is the molten rock itself, while a magma chamber is the underground cavity or reservoir where magma is stored before it is eventually released to the surface through a volcanic eruption.

To understand this better, imagine that magma is like water and a magma chamber is like a water tank. Just as water is stored in a tank before it is used, magma is stored in a magma chamber before it is released through a volcanic eruption.
