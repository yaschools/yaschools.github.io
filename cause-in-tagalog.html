There are many reasons/causes why the LGBTQ community has lost their rights, including:

Discrimination: Discrimination is one of the primary reasons why the LGBTQ community has lost their rights. They are often discriminated against in employment, housing, and public accommodations, which can lead to a lack of access to basic services and opportunities.

Lack of legal protection: In many countries, the LGBTQ community does not have legal protection, which leaves them vulnerable to discrimination and violence. This lack of legal protection also means that they do not have access to justice when their rights are violated.

Social stigma: The social stigma surrounding the LGBTQ community has led to their marginalization and exclusion from society. This stigma can lead to a lack of acceptance and support from family, friends, and the wider community, which can affect their mental health and well-being.

Political opposition: The political opposition to LGBTQ rights has played a significant role in the loss of their rights. Some politicians and religious leaders have actively campaigned against LGBTQ rights, leading to the enactment of laws and policies that discriminate against the community.

Lack of education and awareness: The lack of education and awareness about the LGBTQ community has led to a lack of understanding and acceptance. This lack of education and awareness can lead to discrimination and prejudice towards the community.

Overall, the loss of LGBTQ rights can be attributed to a complex interplay of social, political, and economic factors, which must be addressed to achieve greater equality and inclusion for the community.
