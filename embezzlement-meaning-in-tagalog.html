20. Which type of Corruption disrupts the economy and negates the rule of law? a. Collusion b. Embezzlement c. Petty d. Grand


Answer:

D. GRAND

Explanation:

Grand corruption is the type of corruption that disrupts the economy and negates the rule of law. It refers to corruption at the highest levels of government, such as the abuse of high-level positions of power for personal gain. Grand corruption can involve large sums of money and can have serious consequences for the economy and society as a whole. It can undermine trust in government and institutions, lead to economic inefficiency and inequality, and harm the overall development of a country.
