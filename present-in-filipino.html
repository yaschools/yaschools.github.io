3. how can the experience of the Filipino writers during the contemporary and present period help you understand the present traits, traditions, and culture of filipinos?​


Answer:

The diversity and richness of Philippine literature evolved side by side with the country's history. This can best be appreciated in the context of the country's pre-colonial cultural traditions and the socio-political histories of its colonial and contemporary traditions.          

      The average Filipino's unfamiliarity with his indigenous literature was largely due to what has been impressed upon him: that his country was "discovered" and, hence, Philippine "history" started only in 1521.

      So successful were the efforts of colonialists to blot out the memory of the country's largely oral past that present-day Filipino writers, artists and journalists are trying to correct this inequity by recognizing the country's wealth of ethnic traditions and disseminating them in schools and in the mass media.

      The rousings of nationalistic pride in the 1960s and 1970s also helped bring about this change of attitude among a new breed of Filipinos concerned about the "Filipino identity."

10. essay about filipino tradition past and present ​


Bayanihan

Naranasan mo na ba ang bayanihan sa ating bansa? Ito ang diwa ng komunal na pagkakaisa o pagsisikap upang makamit ang isang partikular na layunin. Ang isang tanyag na halimbawa nito ay ang mga kapitbahay na nagdadala ng isang kubo o bahay sa isang bagong lokasyon. Ginagamit ito ng mga tao sa kasalukuyan upang ilarawan ang isang pagbuhos ng espiritu ng pamayanan - habang ibinibigay ng mga tao ang kanilang lahat sa karaniwang kabutihan, nang hindi inaasahan ang pagkilala o personal na pakinabang.

Panliligaw (Courtship)

Tayong mga Pilipino ay napaka romantikong pagdating sa usapin sa puso. Ang Serenading o Harana sa Tagalog ay isa sa pinakatanyag na uri ng panliligaw upang maipakita na ang isang lalaki ay seryoso sa kanyang hangarin sa isang babae. Ang isang serenade ay mangangailangan ng binata na kumanta ng isang love song sa harap ng bahay ng dalaga. Karaniwan, kasama niya ang kanyang mga lalaking kaibigan na kumikilos bilang back-up singers. Ang lalaki mismo o ang kanyang kaibigan ang tumugtog ng instrumento, karaniwang isang gitara, na nagbibigay ng background na musika sa kanyang kanta.

Pamahiin (Superstition)

Sa Pilipinas, lumago ang mga pamahiin na pamahiin sa buong bansa. Ang mga paniniwalang ito ay nagmula sa iba't ibang mga kasabihan at paniniwala ng ating mga ninuno na naglalayong maiwasan ang panganib na mangyari o upang pigilan ang isang tao na gumawa ng isang partikular na bagay.

They developed agricultural practices that allowed them to cultivate rice and other crops, which became staple foods in the Filipino diet. The early Filipinos also developed a strong sense of community and family, which is still evident in the present-day Filipino culture. They valued their traditions and customs, which are still celebrated today through various festivals and cultural events. The early Filipinos also developed a unique system of beliefs and practices, which gave rise to various forms of traditional healing and spiritual practices that are still practiced today. Additionally, the early Filipinos were influenced by various other cultures that interacted with them, such as Chinese, Malay, and Spanish cultures, which also left a lasting impact on Filipino culture. Overall, the early Filipino society had a profound influence on the way of life of the early Filipinos and played a significant role in shaping the culture and traditions of present-day Filipinos.

16. What important Filipino culture is presented in the tinikling?​


Answer:

TINIKLING

Tinikling is a traditional Philippine folk dance which originated during the Spanish colonial era. The dance involves two people beating, tapping, and sliding bamboo poles on the ground and against each other in coordination with one or more dancers who step over and in between the poles in a dance.

Explanation:

IMPORTANT

The Tinikling is an important dance to the Philippines in a variety of ways. Through unique props, clothing, and entertaining dance movements, it represents many of the values that are important to the Philippines such as nature and life.
