21. differentiate executive clemency, parole and probation based on the process, the granting authority, and the person who can avail of these program.​


Answer:

Executive Clemency: Process - involves a formal application to the President, who is the granting authority. Person who can avail - individuals who have been convicted of a crime. Parole: Process - involves a formal application to the parole board which is the granting authority. Person who can avail - individuals who have been convicted of a crime and have served a portion of their prison sentence in confinement. Probation: Process - involves a formal application to the court which is the granting authority. Person who can avail - individuals who have been charged with a crime but have not been convicted.

30. Is parole part of executive clemency? Why?​


Answer:

Executive Clemency” refers to Reprieve, Absolute Pardon, Conditional Pardon with or without Parole Conditions and Commutation of Sentence as may be granted by the President of the Philippines.

Answer:

Yes, parole is considered a part of executive clemency. Executive clemency refers to the power of the executive branch of government to pardon or commute the sentence of someone convicted of a crime. Parole is a form of conditional release granted to prisoners who have served a portion of their sentence, and it is typically granted by a parole board, which is a part of the executive branch of government. While parole is not technically a pardon or a commutation of sentence, it is still considered a form of executive clemency because it involves the conditional release of a prisoner before the expiration of their sentence.
