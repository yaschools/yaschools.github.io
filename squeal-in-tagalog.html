In Greek Mythology, Mnemosyne (pronounced neh-mow-zeen) was the Goddess of Memory and mother to the Nine Muses. She is considered one of the Titans, the predecessors to the Greek Gods, and those who originally created the universe. The daughter of the Titans, Uranus (Heaven) and Gaia (Earth), Mnemosyne represents individual and collective memory. She is often cited as the source of language and writing, particularly the role of memorization, on which much of early Greek culture depended.
