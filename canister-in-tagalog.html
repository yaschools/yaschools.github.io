2. The pressure of a gas in a sealed canister is 350.0kPa at a room temperature of 15oC. The canister is placed in a refrigerator that drops the temperature of the gas by 20K. What is the new pressure in the canister?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{350.0 kPa} \\ & T_1 = 15^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{288 K} \\ & T_2 = T_1 - \text{20 K} = \text{288 K} - \text{20 K} = \text{268 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Gay-Lussac's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{P_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_2}{T_2} \\ P_2T_1 & = P_1T_2 \\ \frac{P_2T_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ P_2 & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{350.0 kPa})(\text{268 K})}{\text{288 K}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{325.7 kPa}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new pressure in the canister is 325.7 kPa.

24. how many moles of gas are in a 30 L scuba canister if the temperature of the canister is 300K and the pressure is 200 atm


Hello!

How many moles of gas are in a 30 L scuba canister if the temperature of the canister is 300K and the pressure is 200 atm

We have the following data:

V (volume) = 30 L

n (number of moles) = ? (in moles)

T (temperature) = 300 K

P (pressure) = 200 atm

R (gas constant) = 0.082 atm.L / mol.K

We apply the data above to the Clapeyron equation (gas equation), let's see:

[tex]P*V = n*R*T[/tex]

[tex]200*30 = n*0.082*300[/tex]

[tex]6000 = 24.6\:n[/tex]

[tex]24.6\:n = 6000[/tex]

[tex]n = \dfrac{6000}{24.6}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{n \approx 243.90\:moles}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

The number of moles is approximately 243.90 moles
