7. if unemployment and underemployment are compared which would give a more serious repercussion on the economic growth, the former or the latter? why?


Answer:

Both unemployment and underemployment have negative effects on economic growth, but unemployment is generally considered to have a more serious repercussion.

Unemployment refers to individuals who are not currently employed and are actively seeking work. When there is a high rate of unemployment in a country, it means that there are a large number of people who are not contributing to the economy through productive work. This can lead to a decrease in consumer spending, a decrease in tax revenue, and a decrease in the overall economic output of the country.

On the other hand, underemployment refers to individuals who are employed but not working to their full potential, either because they are in part-time jobs, low-skilled jobs, or jobs that do not fully utilize their skills and education. While underemployment can still have negative effects on economic growth, it is generally considered to be less serious than unemployment because these individuals are still contributing to the economy to some extent.

Therefore, unemployment would give a more serious repercussion on the economic growth compared to underemployment because it directly affects the size of the workforce and the overall productivity of the economy.

Title: The Perils of Nationalism

Grabber: Good afternoon everyone. Today, I want to talk about a subject that has been the cause of both great triumphs and devastating tragedies throughout history: nationalism. Nationalism, the belief that one's country is superior to all others, has led to many conflicts and wars throughout history. It is a force that can inspire people to greatness and encourage them to work for the betterment of their country. However, when taken too far, nationalism can become a dangerous ideology that can lead to persecution, violence, and even genocide.

Introduction of the topic: Nationalism has been a significant force throughout history. It is a belief that is often instilled in us from an early age. Many of us grow up with a sense of pride in our country and a desire to protect it. However, when this pride turns into an excessive belief in the superiority of one's country, it can lead to dangerous consequences.

Body: Nationalism has had a significant impact on history, both positive and negative. On the positive side, nationalism has been the driving force behind many successful revolutions, such as the American and French Revolutions, which were fought to overthrow oppressive regimes and establish democratic governments. Nationalism has also played a crucial role in the struggle for civil rights and equality, as people have rallied around a common identity to fight for their rights.

However, the negative repercussions of nationalism cannot be ignored. The extreme form of nationalism, which is often associated with authoritarian regimes, can lead to devastating consequences. Examples of such regimes include Nazi Germany, which used nationalism to justify the persecution and murder of millions of Jews, and Rwanda, where ethnic nationalism led to the genocide of hundreds of thousands of Tutsis.

Conclusion: Nationalism is a complex and powerful force that has had both positive and negative effects on history. While it can inspire people to work towards the betterment of their country, it can also lead to persecution, violence, and even genocide. It is essential to recognize the dangers of extreme nationalism and strive towards a more inclusive and tolerant society.

Call to action statement: As individuals, we can work towards promoting a sense of unity and inclusivity within our communities. We must recognize that diversity is a strength and work towards building a society that is accepting of all people, regardless of their race, ethnicity, or nationality. Only by rejecting extreme forms of nationalism and embracing diversity can we build a better and more peaceful world.

Mindfulness and Peaceful Activism

Explanation:

In this era of extremism, facing an Internet-driven world where even basic facts seem distorted beyond being able to find even the most suitable simple agreement, the intentions behind mindfulness matter more than ever. While it seems like the word ‘mindfulness’ is becoming almost tiresome to some, the word itself is beside the point. Mindfulness doesn’t represent a cliché like be in the moment, it means building skills that allow for more intention and equilibrium while living through whatever we face.All of us get caught up on autopilot, driven by habits around emotions, our behavior, and also habitual assumptions. These unconscious patterns undermine our well-being and impact everyone around us too. We’re stressed and fearful, so we reactively lash out or shut down or blame or whatever else. Building traits that allow us to see life objectively, and to be more intentional in our choices, benefits us all — regardless of any stance as a Democrat, Republican, or otherwise.Everyone, even those we most disagree with, seeks happiness and relief from suffering. Taking a stand, we can still remain open to this perspective. Every action has repercussions, even ones we feel are completely just, so we better balance whatever needs to get done now with the impact it may have on the future. How, then, might basic concepts of mindfulness integrate with social action?· Get out of autopilot. The recent election was a moment of awakening for many. Having been complacent about politics, they now feel resolved to act. That same concept holds true regardless of which side of the election you were on — politicians count on us believing what they say at face value. Voting (or not voting), speaking (or not speaking), and acting (or not acting) purely out of habit, living life without awareness, makes us far less likely to make useful choices for ourselves and our country. Right now, today, what is something you could do to stand up for your beliefs?· See life exactly as it is. One of the most insidious aspects of how we think is that assumptions get labeled fact. Taking advantage of that tendency, a disturbing aspect of our Internet-driven culture is that viral stories spread instantly and are assumed real if they fit with our views — even when intentionally seeded with misinformation. Yet some basic facts actually hold true for everyone; for example, gravity works on earth. In your personal relationships, with your children, with your families, encourage everyone to step back and reexamine their basic assumptions. Around race, taxes, environment, health care, education, or anything else, pause, reflect, and seek out whatever objective reality is to be found. Beyond politics, hold ideas up to the light, and ask yourself of each belief you encounter, Is it true?· Practice responsiveness, instead of reinforcing reactivity. In any situation, we control only our own actions. Through them we either increase or decrease conflict. Even taking concrete steps that stand up for what we feel is right, intention and awareness of the larger picture can guide nuanced choices, but only if we stay out of autopilot and fear-driven reactivity. Protest and stand resolute, try to engage in conversation across the aisle, and through it all aim for agreement and collaboration instead of escalating conflict when possible.· Cultivate compassion. We’re all struggling to find happiness in life, however messed up our approach to there sometimes appears from the outside. Mindfulness builds from self-compassion, the capacity to approach ourselves with the same kindness we treat our best friend. When feeling beleaguered and beaten up, we become miserable, lose faith, and may even mistreat those around us. More than that, we actively seek to recognize the pain and suffering of everyone else, even those who disagree with us. While the intention of compassion is nothing more that compassion, it’s also true that relieved of fear and suffering, anyone’s perception of the world would shift. In essence, we protect ourselves when making life easier for someone else. Compassion of this kind doesn’t mean becoming a doormat; compassion for others and self-protection happen all together.How to move forward during these troubling times? Seeing life clearly as it is right now may mean taking direct action that stands up for whatever you feel right. At the same time, anger and strife lead to more anger and strife. Being baited into conflict strengthens divides and often builds resolve in your perceived opponent. Wake up, check in with reality as you best see it, and then take firm steps to care for our world — all while compassionately seeing that all of us in our own unique way suffers and seeks relief from suffering every day.
