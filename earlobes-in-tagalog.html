In representing a trait whether it is dominant or recessive you must know the basic terms first.

Terms you need to know:

Allele - a letter represents a genetic trait (a letter of your of choice)Dominant - It is often represtented by capital letters and this trait always shows. (Ex. TT or Tt = tall)Recessive - Represented by small letters and requires two identical alleles to determine the trait. (Ex. tt = dwarf)Phenotype - The physical trait. (Ex. Tall, short, blue eyes etc.)Genotype - The gene type of a person and represented by the alleles. ( Ex. GG, Gg, gg)Homozygous  - pure breed having two identical alleles. (Ex. GG or gg)Heterozygous - Hybrid which having two different alleles. (Ex. Aa)

And now for the dominant part (which is kinda pa special lol), there's a certain law that states that a single dominant allele (meaning isang capital na letter) is enough to masked off or hide the recessive trait. So basta may isang capital na letter matic na ang trait niya ay 'yung sa dominant — and this principle is called the law of dominance.

Eh paano po makikita 'yung recessive trait?

Recessive traits will only shows if there are two identical copies of recessive alleles. Since recessive alleles are often represented by small letter, both copies of allelle "aa, bb, cc" are needed to determine the trait. To put it simply, para masabing recessive, kailangan ng dalawang small letters para makita ang recessive traits.
