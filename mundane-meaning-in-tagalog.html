26. How can you find beauty in mundanity (5-10 sentences) Pasahot ASAP


Answer:

Finding beauty in mundanity is a matter of perspective and mindset. Here are a few practical ways to find beauty in mundanity:

Slow down: When we're rushing through our day, it's easy to miss the beauty that's all around us. By slowing down and taking the time to notice the small things, we can find beauty in the mundanity.

Look for patterns and symmetry: Mundane things can often have unexpected symmetry and patterns that are pleasing to the eye. Take a moment to notice these patterns and symmetry in everyday objects and scenes.

Appreciate the small things: Sometimes, the beauty in mundanity lies in the small things. Take a moment to appreciate the small details in your surroundings, like the way the light hits a leaf or the way a bird sings.

Practice gratitude: Gratitude is a powerful tool that can help you find beauty in mundanity. Take a moment each day to appreciate the things you often take for granted.

Create something: Creating something, whether it's art, music, or writing, can help you find beauty in mundanity. By putting your own perspective and creativity into the world, you can find beauty in the things around you.

By actively looking for beauty in mundanity and being mindful and appreciative of the world around us, we can find beauty in the everyday and make our lives more enjoyable


27. Which word best completes the sentence? Angelina thought the rain on her wedding day was a(n) _______ sign, but the day could not have gone more perfectly. A. mundane B. petulant C. accentuated D. ominous


The word that best completes the sentence is OMINOUS. It is ominous because of the context clues within the sentence. The context clues show that it must be related to weddings and rain and must be opposite to “perfect”.

Context CluesDefinition of Context Clues

Context clues are clues, hints, or signs found in a text, paragraph, or sentence that suggests a meaning of a new word. Context clues are either pointing out synonyms, antonyms, definitions, examples, or explanations.  

Examples of Context CluesSynonyms. It is two words that mean or closely mean the same thing.  (Chris is so pretentious, arrogant, and vain.)Antonyms. It is two words that mean or closely mean opposite things.  (When Vince met Rein, he thought he was the most pretentious person he has ever met. But when Vince got to know him, he was actually modest and humble.)Definition. It is a statement that is the precise meaning of a word.  (Paul made excessive claims about his properties. He is such a pretentious man.)Example. It is a thing that displays the standard or the usual model.  (Lee always wears fake luxury brands, such pretentious actions.)Explanation. It is a statement that conveys reason or clarification of something. (Everyone knew that Ray lied about his grades, thus everyone knows he is pretentious.)

Here is an example for connecting synonymous unfamiliar words in an excerpt from “The Raven” by Edgar Allan Poe (https://brainly.ph/question/1664288).

Now, let us figure out the word and the context clues for the sentence.

Angelina thought the rain on her wedding day was a (n) _______ sign, but the day could not have gone more perfectly.

Things to note:

Rain on a wedding day is believed to be a bad omen or sign. It is supposed to be bad luck. It is hinting toward ominous, from the root word omen.The word “but” was used. It means that the following phrase is the opposite of what Angelina originally thought it was going to be. Since the day was “perfect”, the word must be opposite to it which again points to ominous.To make sure, we check the meanings of the unfamiliar words. Mundane means dull. Petulant means are sulky. Accentuated means prominent. Ominous means are unpleasant.We conclude that the answer is ominous.
