16. what is one reason the Bolsheviks wanted to overthrow Russia's professional government in 1917


One reason the Bolsheviks wanted to overthrow Russia's professional government in 1917 was their belief in Marxist ideology and the idea of a proletarian revolution. The Bolsheviks believed that the existing government represented the interests of the ruling classes, the bourgeoisie, and that it was necessary to overthrow this government in order to establish a socialist state that would represent the interests of the working class. They saw the February Revolution, which had overthrown the monarchy and established a provisional government, as insufficient and believed that a more radical revolution was needed to achieve their goals. Additionally, the Bolsheviks opposed Russia's involvement in World War I and saw the war effort as a drain on the country's resources and a distraction from the revolution.
