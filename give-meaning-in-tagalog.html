4. give the meaning, part of speech and tagalog translate


noun / pangngalan - names a person, place, things, animals, events
adjective / pang-uri - describes the noun
pronoun / panghalip - replaces noun
preposition / pang-ukol - shows the connection between nouns, pronouns, and phrases to other words in the sentence
verb / pandiwa - expresses action, existence, action words 
adverb / pang-abay - describes verb, an adjective and and adverb
conjunction / pangatnig - connects words, phrases and sentences
article / pantukoy - signals the presence of a noun : a, an, the

10. 30 Tagalog words with meaning and examples. or you can at least give me 5 words Thank you.


Answer:

Bayani

The noun “bayani” stands for someone who is a hero or patriot. However, this isn’t just a normal hero; a Filipino “bayani” knows that it’s important to use their bravery, courage and kindness to further the human race or the cause of the larger group.

Magmuni-muni

Magmuni-muniThe verb “Magmuni-muni” means to ponder, speculate, muse or even meditate on something. This is a deep contemplation of something big. You might “magmuni-muni” on the state of global warming.

Maniwala

ManiwalaIn Tagalog, the verb “maniwala” means that you have a belief in something. This could be a belief in your nation or just confidence in what your friend is saying. For example, “Maniwalà ka man o hindî” means “whether you believe it or not.”

Kinaiya

Kinaiya“Kinaiya” is a noun that describes your inner character. These aren’t just your good traits either. It is all the traits or characteristics that make you who you are.

Maharlika

MaharlikaAs a noun, “maharlika” shows your noble character or that you are a noble warrior. The pronunciation of the word gives it an almost sing-song like quality.

Pahimakas

PahimakasTo say your final goodbye or last farewell, you would use the noun “pakimakas.” However, if you are parting or saying farewell, “pahimakas” can be used as an adjective too.

Tadhana

TadhanaYou can’t talk about words with powerful meaning without talking about your fate. The noun “tadhana” is a Tagalog word showing something that will come to be or a destiny beyond your control.

Timpi

TimpiThe noun “timpi” not only shows your moderation and self-control, but it can also be used to describe calm. As an adjective, “timpi” can be used to describe someone who is faithful or constant.

Magkaugnay

MagkaugnayNot only can “magkaugnay” be used as an adjective to show you are related to each other, but it also works to show an interconnectedness to someone.
