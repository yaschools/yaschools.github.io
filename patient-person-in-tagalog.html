17. A health worker surveyed 100 patients in a quarantine facility to find out how many patients experience the symptoms of COVID-19. 31 patients have high fever, 26 patients have dry cough, and 12 patients experience both symptoms. Find the probability of:Patients who have high fever only Patients who have dry cough only Patience who have same symptoms Patients who are asymptomatic​


Answer:

PA BRAINLIEST NAMAN PO PLEASE

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the probability, we first need to find the number of patients who have each specific symptom.

Number of patients who have high fever only = (Total patients with high fever) - (Patients with both symptoms) = 31 - 12 = 19

Number of patients who have dry cough only = (Total patients with dry cough) - (Patients with both symptoms) = 26 - 12 = 14

Number of patients who have same symptoms = Patients with high fever only + Patients with dry cough only + Patients with both symptoms = 19 + 14 + 12 = 45

Number of patients who are asymptomatic = Total patients - Patients with symptoms = 100 - (31 + 26 - 12) = 55

Therefore, the probabilities are:

Patients who have high fever only: 19/100 = 0.19 or 19%

Patients who have dry cough only: 14/100 = 0.14 or 14%

Patients who have same symptoms: 45/100 = 0.45 or 45%

Patients who are asymptomatic: 55/100 = 0.55 or 55%
