18. What is characteristics of xrays


Answer:

X-rays have waveform characteristics similar to other electromagnetic waves. Namely, they have velocity, wavelength, frequency and amplitude. When x-rays encounter matter, their characteristics are determined by the short wavelength of the radiation.

Explanation:

Answer:

Characteristic X-rays are produced when an element is bombarded with high-energy particles, which can be photons, electrons or ions (such as protons). When the incident particle strikes a bound electron (the target electron) in an atom, the target electron is ejected from the inner shell of the atom. After the electron has been ejected, the atom is left with a vacant energy level, also known as a core hole. Outer-shell electrons then fall into the inner shell, emitting quantized photons with an energy level equivalent to the energy difference between the higher and lower states. Each element has a unique set of energy levels, and thus the transition from higher to lower energy levels produces X-rays with frequencies that are characteristic to each element.[2]

21. He specializes in analyzing images such as xray


️A radiologist specializes in analyzing images such as x-ray.

About Radiologist

A radiology specialist or also known as a radiologist is a specialist doctor who focuses on radiological examinations, in order to detect, diagnose, and treat a disease using imaging procedures. These procedures are X-rays, CT scans, magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), nuclear medicine, and ultrasound.

To be able to hold the title of radiology specialist, a doctor must first take a radiology specialist education program. Radiology specialist education is the branch of medicine that uses radiation to scan the inside of the body, to detect and treat disease.

The Duties of a Radiology Specialist

In general, radiology specialists have several main tasks, namely:

• Determine the most effective and safe imaging test method for participants.

• Carry out radiological examinations with a radiographer (radiology technician).

• Analyze, evaluate, and read out the results of radiology examinations of participants.

• Determine the type of disorder and the severity of the patient's condition.

• Suggest follow-up examinations or treatment for patients, if needed
