15. distance from the heel of one foot to the heel of the other foot when walking​


Answer:

Step length is the distance between the heel contact point of one foot and that of the other foot. Stride length is the distance between the successive heel contact points of the same foot. Normally, stride length = 2 x step length. Cadence is the rate at which a person walk, expressed in steps per minute.

For your eyes

Eye cup

Lean forward with your elbows on your desk.

Cup your hands and place them lightly over your closed eyes. Hold for a minute, while breathing deeply in and out.

Slowly uncover and open your eyes. Repeat 2 times.

Eye roll

Close your eyes. Slowly roll your eyeballs clockwise all the way around. Repeat 3 times.

Eye rest

Every 20 minutes, look away from the computer screen. Focus on an object at least 20 feet away. Stay focused on this object for a full 20 seconds.

For your neck and head

Warm-up

Drop your head gently to your chest. While breathing in, slowly roll your head up to your left shoulder. While breathing out, slowly roll your head back to center. Repeat to the right.

Repeat 3 times on each side.

Head tilt

Sit up straight. Tuck in your chin.

Slowly tip your head to the left. Return to the center. Then, tip your head to the right.

Repeat 3 times on each side.

Head turn

Sit up straight.

Repeat 3 times on each side.

Folk dancing has five fundamental positions.The First Position. The feet are straightened outward and the heels are maintained together. Raise arms in front of the chest in a circle.The second Position. One foot separates the feet, and they are straight outward in a straight line. Sideways open arms with a lovely curvature lifted below shoulder level.Third position. The heels are positioned together, one in front of the other, and the feet are turned outward. Raise one arm aloft while keeping the other in the second position.Position number four. If you drew a line from one foot's heel to the other's toes, you'd have the sides of a square. The feet are separated by one foot and turned outward with one foot in front of the other, parallel to each other.In the fifth spot.
