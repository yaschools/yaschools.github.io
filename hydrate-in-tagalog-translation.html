24. A certain hydrate is found to have the composition 20.3% Cu, 8.95% Si, 36.3%F and 34.5%H2O. What is the EF of this hydrate?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{Cu}} = \text{20.3 g} \\ & mass_{\text{Si}} = \text{8.95 g} \\ & mass_{\text{F}} = \text{36.3 g} \\ & mass_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} = \text{34.5 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{Cu}} = \text{20.3 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{63.55 g}} = \text{0.319 mol} \\ & n_{\text{Si}} = \text{8.95 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{28.09 g}} = \text{0.319 mol} \\ & n_{\text{F}} = \text{36.3 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{19.00 g}} = \text{1.91 mol} \\ & n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} = \text{34.5 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{18.02 g}} = \text{1.91 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{Cu}_w\text{Si}_x\text{F}_y \: \cdot \: z\text{H}_2\text{O}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the moles of each element by the least number of moles.

In this case, the least number of moles is 0.319 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & w = \frac{\text{0.319 mol}}{\text{0.319 mol}} = 1 \\ & x = \frac{\text{0.319 mol}}{\text{0.319 mol}} = 1 \\ & y = \frac{\text{1.91 mol}}{\text{0.319 mol}} \approx 6 \\ & z = \frac{\text{1.91 mol}}{\text{0.319 mol}} \approx 6 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{CuSiF}_6 \: \cdot \: 6\text{H}_2\text{O}}[/tex]

Hence, the empirical formula of a hydrate is CuSiF₆ • 6H₂O.
