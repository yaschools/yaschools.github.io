Case 2:

Diversity can be simple or complex. Simple diversity refers to the visible differences among people, such as race, ethnicity, gender, age, and physical abilities. Complex diversity, on the other hand, refers to the less visible differences among people, such as their values, beliefs, and experiences.

-ang diversity ay maaaring simple o kaya naman ay complex. Ang simple diversity ay tumutukoy sa mga nakikitang pagkakaiba ng mga tao tulad ng kanilang lahi, etnisidad, kasarian, edad, at kakayahang pisikal. Ang complex diversity naman ay tumutukoy sa mga hindi nakikitang pagkakaiba ng mga tao tulad ng kanilang mga paniniwala, mga karanasan, at mga valores.

Case 5:

Diversity can have positive and negative effects on a group or organization. On the one hand, diversity can bring in new ideas, perspectives, and skills that can enhance creativity, innovation, and problem-solving. On the other hand, diversity can also lead to conflicts, misunderstandings, and discrimination.

-ang diversity ay maaaring magdala ng positibo at negatibong epekto sa isang grupo o organisasyon. Sa isang banda, ang diversity ay maaaring magdala ng mga bagong ideya, pananaw, at kasanayan na maaaring magdulot ng pagpapahusay sa paglikha ng mga bagong solusyon. Sa kabilang banda naman, ang diversity ay maaari ring magdulot ng mga alitan, hindi pagkakaintindihan, at diskriminasyon.

21. 1. A sentence is5. A complex sentence is​


1. A sentence is a set of words that is complete in itself, typically containing a subject and predicate, conveying a statement, question, exclamation, or command, and consisting of a main clause and sometimes one or more subordinate clauses.

2. A complex sentence is formed by adding one or more subordinate (dependent) clauses to the main (independent) clause using conjunctions and/or relative pronouns. A clause is a simple sentence. Simple sentences contain only one clause (verb group). Complex sentences contain more than one clause (verb group).

Answer :Simple Sentences:

1. The sky is blue.

2. The sun is bright.

3. I like to read.

4. She is happy.

Compound Sentences:

1. The sky is blue, but the sun is bright.

2. I like to read, and she likes to write.

3. He went to the store, and she went to the park.

4. She was tired, so she went to bed early.

Complex Sentences:

1. Although the sky was blue, the sun was still bright.

2. I like to read because it stimulates my mind.

3. She was happy because she accomplished her goal.

Compound-Complex Sentences:

1. The sky was blue, but the sun was still bright, and she was happy because she accomplished her goal.

3. She was tired, so she went to bed early, yet he stayed up late.

4. I went to the store to buy some food, and she went to the park to take a walk, but neither of us had enough money.
