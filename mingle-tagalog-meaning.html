23. What is the ability to mix and mingle diffrent types and habits of people ​


Answer and Explanation:

Mixing and mingling people from different types and habits can be an interesting, entertaining and rewarding experience. It opens up the opportunity to develop new relationships and discover a more diverse world. Here are five tips on how to do it right:

1) Mixing can mean breaking away from your usual way of socializing. For example, meeting someone at a bar instead of in the workplace is on the list of situations where you should change your routine up.

2) Find the person who wants to mix. This isn't someone who likes to hang out on the couch all night long. Instead, look for people with a passion and energy that want to get out and meet new people.
3) Show up as yourself. If you show up looking or acting like something you aren't, it will be obvious to others that you are not being genuine or authentic. Try not to do that unless you're 100% sure it's part of your personality or what brings out your true self.
4) Avoid cliques. Those who like to hang out in cliques usually can not stand others invading their territory. If you see cliques forming, find another group of people or look for someone to break out a clique with you.
5) Be excited about your surroundings and the people around you. If you are excited, positive and interested in the people around you, it will come through.

29. How did Jesus mingle with the poor and outcast in His time? What does it mean to be in touch with the poor and needy?​


How Jesus Mingled with the Poor and Outcast in His Time

An example of the ways that showed how Jesus mingled with the poor and outcast in His time is when He proclaimed the Good News to them. He did not only speak to those who are well-off, scholars, or Pharisees, but He made Himself available to all. Another good example is when He ate with tax collectors and sinners. In their time, tax collectors and sinners are considered outcasts of society.

Additional Ways on How Jesus Mingled with the Poor and Outcast in His Time

Below are some more ways on how Jesus mingled with the poor and outcast in His time:

He touched and cleansed the lepers who are considered unclean.Jesus spoke Good News even to the Samaritans who were enemies of the Jews. He healed a bleeding woman who lost all her properties to doctors who cannot make her well.

What It Means to Be in Touch with the Poor and Needy

According to Jesus which He has spoken in the Bible, what it means to be in touch with the poor and needy includes the following (and many more):

You should give not only to your relatives or friends who can repay you but also to the poor, crippled, lame, and blind because they cannot repay you but the Lord will. Love your enemies - this may include the ones you don't like and outcasts of society - because in so doing, you live up to becoming a child of God.

In other words, to be in touch with the poor and needy gives the following qualities:

Having compassionBeing generous and merciful Being kind and justTreating others equallyShowing care and empathy
