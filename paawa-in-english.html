Answer:

1. The Enhance Basic Education Act of 2013 (K-12 Law) states that curriculum should be 'culture sensitive", "contextualized and global." To which guiding principles do these relate? Explain.

The three principles mentioned in the K-12 Law, “culture sensitive”, "contextualized" and "global" relate to the guiding principle of teaching based on respect for diversity and differing cultures. In line with this principle, teachers should create an environment that values the heritage and culture of all students, be aware of the cultural backgrounds and perspectives of their students, and incorporate culture into their curriculum by making it applicable to students’ lives and interests. Furthermore, the curriculum should be global in its perspective, taking into account ideas and perspectives from around the world.

2. "Don't handicap your children by making their lives easy," of Robert A. Heinlein, shows what guiding principle/s in teaching of AP?

The quote from Robert A. Heinlein shows the guiding principle of teaching that allows children to develop independence and autonomy. This is based on the idea that teachers should not over-structure the learning environment, but rather encourage students to take an active role in their own learning. Teachers should also promote problem-solving and critical thinking skills in their classrooms, as well as provide opportunities for exploration and discovery. Ultimately, these teaching principles encourage children to develop their own ideas and interests and to become self-directed learners.
