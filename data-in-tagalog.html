Answer:

Southeast Asia is a geographically expansive and populous region characterized by fascinating social and cultural variation. Particularly striking to the outside observer is the region’s ethnic and religious diversity. The majority of the countries in this region are home to dozens of different ethnic groups (and in some cases, hundreds), many with their own distinct languages, cultures, and styles of dress. Many of these groups have their own systems of religious belief and practice as well. Groups living in highland areas tend to follow religions involving animistic beliefs and practices, which are based on the idea that spiritual or supernatural powers organize and animate the natural world or material universe. In animistic traditions, plants, animals, and other natural phenomena (mountains, streams, etc.) are assumed to have souls or to be inhabited by spirits. 25. Differentiate the categories of computers, According to purpose, According to types of data handled and According to size or capacity. Explain Briefly (tagalog or english)​


Answer:

According to purpose:

-General purpose computers: These are computers that can be used for a variety of tasks. They can be used for word processing, spreadsheets, email, and browsing the internet, among other things.

-Special purpose computers: These are computers that are designed to perform a specific task. Examples include cash registers, industrial robots, and point-of-sale systems.

According to types of data handled:

-Analog computers: These computers process analog data, which is data that is continuous in nature. Analog data can include things like temperature, pressure, and sound.

-Digital computers: These computers process digital data, which is data that is discrete in nature. Digital data can include things like numbers, text, and images.

According to size or capacity:

-Mainframe computers: These are the largest and most powerful computers. They are typically used by large organizations for mission-critical applications.

-Midrange computers: These are smaller than mainframes but more powerful than personal computers. They are typically used by medium-sized organizations for applications such as database servers and email servers.

-Personal computers: These are the smallest and least powerful computers. They are typically used by individuals for tasks such as word processing and browsing the internet.
