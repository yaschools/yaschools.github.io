1. Set boundaries: It is important to set boundaries for yourself and your relationships, especially when it comes to social media. Decide how much time you want to spend on social media and when you want to disconnect.

2. Communicate effectively: Effective communication is key to maintaining healthy relationships. Make sure you are clear and concise with your words and actively listen to others.

3. Practice empathy: Try to understand the perspective of others and put yourself in their shoes. This can help you build stronger connections and foster healthy relationships.

4. Be honest: Honesty is crucial in any relationship. Be truthful with yourself and others about your feelings and intentions.

5. Practice self-care: Take care of yourself physically, mentally, and emotionally. When you feel good about yourself, you are better equipped to maintain healthy relationships.

6. Be present: Be present in the moment and give your full attention to the people in your life. Avoid the temptation to constantly check your phone or social media.

7. Respect differences: Everyone is different, and that is okay. Respect the differences in others, and try to find common ground.

8. Avoid comparison: Social media can create a culture of comparison. Remember that everyone's journey is different, and try not to compare yourself to others.

9. Nurture your relationships: Relationships require effort and attention. Make time for the people in your life and show them that you care.

10. Seek help if needed: If you are struggling to maintain healthy relationships, don't be afraid to seek help from a trusted friend, family member, or professional.
