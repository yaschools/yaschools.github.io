8. it is better to keep pace with the desire of a people than to give way before them; the former begets sympathy and love, the latter contempt and anger". What does the qoutation remind you of?​


This quote reminds me of the importance of considering and respecting the desires and wishes of others, rather than imposing one's own will or ignoring the needs and preferences of others. It suggests that by taking the time to listen to and understand the perspectives of others, we can build trust and goodwill, rather than causing resentment or anger.

The quote could be applied in many different contexts, including relationships, leadership, and community building. In any situation where one person or group is interacting with another, it is important to be mindful of the feelings and needs of the other party, and to try to find a balance between asserting one's own interests and being responsive to the needs and desires of others.

27. concern for others begets concern ​


The statement says that if we show concern for others, we also receive concern from others.  Basically, whatever we give, we also receive.  In contrast, what we feed ourselves with are the things we are able to give towards other people.  

For example, if we show compassion for other people, we also are shown compassion.  Same goes with respect.  Our good deeds result to good responses as well.  Moreover, if we are filled with kindness, most likely, we will also be shown kindness from those we encounter along the journey of our lives.  As the best cliché goes, what you sow is what you reap.
