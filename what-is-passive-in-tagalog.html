9. 1. What is the active voice? 2. What is the passive voice? 3. What is the difference between active and passive voice? 4. How to transform active to passive voice and vice versa? ​


Answer:

1.) The active voice describes a sentence where the subject performs the action stated by the verb. It follows a clear subject + verb + object construct that's easy to read.

2.) Passive voice produces a sentence in which the subject receives an action.

3.) At the most basic level, the active voice emphasizes the person or agent who performs an action, in short, the “actor.” The passive voice emphasizes the recipient of the action or sometimes the action itself.

4.) Rules for changing Active voice to Passive voice: Simply exchange the places of the subject and the object. The subject should become the object and vice-e-versa while changing a sentence from Active to Passive voice or reverse.

17. What is passive voice


Voice is the property of the verb that shows whether the subject is the doer or the receiver of the action in the sentence.

   A verb is in the active voice when the subject does the action.
 
       e.g. I did the script.
 
   A verb is in the passive voice when the action is done to the subject. In other words, the subject receives the action.
 
      e.g. The script was done because the girl hates cramming.
