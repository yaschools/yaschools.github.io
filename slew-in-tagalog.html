29. Slew rate determines how fast the output can change in response to a step input.


Answer:

The slew rate of an op amp or any amplifier circuit is the rate of change in the output voltage caused by a step change on the input. It is measured as a voltage change in a given time - typically V/ us or V/ ms. A typical general purpose device may have a slew rate of 10 V/ microsecond.
