4. Find the volume of water in a horizontal cylindrical tank if it is 7/8 full. Radius of the tank is 600mm and the length of the tank is 5m if the tank is placed in the vertical position, what is the depth of water inside the tank.


vol of tank   =   πr²h    =   π (0.6)² 5
vol of water   =   7/8 π (0.6)² . 5
   let h  = depth of water when the tank is placed vertically
  thus    π (0.6)² h  =   7/8 π(0.6)² .5
             h  =   7/8 π(0.6)² .5/ π(0.6)²
                  =   7/8 .5 =   4.375m

24. tank A has 26.1 L More water than tank B. after 8.5 L of water from tank b to tank A. tank A has thrice as much water as tank B. how much water was in tank A at first?


Answer:

56.15 L

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

- Tank A has 26.1 L more water than tank B:

a = b + 26.1

- After 8.5 L of water (was transferred) from tank B to tank A, tank A has thrice as much water as tank B:

a + 8.5 = 3(b - 8.5)


Solution:

1. Insert the value of a from the 1st equation in the 2nd equation:

(b + 26.1) + 8.5 = 3(b - 8.5)

2. Solve for b.

b + 26.1 + 8.5 = 3b - 25.5

b + 34.6 = 3b - 25.5

34.6 + 25.5 = 3b - b

60.1 = 2b

b = 30.05

3. Insert the value of b in the 1st equation, then solve for a:

a = 30.05 + 26.1

a = 56.15

Tank A contained 56.15 L of water at first.


Proof:
