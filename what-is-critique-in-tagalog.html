What is critique?

A critique is a careful analysis of an argument to determine what is said, how well the points are made, what assumptions underlie the argument, what issues are overlooked, and what implications are drawn from such observations. It is a systematic, yet personal response and evaluation of what you read.

Doing so will help you develop more balanced arguments, express yourself clearly, read critically, and absorb information efficiently.

16. 1.What I know about writing a critique 2.what i want to learn about writing a critique 3.what i learned about writing a critique​


Answer:

1. What I know about writing a critique:

Writing a critique is an evaluation of a piece of work or a performance. It involves analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of the work, identifying the author's message or purpose, and providing constructive feedback on how the work can be improved. When writing a critique, it is important to be objective and support your opinions with evidence from the work. Additionally, a critique should be written in a clear and concise manner.

2. What I want to learn about writing a critique:

There are several aspects of writing a critique that I would like to learn more about, such as:

How to provide feedback in a way that is constructive and helpful to the author

How to identify the author's intentions and message, even if they are not explicitly stated

How to balance criticism with praise and acknowledge the strengths of the work

How to structure a critique in a clear and organized manner

3. What I learned about writing a critique:

After conducting research and practicing writing critiques, I have learned several key points about writing a critique:

It is important to approach the work with an open mind and avoid letting personal biases or opinions cloud your judgement.

Providing specific examples from the work can help to support your opinions and make your critique more persuasive.

When identifying weaknesses, it is important to provide suggestions for improvement rather than simply criticizing the work.

A well-structured critique should include an introduction, a summary of the work, an evaluation of the strengths and weaknesses, and a conclusion.

Tagalog Version:

Pagsusuri sa "Talambuhay ng Isang Bata" ni Jose Garcia Villa

Ang "Talambuhay ng Isang Bata" ni Jose Garcia Villa ay isang kwentong naglalarawan ng mga karanasan ng isang binatang nais magpakasal sa murang edad. Sa kabila ng kanyang mga pangarap, nagpapakita ang kuwento ng mga kahirapan na nakapalibot sa mga taong nag-aasam ng madaliang pagpapakasal.

Sa aking palagay, isa sa mga kahinaan ng kwentong ito ay ang pagiging di-tumpak ng pagkakalahad. Dahil sa kawalan ng malinaw na pagpapakilala ng mga tauhan, mahirap para sa mambabasa na maunawaan ang kanilang mga motibasyon at karanasan. Bukod pa rito, may mga bahagi ng kuwento na tila napakabilis ng mga pangyayari at hindi sapat na napapaliwanag, tulad ng pagpapakasal ni Blas at Tona sa kabila ng kanilang mga magulang na nagtutol.

Gayunpaman, hindi maikakaila na may mga tagpo sa "Talambuhay ng Isang Bata" na nagpapakita ng mas malalim na kahulugan. Halimbawa, ang pagkakaiba sa mga pangangailangan ng magkarelasyon ay isang mahalagang mensahe na ipinapakita ng kwento. Ipinapakita rin nito ang epekto ng masyadong maagang pagpapakasal sa buhay ng isang tao.

Sa pangkalahatan, bagamat may mga kahinaan, nakapagbigay pa rin ng matututuhan ang "Talambuhay ng Isang Bata". Ipinapakita nito ang mga epekto ng masyadong maagang pagpapakasal at ang kahalagahan ng pag-iisip ng mabuti bago tumugon sa mga pangangailangan ng pagmamahal.

English Version:

Critique of "Footnote to Youth" by Jose Garcia Villa

"Footnote to Youth" by Jose Garcia Villa is a story that portrays the experiences of a young man who desires to get married at a young age. Despite his dreams, the story depicts the hardships that surround those who seek early marriage.

In my opinion, one weakness of this story is its imprecise presentation. Due to the lack of clear character introductions, it is difficult for readers to understand their motivations and experiences. Additionally, there are parts of the story that seem to move too quickly and are not adequately explained, such as Blas and Tona's decision to get married despite their parents' objections.

However, it cannot be denied that "Footnote to Youth" has some deeper meanings. For example, the difference in the needs of a relationship is an important message that the story conveys. It also shows the effects of early marriage on a person's life.

Overall, despite its weaknesses, "Footnote to Youth" still provides valuable lessons. It portrays the effects of early marriage and emphasizes the importance of thinking carefully before responding to the needs of love.


19. what is independent critique​


Answer:

What is a critique?

A critique is a genre of academic writing that briefly summarises and critically evaluates a work or concept. Critiques can be used to carefully analyse a variety of works such as:

Creative works – novels, exhibits, film, images, poetry

Research – monographs, journal articles, systematic reviews, theories

Media – news reports, feature articles

Like an essay, a critique uses a formal, academic writing style and has a clear structure, that is, an introduction, body and conclusion. However, the body of a critique includes a summary of the work and a detailed evaluation. The purpose of an evaluation is to gauge the usefulness or impact of a work in a particular field.
