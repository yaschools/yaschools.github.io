If your price is too low, you will not make much of a profit. If your price is too high, you will also probably not make much of a profit because fewer people will buy what you are selling. The best price is the price that leads to the maximum profit. Suppose Ka Ditha's profit y can be found by y = x²+24x 60, where x represents the price of each Suman Murwekos. What price should she charge to receive the maximum profit?​

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the price that will maximize Ka Ditha's profit, we need to find the maximum value of the profit function y = x^2 + 24x - 60. This can be done by finding the vertex of the parabola defined by this function. The vertex of the parabola is at the point (-b/2a, f(-b/2a)), where a, b, and c are the coefficients of the quadratic function in the standard form y = ax^2 + bx + c. In this case, a = 1, b = 24, and c = -60, so the vertex is at (-24/2, -24^2/4 - 24(-24/2) - 60) = (-12, 36). The maximum value of the function is therefore 36, which occurs at x = -12. Therefore, Ka Ditha should charge P12 for each Suman Murwekos to maximize her profit.
