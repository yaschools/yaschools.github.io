12. what is the meaning of officical seal of catanduanes


The official seal of Catanduanes is a symbol that represents the identity and history of the province. It features several elements that depict the natural resources, cultural heritage, and achievements of Catanduanes.

At the center of the seal is the image of the Philippine eagle, which represents the province's pride and protection of its natural resources. Surrounding the eagle is a body of water, which symbolizes the island's surrounding waters and the fishing industry that is an important source of livelihood for the people.

The coconut tree, which is one of the major crops in the province, is also depicted in the seal. The mountain ranges represent the island's rugged terrain, while the rice fields and cogon grass represent the agricultural and pastoral lands.

Above the seal is the name of the province, Catanduanes, while the bottom part bears the words "Land of the Howling Winds." This pertains to the province's location in the eastern seaboard, where it is frequently hit by typhoons and storms.

Short answer: The official seal of Catanduanes serves as a visual representation of the province's natural beauty, cultural heritage, and resilience.

15. Meaning of official seal in navotas


The fish formed as letter “S” and “J” represents the Barangay’s initials “San Jose”. The Officials agreed to choose a “fish” for the reason that, Navotas is considered to be a very important fishing community with 70% of its population deriving their livelihood directly or indirectly from fishing and its related industries.

The façade of San Jose de Navotas Parish Church was incorporated on the Logo for the reason that it is considered to be the biggest Landmark of the Barangay. The 28 stars represent the streets that is under the jurisdiction of Barangay San Jose.

While the seven fishes beneath the seal, act as the Sangguniang Barangay Members (Council), and the fish at the lower center of the artwork represents the Barangay Captain.

23. QUEZON CITY SEAL MEANING


Answer:

Greatness, Incomparable Leadership, and Educational Development

Explanation:

In summary, the Quezon City seal main components stands for Manuel L. Quezon's Greatness, and Incomparable Leadership and the city's Educational Development.

Learn more about Manuel L. Quezon here: https://brainly.ph/question/37473

The current seal has a yellow background and white triangle frames in the middle. Inside the frame contains the Quezon Memorial Shrine at the center, divided by a red and blue field at the back. Within the red divider is the gavel that represents MLQ's incomparable leadership, and on the blue divider is Lamp of Knowledge.

Within the white triangular frames are the words, "LUNGSOD QUEZON" in all caps, with 3 stars within the texts that over-all describes the 3 main components.

At the bottom inside the white frame contains the words, "Kalakhang Maynila" (Metro Manila). Originally, it was "Punong Bayan ng Pilipinas" as Quezon City was the former capital of the Philippines until the Presidential Decree of former President Ferdinand Marcos on May 1976, then it was changed to Republika ng Pilipinas, then eventually changed to Kalakhang Maynila.

Overall, the colors of the Quezon City seal is also patterned to the colors of the Philippine National Flag.
