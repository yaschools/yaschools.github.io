9. ibigay ang description ngCrust-Mantle-Outer core-Inner core-(in tagalog po)​


Answer:

“Crust” describes the outermost shell of a terrestrial planet. ... Earth has three layers: the crust, the mantle, and the core. The crust is made of solid rocks and minerals. Beneath the crust is the mantle, which is also mostly solid rocks and minerals, but punctuated by malleable areas of semi-solid magma.

The mantle is the mostly-solid bulk of Earth's interior. The mantle lies between Earth's dense, super-heated core and its thin outer layer, the crust. The mantle is about 2,900 kilometers (1,802 miles) thick, and makes up a whopping 84% of Earth's total volume.

Earth's outer core is a fluid layer about 2,400 km (1,500 mi) thick and composed of mostly iron and nickel that lies above Earth's solid inner core and below its mantle. Its outer boundary lies 2,890 km (1,800 mi) beneath Earth's surface. ... Unlike the inner (or solid) core, the outer core is liquid.

The inner core is a hot, dense ball of (mostly) iron. It has a radius of about 1,220 kilometers (758 miles). Temperature in the inner core is about 5,200° Celsius (9,392° Fahrenheit). ... The inner core's intense pressure—the entire rest of the planet and its atmosphere—prevents the iron from melting.

23. Explain this WordMantle outer MantleInner MantleCoreOuter Core Inner Core​need explanation


The Mantle: The mantle is the largest layer of the Earth. It is 2900km thick. It includes the lithosphere and athenosphere. It is relatively flexible—it flows like very viscous liquid.

Outer Mantle: The lithosphere is the solid, outer part of Earth, including the brittle upper portion of the mantle and the crust.

Inner Mantle: The athenosphere is the part of the mantle underneath the lithosphere. It is made of molten rock and metal so that it "flows” like hot asphalt. The athenosphere is the part of the mantle that moves and causes the tectonic plates of the crust to move as well.

Core: The core of the Earth is like a ball of very hot metals. The core is divided into 2 layers—Outer and Inner. The core is VERY hot and has SO much pressure that if you were to go there—you would be squished into something even smaller than a marble.

Outer Core: The outer core is liquid metal iron and nickel with 10% sulfur and/or oxygen. The outer core is 2,250km thick and has a temperature of 4000-9000 degree fahrenheit.

Inner Core: The inner core is solid metal due to the extreme heat and pressure. It is composed of iron and nickel. It is responsible for the magnetic field the Earth generates. The inner core is 800km thick and is 9000 degree fahrenheit.

27. why is it advisable to place tissue paper over the inner hoop or wrap the inner hoop with a thin material?​


Answer:

Have you ever been stitching along, and suddenly you realize that the fabric in your hoop has loosened and the tension is no longer even? Some hoops prevent this better than others, but even a basic wooden hoop can hold your fabric evenly if you add a binding.

Some stitches customize the outside of their hoops with some wrapping for decorative purposes (think of it as painting hoops, but with fabric!), but binding a hoop is like doing the same thing with a hidden purpose.

You may have seen hoops that have binding on them and wondered why one or both pieces of the hoop have a wrapping. Keeping even tension is one reason why, but another benefit is that the binding helps cushion the embroidery fabric and minimizes damaging creases.

The cushioning is helpful when you need to move your hoop around on a larger piece and the hoop will go over completed stitches. In these situations, wrapping both hoops is usually a good idea.  

While many stitchers choose to buy more expensive hoops to keep their fabric held tightly, it's good to have a fast and easy way to make a simple wood hoop into a better-functioning tool. Not to mention, it brings new life to vintage hoops, making them suitable for framing embroidery and keeping your work held in place.
