1.Light

Denotative: A. Something that makes vision possible.

B. The sensation aroused by stimulation of the visual receptors.

C. Electromagnetic radiation of any wavelength that travels in a vacuum with a speed of 299,792,458 meters (about 186,000 miles) per second specifically; such radiation that is visible to the human eye.

Connotative: Light is a source of illumination, whether a natural one (like the sun) or an artificial one (like your lamp). Like light itself, the word can take a lot of different forms- it cam be a noun, an adjective, or a verb, and it can mean "bright" or "not heavy".

2.Blue

Denotative: Blue (color blue)

Connotative: Blue (feeling sad)

3.Twilight

Denotative: The light from the sky between full night and sunrise or between sunset and full night produced by diffusion of sunlight through the atmosphere and it's dust also : a time of twilight.

Connotative: An intermediate state that is not clearly defined lived in the twilight of neutrality.

4.Banana

Denotative: A banana is a curved, yellow fruit with a thick skin and soft sweet flesh.

Connotative: To "go bananas" is a slangy way of saying "go crazy" or act ridiculous, and if someone refers to the "top banana", they mean the most important person in a particular group.

5.Storm

Denotative: Just means literal meaning. So a "storm" is an event of precipitation, often accompanied by wind.

Connotation: The feeling and association of the word "storm". It generally has a negative, hectic connotation, and is associated sadness, chaos, hardship or foreboding omens.

21. Positive connotation and negative connotation


Positive connotation refers to the emotional association or connection that a word has with something that is considered good or desirable. Negative connotation refers to the emotional association or connection that a word has with something that is considered bad or undesirable. For example, the word "home" has a positive connotation, as it is associated with warmth, security, and comfort. The word "hovel," on the other hand, has a negative connotation, as it is associated with squalor and poverty.

Ang Denotasyon at Konotasyon

Denotasyon ay ang kahulugan ng salita. Ito din ay literal o ito ang totoong kahulugan ng salita

Konotasyon ay ang mga salita o parilala na malalim na kahulugan.

30. meaning in denotation and Connotation in tagalog​


Answer:

Explanation:

﻿denotation

Ang konotasyon ay isang salita na naglalaman ng matalinghagang kahulugan o hindi isang tunay na salita. Samantala, ang denotasyon ay isang salita na may tunay na kahulugan at tulad ng ginagamit natin araw-araw.

Madalas tayong makatagpo ng mga konotasyon sa isang tula, maikling kuwento, at ilang iba pang akdang pampanitikan ng sining. Ang pagkakaroon ng konotasyong ito ay naglalayong pagandahin ang pagpapahayag ng pangungusap sa isang salita. Samantala, ang denotasyon ay napakadaling mahanap dahil ito ay isang salita na aktwal na nakasulat sa isang pangungusap.

Mga katangian ng mga salitang may denotasyon ng kahulugan :

Ang kahulugan ng salita kung ano ito.Ang kahulugan ng salita ayon sa mga resulta ng mga obserbasyon.Kahulugan na direktang nagpapakita sa sanggunian o pangunahing kahulugan.

Katangian ng mga Salitang may Konotasyon :

Ang kahulugan ay hindi totoo.Karagdagang mga kahulugan na itinalaga sa isang konseptong kahulugan.Karagdagang kahulugan sa anyo ng halaga ng lasa.
