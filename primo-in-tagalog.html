20. covered under general bernardo primo de vera​


Answer:

hello :>

Explanation:

Fernando Primo de Rivera y Sobremonte, 1st Marquess of Estella, 12th Count of Peña Vélez, 17th Count of Torres Rovellas, 23rd Count of Sobremonte (24 July 1831 – 23 May 1921) was a Spanish politician, and soldier.

Fernando Primo de Rivera was the son of Antonio Hermenegildo Primo de Rivera y Sobremonte, 22nd Count of Sobremonte (1787–1833) and his wife Ana María de Torres Rovellas y Peña Vélez (1811–1865); she was the daughter of Miguel de Torres Rovellas y Peña Vélez, 13th Count of Torres Rovellas, 8th Marquess of Peña Vélez (1764–1851). His grandfather was Bértrand Primo de Rivera (1741–1813), 21st Count of Sobremonte: he was a Spanish general under the Spanish Resistance against Napoleon Bonaparte.

He served in several wars, including the 1848 and 1866 Madrid insurrections and the second Carlista War. When forces under his command in the second Carlist War captured Estella, he was named Marquess of Estella. He was the Spanish Governor-General of the Philippines from 1880 to 1883. In 1897, he again became the Spanish Governor-General of the Philippines. He temporarily suspended hostilities in the Philippine Revolution through negotiations with Emilio Aguinaldo in the Pact of Biak-na-Bato and acted briefly as Governor-General of the Philippines. He was a Minister and the 70th Prime Minister of Spain for one day between 30 and 31 December 1874. He was created the 1st Marquess of Estella on 25 May 1877, the 1,124th Knight of the Spanish Order of the Golden Fleece and the 287th Grand Cross of the Royal Order of Our Lady of the Concepcion of Vila Viçosa of Portugal in 1879.

He was married in Sevilla on 18 June 1857 to María del Pilar Arias-Quiroga y Escalera (Sevilla, Alcolea del Río, c. 1835 – 10 May 1894), 745th Dame of the Royal Order of Queen María Luisa, daughter of Juan Arias-Quiroga y Mejías, 7th Marquess of Arias-Quiroga and his wife María Manuela de Escalera y Fernández de Peñaranda, daughter of Roberto de Escalera y Fernandez de Peñarada, 46th Lord of Peñaranda, by whom he had no issue. He was also the uncle of Miguel Primo de Rivera, the Spanish dictator.

23. The primo viaggio intorno al mondo's main topic


Answer:

The Report of the first voyage around the world is the account of Ferdinand Magellan's expedition , drawn up by Antonio Pigafetta and published between 1524 and 1525 .

Explanation:

Written in a bizarre Italian - Venetian language mixed with Spanish words , it is the most important document among all those that have come down to today relating to the historic enterprise. It is in fact the only complete report that offers uninterrupted information from departure to return; but his interest is given above all by the extensive and precise descriptions of the places - especially those, such as Patagonia , the Philippines and the Moluccas , where Pigafetta made a long abode. To reach the legendary island of spices it was necessary to cross El Paso, a strait that did not exist in the approximate cartography of the time, but clearly depicted in Magellan's vision.

28. kontibyuson ni Fernando primo de Rivera​


Fernando Primo de Rivera

Nagsilbi siya sa maraming mga digmaan, kabilang ang mga pag-alsa noong 1848 at 1866 sa Madrid at ang pangalawang Digmaang Carlista. Nang ang mga puwersa sa ilalim ng kanyang utos sa ikalawang Carlist War ay nakuha si Estella, siya ay pinangalanang Marquess ng Estella. Siya ang Gobernador-Heneral ng Espanya ng Pilipinas mula 1880 hanggang 1883.

Noong 1897, siya ay muling naging Gobernador-Heneral ng Espanya ng Pilipinas. Pansamantalang isinuspinde niya ang poot sa Rebolusyong Pilipino sa pamamagitan ng negosasyon kasama si Emilio Aguinaldo sa Pact ng Biak-na-Bato at kumilos sandali bilang Gobernador-Heneral ng Pilipinas. Siya ay isang Ministro at ang ika-70 Punong Ministro ng Espanya para sa isang araw sa pagitan ng 30 at 31 ng Disyembre 1874.

Nilikha siya ng 1st Marquess ng Estella noong Mayo 25, 1877, ang 1,124th Knight ng Order ng Espanya ng Golden Fleece at ang 287th Grand Krus ng Royal Order ng Our Lady of the Concepcion ni Vila Viçosa ng Portugal noong 1879.
