St. Josephine Bakhita was a Sudanese-born former slave who lived in Italy in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. She is recognized as a Christian saint and is celebrated on February 8th.

St. Josephine Bakhita's life was a testament to her unwavering faith in God and her commitment to serving others. Despite enduring unspeakable suffering and trauma as a slave, she never lost hope or faith in God's love and mercy. After gaining her freedom, she became a member of the Canossian Sisters, a Catholic religious congregation dedicated to serving the poor and disadvantaged.

As a Canossian Sister, St. Josephine Bakhita lived a life of humble service and selflessness. She worked tirelessly to help others, especially children who were orphaned or in need of care. Her gentle and loving nature earned her the nickname "Mother Moretta" (which means "little black mother") from those who knew her.

In addition to her acts of service, St. Josephine Bakhita was also known for her deep prayer life and devotion to God. She would often spend hours in prayer and contemplation, seeking guidance and strength from the Lord.

Through her life of service and devotion, St. Josephine Bakhita demonstrated what it means to be a Christian witness. She showed that faith in God can sustain us through even the most difficult trials, and that true happiness comes from serving others and living a life of love and humility.
