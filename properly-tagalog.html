Answer:

Independent Practice:

Directions: Give your belief in the superstitions as shown in the given viewing materials.

1. (Usog) -  when a sudden or unwanted compliment "bati" was given by somebody who radiates strong energy, an ailment or discomfort will occur to the person who received the usog.

For the person to heal, the "nagbati/bumati" should rub their saliva on the belly button of the person who received the "usog"

2. ( Friday the 13th ) - rooted to the last supper, where 13 people were present. held on Maundy Thursday, including Jesus and his 12 apostles (one of whom, Judas, betrayed him).

Thus also rooting the Good Friday, and the crucifixion of Christ happened because the 13th person, Judas betrayed Jesus.

3. (Avoid cutting your nails at night) - A japanese superstition where it believes that our nails are part of our life, and cutting them at night makes us vulnerable to the entities and the spirits that could hurt us or bring bad luck.

4. (Wearing a polka dots on new year) - polka dots resemble the coins which is also a symbol of abundance. Wearing clothes with polka dots design on them is a way to attract wealth and let abundance prosper for the whole year that will come.

5. ( Knock when the lizard clacks) - Lizards are creatures who are also connected by nature. Lizards clacking, also referred as a knock means they are bringing good luck and blessing inside the house, greeting them back with a knock means that we are acknowledging their presence and the energy they want to bring inside the house.

Or it could also be attached to another superstition, "knocking on wood to avoid the bad luck". Animals are known to have predictions, and hearing them clacking is similar to the warning of elders.
