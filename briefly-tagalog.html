1. Exosphere. The exosphere is the most distant atmospheric region from Earth's surface.

2. The ionosphere stretches roughly 50 to 400 miles above Earth's surface, right at the edge of space. Along with the neutral upper atmosphere, the ionosphere forms the boundary between Earth's lower atmosphere — where we live and breathe — and the vacuum of space.

3. The mesosphere is the least-understood part of Earth's atmosphere. It is too high for aircraft or weather balloons to operate, but too low for spacecraft

4. Stratosphere. The next layer up is called the stratosphere. The stratosphere extends from the top of the troposphere to about 50 km (31 miles) above the ground. The infamous ozone layer is found within the stratosphere.

5. Above: The space shuttle orbits in the thermosphere, a tenuous layer of our atmosphere that gets hotter and expands during solar maximum. The puffed-up thermosphere increases drag on Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites

6. Troposphere. The troposphere is the lowest layer of our atmosphere. Starting at ground level, it extends upward to about 10 km (6.2 miles or about 33,000 feet) above sea level. We humans live in the troposphere, and nearly all weather occurs in this lowest layer.

21. Differentiate the categories of computers, According to purpose, According to types of data handled and According to size or capacity. Explain Briefly (tagalog or english)​


Answer:

According to purpose:

-General purpose computers: These are computers that can be used for a variety of tasks. They can be used for word processing, spreadsheets, email, and browsing the internet, among other things.

-Special purpose computers: These are computers that are designed to perform a specific task. Examples include cash registers, industrial robots, and point-of-sale systems.

According to types of data handled:

-Analog computers: These computers process analog data, which is data that is continuous in nature. Analog data can include things like temperature, pressure, and sound.

-Digital computers: These computers process digital data, which is data that is discrete in nature. Digital data can include things like numbers, text, and images.

According to size or capacity:

-Mainframe computers: These are the largest and most powerful computers. They are typically used by large organizations for mission-critical applications.

-Midrange computers: These are smaller than mainframes but more powerful than personal computers. They are typically used by medium-sized organizations for applications such as database servers and email servers.

-Personal computers: These are the smallest and least powerful computers. They are typically used by individuals for tasks such as word processing and browsing the internet.
