Answer:

Mindoro,Philippines

This Buri or Bay-Ong basket is a traditional hand woven container for the Mangyans of The Philippines.

Soft, narrow strips of Buri leaf are intricately woven into baskets by the women of the Tribe.

The shapes weaved with the Palm leaf are symbols that are sacred to the Mangyans. An example of this is the Pakudos design as this is used to ward off evil spirits and also their form of a Mandala (symbol of the Universe).

This is an ideal container for toiletries, flowers, stationary, just about anything you want.

16. Saan matatag puaan ang buli​


Explanation:

A flowering buli or buri palm (Corypha elata Roxb.). These trees, which can be really huge, only flower once in their life, after which they produce a large number of small round fruits, and die. The trunk, that is before they die, is a source of palm flour, used in such traditional delicacies as binignit. The leaves can be up to five meters long. From this tree, three types of fibers are obtained: buri, raffia, and buntal. These are turned in a whole range of products, such as fans, hats, baskets, shoes, etc
