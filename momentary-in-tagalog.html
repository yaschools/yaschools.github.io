25. Which of the following is a possible cause for emotional eating? Select one: a. chronic stress b. atypical depression c. momentary stress d. all of the given choices Previous page


Emotional Eating:

Answer:

D. All of the given choices.

Explanation:

Emotional eating refers to the practice of consuming large quantities of food often referred to as comfort foods in response to feelings instead of hunger. Emotional eating might lead to binge eating disorder. People who suffer from this type of disorder tend to consume a large amount of food once but were not eliminating it.  

Definition of emotional eating: https://brainly.ph/question/761938

Binge Eating Disorder:

Binge eating disorder is characterized by compulsive eating. People who are indulging in compulsive eating eat a huge amount of food once but they do not eliminate it. Binge eating disorder may be a sign that the person uses food to cope with depression or strong emotions. Binge eating is different from normal appetite increases or overeating during holidays.  

People with binge eating disorder eat great amounts of food regularly. They eat so fast and most often do something while eating  like doing homework, playing computer games, reading books,  watching TV, and others. They do not stop eating even if they are already full which resulted to being overweight or obese due to the numerous calories they have taken which is more than what their bodies can utilize.

Definition of binge eating: https://brainly.ph/question/761979

Ways to Avoid Binge Eating Disorder: Avoid boredoms or dullness. Cope with stress.  Do some exercises. Get help. Have enough sleep. Note the food you eat. Recognize your body. Stay away from temptation. Stop dieting.

Try to do something worthwhile instead of eating. Do some activities such as biking, gardening, painting, reading, walking, and many others.

Find alternative ways to handle stress and other feelings instead of consuming foods or eating. The individual could do some exercise, meditate, or just a simple breathing exercise.

Exercise this will not only help one lose weight but will also reduce stress as well. It gives a natural mood - boosting effect that will help him stop that emotional eating habit.

Building a solid support network including families, friends, or joining a support group can help address the problem. Seek medical help is also an option. Consider that avoiding binge eating might develop a healthier relationship with foods. It could be a relationship based on nutritional needs and not on emotional needs. One must eat for his health and nutrition. Having a well - balanced meal plan and choosing healthy foods can help one avoid binge eating.

Take three regular meals and choose healthy snacks. Instead of eating when tired, it is better to take a nap or sleep earlier to boost energy.

Taking notes of the food you eat, the time, quantity, and the feeling while eating those will help one see the pattern whether he is overeating when feel stressed, upset, hurt or angry. He will see the relation between his mood and binge eating.

A person must know when he is physically hungry. If he have just eaten and feel like eating again, just ignore it. It is just emotional hunger.

Do not keep junk foods and other unhealthy snacks in the refrigerator or cabinet.

Depriving oneself of food will just trigger food craving and will develop to an urge of overeating. What one can do is to focus on eating nutritious foods in moderation and avoid being awkwardly full.

Do not skip meals for this often leads to binge eating later in the day. Take breakfast then have a complete lunch and dinner on scheduled mealtime. Taking a snack in between is alright but be sure to choose healthy snacks.

Ways to avoid binge and emotional eating: https://brainly.ph/question/812566
