29. Missy Diwater, the former platform diver for the Ringling Brothers’ Circus had a kinetic energy of 15,000 J just prior to hitting the bucket of water. If Missy’s mass is 50 kg, what was her velocity? help asap<3


Hint:

Kinetic energy is directly proportional to the mass of the object and to the square of its velocity:

[tex]\sf K_E=\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2\\\\where\\K_E=kinetic\;energy\\m=mass\;of\;an\;object\\v=velocity\;of\;an\;object[/tex]

Solution:

Solving for the velocity:

[tex]\sf K_E=\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2\\\\2K_E=mv^2\\\\\dfrac{2K_E}{m} =v^2\\\\v=\sqrt{\dfrac{2K_E}{m} }\\\\Given:K_E=15,000\;J,m=50\;kg\\\\v=\sqrt{\dfrac{2(15,000\;J)}{50\;kg}} \\\\\boxed{\sf v=24.5\;m/s}[/tex]

Thus, the velocity of Missy is 24.5 m/s.


30. Missy Grey, the former platform diver for the Ringling Brother's Circus, had a kinetic energy of 12 525 J just prior to hitting the bucket of water. If Missy's mass is 42 kg, then what is her speed?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for v

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]2KE = mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]v^{2} = \dfrac{2KE}{m}[/tex]

[tex]v = \sqrt{\dfrac{2KE}{m}}[/tex]

[tex]v = \sqrt{\dfrac{2(\text{12,525 J})}{\text{42 kg}}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{v = \text{24.42 m/s}}[/tex]

Therefore, Missy's speed is 24.42 m/s.
