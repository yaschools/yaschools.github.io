15. Our AP teacher asked our position in this issue and we can choose whether we agree or not. So I want to ask your sides. Is same-sex marriage Constitutional in the Philippines? If yes, are you in favor of legalizing it? ​


Answer:

Yes, Yes

Explanation:

"Marriage, as an inviolable social institution, is the foundation of the family and shall be protected by the State." - Article XV, Section 2 of 1987 Constitution.

"From its plain text, the Constitution does not define or restrict marriage on the basis of sex, gender, sexual orientation, or gender identity or expression." - Falcis v. Civil Registrar G.R. No. 217910.

Saying marriage written in the 1987 Constitution as "union between a man and a woman" invalidates the polygamous marriage in Muslim minority as defined under Presidential Decree 1083 or Code of Muslim Personal Laws. That's why framers did not attempt to put "between a man and a woman" because it would discriminate Muslim people.

We can say that same-sex marriage can be accommodated by the plain text of 1987 Constitution due to absence of express prohibition. But the Congress has the right to limit and define it through legislation like in the Family Code. So, the only obstacle for legalization of same-sex marriage is the family code but once the Congress amends it, it will not be declared as unconstitutional because our constitution does not specifically restrict it.

It is noted that plain text should prevail over the intention of framer's. The intention, deliberations and opinions of framers can be persuasive but cannot override the plain and simple meaning of the constitutional text. - CLU v. Executive Secretary.

As a general supporter of minority and marginalized groups, I am in favor of same-sex marriage. No rights being taken away because it won't force straight to become gay but only give more rights to LGBTQIA+. Remember, sexual orientation is not a choice but being religious is. Yet we can't discriminate someone on the basis on his/her religion so how much more to LGBT people for being their true selves. This is my same argument for people of colors like blacks. How can we prejudice on someone's color which is beyond their control but has no significant effect on their ability?
