2. ano ang kahulugan ng plunder


Answer:

Ang plunder ay maaaring nangangahulugang ninakaw na mga kalakal o pera na nakuha nang hindi tama, o ang gawa ng pagkuha ng mga bagay na iyon.

Ang pandarambong ay isang krimen na tiyak sa mga batas ng Pilipinas. Ang pandarambong ay isang malubhang pagkakasala. Ang sinumang sisingilin, na may nakapilit na ebidensya, sa pagkakasala na ito ay hindi maaaring mag-post ng piyansa.

Ang krimen ng pandarambong dati ay parusahan ng kamatayan, ngunit walang sinuman sa mga kinasuhan ng pandarambong sa kasaysayan ng bansa ang ibigay ang parusang kamatayan.

Sa kasalukuyan, ang parusa para sa pandarambong ay ang mga sumusunod: pagkabilanggo panghabambuhay, pag-pagkawala ng yaman na hindi nakuha sa pamahalaan, at walang hanggang pag-alis mula sa pampublikong tanggapan.

27. Identify three main methods used by the marcoses to plunder the country's wealth. How did they do it?


Ferdinand Marcos, the former president of the Philippines, and his family are accused of widespread corruption and embezzlement during his rule from 1965 to 1986. Here are three methods that were allegedly used by the Marcoses to plunder the country's wealth:

Plundering of state funds: The Marcoses are accused of using their political power to divert public funds for their own personal use. This could have included embezzling funds from government agencies, awarding contracts to favored companies in exchange for kickbacks, and manipulating the budget process to funnel money to their own projects.

Illicit enrichment: The Marcoses are also accused of using their political power to enrich themselves and their cronies through a variety of illicit means, such as accepting bribes, engaging in insider trading, and using shell companies to launder money.

Asset seizures: The Marcoses are also accused of using the state's power to seize assets from political opponents and private individuals, including businesses, real estate, and other property. These seizures were often carried out through legal means, such as eminent domain, but were also alleged to have involved corruption and coercion.

Overall, the Marcoses are accused of using their political power to enrich themselves and their allies at the expense of the Philippine people, causing widespread economic damage and contributing to the country's ongoing issues with corruption and inequality.
