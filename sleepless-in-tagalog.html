26. tell the rootword,suffix and meaning of the following 1.tasteless 2.player 3.successful 4.biker 5.sleepless​


The word tasteless means having a bland to no taste. Its root word is 'taste' and its suffix is less.The word player is a noun and it means someone who plays a certain game, sport, or any other form of entertainment. The root word is 'play' and the suffix is -er.The word successful is an adjective and it means that something is achieved through a process or accomplished a purpose. Its root word is success and the suffix is -ful.The word biker is a noun. This word refers to someone fond of riding a bicycle. It is the same as the word cyclist. The root word is bike while the suffix is -er.The word sleepless is an adjective. It refers to someone who pulled an all-nighter or basically someone who was not able to sleep. The root word is sleep while the suffix is less.

This exercise is related to affixes. Affixes are words or a few letters added to a root word to create another useful word. The words tasteless, player, and sleepless all have root words that serve as verbs. However, after adding suffixes to them, they became adjectives and nouns.

play (verb) + er (suffix) = player (noun)taste (verb) + less (suffix) = tasteless (adjective)sleep (verb) + less (suffix) = sleepless (adjective)

By observing the formation of these words, we can see the power of suffixes. But please note that we can also add words are a group of letters on other parts of the words.
