5. suppose a woman is pushing a grocery cart with a 450 newton force along a 10 meters aisle, how uh work is done in pushing the cart from one end of the aisle to the other?​


Answer:

The work done in pushing the cart is 3500 J

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to solve the work done in pushing the cart. To do this, we need to get the product of the pushing force to the distance it has traveled.

Formula to use

W = F·d

where

W     is the work, the unit is in Newton-meter (N·m) or Joules (J)

F      is the force, the unit is in Newton (N)

d      is the distance, the unit is in meter (m)

Given information

F = 500 N

d = 7 m

W = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve the work using the given formula

W = F·d

Then, substitute the given data

W = (500N)(7m)

W = 3500 N·m or

W = 3500 J

Therefore, the work done in pushing the cart is 3500 J.


6. 2. aisle Meaning :Pronouncing :Etymology:Syllabication:​


Answer:

aisle

Meaning- a passage between rows of seats in a building such as a church or theater, an airplane, or a train.

Pronouncing- So the "s" got stuck into "aile". But as in "isle" or "island", it was silent, because the French always had a habit of omitting the "s" after a vowel (e.g. "hospital" becomes "hôpital"). Once the word got into the dictionaries as "aisle", we were stuck with it.

Etymology-late Middle English ele, ile, from Old French ele, from Latin ala ‘wing’. The spelling change in the 17th century was due to confusion with isle and influenced by French aile ‘wing’.

Work said to be done when the object experiencing displacement while we apply the force on object. Work is the product of displacement and force applied on an object in the same direction as the force applied. So, we can conclude that Work is product of two vectors, force and displacement. Work itself is in scalar quantity and has SI unit in Joule (J). In physics subject, Work formula given:

W = F . s

where,

W = Work (Newton-meter [N.m] or Joules [J] unit)

F = Force (Newton [N])

s = displacement (distance unit [m})

According to the question, a woman is pushing a grocery cart with a 650 N along the 12 meters aisle. Given that,

F = 650 N

s = 12 m

W = ?

So, we answer the question to solve how much the work is done by substitute the data such as,

W = 650 . 12W = 7,800 J

13. A woman is pushing a grocery cart with a 700 newton force along the 5 meter aisle, how much work is done in pushing the cart from one end of the aisle to the other?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd

W = (700 N)(5 m)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{3,500 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 3,500 J.
