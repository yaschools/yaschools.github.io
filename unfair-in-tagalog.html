Answer:

Life isn’t unfair, and life isn’t fair.

Fairness is just your perception of worth and expectations of reciprocity by the world. “If you put in hard work you should reap the benefits.” “If you’re a good person, good things should happen to you.” You do the right thing, and in return the right things should come to you.

But life isn’t this. Life just is. Not immoral, but amoral. The world just happens, usually you are able to predict small events ahead of time, and “doing the right thing” in response to them can lead to wanted outcomes.

Sometimes things outside of your control just comes in and invalidates your actions. But this isn’t unfair. This is life.

23. unfair bias or prejudies?


Answer:

Unfair bias and prejudice are related concepts that both involve making unfair judgments or assumptions about a person or group of people based on irrelevant or inaccurate factors.

Bias refers to a tendency to favor or oppose something or someone, often without conscious awareness or intention. Unfair bias occurs when this tendency leads to unjust or discriminatory treatment of individuals or groups based on factors such as race, gender, age, religion, or sexual orientation.

Prejudice, on the other hand, involves preconceived opinions or attitudes about a person or group that are not based on reason or experience. Like bias, prejudice can lead to unfair treatment or discrimination.

Both unfair bias and prejudice can have negative consequences for individuals and society as a whole, perpetuating inequality and limiting opportunities for those who are unfairly judged or discriminated against. It is important to recognize and challenge these biases and prejudices in ourselves and others in order to promote fairness and equity.

Unfair bias and prejudice both refer to negative attitudes or beliefs towards a particular group or individual. However, there is a subtle difference between the two:

This bias can manifest in various forms, such as racism, sexism, homophobia, or ageism. It often leads to unfair treatment and discrimination towards the targeted group or individual.

Like unfair bias, prejudice can lead to unfair treatment and discrimination.
