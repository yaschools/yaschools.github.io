1. contrition is perfect when______contrition is imperfect when_____Atonement or satisfaction refers to______​


Answer:

1.If detestation of sin arises from the love of God, who has been grievously offended, then contrition is termed "perfect".

2.if it arise from any other motive, such as loss of heaven, fear of hell, or the heinousness of guilt, then it is termed "imperfect contrition", or attrition.

3.atonement, the process by which people remove obstacles to their reconciliation with God. It is a recurring theme in the history of religion and theology.

10. Write a Short Reflection in 3-5 sentences on the Prayer "Act of Contrition."​


THE ACT OF CONTRITIONO my God, I am heartily sorry for having offended You, and I detest all my sins because of Your just punishments, but most of all because they offend You, my God, who are all-good and deserving of all my love.  I firmly resolve, with the help of Your grace, to sin no more and to avoid the near occasion of sin.

REFLECTIONAn Act of Contrition is a Christian prayer genre that expresses sorrow for sins. It may be used in a liturgical service or be used privately, especially in connection with an examination of conscience. The Catholic Church does not restrict the term "act of contrition" to any one formula.#CarryOnLearning

21. 1. What are the three forms of penance? Describe each.2. What is contrition? What is the difference between perfect and imperfect contrition? ​


Answer:

1)Scripture and the Fathers insist above all on three forms, fasting, prayer, and almsgiving, which express conversion in relation to oneself, to God, and to others." Also mentioned are efforts at reconciliation with one's neighbor, and the practice of charity "which covers a multitude of sins" as in 1 Peter 4:8.

2)Perfect contrition (also called contrition of charity) is a repentance for sin that is motivated by faith and the love of God. It contrasts with imperfect contrition, which arises from a less pure motive, such as common decency or fear of Hell.

27. •AdorationContrition. Thanksgiving​


Answer:

These are supposedly ACTS

Adoration

Contrition

Thanksgiving

And lastly

Supplications

Explanation:

These are the fundamentals of prayer. Adoration is you have to adore God and praise. Contrition is the prayer of reparation or begging God to forgive our sins. Thanksgiving is being gratitude on the graces that are received. Supplications is the petition of prayer for begging God of what we need.

We have to understand that these must be the complete content of our prayers. Most people are just focusing on Supplications without considering the other 3 factors of prayer. You cannot just pray to God to ask anything.

One concrete example of a prayer containing the ACTS is the prayer that God himself taught us and that is the Our Father. Taken from Matthew 6:9-13.
