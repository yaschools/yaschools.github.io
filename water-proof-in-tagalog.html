24. A solution is prepared by mixing 25 ml of ethyl alcohol in 130 ml of water. What is the proof concentration of C2H5OH in this solution


Percent by VolumeDiscussion:

Percent by volume is the ratio of the volume of a solute to the volume of the solution, multiplied by 100. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} × 100}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} × 100}[/tex]

The percent by volume is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

Proof is another unit of concentration on a volume basis. It is used to express the concentration of alcoholic beverages. It is simply twice the percent by volume. The formula for the proof is:

[tex]\boxed{\text{proof concentration} = 2 × \text{percent by volume}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

A solution is prepared by mixing 25 ml of ethyl alcohol in 130 ml of water. What is the proof concentration of C2H5OH in this solution

Given:

[tex]\text{volume of solute = 25 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solvent = 130 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute} = \text{ethyl alcohol}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent} = \text{water}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{proof concentration}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the percent by volume of a solution.

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{25 mL}}{\text{25 mL + 130 mL}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = 16\%[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the proof concentration.

[tex]\text{proof concentration} = 2 × \text{percent by volume}[/tex]

[tex]\text{proof concentration} = 2 × 16[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{proof concentration = 32 proof}}[/tex]

27. twenty five milliliters of alcohol are mixed with 75 ml of water. Find the %V of the solution and proof of the solution


Explanation:

To find the %V (percent by volume) of the solution, we need to first calculate the total volume of the solution, which is the sum of the volumes of the alcohol and the water:

Total volume = 25 ml + 75 ml = 100 ml

The %V of the solution is then the volume of the alcohol as a percentage of the total volume:

%V = (Volume of alcohol / Total volume) x 100%

%V = (25 ml / 100 ml) x 100%

%V = 25%

Therefore, the %V of the solution is 25%.

To calculate the proof of the solution, we can use the formula:

Proof = 2 x %V

Proof = 2 x 25%

Proof = 50

Therefore, the proof of the solution is 50.
