1. why do some pigment move farther move farther up the the chromatogram​


Answer:

Some pigments dissolve in water easier and are pulled with the water farther up the paper. Others are more attracted to the paper and move more slowly. Usually smaller molecules will move farther than larger ones.

6. What happens to sound as you get farther and farther from its source? A. becomes louder B. becomes softer​


Answer:

B. becomes softer

As you get farther away from a sound source, the intensity (loudness) of the sound decreases. This is because sound waves spread out in all directions from their source, and as they travel, they encounter more and more particles in the air which absorb some of the energy in the wave. This reduction in energy causes the sound to become quieter or softer as it gets farther from the source. Additionally, the distance between you and the source increases, meaning that the sound waves have to travel a longer distance to reach you, which also contributes to the decrease in loudness.
