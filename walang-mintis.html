A candy cane is a cane-shaped sweet usually given during Christmas. It is a candy made of two colors, usually red and white. It is usually in mint flavor with a mix of sweet tastes. It is in a form of long with bent top that resembles an image of a walking cane.

Candy canes are traditionally a Christmas holiday candy. The classic candy cane is a white candy with red stripes infused with either peppermint or wintergreen flavors. They are usually about 6 in (15 cm) tall and about 0.25 in (6 mm) thick. Over the years, candy manufacturers have introduced various modifications on this classic look. Today, candy canes vary in size from about 2-12 in (5-30 cm) tall.

The colors of these products are often modified to better reflect the candy's flavor. For example, a green apple candy cane might be colored green with red stripes. Some manufacturers have changed even the familiar "cane" shape. The result is a wide variety of candy cane-type products that are sold throughout the year.

5 Sensory Imagery

1. VISUAL IMAGERY

it engages the sense of sightpertains to graphics, visual scenes, or pictures Physical attributes including:

- color

- sizes

- shapes

-appearance

- Shades, Darkness & Lightness

Example: The full moon rose slowly over the trees covering the farm with its pale glow

2. GUSTATORY IMAGERY

it engages the sense of taste.the writer describes what is being tasted. Those descriptions should create pictures in your mind that make you think about the taste.it includes the 5 basic tastes - sweet, salty, bitter, sour and umami as well as the textures and sensations tied to the act of eating

Example: I closed my eyes to savor the richness of the warm sweet cake

3. AUDITORY IMAGERY

it engages the sense of hearing.auditory imagery describes what we hear, from music to noise to pure silence.auditory imagery may include:

-enjoyable sounds

-noises

-lack of noise

Example: The splattering of rain on the ground was like the sound of bacon being pan-fried.

4. OLFACTORY IMAGERY

it engages the sense of smell.describes what we smell - may include

-fragrances

-odors

Example: She smelled the scent of sweet hibiscus wafting through the air, its tropical smell a reminder that she was on vacation in a beautiful place.

5. TACTILE IMAGERY

it engages the sense of touch.describes what we feel or touchmay include:

-temperature

-textures

-touch

-movements

Other Kinds of Words

There are many kinds of words. There are nouns, verbs, adverbs, adjectives, and many more. Some of them are descriptive words. Descriptive words are words that help describe, explain, or imagine people, things, places, events, and others.  

A specific kind of descriptive words are sensory words. Sensory words are descriptive words that specifically describe how we sense or experience certain things. Several other sensory words include:

monstrous,  fluffy,  thundering,  stinky and  salty.  

These words describe something you see, feel, hear, smell, and taste, respectively.
