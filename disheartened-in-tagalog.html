14. Rewrite the following sentences using the adverbs in the parentheses. Susan is disheartened by what others say behind her back. (often)


Task 6: Controlled Practice

Answer:

Adverb is a part of speech that has the same function as the adjective which describes a noun and pronouns. While adjective focuses on beings/situations, adverb focuses on the action word. It describes a verb, adjective and another adverb.  It is essential that these two exist to add information or description about a certain thing or event.

Adverb has 5 kinds, these are:

Adverbs of time- tells when the action was made For example: during; later; yesterday; recently; soon Adverbs of manner- describes how an action or activity is performed For example: selfishly; quietly; suspiciously; successfully; courageously Adverbs of degree-describes the intensity of an action For example: very; extreme; enough; too; just Adverbs of place- indicates location For example: outside; somewhere; nowhere; here Adverbs of frequency- describes how often an action is done For example: regularly; seldom; often; frequently; generally

Susan is disheartened by what others say behind her back. (often)

Susan is often disheartened by what others say behind her back.
