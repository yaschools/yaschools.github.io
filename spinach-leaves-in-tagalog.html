13. what are the characteristics of water spinach​


Answer:

Water spinach leaves are usually green and arrowhead or lanceolate in shape. The leaves grow alternately on the stems of the plant. The leaves vary from typically sagittate (arrow head-shaped) to lanceolate, 5-15 cm (2-6 in) long and 2-8 cm (0.8-3 in) broad. The margins are entire or angular, and sublobed.

22. What is the Spinach Cycle?


Answer:

The Spinach Cycle is an innovative food cycle that focuses on growing, harvesting, and eating spinach. It is designed to reduce food waste, increase nutrition, and improve access to fresh veggies. The cycle begins with planting spinach seeds in a container garden. Once the plants have matured, they can be harvested and eaten. Any leftover spinach can be composted, which can be used to fertilize the container garden and help the next cycle of spinach to grow.

27. nutritive value of spinach​


Answer:

Since the days of Popeye, spinach has been famous for its ability to make you "strong to the finish." While this leafy green won’t cause your biceps to inflate like balloons, it is dense in vitamins and minerals, low in calories and versatile in cooking. 

Spinach may also help with several health conditions, according to Megan Ware, a registered dietitian nutritionist based in Orlando, Florida. “Eating spinach is beneficial for maintaining healthy skin, hair and strong bones, as well as helping with digestion, lowering the risk for heart disease and improving blood glucose control in diabetics,” she told Live Science.
