2. In 2020, Mark and Alvin were of the same height. This year, Alvin grew tallerby 5 centimeters a d Mark grew 4 centimeters taller than Alvin. If their totalheight is 254 centimeters, find Alvin's height in 2020?​


Answer:

Given that the total of their heights is 254 centimeters, then the height of Alvin in 2020 is 120 centimeters.

Step-by-step explanation:

We would be able to solve this problem with the help of a mathematical equation or statement. The importance of the mathematical equation is to allow us to make generalizations and objectively provide accurate answers for the word problem.

Variable Representations Let [tex]x[/tex] be their heights in 2020. We assume the same variable since their heights during the said year are equal. In 2021, Alvin's height increases by 5 centimeters, so we can express it as [tex]x+5[/tex].In 2021, Mark's height increases 4 centimeters than Alvin's. So, if Alvin's height in 2021 is [tex]x+5[/tex], then Mark's height should be [tex](x+5)+4[/tex].

We would be able to construct the equation by getting the sum of their heights in 2021 and equate to 254.

                 [tex]\begin{array}{cccc}\text{Alvin's height}&+&\text{Mark's hieght}\\x+5 &+&(x+5)+4&=254\end{array}[/tex]

Combine like terms on the right side. Apply the inverse operation to solve for the value of [tex]x[/tex].

               

                 [tex]\begin{aligned}x+5+(x+5)+4&=254\\2x+14&=254\\2x+14-14&=254-14\\2x&=240\\\frac{2x}{2}&=\frac{240}{2}\\x&=120\end{aligned}[/tex]

This means to say that Alvin's height in 2020 is 120 centimeters. If you were to ask what are their respective heights in 2021, we can use the individual expression and substitute the value of [tex]x=120[/tex] into each expression.

      Alvin's height in 2021:

              [tex]\begin{aligned}x+5&=120+5\\&=125\end{aliogned}[/tex]

     Alvin's height in 2021 is 125 centimeters.

     Mark's height in 2021:

               [tex]\begin{aligned}(x+5)+4&=(120+5)+4\\&=125+4\\&=129\end{aliogned}[/tex]

     Mark's height in 2021 is 129 centimeters.

14. Alvin travels 20mN in 120s, 30mE in 180s, 20kmS in 120s then stopped. What is Alvin's average speed?​


To calculate Alvin's average speed, we need to find the total distance he covered and divide it by the total time taken.

Total distance = Distance traveled North + Distance traveled East + Distance traveled South

Total distance = 20mN + 30mE + 20kmS = 20m + 30m + 20km = 20.03 km

Total time = 120s + 180s + 120s = 420s

Average speed = Total distance / Total time

Average speed = 20.03 km / 420s

Average speed = 0.0477 km/s or 47.7 m/s (approx)
