11. How you will know if the problem is multi step non routine and multi step routine


Answer:

You have to do the UNDERSTAND, PLAN, SOLVE and CHECK.

Step-by-step explanation:

To do that, here is an example:

PROBLEM: Ricky bought a TV for Php 13,495.50 and an air-condition for Php 8,155.00. How much change will he receive from 25 pcs of Php 1,000 peso bill?

1) UNDERSTAND

a. What is asked in the problem?

- the change that Ricky will receive.

b. What are the given facts?

- TV for Php 13, 459.500.

- air-condition for Php 8,155.00;

- and 25 pcs of Php 1,000 peso bill.

c. What is/are the hidden question/s in the problem?

- the total amount of TV and air-condition.

- the total amount of 25 pcs of Php 1,000 peso bill.

2) PLAN

a. What operation will you use to solve the hidden question/s?

- addition and multiplication.

b. What is/are the number sentence/s for the hidden question/s?

- 13,495.50 + 8,155.00 = N

- 25 * 1000 = N

c. What operation will you use to solve what is asked?

- subtraction.

d. What is the complete number sentence?

- 25,000 - (13,495.50 + 8,155.00) = N

3) SOLVE

a. Add the total amount of TV & air-condition.

- 13,495.50 + 8,155.00 = 21, 650,50

b. Multiply the amount of 25 pcs of Php 1,000 peso bill.

- 1,000 * 25 = 25,000

c. Subtract the total amount of TV and air-condition with the Php 25,000 peso bill.

- 25,000.00 - 21,650.50 = 3,349.50

          Therefore, Ricky received Php 3,349.50 for his change.

4) CHECK

- 25,000.00 - 3,349.50 = 21,650.50

19. 1. What is Multi-Tester? 2. List down the parts of a multi tester. 3. How do you care and the maintenance of the multi tester? 4. How to read the meter scale of the multi-tester.​


Answer:

1.A multimeter is a measuring instrument that can measure multiple electrical properties. A typical multimeter can measure voltage, resistance, and current, in which case it is also known as a volt-ohm-milliammeter, as the unit is equipped with voltmeter, ammeter, and ohmmeter functionality.

2.The parts of a multitester are a scale, a needle or pointer, an adjustment screw, a zero-ohm selector, a range selector knob, some ports and test probes.

3.Calibrate the multimeter at frequent intervals.Protect the instrument from dust, fumes, moisture and heat.Renew ohmmeter batteries.When checking the resistance of the circuit, make sure the switch off the power to the circuit.

4.Set your multimeter to the ohm meter on the selector knob.Plug the black probe into the common port.Insert the red probe into the jack marked for ohms.Gently tap the red and black tips together. Your reading should be 0.5 ohms or less.If your reading is higher than that, replace the probes.
