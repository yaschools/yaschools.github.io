Hence, a proposal has been brought up to the table - teach Sign Language Education in Philippine schools.

     Roughly 1.23% of the entire Philippine population is deaf, mute, or hearing-impaired. The calculated deaf population as of 2009 already stands at 241,624 for those who are totally deaf; and 275,912 for those who are partially deaf. That means that over 517,000 Filipinos have only little to no options to communicate because of their physical inabilities- this is where Sign Language Education comes in. Though there are many schools in the Philippines specifically designed to learn sign language, there are not many traditional schools that include sign language in their curriculums.
