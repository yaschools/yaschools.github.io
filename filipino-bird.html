Pied Piper taleThe Pied Piper of Hamelin is the title character of a legend from the German town of Hamelin (Hameln). The tradition extends back to the Middle Ages, with the oldest accounts depicting a piper clad in colorful ("pied") clothes who was employed by the town to trap rats with his magical pipe. When the citizens refuse to pay for the promised service, he retaliates by utilizing the mystical power of his instrument on their children, leading them away like rats. This version of the story became legendary and was cited by Johann Wolfgang von Goethe, the Brothers Grimm, and Robert Browning, among others.
