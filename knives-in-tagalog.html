Express the number of knives as a fraction of the total number of forks and knives.​


a) The ratio of the number of knives to the number of forks is 1:7, or 1/7.

b) If there are 7 times as many forks as knives, then for every 1 knife, there are 7 forks. Therefore, the fraction of forks is 7/(1+7) = 7/8, and the fraction of knives is 1/(1+7) = 1/8.

c) Alternatively, you can express the number of forks and knives as fractions of the total number of forks and knives by using the total number of forks and knives as the denominator. If there are 7 times as many forks as knives, and the total number of forks and knives is 7+1=8, then the fraction of forks is 7/8 and the fraction of knives is 1/8.

Answer:

1. What are the different kinds of knives?

Answer: There are 9 different knives in total. Kitchen knives. butcher knives, french knife, roast beef slicer, boning knife, fruit and salad knife, citrus knife, paring knife and bread knife.

2. Tell the uses of the different knives.

Answer:

Kitchen Knife - Used for peeling onions and slicing carrots, to carving a roast or turkey.

Butcher Knife - It is used to section raw meat, poultry, and fish. It can be used as a cleaver to separate small joint or to cut bones.

French Knife - It is used to chop, dice, or mince food.

Roast Beef Slicer - It is used to slice roasts, ham, and thick, solid cuts of meats.

Boning Knife - It is used to fillet fish, chicken, pork or beef. (Filleting is the process of removing the meat from it's bones.)

Fruit and Salad Knife - It is used to prepare salad greens, vegetables, and fruits.

Citrus Knife - It is used to section citrus fruits.

Paring Knife - It is used for intricate cutting, peeling, mincing and dicing.

Bread Knife - It is used to cut soft bread without crushing it.
