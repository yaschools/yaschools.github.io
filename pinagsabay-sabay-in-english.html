29. Thai:what is Sabai Di mai in english?


It means "sick", "ill", and can even be used as a euphemism for "hungover". Suffix the word dee, or "good", to sabai, and you get the standard Thai greeting:sabai dee mai?, or "Are you well?" Thai offers many ways to intensify an adjective. ... It is wellness almost beyond words, the Thais' heaven on earth.
