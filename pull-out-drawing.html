3. It is part of the respiratory system that pulls down to draw air into the lungs and relaxes and moves up to help breathe out​


Answer:

Diaphragm

Explanation:

You are describing the action of the diaphragm,

Answer:

The part of the respiratory system that you are describing is called the diaphragm. It is a large muscle located at the base of the lungs that plays a crucial role in the process of respiration.

When you inhale, the diaphragm contracts and moves downward, creating more space in the chest cavity and allowing air to flow into the lungs. When you exhale, the diaphragm relaxes and moves upward, helping to push air out of the lungs. The diaphragm works in concert with other muscles of respiration, such as the intercostal muscles (between the ribs) and the muscles of the neck and abdomen, to help regulate the flow of air in and out of the lungs.

The diaphragm is an important part of the respiratory system and is essential for maintaining proper breathing and ensuring that the body receives an adequate supply of oxygen.
