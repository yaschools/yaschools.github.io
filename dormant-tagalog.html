19. what are dormant volcanoes in the philippines?


 

Volcanoes that have record of eruptions can be classified as active or dormant, which means lessened volcanic activity such as Taal, Mount Kanlaon, and Hibok-Hibok, among others.

 

On the other hand, volcanoes that have no record of eruption during the known history of existence is classified as inactive or extinct. The two are also basically different because, unlike extinct, an inactive volcano can be reactivated and once again erupt. To note, the Mount Pinatubo has no known eruption and considered to be inactive ever since until its explosive eruption in 1991.

28. Similarities of active and dormant volcano


Answer:

Classification of Volcanoes

Volcanoes are classified into three: active, dormant, or extinct. Active volcanoes are those volcanoes that have erupted and may erupt again. Dormant volcanoes are those volcanoes that have not erupted for quite a long time but may erupt in the future. Extinct volcanoes are those volcanoes that have erupted in the past but have not erupted for a long time, may not erupt in the future, and may even be dead already.
