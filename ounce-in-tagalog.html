14. Patrick's favorite shade of purple paint is made with 4 ounces of blue paint for every 3 ounces of red paint. Which of the following paint mixtures will create the same shade of purple? 33 ounces of blue paint mixed with 4 44 ounces of red paint B 8 88 ounces of blue paint mixed with 6 66 ounces of red paint C 6 66 ounces of blue paint mixed with 8 88 ounces of red paint D 2 0 2020 ounces of blue paint mixed with 1 5 1515 ounces of red paint E 1 2 1212 ounces of blue paint mixed with 1 6 1616 ounces of red paint


Answer:

The paint mixtures that will create the same shade of purple is (B) 8 ounces of blue paint mixed with 6 ounces of red paint or (D) 20 ounces of blue paint mixed with 15 ounces of red paint.

Step-by-step explanation:

To know which paint mixture will create the same shade of purple with 4 ounces of blue paint for every 3 ounces of red paint, we need to present the problem using ratio and proportion.

Ratio and Proportion

What is RATIO?

Ratio is a comparison of two or more numbers that indicates their sizes in relation to each other. It indicates how many times one number contains another.

Ratios can be shown in different ways:

Word Form (to) a to b

Fraction Form (/) a/b

Colon Form (:) a:b

Going back to the problem above, let us express the ratio of blue paint to red paint using the fraction form.

4 ounces of blue paint for every 3 ounces of red paint = ‌4⁄3‌

Let us also give the ratio of the other paint mixtures.

a. 3 ounces of blue paint mixed with

4 ounces of red paint = ¾

b. 8 ounces of blue paint mixed with 6 ounces of red paint = ‌8⁄6‌

c. 6 ounces of blue paint mixed with

8 ounces of red paint = ‌6⁄8‌

d. 20 ounces of blue paint mixed with 15 ounces of red paint = ‌20⁄15‌

e. 12 ounces of blue paint mixed with 16 ounces of red paint = ‌12⁄16‌

Now that we are done with their ratios, we can now find which paint mixture is proportion to 4⁄3‌.

Proportion

What is proportion?

Proportion is a statement of equality between two ratios. It is the name that we give to a statement that two ratios are equal.

How to tell if two ratios are equal?

Using Cross Multiplication

We can tell if ratios are proportion by cross multiplication. These are the steps to cross multiply:

1. Multiply the extremes.

Extremes are the outer numbers. These are the numerator of the first fraction and the denominator of the second fraction. Place the product to the left of the equation.

2. Multiply the means.

Means are the middle numbers. These are the denominator of the first fraction and the numerator of the second fraction. Place the product to the right of the equation.

3. Compare the two products.

If they are the same, the ratios are in proportion.

Solution:

a. (4 × 4 = 16) 4⁄3‌ × ¾ (3 × 3 = 9) ✘

b. (4 × 6 = 24) 4⁄3‌ × 8⁄6‌ (3 × 8 = 24) ✔

c. (4 × 8 = 32) 4⁄3‌ × 6⁄8‌ (3 × 6 = 18) ✘

d. (15 × 4 = 60) 4⁄3‌ × 20⁄15‌ (3 × 20 = 60) ✔

e. (16 × 4 = 64) 4⁄3‌ × 12⁄16‌ (3 × 12 = 36) ✘

We got two possible answers, letters B and D. Both of them is equal to the original ratio of paint mixture.

4⁄3‌ = 8⁄6‌ = 20⁄15‌

Even though some ratios may look very different, as long as they have equal values they are in proportion.

Types of Proportion:

Directly Proportional and Inversely Proportional

Directly proportional:

It is a proportion where as one amount increases, another amount increases at the same rate. The symbol for directly proportional is ∝.

Inversely Proportional:

It is a proportion where one value decreases at the same rate that the other increases.
