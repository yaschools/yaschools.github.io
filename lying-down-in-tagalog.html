10. How can a person lying upside down can still eat or drink?


Whenever we swallow food or drink fluids, the muscles in the esophagus contracts to send the food and drinks down. This is called peristalsis. This doesn't rely on gravity, so the food/drink doesn't go back to the mouth even if you're upside down. As soon as the food/drink exits the esophagus and enters the stomach, the sphincter closes, keeping it inside your stomach.

29. Is your heart rate lower when lying down or sleeping? Explain.


Answer:

For most people, their sleeping heart rate will fall to the lower end of the normal resting heart rate range of 60–100 BPM. In deep sleep, the heart rate may fall below 60 BPM, especially in people who have very low heart rates while awake.

Explanation:

For most people, their heart rate will fall to the lower end like normal resting heart rate, and for other people, their heart rate may fall below 60 BPM when they're in deep sleep, especially in people who have low heart rates while awake. Our heart rate is generally lower when we are asleep or in a relaxed state. This is also known as our resting heart rate.
