What lies beneath both the land and oceans of our planet are tectonic plates which made up the surface of the Earth. The mountains that we can see today or the reported volcanic eruptions are caused by the movements of the tectonic plates.

The abrupt release of elastic strain that has accumulated over a long time along fault line and/or volcanic activity which causes sudden movement of the Earth is called earthquake. When there is large earthquake, the ground surface of the Earth shakes because of the propagated formed seismic waves that pass through the Earth.

The vital information of the earth's internal structure is usually contained by these seismic waves.
