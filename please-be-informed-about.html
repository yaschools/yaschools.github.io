Create a List of Questions

After reading through the scholarship essay prompt, breathe, and make a list of smaller questions you can answer, which relate to the big essay prompt question.

Let’s say the main essay prompt question asks you, “What were challenges or barriers you had to work to overcome?” Then the smaller questions might be something like:

What is your background? Family, finances, school.

What was challenging about that background?

What’s your greatest accomplishment? How did you get there? How have previous challenges influenced your goals?

Think of these questions as mini-prompts. They explain your story and help you answer the main essay prompt with more details than if you just answered it without a plan in place.

Brainstorm and Outline

After considering smaller questions, it’s time to brainstorm your answers. Take out a pen and paper – or open up a document on a computer – and take your time in answering each mini-prompt. Organize your responses in order:

Intro to main essay prompt.

Answer about 3 mini-prompt questions.

Conclude by rewriting the answer to the main essay prompt with a summary of your mini-prompt answers.

This organization will help you stay on topic and answer the prompt directly. (Or check out these 6 scholarship essay examples for alternative essay structures.)

Explanation:

It occurs when one person intentionally or repeatedly behaves in a manner that is intimidating, threatening, humiliating, or offensive towards another person. Harassment can take many forms, including physical, verbal, sexual, and online bullying. It is a problem that affects people of all age groups, genders, sexual orientations, and backgrounds.

The impact of harassment can be devastating, causing the victim to experience feelings of fear, anxiety, stress, and depression. It can also affect their performance at work or school, leading to absenteeism, decreased productivity, and even loss of employment or education. Harassment should never be tolerated, and steps need to be taken to prevent it from occurring. One such step is education. It is essential to educate individuals on what constitutes harassment and the impact it can have on the victim. Companies and institutions need to develop policies and procedures for reporting harassment and create an environment where victims feel comfortable coming forward.

Another critical step in prevention is holding offenders accountable for their actions. Society needs to encourage individuals to speak out against harassment and to support victims. It also needs to create a legal framework that punishes perpetrators of harassment and protects victims from further abuse.

In conclusion, harassment is a serious social issue that affects many individuals in our society. It can have a devastating impact on the victim's life and should never be tolerated. Educating individuals on the nature of harassment, developing policies and procedures for reporting it, and holding offenders accountable are all essential steps in preventing harassment. It is up to every individual and society as a whole to take action to ensure that harassment is eradicated from our workplaces and daily lives.

20. Please give me information about the disease prosopagnosia. Thank you in advance.


Answer:

Prosopagnosia is a neurological disorder characterized by the inability to recognize faces. Prosopagnosia is also known as face blindness or facial agnosia. The term prosopagnosia comes from the Greek words for “face” and “lack of knowledge.” Depending upon the degree of impairment, some people with prosopagnosia may only have difficulty recognizing a familiar face; others will be unable to discriminate between unknown faces, while still others may not even be able to distinguish a face as being different from an object.
