19. In the corner of a cellar is a pyramidal heap of coal. The base of the heap is an isosceles right triangle whose hypotenuse is 25m., and the altitude of the heap is 10m. If there are 75m3 in a ton of coal, how many tons are there in the heap?​


Answer:

To find the number of tons of coal in the heap, we first need to determine the volume of the heap. To do this, we can use the formula for the volume of a pyramid, which is $V = \frac{1}{3} Bh$, where $B$ is the base area of the pyramid and $h$ is the height.

In this case, the base of the pyramid is an isosceles right triangle with a hypotenuse of 25 meters, so the sides of the triangle are each $\frac{25}{\sqrt{2}}$ meters. This means the base area of the pyramid is $B = \frac{1}{2} ab = \frac{1}{2} \left(\frac{25}{\sqrt{2}}\right)^2 = \frac{625}{2\sqrt{2}}$ square meters.

The height of the pyramid is 10 meters, so the volume of the pyramid is $V = \frac{1}{3} Bh = \frac{625}{6\sqrt{2}}$ cubic meters.

Since a ton of coal has a volume of 75 cubic meters, the number of tons of coal in the heap is $\frac{V}{75} = \frac{\frac{625}{6\sqrt{2}}}{75} = \frac{625}{450\sqrt{2}}$ tons.
