21. Compare and contrast the tagalog, iloko, hiligaynon version (story about: why the sky is high


Indicated below are the comparison and contrast of the Tagalog, Iloko and Hiligaynon version of Why the Sky is High.

Why the Sky is High Tagalog Summary:

In the old days when the sky was still low, there were two brothers named Ingat and Daskol. Ingat, as his name suggests, was very careful so he was their father’s righthand. Ingat was helping with the work in the field and their parents were pleased with him.

Meanwhile, Daskol was given the responsibility of doing household chores because Ingat and Daskol have no sister. He also did the pounding of the rice grains that his father and Ingat harvest. Being impatient and careless, Dakol did not like pounding rice. One day, he needed to pound more volume than usual. He was irritated because every time he raised the pestle, it hit the sky and the sky would be raised. Daskol pounded the rice grains quickly, and he did not notice that the sky was rising. When he looked up, he was surprised to see that the sky was already up – where it is situated today.

Why the Sky is High Iloko version:

In the old days, the only people present on earth were a man and a woman. One day, they ate venison which they got from the man’s hunting. They wanted to eat the bone marrow, but the bones were very big, so they thought of pounding the bones.

Before pounding, the woman removed her comb and necklace and hung them on the sky. Whenever the woman pounded, she would hit the sky, so she requested the sky to rise higher. There came thunders, and the sky rose higher. The pestle hit the sky, so she requested the sky to rise even more higher. It thundered, and the sky rose even higher. Still, the pestle hit the sky. So again, she requested the sky to rise higher. There came thunders, and the sky rose as high as it could be.

When the woman remembered the comb and necklace that she hung, it was too late. They already became the moon and the stars.

Why the Sky is High Hiligaynon:

In the old days when the sky was still low, it could be reached by an ordinary stick. The people said that they were made low by God so that he could hear His people whenever they called Him. Moreover, it was made low so that God could send His blessings to earth whenever men needed them. Because of this, people did not need to work because everything they needed is being provided for. Whenever they eat, they would simply call God, and before they even finish their request, the food is already at their table.

However, after the expulsion of Adam and Eve, God made men to work for a living. People created a custom of holding feasts, when they get a rest from their labors.

One day, one of the chiefs named Abing, held a feast. Many people attended the feast to enjoy it. A sayao, or native war-dance, was given in honor of the men belonging to Abing. The dancing men acted with brandishing spears. While acting, one of the actors, who was drunk, showed his skill but he forgot that the sky was too low. When he darted the spear, he happened to strike the sky, and one of the gods was wounded. This angered God so he raised the sky as it is high up today.
