24. Which is not application of TLC? A. to check purity of organic compound B. to identify sugar in urine C. in forensic science D. to determine moisture


Answer:

D. to determine moisture is not an application of TLC. TLC, or thin layer chromatography, is a technique used to separate and identify the components of a mixture. It is often used in chemistry and biochemistry to check the purity of organic compounds, identify sugars in urine, and in forensic science to identify unknown substances. However, it is not used to determine moisture content

Used to separate volatile components of a mixture with low B.P. Why must the spot be applied to the TLC plate above the level of development solvent? ... Low B.P. and thus more likely to evaporate off the plate before separation can occur.
