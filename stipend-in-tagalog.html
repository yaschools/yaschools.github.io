6. Instructions Solve the following word problems. Show your complete solutions.1. Audrey, a student, is selling yema for her economics class. They are required to have a profit of at least ₱200.00 by the end of the 3rd quarter. It costs Audrey ₱3.75 to make one yema and she could sell it for ₱5.00. At least how many yemas should Audrey sell to surpass the quota?2. Jessica loves rabbits so she enrolled herself in the Agriculture elective in her senior year. Their teacher required them to build a fence for a rectangular rabbit enclosure whose length is 1 meter less than twice its width. They are only given 16 meters of fencing and they can’t afford to buy more. What is the area of the largest possible enclosure that she can build with the given resources?3. A Physics teacher rolls a ball influenced by air friction and the velocity of the ball (V) is = 40 − 16, where is in seconds. At what time duration will the velocity of the ball be between 16 and 32 feet per second?4. A new scholarship program has just been opened. Passers are given two choices for their monthly stipend scheme. Option A will allow the applicant to receive a fixed stipend of ₱4000.00 a month. Option B will allow the applicant to receive just ₱2500.00 on the first month but the stipend will increase by ₱250 each month. Bea took the option A while Pete took Option B. Assuming that both students are frugal and they save exactly half of their total stipend, after how many months will Pete have more monthly savings from their stipends than Bea?


Answer:

1. Let x be the number of yemas Audrey needs to sell to surpass the quota.

The profit for one yema is ₱5.00 - ₱3.75 = ₱1.25.

So, Audrey's total profit for selling x yemas is 1.25x.

To surpass the quota of ₱200.00, we must have:

1.25x ≥ 200

Solving for x, we get:

x ≥ 160

Therefore, Audrey should sell at least 160 yemas to surpass the quota.

2. Let w be the width of the rectangular rabbit enclosure.

From the problem, we know that the length is 1 meter less than twice the width, or:

length = 2w - 1

The total length of fencing is equal to the perimeter of the rectangle, which is:

2(length + width) = 2(2w - 1 + w) = 6w - 2

We are given that this perimeter is equal to 16 meters, so:

6w - 2 = 16

Solving for w, we get:

w = 3

Therefore, the width of the enclosure is 3 meters, and the length is:

length = 2w - 1 = 2(3) - 1 = 5 meters

The area of the enclosure is:

area = length x width = 5 x 3 = 15 square meters

So, the largest possible enclosure that Jessica can build with the given resources has an area of 15 square meters.

3. We are given that the velocity of the ball is given by the formula V = 40 - 16t, where t is in seconds.

To find the time duration when the velocity is between 16 and 32 feet per second, we need to solve the inequality:

16 ≤ 40 - 16t ≤ 32

Subtracting 40 from all parts of the inequality, we get:

-24 ≤ -16t ≤ -8

Dividing by -16 (and reversing the direction of the inequality), we get:

2/3 ≥ t ≥ 1/3

Therefore, the velocity of the ball will be between 16 and 32 feet per second when t is between 1/3 and 2/3 seconds.

4. Let n be the number of months that have passed.

For Option A, the monthly stipend is ₱4000.00, so the total stipend after n months is 4000n.

For Option B, the monthly stipend starts at ₱2500.00 and increases by ₱250.00 each month. Therefore, the monthly stipend after n months is:

2500 + 250(n-1) = 250n + 2250

Assuming that both students save exactly half of their total stipend, their monthly savings are:

Bea: 4000n/2 = 2000n

Pete: (250n + 2250)/2 = 125n + 1125

We want to find the value of n when Pete's monthly savings is greater than Bea's. Therefore, we need to solve the inequality:

125n + 1125 > 2000n

Subtracting 125n from both sides, we get:

1125 > 1875n

Dividing by 1875, we get:

n < 0.6

Therefore, after 0.5 months (or 2 weeks and 3 days), Pete will have more monthly savings from their stipends
