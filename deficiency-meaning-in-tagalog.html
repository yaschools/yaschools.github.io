4. 7. Protén - energy malnutritin (Pem) 2. micronutrient Deficiency 3. vitamin A Deficiency (VAD) 4. Iron definciemy Anemia (IDA) 5. lodine defiency disorder CIDE 5 major deficiency disorder what meaning


Answer:

Protein-energy malnutrition (PEM) is a condition in which the body does not get enough protein and energy from the diet, leading to malnutrition and other health problems.

Micronutrient deficiency refers to a deficiency of essential vitamins and minerals that are required in small amounts for the proper functioning of the body.

Vitamin A deficiency (VAD) is a condition in which the body does not get enough vitamin A, which is essential for vision, growth, and immune system function.

Iron deficiency anemia (IDA) is a condition in which the body does not have enough red blood cells due to a deficiency of iron, which is necessary for the production of red blood cells.

Iodine deficiency disorder (IDD) is a condition in which the body does not get enough iodine, which is essential for proper thyroid function. Lack of iodine can lead to goiter, cretinism, and other health problems.

6. meaning and deficiency of regular or full diet​


Answer:

A regular diet is one that consists of a variety of foods that provide all the nutrients your body needs. A full diet is one that includes all the food groups and provides enough nutrients to meet your daily needs.

A regular or full diet is a type of diet that provides a balanced and nutritious intake of food to meet the body's energy and nutrient requirements. It includes a variety of food groups such as grains, fruits, vegetables, protein, and dairy. A regular or full diet is typically recommended for individuals with no medical conditions or dietary restrictions.

A deficiency in a regular or full diet occurs when the individual does not consume enough nutrients required by the body for optimal function. This may happen due to various reasons such as poor eating habits, limited access to healthy food options, or medical conditions that affect nutrient absorption or metabolism. Deficiencies in a regular or full diet may result in malnutrition, which can lead to various health problems such as stunted growth, weakened immune system, and cognitive impairment.

30. Importance of DeficientNutrientSigns and SymptomsFood Source to Correctthe DeficiencyMicronutrientDeficiency/Condition1. Iron-DeficiencyAnemia2. Vine Deficiency3. Night BlindnessWhat is theDeficientNutrient?4. Lack of Calcium​


Iron deficiency anemia is the most common type of anemia worldwide. Some people have obvious symptoms, while others experience none at all. This often depends on the severity of the anemia. Common signs and symptoms include tiredness, pale skin, feeling short of breath, and dry and damaged hair and skin.

Night Blindness (Nyctalopia) is a symptom of an underlying disease such as a retina problem. The blindness prevents you from seeing well at night or in poor lighting. There are many possible causes and treatment depends on identifying that cause.

Hypocalcemia, also known as calcium deficiency disease, occurs when the blood has low levels of calcium. A long-term calcium deficiency can lead to dental changes, cataracts, alterations in the brain, and osteoporosis, which causes the bones to become brittle. A calcium deficiency may cause no early symptoms.
