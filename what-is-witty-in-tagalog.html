14. what is meaning of witty​


Answer:

The adjective witty can be used to describe those quick little funny remarks that often demonstrate a sharp, biting humor delivered in a playful manner. Witty usually describes things that are off-the-cuff amusing, rather than remarks that are elaborate or carefully planned in advance. This adjective is usually used in a complimentary way, but, like its cousin clever, it can be used sarcastically, as when someone who doesn't appreciate your one-liners says, "Well, aren't we witty today." 18. imagine that you are the minister of the emperor. What witty tricks you will use to show that the weavers are wrong to the Emperor


Answer:

As a minister of the Emperor, my main responsibility would be to ensure that the Emperor makes well-informed and wise decisions. In the case of the weavers who have convinced the Emperor that they have made a magnificent fabric that only the wise can see, I would need to use some witty tricks to show that the weavers are wrong without offending them.

Here are some possible witty tricks that I might use to show the weavers are wrong to the Emperor:

Compliment the weavers on their skills: I would start by complimenting the weavers on their skills and expertise, thanking them for their hard work and dedication. Then, I would ask them to demonstrate how the fabric works, suggesting that I may not be wise enough to see it myself. This would put them on the spot, and they may not be able to provide a convincing explanation, thus exposing the fraud.

Use a child's perspective: I might suggest that we bring in a child to see the fabric since they are known for their innocent and honest opinions. I would then ask the child to describe what they see, knowing that they would not be affected by the weavers' influence. The child's response would prove that there is nothing there to see, thus exposing the weavers' lies.

Pretend to see the fabric: I could pretend to see the fabric to make the weavers believe that they have succeeded in their deception. I would then ask them to weave me a suit from the same fabric, giving them specific instructions on what I want. Since the fabric does not exist, they would be unable to make the suit, exposing their deception to the Emperor.

EXPLANATION

Overall, my main goal would be to show the weavers' deception without causing any harm or offense to them. By using witty tricks, I could expose the fraud and protect the Emperor from making a decision based on false information.

30. These are short, witty paragraphs either serous or light​


Editorials of argument and persuasion take a firm stand on a problem or condition. They attempt to persuade the reader to think the same way. This editorial often proposes a solution or advises taking some definite action.

v Editorials of information and interpretation attempt to explain the meaning or significance of a situation or news event. There is a wide variety of editorials in this category, ranging from those which provide background information to those which identify issues.

v Editorials of tribute, appreciation or commendation praise a person or an activity.

v Editorials of entertainment have two categories. One is the short humorous treatment of a light topic. The second is a slightly satirical treatment of a serious subject. (Satire is the use of sarcasm or keen wit to denounce abuses or follies. While it ridicules or makes fun of a subject it has the intent of improving it.)
