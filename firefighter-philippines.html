1.A firefighter, also known as a fireman, is a highly skilled man or woman who works to combat and extinguish fires. They also take steps to prevent fires, act as emergency medical technicians (EMT) and investigate the causes of fires.

13. A firefighter with a mass of 70 kg slides down a vertical pole, accelerating at 4 m/s2. The net force that acts on the firefighter is


Answer:

550N

Explanation:

By Newton’s second law, the sum of the forces,  ΣF , is equal to the product of the mass,  m , and its acceleration:

ΣF=ma

In this case, we have two forces. The downward force from gravity is the weight,  W , of the firefighter:

W=mg

where  g=9.81ms2  is the acceleration due to gravity. In the opposite direction, we have the force of friction,  Ff , opposing the force of gravity.

If we assume that the downward direction, in the direction of the motion, is positive, then we have:

ΣF=ma

W−Ff=ma

mg−Ff=ma

Ff=mg−ma

Ff=m(g−a)

Ff=(70kg)(9.81−2)ms2N⋅s2kg⋅m
