15. circle O is inscribed in a equilateral triangle ABC. if AB= 8cm, find the circumference of the inscribed circle.


Find the length of the triangle's height by using the pythagorean theorem and the properties of a triangle. Divide the base into two parts using line AD, each having 4cm as its length. Then use the theorem: (AD)^2 = 8^2 - 4^2; AD = 6.93. Find the triangle's area: bh/2 = (8*6.93) / 2 = 27.72 cm^2 . An equilateral triangle can be divided into three equal triangles labeled as triangle AOB, triangle AOC and triangle BOC. Then divide each triangle's base into two equal parts to determine its height. This also shows that the height is the same as the radius of the circle. Althogether they have the same heights (radius) and bases which means they all have the same area. We can derive a formula by using this information: Total Area of Triangle = ar/2 (Triangle AOB) + br/2 (Triangle AOC) + cr/2 (Triangle BOC). Since we already computed the value of the total area of the triangle and lengths of the sides are given we then can find the value of the radius of the circle. Derive a formula from this information: r = (2A) / (a+b+c) = (2*27.72) / (8+8+8) = 2.31cm. We can then determine the circle's circumference: (2)(3.14)(2.31) = 14.51cm. Circumference ≈ 14.51 cm or 15 cm

18. a square is inscribed in a cirle that is inscribed in a square. find the ratio of the areas of e two squares


CONSIDER THE SQUARE INSCRIBED IN A CIRCLE

   The diagonal of this square is equal to the diameter of the circumscribed circle. That is twice is twice the radius, 2r.

   By pythagorean, with square whose side is [tex]s_{1}[/tex] and diagonal 2r,

           [tex](s_{1}) ^{2} +(s_{1}) ^{2} = (2r)^{2}[/tex]

[tex]2s_{1} ^{2} =4r^{2}[/tex]

thus, [tex]s_{1} =\sqrt{2} r[/tex]

therefore, the area of the smaller square is

            [tex]A=(s_{1} )^{2}[/tex]

[tex]A=(\sqrt{2r}) ^{2}[/tex]

[tex]A=2r^{2}[/tex]


CONSIDER THE CIRCUMSCRIBED SQUARE(BIGGER SQUARE)

   The length of sides of this square is equal to the diameter of the inscribed circle. That is, [tex]s_{2} =2r[/tex]. Thus, the area of the bigger square is

[tex]A=(s_{2}) ^{2} =(2r)^{2}[/tex]

[tex]A=4r^{2}[/tex]

The ratio of the area of the bigger square to smaller square:

[tex]\frac{4r^{2} }{2r^{2} } =\frac{4}{2} =\frac{2}{1}[/tex]
