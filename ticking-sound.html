17. 15. Ticks carry bacteria that cause Lyme disease. Ticks do not get Lyme disease, but they can transfer the bacteria to humans, who can get the disease. Which statement best describes the relationships among the bacteria, the ticks, and the humans? A. The relationship between the bacteria and the ticks is competition, and the relationship between the ticks and the humans is predaton B. The relationship between the bacteria and the ticks is competition, and the relationship between the ticks and the humans is parasitismi C. The relationship between the bacteria and the ticks is commensalism, and the relationship between the ticks and the humans is parasitism. D. The relationship between the bacteria and the ticks is commensalism, and the relationship between the ticks and the humans is predation.​


Answer

The answer is letter C.

Explanation

First are the bacteria and ticks. In a commensalism connection, one of them benefits from the relationship while the other is neither benefited nor harmed by the other. The tick's role, much like in the scenario, is to become the bacteria's carrier, but it receives no benefit from the relationship.

When it comes to ticks and humans, the ticks gain from humans, but the humans do not. Predation or parasitism are the only two options for this part. However, in a predation situation, the prey dies quickly. In our given case, however, the ticks slowly gain from human host. Hence, they have a parasitism type of relationship.
