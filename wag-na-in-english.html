And like citations, it provides both the writer and the reader a direct route of access to the source of information provided for the usage of the research paper. Even some of the longest and most detailed research papers will contain a list of cited primary and secondary references that have helped the writer bolster their ideas and cogitations mentioned in their documents and journals. The inclusion of primary and secondary sources also function as a form of circumstantial evidential documentation to prove or further support the main concept or idea of a piece of scholastic research, and subsequently, it helps show the reader that many of the statements presented in research are based on facts and figures rather than biased and prejudiced opinions that prevent the primary conceptualization of one's research from getting through to the reader.
