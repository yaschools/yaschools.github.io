6. Kerosene has a specific density of 0.82. What is the volume of kerosene given a mass of 6.4kg?


To get the volume of kerosene, the density formula will be used since the specific density and mass is given. The formula of density is [tex]\frac{mass}{volume}[/tex]. The density formula is explained well in https://brainly.ph/question/80952.

Solution:

[tex]Density = 0.82\\Volume = 6.4 kg[/tex]

If Density is [tex]\frac{mass}{volume}[/tex], therefore, volume is [tex]\frac{mass}{density}[/tex].

[tex]Volume = \frac{mass}{density} \\Volume = \frac{6.4 kg}{0.82} \\Volume = 7.80487804878 m^{3} \\Volume = 7.80 m^{3}[/tex]

Answer:

The volume of kerosene is 7.80 m³. Note that m³ is used because the given mass is in kilograms (kg) since the SI unit of density is kg/m³.
