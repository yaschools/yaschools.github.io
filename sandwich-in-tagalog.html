14. What's In Identify the following types/classification of sandwiches. 1. Tea sandwiches 2. Multidecker sandwich 3. Toasted sandwiches 4. Deep fried sandwich 5. Quesadillas 6. Pinwheel sandwiches 7. Grilled sandwich 8. Regular cold sandwich​


1. Tea sandwiches - is a small prepared sandwich meant to be eaten at afternoon teatime to stave off hunger until the main meal.

2. Multi decker sandwich - Are made with more than two slices of bread (or rolls split into more than two pieces) and with several ingredients in the filling.

3. Toasted sandwich - is a sandwich in which the filling between two slices of bread is itself a thin slice of toasted bread, which may be buttered.

4. Deep fried sandwich - is any combination of ingredients enclosed by slices of bread, rolls or batter before being submerged in oil and cooked until crisp on the outside and warm on the inside.

5. Quesadillas - is a Mexican dish consisting of a tortilla that is filled primarily with cheese, and sometimes meats, spices, and other fillings, and then cooked on a griddle or stove.

6. Pinwheel sandwich - are mini-sandwiches that have been rolled up in either a flour tortilla (or tortilla wrap) or flattened bread.

7. Grilled sandwich - is a hot sandwich typically prepared by heating one or more slices of cheese between slices of bread, with a cooking fat such as butter, on a frying pan, griddle, or sandwich toaster, until the bread browns and the cheese melts.

8. Regular cold sandwich - A plain sandwich is made up of two slices of bread, preferably a day-old bread, toasted if desired, and on which butter can be readily spread.
