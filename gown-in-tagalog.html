» Since order is not important. We will be using the Formula of Combination.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{\color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \quad\boxed{\ \rm _nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)! \ }} \end{aligned} [/tex]

» Find the number of combinations of 10 gowns taken 5 at a time.

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{10!}{5!(10 - 5)! }\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{10!}{5! \ 5! }\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{10 \cdot 9 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 \cdot 6 \cdot \cancel{5!}}{5 \cdot 4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot \cancel{ 5!} }\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = \frac{ \cancel{10} \ ^{2} \cdot 9 \cdot \cancel{ \: 8 \: } \ ^{2} \cdot 7 \cdot \cancel{ \: 6 \: }}{ \cancel{ \: 5 \: } \cdot \cancel{ \: 4 \: } \cdot \cancel{ \: 3 \: } \cdot \cancel{ \: 2 \: }}\\ [/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = 2 \cdot 9 \cdot 2 \cdot 7[/tex]

[tex] _{10}C_5 = 252[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are 252 ways of 5 mannequins be selected from 10 new gowns.
