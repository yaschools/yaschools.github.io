3. classification of cauliflower​


Cauliflower is one of several vegetables in the species Brassica oleracea in the genus Brassica, which is in the Brassicaceae family. It is an annual plant that reproduces by seed. Typically, only the head is eaten – the edible white flesh sometimes called "curd".

Scientific name: Brassica oleracea var. botrytis

Higher classification: Wild cabbage

5. MAKE A VENN DIAGRAM ABOUT CUTTING AND TRIMMING CAULIFLOWER AND BROCCOLI​


[tex] \blue{\rule{40pt}{99pt}} \red{\rule{40pt}{9999999pt}} [/tex]

Cauliflower

First , Set the cauliflower on a cutting board. Cut off the leaves. Cut a circle around the core and remove it, along with the florets from the stem. Continue cutting and pulling away the rest of the florets.

Then , Cut the larger florets into smaller pieces. All the florets should be uniformly sized to ensure that they cook evenly. Then cut the core in half lengthwise, then into 5 or 6 smaller pieces.

____________________________

Broccoli

First , Pull or cut away any leaves from the stalk and hold it down on a cutting board with your nondominant hand. Cut away the outer layer of florets. Continue cutting off the rest of the florets.

Then , Halve or quarter the larger florets into smaller pieces if needed, and cut away the woody outer skin from the stalk. Slice the stems into thin strips.
