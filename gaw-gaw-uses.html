22. B. Make a simple but concise critique for the song " Gawing Langit Ang Mundo"using Marxism. Rubrics: Content - 10; Organization - 5; Grammar and Mechanics - 5 = Total 20 pts​


Answer:

Introduction:

The song "Gawing Langit Ang Mundo" by Mike Hanopol has gained popularity in the Philippines and has been interpreted from various perspectives. One lens through which to critique this song is Marxism, which examines social inequality and class struggle in relation to capitalist systems.

Summary:

The song "Gawing Langit Ang Mundo" reflects a desire to make the world heavenly, but also acknowledges the challenges faced by the poor and marginalized. The lyrics highlight the stark contrast between the wealthy who enjoy a heavenly world and the poor who suffer. The song portrays the disparities in society and the harsh reality of social inequality.

Analysis:

From a Marxist perspective, the song can be seen as critiquing the capitalist system. It highlights how the wealthy class, represented by the reference to a "heavenly" world, enjoys privileges and benefits while the less fortunate, represented by the poor, struggle in an unfair system. The lyrics also reflect the exploitation of labor and the unequal distribution of wealth and power, which are central issues in Marxist ideology.

Furthermore, the song can be interpreted as a call for social change and addressing the class struggle and inequality that exist within a capitalist framework. It suggests a desire to transform the world into a more just and equitable place where everyone can enjoy the "heavenly" state, rather than it being limited to a select few.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, "Gawing Langit Ang Mundo" can be critiqued from a Marxist perspective as a reflection of social inequality and class struggle within a capitalist system. The song draws attention to the disparities in society, critiques the exploitation of labor, and calls for social change. It encourages a reevaluation of the existing social structures and the pursuit of a more equitable and just society.
