15. What is embracing diversity in paragraph


Diversity means an existing range of differences across a multitude of specimen. In this day and age, diversity often refers to an existing difference in race, culture, age, beliefs, and gender in any social setting.

For example, diversity can exist in a university. One university may accept students of different cultural and religious backgrounds. Not only that, they may even accept students from different countries or provinces, young and old, and all possible genders existing.

Now, why is it important to embrace diversity?

In a modern world such as ours, we are no longer barred by distance. Everything we wish to learn, see, hear, and experience are just a click away. Many people can see the world through the internet, make friends virtually, and witness customs through different media available.

This means that stereotypes and misconceptions about people who are a bit different from us can now be corrected. Unlike many years ago, people only relied on stories passed on from one generation to another or limited experiences to make a sweeping generalization about other people. Obviously, people are more complex than one negative or positive story or experience.

That is why when one person's eyes and mind is opened to all the differences that exists in society, he learns that there is no one right way to do or view things. Embracing diversity, despite not fully understanding differences shows that a person is kind, open-minded, and willing to learn.
