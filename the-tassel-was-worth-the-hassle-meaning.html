18. 2. You would like to put tassel around a square table cloth. If its diagonal measures 8 feet, what is the length of the tassel you need to buy?pa helppppp​


PYTHAGOREAN THEOREM

To solve this problem, we will use the Pythagorean Theorem, which relates the sides of a right triangle. Since a square is a special type of right triangle (where the two legs are equal), we can use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the length of the sides, and hence the length of the tassel needed.

Step 1: Draw a diagram.

Draw a square and label its diagonal as 8 feet, and label its sides as "s".

Step 2: Use the Pythagorean Theorem.

The Pythagorean Theorem states that in a right triangle,where "a" and "b" are the legs (sides adjacent to the right angle) and "c" is the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle), [tex]a^2 + b^2 = c^2[/tex].

In this case, the diagonal of the square is the hypotenuse, so we can write: [tex]s^2 + s^2 = 8^2[/tex]

Simplifying and solving for "s":

[tex]2s^2 = 64 s^2 = 32 s = sqrt(32) s ≈ 5.66 feet[/tex]

Step 3: Find the length of the tassel. Since the tassel must go around the perimeter of the tablecloth, we simply multiply the length of one side by 4: 4s ≈ 4(5.66) ≈ 22.64 feet Therefore, the length of the tassel needed is approximately 22.64 feet.
