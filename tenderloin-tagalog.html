(10 points) _________1. To save time, a kitchen staff cuts meat tenderloin into of different thickness and uneven surface. _________2. Cling wrap are used to shape tenderloins before cutting then into scallops. _________3. When preparing meat, fresh is gently scraped to reveal the bones. _________4. Roasts are tied very tightly to secure meat stuffing. _________5. Meat is place in a dry place while being marinated.


1. STOP - Cutting meat of different thickness and uneven surface can result in uneven cooking and may affect the quality of the dish.
2. GO - Using cling wrap to shape tenderloins before cutting them into scallops can help ensure even cooking and presentation.
3. STOP - Scraping fresh meat to reveal the bones can lead to contamination and should be avoided.
4. GO - Tying roasts tightly can help keep the stuffing in place and result in a more uniform shape during cooking.
5. STOP - Meat should not be left in a dry place while being marinated as this can lead to it drying out. It should be covered and kept in the refrigerator.

Answer:

For the tenderloin cuts of meat, a good marinade would be a mixture of soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, and black pepper. This would give the meat a savory and slightly tangy flavor.

For the adobo dish, a traditional marinade would include soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. This marinade would give the dish a tangy and savory flavor.

For the menudo dish, a good marinade would be a mixture of tomato sauce, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, and garlic. This would give the dish a slightly sweet and tangy flavor
