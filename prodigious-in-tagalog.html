Prodigy Speech:

Hello   everyone.   Most   of   us   are   fascinated   to   children   who   execute   outstanding   deeds academically   or   artistically.   They   have   hidden   talents   that   not   all   ordinary   children   can accomplish. Thus, we say that these children are gifted. From all possible types of gifted children, the exceptionally highly developed abilities in very young children attract constant attention of researchers and lay people. The most fascinating thing about child prodigies is that they are able to do something that is usually accomplished only by adults. However, similar talents and gifts in adults are not so impressive. This is exactly where the puzzle is hidden: in the age of child prodigies. Age means development and this is a key to the explanation of the nature of the child prodigy phenomenon.  

The importance of the concept of age for explaining the prodigy phenomenon can be found in Feldman’s (1986) definition of prodigy. According to him, “a prodigy is a child, who before the age of 10, performs at a level of an adult professional in some cognitively demanding field.”
