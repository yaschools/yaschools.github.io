Answer:

Guilt and grower practices are two different approaches to learning and personal development.

Guilt practices are based on the idea that we need to constantly criticize and judge ourselves in order to improve. People who use guilt practices tend to focus on their mistakes and failures, and they may engage in negative self-talk, self-blame, and self-punishment as a way to motivate themselves. For example, a student who uses guilt practices may beat themselves up for getting a low grade on a test, and use that guilt to motivate themselves to study harder next time.

Grower practices, on the other hand, are based on the idea that we can learn and grow through experience and reflection. People who use grower practices tend to focus on their strengths and opportunities for growth, and they may engage in positive self-talk, self-reflection, and self-compassion as a way to motivate themselves. For example, a student who uses grower practices may recognize that they struggled on a test because they didn't study effectively, and use that reflection to develop better study habits in the future.

Overall, guilt practices can be detrimental to our well-being and personal growth because they can lead to feelings of shame, low self-esteem, and burnout. In contrast, grower practices can help us develop resilience, self-awareness, and a growth mindset, which can lead to greater satisfaction and success in our personal and professional lives.
