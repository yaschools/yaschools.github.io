26. A submarine is cruising at a depth of 30m it climbs 12m then dives 21m and then dives again 13m at what depth is submarine then


Since we're talking about depth, we'll use negative values for every time the submarine goes down, and positive values every time it goes up. Let's set the surface of the water at 0m. We start at a depth of 30m:

-30m which then climbs 12m (+)

-30m + 12m = -18m then it dives 21m (-)

-18m - 21m = -39m then it dives 13m again (-)

-39m - 13m = -52m

To check, let's put all the values in one equation:

depth = -30m + 12m + (-21m) + (-13m)

= -30m + 12m - 21m -13m

= -30m + 12m - 34m

= -30m + (-22m)

= -30m - 22m

depth= -52m

Both answers match, so our computation is correct.

The submarine is 52m below the surface of the water.
