Answer:

When a cake is cut, the exposed edges of the remaining cake are susceptible to drying out. This can result in a less appetizing texture and flavor, and also make the cake less enjoyable to eat. By cutting the cake as needed, we can reduce the amount of time the cake is exposed to air, and therefore reduce the risk of drying out.

In addition, placing a piece of parchment paper or plastic wrap on the cut surface of the cake helps to prevent moisture from escaping. This creates a barrier between the cake and the air, helping to preserve the moisture and freshness of the cake.

Overall, cutting cakes as needed and using parchment paper or plastic wrap to cover the cut surface can help to ensure that the cake stays moist and delicious, and is enjoyed to the fullest extent.
