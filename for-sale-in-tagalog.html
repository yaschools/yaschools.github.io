The text is discussing the concept of cost of sales and how it differs between a manufacturing business and a trading business.

For a manufacturing business, the cost of sales refers to the expenses incurred in producing the products to be sold. This includes the cost of raw materials, labor, and other necessary expenditures such as overhead costs associated with the production process. These costs are then factored into the price of the finished product, along with a margin for profit.

For a trading business, on the other hand, the cost of sales refers to the cost of acquiring and transporting the products to be sold. This includes the cost of merchandise, such as inventory and supplies, and the expenses associated with getting the products to the consumer, such as shipping and handling costs. These costs are also factored into the price of the product, along with a margin for profit.

In both cases, the cost of sales is an important factor in determining the profitability of the business. By accurately calculating the cost of sales, a business can better understand their profit margins and make informed decisions about pricing and inventory management. It is essential for businesses to carefully monitor their cost of sales to ensure they are operating efficiently and effectively.
