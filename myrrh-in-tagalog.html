Historical Timeline: Development of Cosmetics

1) Ancient times - People use natural materials such as myrrh, thyme, marjoram, chamomile, lavender, lily, peppermint, rosemary, cedar, rose, aloe, olive oil, sesame oil, and almond oil as perfumes and ointments for skincare.

2) 4th century BCE - Ancient Greeks and Romans use lead-based cosmetics for depilatory purposes.

3) 17th century - Distillation of essential oils becomes more accessible, leading to the production of perfumes and fragrances.

4) 19th century - Scientific advances result in the production of synthetic dyes and chemicals, leading to the development of artificial colors for cosmetics.

5) 20th century - Modern cosmetics industry emerges, and products such as mascara, lipstick, and foundation become widely available. Ingredients such as salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, and alpha-hydroxy acids are introduced for skincare. Advances in technology lead to the development of waterproof and long-wearing cosmetics. Natural and organic ingredients become more popular, and animal testing is banned in many countries.
