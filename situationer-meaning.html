FURTHER EXPLANATION:

TEXT AND DISCOURSE

Most people think text and discourse are the same. That's why text and discourse are often used together. But it turns out that the two are not the same. As Stubbs argues, according to Stubbs, text and discourse are two different things. Text is a non-interactive monologue speech. Meanwhile, discourse is interactive speech.

ACCEPTABILITY

Acceptability has the meaning of acceptance, suitability and appropriateness.

COHESION

Cohesion is the relationship between propositions that are stated explicitly by the grammatical and semantic elements in the sentences that make up the discourse. Cohesion is a formal aspect of grammar, especially in the syntactic organization consisting of sentences in order to produce a complete text.

INTERTEXTUALITY

Intertextual is an approach to understand a text as an insertion of other texts. Intertextual is also understood as a process to connect texts from the past with texts of the present. A text is understood not to stand alone.

TEXT

Text is defined as a unit of language and  refers to a communicative event. However, the language unit in question is not a grammatical language unit such as a clause or sentence and its size is not determined. The language units used are complete in writing such as books, letters, magazines, newspapers, written documents, and so on.

Women in Sectoral Situationer

Based on research and statistics, women in the agricultural sector are often faced with various forms of oppression and discrimination. Women comprise a significant portion of the agricultural workforce, particularly in developing countries, but they often have limited access to resources, credit, and technology. They are also paid less than their male counterparts and have less access to land ownership and tenure. Additionally, women in the agricultural sector often face gender-based violence and harassment. These challenges hinder women's participation and success in the sector, limiting their opportunities for economic empowerment and overall development.
