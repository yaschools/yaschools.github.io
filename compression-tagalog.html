23. Datacompression to reduced and de compression​


In signal processing, data compression, source coding,[1] or bit-rate reduction is the process of encoding information using fewer bits than the original representation.[2] Any particular compression is either lossy or lossless. Lossless compression reduces bits by identifying and eliminating statistical redundancy. No information is lost in lossless compression. Lossy compression reduces bits by removing unnecessary or less important information.[3] Typically, a device that performs data compression is referred to as an encoder, and one that performs the reversal of the process (decompression) as a decoder.

25. Gas can be compressed. Give anexample of when you can compress gas​


Common compressed gases are: propane, nitrogen, chlorine, helium, and oxygen.

Gas is that state of matter in which molecules have much space available between them, so when pressure is applied on gas molecules come closer filling the available space between them. Hence when they come closer their volume reduces and gets compressed.

Two examples of compressed gases which we use in our daily lives are: Liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) - The liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) cylinder that we get in our home for cooking or the oxygen supplied to hospitals in cylinders is compressed gas
