19. what does altitude means


Altitude is defined as the height or point at which an object is located in relation to a point of reference. The point of reference can either be ground level or sea level.

Altitude is useful in the study of the atmosphere. It lets us describe and note the different levels at which the atmosphere changes in characteristics. It also serves a major purpose in launching space flights and even commercial air flights. Pilots should know at which altitude there would be least turbulence to avoid accidents or unpleasant flights.

21. 2. what is the relationship of temperature and altitude in the mesosphere?a. the temperature and altitude increasesb. the temperature and altitude decreasesc. the temperature decreases as the altitude increasesd. the temperature increases as the altitude decreases​


The correct answer is C. In the mesosphere, the temperature decreases as the altitude increases. This is because the mesosphere is the layer of the Earth's atmosphere located between the stratosphere and the thermosphere, and it is characterized by very low atmospheric pressure and low temperatures. As you go higher in the mesosphere, the air molecules become less dense, so there are fewer collisions between them, which leads to a decrease in temperature. In fact, the coldest temperatures in the Earth's atmosphere are found in the upper mesosphere, with temperatures as low as -90°C (-130°F) at the mesopause, which is the boundary between the mesosphere and the thermosphere.


22. the altitude ∆YES,is the geometric mean between m and n. what is the proportion​


The proportion can be set up as follows:

m : ∆YES = ∆YES : n

This can also be written as:

m/∆YES = ∆YES/n

By taking the square root of both sides, we get:

sqrt(m/∆YES) = sqrt(∆YES/n)

This simplifies to:

sqrt(m) / sqrt(∆YES) = sqrt(∆YES) / sqrt(n)

Multiplying both sides by sqrt(∆YES) gives us:

sqrt(m) = ∆YES

and

sqrt(n) = ∆YES

Therefore, the proportion is:

m : ∆YES : n = sqrt(m) : ∆YES : sqrt(n)
