Answer:

The fascination of Filipinos with Korean culture, also known as "Korean Wave" or "Hallyu," can be attributed to several factors. Here are some of them:

Popularity of K-Pop and Korean dramas: K-Pop music and Korean dramas have gained massive popularity worldwide, including in the Philippines. Many Filipinos enjoy the catchy music and captivating storylines, which may have led to an interest in Korean culture as a whole.

Relatable themes: Many Korean dramas feature universal themes that Filipinos can relate to, such as family, love, and friendship. This connection can lead to a deeper appreciation for Korean culture.

Cultural similarities: Korea and the Philippines share some cultural similarities, such as respect for elders and a strong sense of family values. These similarities may make Korean culture more accessible and relatable to Filipinos.

Social media and the internet: The widespread use of social media and the internet has made it easier for Filipinos to access and learn about Korean culture. This has also enabled fans to connect with each other and share their love for K-Pop and Korean dramas.

Overall, the obsession with Korean culture in the Philippines can be attributed to a combination of factors, including the popularity of K-Pop and Korean dramas, relatable themes, cultural similarities, and the widespread availability of Korean content on social media and the internet.
