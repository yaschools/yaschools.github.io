you can also read this about interval.

In music, the whole distance between two tones, whether they are produced consecutively (melodic interval) or simultaneously (melodic interval), is known as the interval (harmonic interval). An inverted interval is one in which the lower pitch of a simple interval is pushed up an octave to become the higher pitch, giving it a new name.

The link between two different musical pitches is known as an interval. The first two notes (the first "twinkle") and the second two notes (the second "twinkle"), for example, are at a one-fifth interval in the song "Twinkle Twinkle Little Star."

The music we listen to on a daily basis is shaped by intervals. Music intervals control the tone of a song as well as the construction of a chord. It's important not only to know how intervals in music function, but also to know how to name them by ear.
