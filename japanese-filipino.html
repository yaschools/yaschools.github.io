JAPANESE AND FILIPINO CULTURE

The world is composed of different countries with different rules, regulations and laws. Within each country, the rules, regulations and laws are applied. It is important to recognize that people from different cultures are different in a variety of ways, including the different ways of looking at things, different ways of dressing up clothes, different ways of expressing personality or goodness, different ways of acquiring values and traditions and many more.　

Japanese and Filipinos for an example have similarities and differences in their culture. Both countries are known for being hospitable. They both respect each other’s culture. When it comes to food especially in presenting it, they both see to it that the food looks beautiful and appealing to the eye. However, Filipinos only do this during special occasions. Breakfast is considered the most important meal of the day. They both prefer eating rice three times a day. Japanese and Filipinos both have their superstitions regarding the use of their eating utensils.

On the other hand, there are many differences between Filipinos and Japanese. When it comes to greetings, Japanese are very aware of Western habits, and they often greet someone with a handshake. They usually bow to their elders. Japanese people call others with their last name. In contrary, close female Filipino friends may hug and kiss when they greet while close male friends may hug and tap each other’s back or just by a handshake. They don’t bow when they greet someone. They smile at other people whenever they greet them. Filipinos use Mr., Ms. or Mrs. plus their name or surname in addressing someone until they are allowed to call them by their name or nickname. During New Year, they go to shrines and wear their kimonos. Filipinos only have Valentine’s Day. They have double dates or gathering. Filipino couples usually have their dates at the mall and watch movies. The guy would usually pay for the date. During New Year, couples usually visit each other’s family and eat with them.The differences of each country make them unique and special.

Filipino costumes - The tagalog of barong is used by men and women (a formal dress, often made of a thin fabric and richly broded). It looks like a long-sleeved tunic or shirt and is worn over a normal shirt.

Japanese costumes - The traditional dress of Japan is the kimono. Kimonos, which are generally made of silk, have large sleeves and reach from the shoulders all the way down to the heels. They are tied with a wide belt called an obi.
