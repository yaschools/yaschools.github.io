18. explain Ephesians chapter 5 verse 25 31​


Ephesians 5:25-31 talks about the specific roles of husbands and wives. Paul tells husbands to “Love your wives as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her.” He then says “Husbands, go ahead and love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave up his life for her”. He goes on to tell wives to “submit to your own husbands, as to the Lord” and that wives should honor their husbands. Finally, he says “no one ever hated their own flesh and body, but nourishes, cherishes and loves it, just as Christ does with the church”. The overall message is that husbands and wives should treat each other with the same kind of love and respect that Christ shows for the church.

23. psalm chapter 145 verse 18 reflection


Answer:

The praise of God's magnificence and kindness is found in Psalm 145:18. God's mighty, merciful deeds reveal to us his incomparable essence. The Lord especially takes care of the hungry and the broken. And yet, he is "near to everyone who calls on him" and "full of love" for everyone. Our prayers should reflect the nature of God as revealed in history, Scripture, and above all in Jesus Christ. As Psalm 145:18 reminds us, "The LORD is near to all who call on him, yes, to all who call on him in sincerity." They ought to consider what God wants for us. For instance, it would be ridiculous to implore God to favor actions that were obviously against his revelation. It would be absurd to beg God to be hateful or to support terrible actions. On the other hand, it would be sage to pray by God's nature and purposes. In addition, God is "near" to us when we call on him "in truth," according to Psalm 145:18.
