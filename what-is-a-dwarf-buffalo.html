9. What is “Dwarf Planet”??​


Answer:

A dwarf planet is a celestial body that orbits the sun and is large enough to be spherical, but has not cleared its neighboring region of debris and is not a satellite of a planet. In other words, a dwarf planet is a small, round object in space that is not quite big enough to be considered a full-blown planet. Some examples of dwarf planets in our solar system are Pluto, Eris, and Ceres.

18. In the second experiment of Mendel's work, you cross a dwarf garden pea with another dwarf pea. What will the first generation of peas look like? Choices: 25% dwarf 75% dwarf 50% dwarf all dwarf


In the second experiment of Mendel's work, where a dwarf garden pea is crossed with another dwarf pea, all the first-generation peas will be dwarf. This is because the dwarf trait is a homozygous recessive trait and both parent plants are homozygous for the dwarf allele. Therefore, all the offspring will inherit two copies of the dwarf allele, resulting in a dwarf phenotype. This is known as a monohybrid cross where only one trait is being studied.

25. What is the problem of The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo


Answer:

"The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo" is a traditional folk tale from the Philippines, and it does not have a specific problem that needs to be solved. However, like many folk tales, it teaches a moral lesson and may contain a conflict or challenge that the characters must face.

In this story, the peasant is asked by the king to capture a tiger that has been terrorizing the village. The peasant sets out on his mission and encounters a buffalo that he befriends along the way. When he finally finds the tiger, he tricks it into a pit and brings it back to the king. The conflict in the story is the challenge that the peasant faces in capturing the dangerous tiger, and the moral lesson is the importance of wit and cleverness in overcoming obstacles.
