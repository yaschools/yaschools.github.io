5. write an example sentences using direct and indirect signals​


Answer:

Direct: Mary said, “She is going to the US next month.”

Indirect: Mary said that she was going to the US the following month.

Direct: The sports teacher said, “Run fast, boys.”

Indirect: The sports teacher asked the boys to run fast.

Explanation:

You may have noticed that when we employ direct speech, we use quotes to highlight the speaker's actual words in the first and second instances. In contrast, there is no such thing in indirect communication since the listener relates the same in their own words.

Example: "Lou was sent to the haberdashery to find a new suit. He needed to wear

Because the sentence says that Lou would find a suit at the haberdashery, then

3.Sometimes a word or phrase is clarified by the presentation of the opposite meaning

somewhere close to its use. Look for signal words when applying context clues.

Example: "Lou wanted to go to the haberdashery, but Ann wanted to shop at the boutique.”

The signal word but tells the reader that an opposite thought is going to be stated.

4.Sometimes a word or phrase is not immediately clarified within the same sentence.

Relationships, which are not directly apparent, are inferred or implied. The reader must

28. noun as direct object example sentences​


Answer:

1. Sara returned the dress to the designer.

“Dress” is the direct object in this sentence, being the thing that was returned.

2. The sailor sailed the ship across the Indian Ocean.

“Ship” is the direct object in this sentence, being the thing that was sailed.

3. Sally took them with her to the market.

“Them” is a pronoun functioning as the direct object in this sentence, being who Sally took.

4. Julie invited us to the dinner.

“Us” is a pronoun serving as the direct object, being who Julie invited.

5. Dad drove mom and me to the airport.

“Mom” and “me” are compound direct objects in this sentence, being who Dad drove.
