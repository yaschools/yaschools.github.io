21. What is anti- hazing law


The Anti Hazing Law of 2018 was a law that was enacted in 2018. This replaced the older Act of 1995. This new law strengthened the old anti-hazing law and provided for provisions where school administrations could monitor initiation rites conducted by organizations at their respective schools. It also provided for stiffer penalties for those found to be involved in the act of hazing. In effect, this law made the previous one stronger.

Hazing is an unacceptable act and should not be tolerated. Hence the Anti Hazing Law of 2018 imposes very high penalties for those who are directly involved in the planning and execution of hazing. Section 14 reads as follows:

Penalties. - The following penalties shall be imposed:

"(a) The penalty of reclusion perpetua and a fine of Three million pesos (P3,000,000.00) shall be imposed upon those who actually planned or participated in the hazing if, as a consequence of the hazing, death, rape, sodomy, or mutilation results therefrom;

Meanwhile, for those who are involved as officers or former officers of the organization, but not directly participated in the hazing rites, they can be given "The penalty of reclusion perpetua and a fine of Two million pesos (P2,000,000.00)."  

For those who were present during the hazing, but had no direct participation, the law imposes "The penalty of reclusion temporal in its maximum period and a fine of One million pesos (P1,000,000.00)"  

The punishment of reclusion perpetua means lifetime imprisonment or twenty years and one day to forty years and reclusion temporal means twelve years and one day to twenty years. Mere presence in an event where hazing occurs meant that you will serve 20 years (you will not be given lenience). Hence, it is best that fraternities and sororities not engage in hazing at all. Also, it is best for students not to join these kinds of organizations as they only promote violence.
