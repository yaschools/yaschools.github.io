28. 1. Sarcasm isA. literally conveyedB. used to give a moralC. the use of irony to either vex or humiliate someoneD. the careful choice of word in order not to hurt someone​


Answer:

C. The use of irony to either vex or humiliate someone

Sarcasm is another way of utterance to humiliate or mock someone. Usually, the words are too simple, but the meaning behind them is kind of hurtful for the one who is receiving them. It is an irony because the words are used to mean the opposite of what it really said. See the dialogue below for an example of sarcasm.

The class of Ms. Jones watched a zombie movie. After the movie ended, she asked the class for their insights if ever a "zombie outbreak" happens. The whole class erupted and different voices were heard. One of these is Archie's. Archie had been the last-ranked student in their class ever since.

Archie: That's scary!... What should we do?

Some kids raised their eyebrows. One of them is Jeddi.

Jeddi: You don't have to worry, Archie. The zombies will not hurt you?

Archie: Why? Do I have a kind of superpower?

Some chortled. Soft giggles could be heard.

Jeddi: No, of course not. It's just...zombies eat brains. And...

Archie: And?

Jeddi: And you don't have one.

Waves of laughter erupted.

However, it is just an example of negative sarcasm that is used to taunt or anger someone. In some situations, sarcasm can be combined with wisdom and use wisely, like humor a friend or someone.
