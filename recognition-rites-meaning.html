4. Meaning of Recognition and Awareness in Media and Information Literacy.​


Answer:

Recognition and awareness are important concepts in media and information literacy. Recognition refers to the ability to identify and understand the different types of media and information that are available, such as print, television, radio, and the internet. It involves being able to differentiate between different sources of information and to understand their potential biases and limitations.

Awareness, on the other hand, refers to the ability to critically evaluate and interpret the media and information that we encounter. It involves being able to think critically about the messages and information that we receive, and to question their reliability, validity, and relevance.

Overall, recognition and awareness are crucial skills in media and information literacy, as they enable us to navigate the increasingly complex and diverse landscape of media and information. These skills help us to become more informed and critical consumers of media and information, and to make better decisions about the sources that we trust and the information that we use.

A rite pertains to a religious act or ritual done with solemnity.

Explanation:

Rites are done with a religious concept in observance of the faith of a group of people. It is synonymous to ritual, sacrament, and service. There is a specific order or sequence how things should be done during the ceremony. Particular words and actions are required as cue for the next action to initiate.  Some of the common rites that we can see are:

29. explain the problem recognition or need recognition​

Problem or Need Recognition

This is the first stage of the Consumer Decision Process in which the consumer is able to recognize what the problem or need is and subsequently, what product or kind of product would be able to meet this need.

Answer:

In problem recognition, the consumer recognizes a problem or need or want. The buyer recognizes a difference between his or her actual state and some desired state. The need can be generated by internal stimuli when one of the person’s normal needs − hunger, thirst, sex, etc. rises to a high level sufficient to become a drive.
