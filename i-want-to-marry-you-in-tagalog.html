28. i would have regretted it if i ________ to marry you.


I would have regretted it if I had agreed to marry you.

The phrase had + past tense (verb) is the most appropriate to use. This is because looking from the context clues, the situation it talked about something that didn't actually happen.

It is used to describe a circumstance that would have happened if another situation were to take place.

From my answer, we can say that the person would have only regretted his decision if he had decided to agree marrying the person.

Third Conditional

This is an example of a third conditional, the use of the terms would have + had. It is only used for situations that did not take place, but would definitely have occured if the right condition was met first. 

 

To understand it, here is another example:

I would have done it if I knew I had to.
