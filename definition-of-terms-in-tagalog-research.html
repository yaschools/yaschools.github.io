10. What type of definition is best applied in crafting a research paper? * a. Definition of Terms b. Conceptual Definition c. Operational Definition d. Research Definition


Answer:

Answer: c. Operational Definition

Answer:

In crafting a research paper, the most relevant type of definition would be "c. Operational Definition."

Operational definition refers to defining a concept or variable in terms of the specific procedures used to measure or manipulate it in a study. This type of definition is important in research because it helps to ensure that the research findings are reliable and valid, by making sure that the variables are defined and measured consistently across the study.

Conceptual definitions, on the other hand, define a concept or variable in abstract or theoretical terms, while Definition of Terms defines important terms that may be used in the research paper, and Research Definition outlines the specific scope and purpose of the research study. While all of these definitions can be important in a research paper, operational definitions are the most relevant for ensuring the validity and reliability of research findings.

Definition of terms: This refers to a section in a research study that defines key terms and concepts used in the study. It helps ensure that the reader has a clear understanding of the specific terminology used in the research.

Research design: This refers to the overall plan, strategy, or approach that a researcher uses to conduct a research study. It outlines the methods and procedures to be used to collect and analyze data in order to answer the research questions or hypotheses.

Sources of data: These refer to the different means by which a researcher collects data for a research study. Common sources of data include surveys, interviews, observations, experiments, and existing records or documents.

Explanation:

Definition of Terms:

- Research Design: The plan, structure, and strategy that outlines how a research study will be conducted, including the methods and procedures used to collect and analyze data.

- Sources of Data: The various types of information or materials that are used as evidence in a research study. Common sources of data include surveys, interviews, observations, documents, and secondary sources such as books, articles, and databases.

Research Design:

Research design refers to the framework or blueprint that guides the entire research process, from the formulation of research questions to the collection and analysis of data. It outlines the type of research to be conducted, the methods and techniques to be used, the sampling strategy, and the procedures for analyzing and interpreting data. A well-designed research study should be systematic, rigorous, and transparent, with clearly defined objectives and a sound methodology.

Sources of Data:

Sources of data refer to the various types of information or materials that researchers use to answer their research questions. These can include primary sources, such as surveys, experiments, observations, and interviews, as well as secondary sources, such as books, articles, and statistical data. The choice of data sources depends on the nature of the research question, the availability of data, and the research design. Researchers must also consider issues related to data quality, validity, and reliability when selecting and using data sources.
