7. Why is novelty included as a requirement in patenting?​


ANSWER: An invention is not new and therefore not patentable if it was known to the public before the filing date of the patent application, or before its date of priority if the applicant claims priority of an earlier patent application. The purpose of the novelty requirement is to prevent prior art from being patented again.

29. Why novelty is not an essential part of innovation?


Answer:

Novelty is essential for innovation; but it is not the definitive factor for its success.

Truly novel ideas that are proven to solve a major problem in a unique way, create business opportunities, and may also enrich the state-of-the-art. Technical novelty usually leads to Intellectual Property and differentiators for products and services.

However, novelty depends on the point of view, and it is not binary — it needs to be expressed using a degree, a level of newness. Real-world products and solutions are complex — they encapsulate multiple ideas some of which may be known, while others may be appearing for the first time. The degree of newness reflects how many of those essential features are, indeed, novel.
