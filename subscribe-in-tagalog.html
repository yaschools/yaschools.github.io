Answer:

Yes, that is a correct inference. The survey data shows that 44% of the mobile phone subscribers use smartphones, so you can infer that the probability that a mobile phone subscriber uses a smartphone is 0.44 or 44%. This is based on the fact that the percentage is a way of expressing a proportion or a ratio, and a probability is also a ratio that describes the likelihood of an event occurring. Therefore, a probability of 0.44 or 44% for a mobile phone subscriber to use a smartphone can be inferred from the survey data provided.
