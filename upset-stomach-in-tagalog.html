1. used as antacid to reduce stomach acid, treats indigestion and upset stomach


Answer:

Antacids

Explanation:

Antacids work by counteracting (neutralising) the acid in your stomach. They do this because the chemicals in antacids are bases (alkalis) which are the opposite of acids. A reaction between an acid and base is called neutralisation. This neutralisation makes the stomach contents less corrosive.

9. 3. Calcium carbonate is used as an antacid to relieve heartburn, acid indigestion, and upset stomach. You have intake 6.088g CaCO3 with empty stomach and reacted with 2.852g Hydrochloric acid (HCI) present in the stomach. What mass of CaCO3 remains unreacted? (MM of CaCO3 = 100.09 g/mol;MM of HCl = 36.46 g/mol) CaCO3 +2HCI CaCO3 +2HCI → CaCl2 +CO2 +H2O a. What is the limiting reagent? b. What is the excess reagent? c. How much is leftover in the excess reagent? d. What would be the effects on your health after you intake CaCO3 with an empty stomach? ​


Explanation:

[tex]6.088 \ g \ CaCO_3 \times \frac{1 \ mol \ CaCO_3}{100.09 \ g}\times \frac{1 \ mol \ CaCl_2}{1\ mol \ CaCO_3}=0.0608 \ mol \ CaCl_2 \\

2.852 \ g \ HCl \times \frac{1 \ mol \ HCl}{36.46 \ g} \times \frac{1 \ mol \ CaCl_2}{2 \ mol \ HCl} = 0.0391 \ mol \ CaCl_2 \\

\text{Therefore, the limiting reactant is HCl and the excess reactant is CaCO}_3 \\ \\

0.0391 \ mol \ CaCl_2 \times \frac{1 \ mol \ CaCO_3}{1 \ mol \ CaCl_2}=0.0391 \ mol \ CaCO_3 \ \text{reacted} \\

0.0608 - 0.0391 = 0.0217 \ mol \ CaCO_3 \ \text{left} = 2.1719 \ g \\ \\

\text{You will feel gassy and bloated after taking the medication since the reaction releases carbon dioxide and creates water in gaseous state.}[/tex]

14. Liver, Stomach, Small Intestine explanationspls help need lang po explanation Tagalog po​


Answer:

•LIVER•

a large lobed glandular organ in the abdomen of vertebrates, involved in many metabolic processes.

"cholesterol may accumulate in the liver"

an organ similar to the liver in other animals.

the flesh of an animal's liver as food.

"liver pâté"

•STOMACH•

the internal organ in which the major part of the digestion of food occurs, being (in humans and many mammals) a pear-shaped enlargement of the alimentary canal linking the esophagus to the small intestine

•SMALL INTESTINE•

the part of the intestine that runs between the stomach and the large intestine; the duodenum, jejunum, and ileum collectively

Explanation:

LIVER-Ang atay ay isang organ na kasing laki ng football. Nakaupo ito sa ilalim lamang ng iyong rib cage sa kanang bahagi ng iyong tiyan. Ang atay ay mahalaga para sa pagtunaw ng pagkain at pag-alis sa iyong katawan ng mga nakakalason na sangkap. Ang sakit sa atay ay maaaring namamana (genetic).

STOMACH

-Ang tiyan ay isang muscular organ na matatagpuan sa kaliwang bahagi ng itaas na tiyan. Ang tiyan ay tumatanggap ng pagkain mula sa esophagus. Habang ang pagkain ay umabot sa dulo ng esophagus, pumapasok ito sa tiyan sa pamamagitan ng muscular valve na tinatawag na lower esophageal sphincter. Ang tiyan ay naglalabas ng acid at mga enzyme na tumutunaw ng pagkain.

SMALL INTESTINE

-Ang maliit na bituka o maliit na bituka ay isang organ sa gastrointestinal tract kung saan nangyayari ang karamihan sa pagsipsip ng mga sustansya mula sa pagkain. Ito ay nasa pagitan ng tiyan at malaking bituka, at tumatanggap ng apdo at pancreatic juice sa pamamagitan ng pancreatic duct upang tumulong sa panunaw.
