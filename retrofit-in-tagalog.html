27. Find the meaning of the following words. Resilience, Retrofitting, Residual risk, Risk transfer, Rehabilitation ,Reconstruction, structural and non-structural measures, Underlying disaster risk drivers, Vulnerability​


Answer:

Resilience - the ability of a system, community, or individual to recover quickly from difficulties, or to efficiently adjust to changing circumstances.

Retrofitting - the process of modifying an existing structure to improve its performance, often to meet new safety or building codes, or to improve energy efficiency.

Residual risk - the risk that remains after all mitigation measures have been implemented.

Risk transfer - the transfer of risk from one party to another, through methods such as insurance or risk management strategies.

Rehabilitation - the process of restoring or improving a building or structure that has been damaged or is in disrepair.

Reconstruction - the process of rebuilding a damaged or destroyed building or structure, often with the aim of restoring it to its original condition.

Structural and non-structural measures - measures taken to reduce the risk of disasters that are related to the physical structure of a building, or to the people and systems within it. Structural measures include improvements to the building structure, while non-structural measures may include disaster preparedness training, early warning systems, and evacuation plans.

Underlying disaster risk drivers - factors that contribute to the vulnerability of communities or systems to the impacts of disasters, such as population growth, poverty, land use changes, and climate change.

Vulnerability - the degree to which a system or community is susceptible to harm from a hazard, often related to factors such as the presence of physical or social hazards, poverty, and the ability to access resources for mitigation and recovery.
