1. a. Anak ng Bayan

The katipunan (society) had three membership tiers: the first tier, the second tier, and the third tier. During the society meetings, a first-grader was referred to as a "katipon" (Latin for "associate") and wore a black hood with a triangle of white ribbons. the alphabet Z. Ll. B. the Katipunan letter codes, which stood for "anak ng bayan" ("sons of the people"), the katipon's password, were written inside the white ribbons.

2. b. none of the choices

In order to comprehend "the reality behind the text," historical criticism, sometimes referred to as the historical-critical technique or higher criticism, looks into the beginnings of ancient works.

3. c. Primary Sources

The historical records that historians utilize as proof are known as primary sources.

4. c. Political Caricature

The news of the day can be effectively communicated through images. They can analyze and elucidate stories in ways that publications cannot. They personalize the subject matter more effectively than words or video by portraying the unavoidably human nature of their issues. But more often than not, they are attempting to persuade you than to amuse you. A great political cartoon tries to convince you to agree with the cartoonist's point of view while also provoking discussion about current events.

5. d. none of the choices

Because the islanders stole anything they could from his ship as payment for the food and water they had provided the sailors, Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan gave the place the nickname "Isla de Ladrones" (Island of Thieves).

The letters K, K, K, Zz, Ll, and B are arranged in a white triangle on the Bayani (Patriotcrimson's mask). A red sash with green edging is also worn by the Bayani.

7. d. Nobody
