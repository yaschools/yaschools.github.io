7. how was the "distant future" characterized?​


Answer:

This is by the imaginaries of Geoengineering which is they develop the concept of the distant future as a new way of seeing the future in collective efforts. They say that, while a near future is represented in practical terms and concerned with forming expectations and goals under conditions of uncertainty, a distant future is represented in stylized terms and concerned with imagining possibilities under conditions of ambiguity. Their management research on future-oriented action has developed around problems of the near future. And to explore distant futures, they analyze the case of geoengineering, a set of planetary-scale technologies that have been proposed as solutions to the threat of climate change. They would suggest that distant futures orient collective efforts in distinctive ways, not as concrete guides for action but by expressing critiques and alternatives, that can become treated as as-if realities.

18. what is distant narrator?​


A narrative creates a fictional world by presenting the reader with characters, setting, and events. The term narrative distance describes the proximity of the story’s narrator to the subject he is describing. In other words, narrative distance is the distance between the narrator and other elements of fictional world, such as the story’s characters, setting, and events. Narrative distance ranges from small to large.

22. what are the activities in distant modalities​


Answer:

Online Courses

A course that meets entirely online, requiring no traditional classroom time. Proctored exams may be required. A class may also include some online synchronous activity and still be considered online. Complete degree programs are available online. Visit the Missouri State Online website for more information.  

Blended Courses

Courses that integrate online and traditional face-to-face class activities in a planned, pedagogically valuable manner, with a portion of the face-to-face time reduced specifically 30% to 70% by online activities and specified in the course description.

Off-Campus and Interactive Video

ITV courses utilize BearNet, a two-way, audio/video connection offering live communication between students and teachers at multiple locations. Visit the Off-campus and Interactive Video site for more information.

iCourses

iCourses allow students to stream or download complete course lectures to study whenever they want, wherever they want. Visit the iCourses website for more information.

Assessment in Distance Education

Proctored exams may be used for assessing student learning in distance education modalities. The course syllabus must clearly specify any proctored events or planned synchronous activities. Students require adequate notification of these activities and are expected to arrange their schedules well in advance.

28. what are the speed in distant​


Answer:

Speed is a measure of how quickly an object moves from one place to another. It is equal to the distance traveled divided by the time. ... To find the speed, distance is over time in the triangle, so speed is distance divided by time. To find distance, speed is beside time, so distance is speed multiplied by time

what is speed upon distance?

The formula for speed is speed = distance ÷ time. To work out what the units are for speed, you need to know the units for distance and time. In this example, distance is in metres (m) and time is in seconds (s), so the units will be in metres per second (m/s).

remember

To solve for speed or rate use the formula for speed, s = d/t which means speed equals distance divided by time. To solve for time use the formula for time, t = d/s which means time equals distance divided by speed.
