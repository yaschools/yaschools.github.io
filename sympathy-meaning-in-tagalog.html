10. does sympathy mean feeling sorrow of another person?justify your answer​


Answer:

Sympathy is the feeling of understanding and caring for someone else's feelings, experiences, and situation. It is the ability to put oneself in another person's shoes and to feel compassion or concern for them. Sympathy can include feeling sorrow for another person, but it is not limited to that emotion. It can also include feeling empathy, which is the ability to understand and share the feelings of another person.

Sympathy can also be defined as an act of kindness or support offered to someone who is experiencing difficulty or hardship, it can be an emotional response to another person’s joy or happiness.

In summary, sympathy is a broader term that encompasses a range of emotions and actions related to caring for and understanding others, and it can include feeling sorrow for another person, but it is not limited to that.

16. What is the meaning of empathy and sympathy?


Answer:

Empathy means experiencing someone else's feelings. It comes from the German Einfühlung, or 'feeling into. ' It requires an emotional component of really feeling what the other person is feeling. Sympathy, on the other hand, means understanding someone else's suffering.

Explanation:

Empathy
- is the capacity to understand or feel what another person is experiencing from within their frame of reference, that is, the capacity to place oneself in another's position.
- the ability to understand and share the feelings of another.

Sympathy
- is the perception, understanding, and reaction to the distress or need of another life form.
- feelings of pity and sorrow for someone else's misfortune.
