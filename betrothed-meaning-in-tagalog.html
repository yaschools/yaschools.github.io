Meeting the Family

Answer:

One of the well-known practices we Filipinos do is when a person visits the parent’s house whenever a couple is about to be wed. It is a way of paying respect and asking for the whole family’s blessing. It serves also as an introduction of the new addition to the family, it is called “Pamamanhikan”. The groom-to-be can bring gifts and stuff for the family to have.

Why would “Pamamanhikan” even be done by the groom?

It serves as a respect to acknowledge the groom-to-be It grants full approval of the family members It is tradition to do such

Answering the statement:

The answer is (✓), it is a good Filipino trait for a groom-to-be to visit the woman’s parents. We must value every family member and respect whatever their decisions will be. A family is the most important relationship we had and will have throughout our life.

Other good points of “Pamamanhikan”:

It helps you come out as a respectable man The groom shows great value of family tradition It is an act of respect to the family and to the long-time tradition

A Life Full of Love in Savitri & Satyavan story

King Dyumatsena was of the character of a wise king who knew when it was time for him to leave the throne. Out of love for his people, when his ability to lead was reduced due to blindness, he was willing to leave the palace to live as an ordinary person by praying all the time until death came to pick him up later.

Satyavan is the character of a teenager who is devoted to his parents and is willing to leave the luxuries of the palace to protect his parents. As a teenager, he definitely wanted to enjoy a life full of charm. But he remembers, his existence in the world is through his parents. His love and affection for his parents made him live a simple happy life with his parents.

Savitri wanted to give meaning to the life she had been bestowed with, so that she could gracefully look at Yama when he came to fetch her. What dies is the body, the soul never dies, in life only love is eternal, everything else is only temporary.
