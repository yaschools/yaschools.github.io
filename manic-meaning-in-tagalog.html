18. The compound lithium carbonate, used to treat manic depression, has the formula of Li2CO3. Calculate the number of formula units of lithium carbonate present in a 0.500g sample of lithium carbonate.


Solution:

First, we calculate the molar mass of Li₂CO₃. We write

molar mass = (6.941 g × 2) + (12.01 g × 1) + (16.00 g × 3)

molar mass = 73.89 g

Second, we now know that there are 73.89 g of Li₂CO₃ in a mole of Li₂CO₃ and there are 6.022 × 10²³ formula units of Li₂CO₃ in a mole of Li₂CO₃. The conversion steps are

grams of Li₂CO₃ → moles of Li₂CO₃ → number of formula units of Li₂CO₃

Finally, we combine these steps into one equation. Therefore,

[tex]\text{number of Li₂CO₃ formula units = 0.500 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol Li₂CO₃}}{\text{73.89 g Li₂CO₃}} × \frac{\text{6.022 × 10²³ Li₂CO₃ formula units}}{\text{1 mol Li₂CO₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{\text{number of Li₂CO₃ formula units = 4.07 × 10²¹ formula units}}[/tex]

22. A kind of mental disorder that is also called manic-depressive disorder.​


Answer:

BIPOLAR DISORDER

Explanation:

Bipolar disorder was formerly called manic depression. It is a form of major affective disorder, or mood disorder, defined by manic or hypomanic episodes (changes from one's normal mood accompanied by high energy states). Bipolar disorder is a serious condition. Mania often involves sleeplessness, sometimes for days, along with hallucinations, psychosis, grandiose delusions, or paranoid rage. In addition, depressive episodes can be more devastating and harder to treat than in people who never have manias or hypomanias.
