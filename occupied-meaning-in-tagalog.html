5. Did you mean: A gas sample of 40.0°C occupies a volume of 2.32L.If the temperature is raised to 75.0°C,what will the volume be,assuming the pressure remains constant?​


Given:

V₁ = 2.32 L

T₁ = 40.0°C + 273.15 = 313.15 K

T₂ = 75.0°C + 273.15 = 348.15 K

Required:

V₂

Solution:

V₁ / T₁ = V₂ / T₂

V₂T₁ = V₁T₂

V₂ = V₁T₂ / T₁

V₂ = (2.32 L)(348.15 K) / 313.15 K

V₂ = 2.58 L

Answer:

V₂ = 2.58 L

29. A 350 mL sample of argon gas is collected at 295k and 99.3 kPA.what volume would this gas occupy at STP?STP means standard temperature and pressure (1atm and 273k)


Good Day...

Problem:

A 350 mL sample of argon gas is collected at 295k and 99.3 kPA.what volume would this gas occupy at STP?STP means standard temperature and pressure (1atm and 273k).

We will first find the amount of gas of argon occupying 350 ml at 295K and 93.3 kPa.We will convert 350 ml to L. (L= 350 ml / 1000 = .35 L)We will used the value of R which is = 8.31 L.kPa/K.mol

Given Data:

V (volume) = 0.35 L                                  n (amount of gas) = ? unknown      

P (pressure) = 99.3 kPa                            T (temperature) = 295 K

R (constant for pressure in  kPa) =  8.31 L.kPa/K.mol

Solution:

We will used the PV=nRT

To solve for n we will used n = PV/RT

n = PV/RT

  = 99.3 kPa × 0.35 L / 8.31 L.kPa/K.mol × 295 K

  =  34.755 ÷ 2451.45 /mol

n = 0.014 mol

we will find the volume of 0.014 mol of argon at stp1 mole of any gas occupies a volume of 22.4 L at STP.We can used the conversion factor 22.4L / 1mol

Solution:

0.014 mol × 22.4 L / 1 mol = 0.3136 L  (cancer unit mol)

Answer:

Argon will occupy a volume of 0.3136 L at STP
