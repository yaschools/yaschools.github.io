28. He was in chains when he got caught for his crimes. Is this symbolism, metaphor, imagery, irony? ​


Answer:

The sentence "He was in chains when he got caught for his crimes" is not an example of symbolism, metaphor, imagery, or alliteration. It is an example of irony because the person who committed the crimes is now restrained and unable to commit further crimes. Irony is a literary device that involves a contrast between what is expected and what actually happens. In this case, the expectation is that the person who committed the crimes would be free, but the reality is that they are in chains. Irony can be used to create a sense of humor, to emphasize a point, or to create a sense of tension or drama.
