7. Rogers ordered several meters of silk cloth to Tony for his project in school. Unfortunately, Tony has only 10 meters of cloth in stock. Tony bought the remaining lengths of the cloth to Stephen, a wholesaler, for P300. Tony then sold the cloth from Stephen along with the original stock that he has for a total of P750. If Tony's price per meter of cloth is at least P10 more than Stephen's price, what possible lengths of cloth did Tony buy from Stephen?​


Answer:

Let X be the number of meters of cloth that Tony bought from Stephen.

Tony paid P300 for X meters of cloth, so the price per meter of cloth that Tony paid is 300/X.

Tony sold the cloth that he bought from Stephen along with the original 10 meters of cloth that he had for a total of P750.

The total price for the original 10 meters of cloth that Tony had is 750 - 300 = P450.

The price per meter of cloth that Tony sold the cloth that he bought from Stephen for is 450/X.

The price per meter of cloth that Tony sold the cloth that he bought from Stephen for is at least P10 more than the price per meter of cloth that Tony paid, so 450/X >= 300/X + 10.

This simplifies to 150/X >= 10.

Dividing both sides by 15, we get 10/X >= 1.

Since X is the number of meters of cloth that Tony bought from Stephen, X must be at least 10 meters. Therefore, the possible lengths of cloth that Tony bought from Stephen are 10 meters or more.

18. "save yourself with a gazelle from the hand of the hunter ,like a bird from the hand of the fowler​" what does it mean?


Answer:

The general context of this passage is that of being trapped in a bad loan, by cosigning or offering collateral for another person (Proverbs 6:1–4). Scripture doesn't forbid all forms of borrowing or lending, but it does speak against unfair interest (Deuteronomy 23:19–20) and other unreasonable approaches. Solomon tells his son in this verse to free himself from the trap he fell into by "[putting] up security" for an unreliable person.

In Bible times, a person in default of payment might become the creditor's slave and lose everything he had. Even his family members might be taken as slaves by the creditor. There are risks associated with cosigning loans today, but in most modern cultures, those pale in comparison to what happened to debtors in the past.

Solomon uses two similes to emphasize the speed with which a cosigner should act. Gazelles are the smallest antelope in Palestine, noted for beauty and speed (1 Chronicles 12:8). When a gazelle was aware of danger, it would dart away from the danger as quickly as possible. Similarly, when hunted, birds try to fly away as quickly as possible. A cosigner may not realize the danger involved in cosigning for a high-interest loan, but his lack of awareness does not reduce the risk.

29. The rationale of positioning a conscious client with functional gag reflex into semi-fowlers with head turned to one side for oral suctioning is


Answer:

The purpose of oral suctioning is to maintain a patent airway and improve oxygenation by removing mucous secretions and foreign material (vomit or gastric secretions) from the mouth and throat (oropharynx). Oral suction is the use of a rigid plastic suction catheter, known as a yankauer (see Figure 5.3), to remove pharyngeal secretions through the mouth (Perry et al., 2014). The suction catheter has a large hole for the thumb to cover to initiate suction, along with smaller holes along the end, which mucous enters when suction is applied. The oral suctioning catheter is not used for tracheotomies due to its large size. Oral suctioning is useful to clear secretions from the mouth in the event a patient is unable to remove secretions or foreign matter by effective coughing. Patients who benefit the most include those with CVAs, drooling, impaired cough reflex related to age or condition, or impaired swallowing (Perry et al., 2014).
