2. 13. cigarette Smoking increases one's risk of a stroke. His or her chance of developing brain aneurysm is raised. Aneurysm is an arterial dilation caused by a weaking in the blood vessel wall.​


Explanation:

Yes, the statement is true. Cigarette smoking has numerous harmful effects on the body, and one of them is an increased risk of stroke. Smoking can cause atherosclerosis, which is the buildup of plaque in the arteries that can lead to narrowing and hardening of the blood vessels. This condition can cause high blood pressure, which can lead to a stroke. Additionally, smoking can also weaken the walls of the blood vessels, making them more susceptible to developing aneurysms. A brain aneurysm is a particularly dangerous type of aneurysm that can rupture and cause bleeding in the brain, leading to a stroke or even death.

24. What is aneurysm? please answer it​


An aneurysm is the enlargement of an artery caused by weakness in the arterial wall. Often there are no symptoms, but a ruptured aneurysm can lead to fatal complications. An aneurysm refers to a weakening of an artery wall that creates a bulge, or distention, of the artery.

An aneurysm occurs when part of an artery wall weakens, allowing it to balloon out or widen abnormally. The causes of aneurysms are sometimes unknown. Some may be congenital, meaning a person is born with them. Aortic disease or an injury may also cause an aneurysm.

26. what are the causes of aneurysm?


Aneurysms have a variety of causes including high blood pressure and atherosclerosis, trauma, heredity, and abnormal blood flow at the junction where arteries come together. There are other rare causes of aneurysms. Mycotic aneurysms arecaused by infections of the artery wall.

Causes of aneurysms

a weakness in the blood vessel wall that is present from birth (congenital aneurysm) high blood pressure (hypertension) over many years resulting in damage and weakening of blood vessels. fatty plaques (atherosclerosis) resulting in a weakness of the blood vessel wall.

Answer:

Causes  

a weakness in the blood vessel wall that is present from birth (congenital aneurysm) high blood pressure (hypertension) over many years resulting in damage and weakening of blood vessels. fatty plaques (atherosclerosis) resulting in a weakness of the blood vessel wall.

EFFECTS:

The bulging aneurysm can put pressure on the nerves or brain tissue. It may also burst or rupture, spilling blood into the surrounding tissue (called a hemorrhage). A ruptured aneurysm can cause serious health problems such as hemorrhagic stroke, brain damage, coma, and even death.
