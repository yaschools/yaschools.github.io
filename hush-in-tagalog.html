It's actually empowering because it promotes the deaf community. Throughout the story, it shows how deaf people can do almost normal things like cooking, feeding the cat, defending herself and also living alone. It is new to see movies that feature people with differences, that's why it makes the story interesting because it's odd for us to see that a deaf person is living alone in the woods.
