20. Which of the following plate interaction produced the Philippine Trench? A. the subduction of the Eurasian Plate beneath the Philippine Sea Plate B. the subduction of the Philippine Sea Plate beneath the Sundaland Plate C. the subduction of the Pacific Plate beneath the Philippine Sea Plate D. the subduction of the Philippine Sea Plate beneath the Pacific Plate​


Answer:

B. the subduction of the Philippine Sea Plate beneath the Sundaland Plate

Explanation:

The Manila Trench results from eastward subduction of the Eurasian Plate (Sundaland Block) beneath the Philippine Mobile Belt. Subduction along the north-trending trench started in late-Oligocene to early-Miocene. It has an average subduction rate at 1 – 2 cm/year, slowing towards the north.


21. what s beneath the earth?​


Answer:

The crust and the lithosphere below (the crust plus the upper mantle) is made of several 'tectonic plates'. These move slowly across the surface of the planet, and most of Earth's volcanoes and earthquakes occur at the boundaries between tectonic plates.
