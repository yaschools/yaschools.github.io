Answer:

. Ascetic: a person who practices self-discipline and abstains from worldly pleasures, often for religious reasons; general class: Person, specific detail: Practices self-discipline and abstains from worldly pleasures.

Answer:

There is something that the pairs of statements all have in common. The use of words like "may" or "seem" are evident in the first one. Meanwhile, the second one does not really on them and simply state a fact.

The first statement in all of these examples is always careful about expressing a possible interpretation. It shows that there is a chance that it is not correct. This is a good way to express some doubt and avoid sounding like an authority on the matter.

On the other hand, the second statement always expresses something certainty without any room for doubt. It is clear that the person who said this has evidence to back up the claim. It always sounds like a fact.
