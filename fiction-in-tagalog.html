It may be overwhelming to think about all the different types of literature, but there are actually two main groups all literature can be categorized under. These two categories are fiction and nonfiction. Fiction includes all written works that are invented or made-up by the author. This includes novels, short stories, and poems. Nonfiction, then, comprises of the written works based on real events. In this way, literature that is nonfiction can help us understand our world. 3: The other type of nonfiction is informative nonfiction. The purpose of informative nonfiction is to explain or inform about a concept or situation. This type of nonfiction exists to provide the reader with factual information. Informative nonfiction includes articles and interviews.

This means that literary nonfiction includes the elements of fiction, like characters, setting, and plot; however, in nonfiction these elements must be real and not imaginary. Once upon a time, there was a small village nestled at the foot of a majestic mountain. The villagers lived a simple and peaceful life, but they always felt a sense of awe and respect for the mountain, which they believed was home to powerful spirits.

One day, a group of strangers arrived in the village. They were a team of scientists who had come to study the mountain's geology and wildlife. The villagers were curious about the strangers, but also a bit wary. They had heard stories about outsiders who had come to exploit their resources and desecrate their sacred sites.

The leader of the team, a woman named Dr. Lee, approached the village elder, a wise and respected man named Kofi. "Greetings, elder," she said with a warm smile. "We come in peace and with respect for your ways. We only wish to learn more about your beautiful mountain and its secrets."

Kofi studied her for a moment, then nodded. "I see no harm in sharing our knowledge with those who seek it with humility and openness," he said. Dr. Lee listened attentively and nodded. "I understand, elder. We will do our best to honor your traditions and protect the mountain's integrity. We are not here to exploit or harm, but to learn and share."

Over the next few weeks, the scientists worked hard to collect data and samples from the mountain, while also respecting the villagers' customs and beliefs. They even invited some of the villagers to join them in their research, and showed them how their findings could benefit the local ecosystem and economy.

As they packed up to leave, Dr. Lee approached Kofi once more. "Thank you for your hospitality and wisdom, elder," she said. "We have learned so much from you and your people. We hope to continue this partnership and friendship in the future."

Kofi smiled and clasped her hand. "The mountain has spoken to me, Dr. Lee," he said. "It has told me that your intentions are pure and your hearts are open. You and your team are always welcome here, as long as you remember to listen to the mountain's voice and honor its spirit."

27. the ratio of the number of fiction books to the number of non-fiction books in a library is 5:2. If there are 1421 fiction and non-fiction books altogethee, how many more fiction than non fiction books are there in the library?​


Answer:

There are 609 more fiction books than non fiction books in the library.

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio Word Problem

What is ratio?

Ratio indicates how many times one number contains another. It shows the the relative sizes of two or more values.

The problem above is a ratio word problem since it is asking for how many more fiction than non fiction books there are in the library based on the ratio of the books.

Given:

ratio of fiction books to non-fiction books - 5:2

total number of books - 1421

Equation:

Let x be the number.

5x + 2x = 1421

Solution:

5x + 2x = 1421

7x = 1421

7x/7 = 1421/7

x = 203

Fiction Books

5x = 5 (203) = 1015

There are 1015 fiction books.

Non-Fiction Books

2x = 2 (203) = 406

There are 406 non-fiction books.

To know how many more fiction than non fiction books there are in the library, we need to subtract.

1015 - 406 = 609

Final Answer:

There are 609 more fiction books than non fiction books in the library.

Checking:

5x + 2x = 1421

1015 + 406 = 1421

1421 = 1421 ✔

Cross multiply.

5:2 = 1015:406

2 × 1015 = 5 × 406

2030 = 2030 ✔
