Answer:

Sucrose Solution

The percentage concentration in the following solution, given 2g sucrose in 80g water is 2.5%.

Explanation:

Here is the solution to the problem:

Given: 2 grams of sucrose and 80 grams water

Find: the percentage concentration in the solution

Solution:

x = 2 grams / 80 grams

x = 0.025

x = 2.5%

Therefore, the answer is 2.5%.

When looking for the percentage concentration, what you need to do is to divide the amount of solute into the total volume of the solvent. By dividing the solute from the solvent, you can get the information on how much concentration is present in the solution. This solution can also be derived, if other elements in the equation will go missing.

.What is the percentage concentration in the following solution: 450 grams of flour in 1 liter of water? Note:1L=1000g

Answer:

percent concentration = 31.03%

Explanation:

Calculating the mass of the solvent

mass solvent = 1 L × (1000 g / 1 L)

mass solvent = 1000 g

Note: The density of water is 1 g/m L or 1000 g/L

Calculating the mass of the solution

mass solution = mass solute + mass solvent

mass solution = 450 g + 1000 g

mass solution = 1450 g

Calculating the percent concentration

percent concentration = (mass solute / mass solution) × 100

percent concentration = (450 g / 1450 g) × 100

percent concentration = 31.03%
