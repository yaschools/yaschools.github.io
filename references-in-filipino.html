16. Discuss three (3) pre-colonial Filipino cultures depicted in Biag ni Lam-ang. Refer to the following summary of the Filipino epic.​


Biag ni Lam-ang is a pre-colonial Filipino epic that tells the story of a young warrior named Lam-ang who possesses extraordinary strength and magical abilities. The epic is rich in cultural details and provides insight into the beliefs, customs, and traditions of pre-colonial Filipino societies. Here are three pre-colonial Filipino cultures depicted in the epic:

Animism - Animism is the belief that everything in nature has a spirit or soul. In the epic, Lam-ang's mother is said to have come from a family of shamans who can communicate with spirits. Lam-ang himself is said to be born with extraordinary strength and magical abilities, which are attributed to his mother's connection with the spiritual world.

Courtship and Marriage - Courtship and marriage are important cultural practices in pre-colonial Filipino societies. In the epic, Lam-ang's courtship of his wife is depicted in detail, including the rituals and traditions that must be observed before the couple can be married. This includes the exchange of gifts, the seeking of blessings from the gods, and the performance of ceremonial dances and songs.

Bravery and Heroism - Bravery and heroism are highly valued in pre-colonial Filipino societies. Lam-ang is depicted as a fearless warrior who uses his strength and magical abilities to defend his people from enemies and monsters. His exploits are celebrated in songs and stories, and he becomes a legendary hero in Filipino folklore.

Overall, Biag ni Lam-ang provides a fascinating glimpse into the rich and diverse cultures of pre-colonial Filipino societies. It highlights the importance of animism, courtship and marriage, and bravery and heroism in these cultures and underscores their enduring influence on Filipino culture and identity
