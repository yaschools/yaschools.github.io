Answer:

1.Plan beforehand. If you're well-prepared, freezing up is less likely to happen. Practice whenever you have the chance, whether it's by yourself or in front of people. Practice until you are at ease and prepared. The assurance that comes from knowing you are ready helps to ease tensions better than anything.

2.Energize yourself. Instead of obsessing on potential problems, boost your positive attitude. A football team should be observed before entering the pitch. To raise their spirits and foster a sense of unity, they gather as a group and pray or sing a team song. You can do this whether you're giving a solo or ensemble performance. Ask a friend to motivate you. Also give one to yourself. Remind yourself that you can do it: "I got this," "I'm ready to do this — here goes!" or "This is going to be fun!"

3.Learn how to relax. Olympic gymnasts and music soloists are only two examples of young performers who emphasize how crucial it is to prepare both for the performance itself and the butterflies that come before it. There may be a long wait until your chance to perform at some competitions. Some folks study yoga and breathing exercises, bring motivational pictures, or create a relaxing soundtrack to help them unwind. While some people need to be calm and motionless to unwind, others need to be active. Make a strategy to apply the technique you discover works best for you before a big performance.

4.Be not terrified of the jittery sensation. Do not panic if you get performance anxiety! Do not be deterred or intimidated by the sensation. Just leave it alone. Remind yourself that it's normal and that it's just your nervous system preparing you for action. Recognize that it is up to you to control it for your benefit. To achieve this, employ your relaxing techniques and uplifting pep talks. So go for it!

Maintain your health. As you spend too much time on rehearsals and preparation before large performances, it's tempting to neglect taking care of oneself. Get enough sleep and eat a balanced meal before your performance to feel and look your best. Additionally, exercise can improve your mood.

5.It's simple to neglect taking care of oneself before major performances since you spend so much time practicing and rehearsing. If you get adequate sleep and eat a good meal before your performance, you'll look and feel your best. Along with sleep and nutrition, exercise is a great strategy to prevent those stress hormones from out of control and can make you feel better.
