24. how can you teach illiterates or non readers to read?


Answer:

Teaching non-readers or illiterate adults to read can be a challenge, but it is definitely possible. There are several methods that can be used to help adult learners become literate. One of these methods is called the "direct approach," which focuses on teaching adults the basic skills of reading, such as recognizing individual letters, their sounds, and how to blend them together to form words. This approach may also involve teaching phonics, which are the rules of reading. Another approach is called the "indirect approach," which focuses on teaching adults to use context clues to guess the meaning of words, rather than sounding them out. This approach also teaches adults how to read for meaning.
