4. Unwinding of DNA is done by which enzyme​


Answer:

During DNA replication, DNA helicases unwind DNA at positions called origins where synthesis will be initiated. DNA helicase continues to unwind the DNA forming a structure called the replication fork, which is named for the forked appearance of the two strands of DNA as they are unzipped apart.

Answer:

Explanation:

It is the enzyme that unwinds the DNA double helix by breaking the hydrogen bonds down the center of the strand. It begins at a site called the origin of replication, and it creates a replication fork by separating the two sides of the parental DNA.


23. What does replication unwinding requires


DNA replication process is very vital to the survival of species. Not only does DNA replication page way to the synthesis of proteins, it also repairs damage for the organism to remain living. However, it is important that DNA replication is a semiconservative replication. Meaning, each copy of the strand contains one original and one new strand.

DNA replication is composed of the following steps: initiation, elongation, and termination. Let's focus on initiation for your question.

Before DNA replicates, it needs to unwind. This unwinding is initiated by the DNA helicases, special enzymes which are able to break the bonds in the DNA to unable it to unwind.  The topoisomerase also helps by relaxing the unwinded strand of the DNA. These two enzymes prepare the DNA for the elongation process.
