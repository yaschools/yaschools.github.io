24. A squirrel family collected 172 nuts to store for the winter. They spread the nuts out evenly among their 666 favorite spots. The squirrels ate the last few nuts that didn't divide evenly . Sadly, a crow stole half the nuts from one of the spots.How many nuts did the crow steal?


Answer:

A squirrel family collected 172 nuts to store for the winter. They spread the nuts out evenly among their 66 favorite spots. The squirrels ate the last few nuts that didn't divide evenly . Sadly, a crow stole half the nuts from one of the spots. How many nuts did the crow steal?

The crow stole 1 nut.

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?

How many nuts did the crow steal?

What are given?

number of nuts - 172

number of favorite spots - 66

Solution:

To know the number of nuts that the crow stole from one of the squirrels' favorite spots, we need to know first the number of nuts that the squirrels put in every spots.

To do that we need to divide the nuts by the number of their favorite spots.

172 ÷ 66 = 2 r. 52

* The squirrels ate the 52 nuts that didn't divide evenly.

* There are 2 nuts for every spot.

The problem says that the crow stole half the nuts from one of the spots, so get the half of 2.

2 ÷ 2 = 1

The crow stole 1 nut.
