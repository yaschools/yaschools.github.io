28. 2.What makes Testimonials different from bias ? Give examples and explain .​


Answer:

Testimonials and bias are different because testimonials are statements or endorsements from individuals who have had direct experience with a product or service, while bias refers to an inclination or preference towards something based on personal beliefs or opinions.

Testimonials are often used by businesses to showcase positive feedback from satisfied customers. These testimonials are typically intended to provide social proof and increase trust in the brand. For example, a website may display customer reviews of a product, which can help potential buyers make informed decisions.

Bias, on the other hand, is a personal inclination or preference that can influence judgment or decision-making. This bias can come in many forms, such as political bias, gender bias, or racial bias. For example, a person may have a bias towards a particular political party, causing them to only seek out and trust information that aligns with their beliefs.

It's important to note that testimonials can still be biased if they are cherry-picked or edited to only showcase positive feedback. In order to avoid bias, it's important to seek out a diverse range of opinions and perspectives.

Overall, while testimonials and bias may seem similar in that they are both based on personal experience, testimonials are intended to provide objective feedback and increase trust, while bias refers to a personal preference that can influence decision-making
