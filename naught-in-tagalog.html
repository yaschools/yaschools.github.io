6. An infinitely long, very thin metal tube with radius R= 980 mm. The figure below shows a section of it. If the linear charge density of the cylinder is λ= 2.43 x10 -7 C/m, what is the appropriate magnitude of the electric field at radial distance r= 2R? The value of the permittivity constant (ε0= Epsilon naught or permittivity of free space) is ε0= 8.85 x 10 -12 C2/ N.m2.


Answer:

The magnitude of the electric field of a metal tube is 2229.6 N/C

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the electric field of a line charge of a metal tube. To do this we will use the formula:

[tex]E=\frac{\lambda }{2\pi r\epsilon_0 }[/tex]

where

E     is the electric field, unit is in N/C

λ     is the linear charge density

r      is the radial distance, unit is in meter, m

ε₀    is the permittivity constant in free space, 8.85 x 10⁻¹² C²/ N.m²

For the given information

R= 980 mm = 0.98 m

r = 2R = 2(0.98 m) = 1.96 m

λ = 2.43 x 10⁻⁷ C/m

E = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve for the electric field of the metal tube using the formula, we have:

[tex]E=\frac{\lambda }{2\pi r\epsilon_0 }[/tex]

Then substitute the given information, we have:

[tex]E=\frac{2.43 x10^{-7} C/m }{2\pi(1.96m)(8.85 x 10^{-12}C^2/ Nm^2}[/tex]

E = 2229.6 N/C

Therefore, the magnitude of the electric field of a metal tube is 2229.6 N/C

Gauss's law is used to calculate electric fields.

Can be formulated

[tex]\rm \Phi E=\dfrac{Q}{\epsilon_o}[/tex]   (equation 1)

ΦE : electric flux

Q is the total charge

ε0 is the electric constant

But it is limited to certain symmetries, namely cylindrical, planar and spherical symmetry

The problem above is in the form of an infinite rod with Cylindrical Symmetry and Coaxial Cylinder

The amount of charge by the Gaussian surface (Coaxial Cylinder), of radius r and length l

q = λl  (equation 2)

The flux through the Gaussian surface for a uniformly charged rod.

[tex]\rm \Phi E=E.2.\pi.r.l[/tex]  (equation 3)

Combination of the 3 equations above

[tex]\rm E(2.\pi.r.l)=\dfrac{\lambda.l}{\epsilon o}[/tex]

[tex]\rm E=\dfrac{\lambda}{\epsilon o.2.\pi.r}[/tex]

given

λ = 2.43 x10⁻⁷ C / m,

r = R = 980 mm = 0.98 m ⇒ 2r = 1.96 m

ε0 = 8.85 x 10⁻¹² C² / N.m².
