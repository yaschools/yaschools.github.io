13. 1. What is pain?2. What causes pain?3. What are the 2 types of pain?4. Differentiate acute from chronic pain?5. What are the different types acute of pain?6. How does a doctor diagnose pain?7. How can we measure pain?8. What are other indicators of pain?9. What are treatment of acute and chronic pain?​


Answer:

1. What is pain?

Answer: Pain is an uncomfortable feeling that tells you something may be wrong.

2. What causes pain?

Answer: People feel pain when specific nerves called nociceptors. For example, touching a hot surface will send a message through a reflex arc in the spinal cord and cause an immediate contraction of the muscles.

3. What are the 2 types of pain?

Answer: There are two main types of pain: nociceptive and neuropathic.

4. Differentiate acute from chronic pain?

Answer: Acute vs. Chronic Pain. Pain is a sign that something has happened, that something is wrong.

5. What are the different types acute of pain?

Answer: Acute pain is short-term pain that comes on suddenly and has a specific cause, usually tissue injury.

6. How does a doctor diagnose pain?

Answer: If you have pain, your doctor has many ways to find out what's causing it. They will ask about your symptoms and your medical history, including any illness, injury, or surgery.

7. How can we measure pain?

Answer: A dolorimeter is an instrument used to measure pain threshold and pain tolerance. Dolorimetry has been defined as "the measurement of pain sensitivity or pain intensity".

8. What are other indicators of pain?

Answer: There are some signs and symptoms that a person may exhibit if they are in pain that can clue you in: Facial grimacing or a frown.

9. What are treatment of acute and chronic pain?

Answer: Acetaminophen is usually recommended as a first line treatment for mild to moderate pain, such as from a skin injury, headache or musculoskeletal condition.
