11. Antihypertensive agent recommended for the protection of cardiovascular diseases is: a) Calcium channel blockers b) Diuretics c) ACE inhibitors d) Alpha antagonists


Answer:

c) ACE inhibitors

Explanation:

c) ACE inhibitors are the antihypertensive agents that are recommended for the protection of cardiovascular diseases. ACE inhibitors work by blocking the action of the angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), which converts angiotensin I to angiotensin II, a potent vasoconstrictor. By blocking the production of angiotensin II, ACE inhibitors can lower blood pressure and improve blood flow to the heart and other organs. They are often prescribed to patients with hypertension, heart failure, and diabetes, as these conditions increase the risk of cardiovascular disease. Calcium channel blockers, diuretics, and alpha antagonists are also used to treat hypertension, but ACE . inhibitors are the preferred agents for cardiovascular protection

Here is a possible experimental protocol, ethical considerations, and expected outcomes for exploring the pharmacological mechanism of action of a patented polymer matrix-loaded alkaloidal extract in terms of diuretic, anti-inflammatory, and analgesic activities:

Diuretic Activity:

Experimental Protocol:

Rats (male or female) are randomly assigned into different groups

A control group of rats is given normal saline

A treatment group of rats is given the polymer matrix-loaded alkaloidal extract

24-hour urine output is measured in both groups and compared.

Ethical Considerations:

Use of animals in experiments must be approved by an institutional animal care and use committee

The animals must be handled and treated humanely

The number of animals used should be minimized

Expected Outcome:

If the alkaloidal extract is found to increase the 24-hour urine output significantly compared to the control group, this would indicate diuretic activity.

Anti-inflammatory Activity:

Experimental Protocol:

Rats (male or female) are randomly assigned into different groups

A control group of rats is given normal saline

A treatment group of rats is given the polymer matrix-loaded alkaloidal extract

Inflammation is induced in both groups of rats by an injection of carrageenan in one of the hind paws

The paw volume is measured at regular intervals to assess inflammation

Ethical Considerations:

Use of animals in experiments must be approved by an institutional animal care and use committee

The animals must be handled and treated humanely

The number of animals used should be minimized

Expected Outcome:

If the alkaloidal extract is found to significantly decrease the paw volume in the treated group compared to the control group, this would indicate anti-inflammatory activity.

Analgesic Activity:

Experimental Protocol:

Rats (male or female) are randomly assigned into different groups

A control group of rats is given normal saline

A treatment group of rats is given the polymer matrix-loaded alkaloidal extract

The pain response is assessed in both groups by applying a noxious stimulus (such as heat or pressure) to the hind paw

The response time (i.e., the time taken for the rat to withdraw its paw) is measured

Ethical Considerations:

Use of animals in experiments must be approved by an institutional animal care and use committee

The animals must be handled and treated humanely

The number of animals used should be minimized

Expected Outcome:

If the alkaloidal extract is found to significantly increase the response time in the treated group compared to the control group, this would indicate analgesic activity.
