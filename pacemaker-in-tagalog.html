1. What is a pacemaker ​


Answer:

A pacemaker is a small device that's placed in the chest or abdomen to help control abnormal heart rhythms. This device uses electrical pulses to prompt the heart to beat at a normal rate. Pacemakers are used to treat arrhythmias (ah-RITH-me-ahs). Arrhythmias are problems with the rate or rhythm of the heartbeat.

Answer:

A cardiac pacemaker, is a medical device that generates electrical impulses delivered by electrodes to cause the heart muscle chambers to contract and therefore pump blood; by doing so this device replaces and/or regulates the function of the electrical conduction system of the heart.

19. Plutonium-239 is used as the energy source for heart pacemakers and space probes. it decays by alpha emission. the nuclear mass of plutonium-239 is 239.006 amu, 234.9934 amu for uranium-235 and 4.00150 amu for helium-4.Calculate the energy lost or gained by 1 mole of plutonium-239.​


The energy lost by 1 mole of Plutonium-239 : -9.699 x 10¹¹ J/mol

Further explanation

Required

the energy lost or gained by 1 mole of plutonium-239.

Analysis​

The energy change of the nuclear reaction is equal to the mass destroyed times c²(Einstein’s equation) :

[tex]\tt \boxed{\bold{E=m.c^2}}[/tex]

Solution

Reaction

₉₄²³⁹Pu⇒₂⁴He+₉₂²³⁵U

Calculate the change in mass (mass of products - mass of reactants).

mass of reactant(Plutonium-239) :

[tex]\tt 239.006~amu[/tex]

mass of products(He+U) :

[tex]\tt 4.00150+234.9934=238.9949~amu[/tex]

Δm=238.9949-239.006=-0.0111 amu

Conversion the mass to kg

1 amu =  1.66054 x 10⁻²⁷kg

[tex]\tt -0.0111\times 1.66054\times 10^{-27}=-1.84\times 10^{-29}[/tex]

Calculate energy lost(c=3.10⁸ m/s)

[tex]\tt E=-1.84\times 10^{-29}\times (3.10^8)^2\\\\E=-1.656\times 10^{-12}~J[/tex]

Calculate/mol plutonium-239.

[tex]\tt E=-1.656\times 10^{-12}J/atom\times 6.02\times 10^{23}atoms/mol=-9.969\times 10^{11}~J/mol[/tex]

30. Electromagnetic waves that can interfere with a pacemaker.


Electromagnetic waves that can interfere with a pacemaker

Implantable cardioverter defibrillators (ICDs) and pacemakers can be hampered by a variety of equipment and machines.

The electromagnetic waves emitted by such devices can interfere with the proper operation of your ICD or pacemaker. Try to stay away from them, or at the very least limit your exposure to them.

Mobile phones

ICDs and pacemakers are both particularly vulnerable to wireless signals from the antennae of phones accessible in the United States.

Radios

Amateur "ham" radios and citizen's band (or CB) radios pose little or no risk to pacemakers. However, they can have an impact on how well an ICD works.

Metal detectors are used to ensure security.

Metal detector interactions are unlikely to induce clinically significant symptoms in the majority of individuals.(PACEMAKER)
