20. why you disagree that is digital technology make children's lives better ?disagree​


Answer:

I disagree that digital technology makes children's lives better, for several reasons:

1. Addiction and overuse: Digital technology, particularly smartphones, tablets, and social media, can be addictive and lead to excessive screen time, which can have negative effects on children's physical and mental health, such as obesity, sleep disorders, anxiety, and depression. Children may become isolated or disconnected from the real world, and have difficulty interacting with others face-to-face.

2. Cyberbullying and online harassment: Digital technology can provide a platform for bullies and predators to target and harm children, particularly through social media or online gaming. Children may be subjected to cyberbullying, hate speech, sexual exploitation, or other forms of abuse that can have lasting effects on their self-esteem and well-being.

3. Lack of privacy and security: Digital technology can expose children to risks such as identity theft, cybercrime, or data breaches, particularly if they share personal information or use unsecured websites or apps. Children may not fully understand the consequences of their online actions, and may inadvertently share sensitive information or engage in risky behaviors.

4. Disconnection from nature and physical activity: Digital technology can discourage children from engaging in outdoor activities, sports, or creative play, which are essential for their physical, cognitive, and social development. Children may become sedentary, bored, or uninterested in the natural world, and miss out on important opportunities to explore, learn, and grow.

5. Inequality and access: Digital technology can exacerbate existing social, economic, and cultural inequalities, particularly for children from disadvantaged backgrounds or rural areas who may not have access to high-speed internet, quality devices, or digital skills. Children may face barriers to education, healthcare, or social participation, and may be left behind in the digital age.

Explanation:

Therefore, while digital technology can offer many benefits and opportunities for children, such as access to information, communication, and creativity, it is important to approach it with caution and balance, and to consider the potential risks and challenges as well. Parents, educators, and policymakers need to work together to promote responsible and safe use of digital technology, and to provide children with diverse and enriching experiences that support their well-being and development.

26. plastic should be banned worldwide?disagree-why disagree?​


Answer:

I disagree that plastic should be banned worldwide. While it is true that plastic has negative effects on the environment, such as pollution and harm to wildlife, it also has many important uses in modern society.

Firstly, plastic has revolutionized the healthcare industry with its ability to create sterile and disposable medical equipment. From syringes to IV bags, plastic has helped to prevent the spread of diseases and infections in hospitals and medical facilities.

Secondly, plastic has many practical uses in everyday life, such as in the construction of buildings and transportation vehicles. Plastic is lightweight, durable, and easily molded, making it an ideal material for many applications.

Thirdly, banning plastic would have a significant impact on the global economy, as many industries rely on plastic for manufacturing and packaging. Millions of jobs would be lost and entire industries would be disrupted if plastic were to be banned worldwide.

Instead of a complete ban on plastic, we should focus on reducing our reliance on single-use plastics and finding ways to recycle and dispose of plastics responsibly. This includes investing in more efficient recycling technologies and creating public awareness campaigns to encourage people to reduce their plastic waste.

In conclusion, while plastic has its drawbacks, it is also an important material with many valuable uses. Instead of a blanket ban on plastic, we should focus on finding more sustainable ways to use and dispose of plastic.
