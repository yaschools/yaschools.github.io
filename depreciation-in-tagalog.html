15. Explain the concept of depreciation.​


Answer:

Depreciation definition

The value of these assets decreases over time after their purchase because of wear and tear (i.e. use of the asset) and obsolescence. Depreciation represents the estimate for how much this value has declined in a given fiscal period.

Answer:

Depreciation is a non-cash business expense that is allocated and calculated over the period that an asset is useful to your business. Every business can take advantage of depreciation by deducting the expense of using up a portion of the value of an asset from taxable income. That result is tax savings.

Explanation:

The term depreciation refers to an accounting method used to allocate the cost of a tangible or physical asset over its useful life. Depreciation represents how much of an asset's value has been used. It allows companies to earn revenue from the assets they own by paying for them over a certain period of time.

Because companies don't have to account for them entirely in the year the assets are purchased, the immediate cost of ownership is significantly reduced. Not accounting for depreciation can greatly affect a company's profits. Companies can also depreciate long-term assets for both tax and accounting purposes.

Depreciation can be compared with amortization, which accounts for the change in value over time of intangible assets.

27. LUCKY19 Corporation purchased an office equipment worth 350,000 pesos with the salvage value of 65,000 and estimated useful life of 5 years. 1. Find the following: a. total depreciation b. annual depreciation c. rate of depreciation d. book value after 3 years 2.  Construct a depreciation schedule.​


a. To find the total depreciation, subtract the salvage value from the cost of the equipment: 350,000 pesos - 65,000 pesos = 285,000 pesos.

b. To find the annual depreciation, divide the total depreciation by the useful life of the equipment in years: 285,000 pesos / 5 years = 57,000 pesos/year.

c. To find the rate of depreciation, divide the annual depreciation by the cost of the equipment: 57,000 pesos / 350,000 pesos = 0.162 or 16.2%.

d. To find the book value after 3 years, subtract the accumulated depreciation from the cost of the equipment: 350,000 pesos - (3 years * 57,000 pesos/year) = 199,000 pesos.
